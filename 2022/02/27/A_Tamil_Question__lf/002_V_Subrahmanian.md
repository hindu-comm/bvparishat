//For a masculine or feminine plural subject (such as an explicit or implied “avar”), it would be “அனுப்பிவிட்டார்”.//

  

Here, in the plural, the correct usage would be
“அனுப்பிவிட்டார்கள்/அனுப்பி விட்டனர்” and not“அனுப்பிவிட்டார்” as suggested by you. This latter usage is only for a respectful addressing of a singular person. For example, when I want to simply say about any man, it would be 'அந்த ஆள் வந்தான்.' If I mean a respected elder, I would say: 'அவர் வந்தார்.' And in plural, whether there is respect or not, it would be 'அவர்கள் வந்தனர்/வந்தார்கள்.' ('They came).
Thus,“அனுப்பிவிட்டனர்” would just mean 'they sent him/her, etc.'
“அனுப்பிவிட்டார்” would simply mean 'He, (a respectful person), sent him away.'
