Namo vidvadbhyah  
AcAryAya priyam dhanam Ah.rtya prajAtantum mA vyavacchetsI.h  
(AnuvAka-11,S’ik.sAvallI – TattirIyopani.sad)  
I shall try to explain taking both S’A’nkarabhA.syam as well as  
B.rhadvivara.nam of VidyAra.nya –  
the complete sentence should be like this – AcAryAya priyam  
dhanamAh.rtya vivAham k.rtvA prajAtantum mA vyavacchetsI.h .  
S’Ankaram – AcAryAya AcAryArtham priyam i.s.tam dhanam Ah.rtya AnIya  
datvA. vidyAni. skrayArtham – the sentence , in fact , ends here  
.Ni.skraya.h=pratyupakAra.h(svayamgrahAs’le.sasukhena ni.skrayam –  
MAgha – 1)  
Further , S’ankara refers to AnuvAka 9 – prajA ca svAdhyAyapravacane  
ca; prajanas’ca svAdhyAyapravacane ca ; prajAtis’ca svAdhyAyapravacane  
ca .  
PrajA = santati.h (prajA syAt santatau jane – Amara.h).  
Prajana.h = .rtukAle bhAryAgamanam ( .rtau upeyAt – 5-1,  
Gautamasm.rti), otherwise bhrU.nahatyAdo.sa.h (bhojanam dvigu.nam  
strI.nAm kAmama.s.tagu.nam sm.rtam).  
PrajAti.h = pautrotpatti.h .  
BhA.syam – AcArye.na cAnujnAta.h anurUpAn dArAn Ah.rtya prajAtantum  
prajAsantAnam mA vyavacchetsI.h. prajAsantatervicchitti.h na kartavyA  
. anutpadyamane’pi putre putrakAmyAdikarma.nA tadutpattau yatna.h  
kartavya ityabhiprAya.h . prajAprajanaprajAtinirdes’asAmarthyAt.  
anyathA prajanas’cetyetadekameva avak.syat .  
VidyAra.nyam – gosuvar.navastrAdirUpam yaddhanam AcAryasya priyam  
tadvidyAdak.si.nArtham sampAdya gurave samarpya tato vivAham k.rtvA  
prajAyA.h putrapautrAdirUpAyA.h tantu.hvistAra.h tasya vyavacchedam mA  
kAr.sI.h .  
In all, it is – vyAkhyAnato vis’e.sapratipatti.h .  
Who is an AcArya ? –  
upanIya tu yas’s’i.syam vedamadhyApayeddvija.h I sAngam sarahasyam ca  
tamAcAryam pracak.sate II  
(Bh.rgu in Manusm.rti). Here ‘dvija’ means BrAhma.na only . K.satriyas  
and Vais’yas can have Adhyayanam but not AdhyApanam .Rahasyam =  
upani.sad .  
AcAram grAhayatIti , Acinoti arthAn iti vA AcArya.h (Niruktam) .  
Such an AcArya cannot be expected to be an example of the following verse –  
guravo bahavassanti s’i.syavittApahAri.na.h I durlabho’yam gururdevi  
s’i.syacittApahAraka.h II  
So both, AcArya and s’i.sya, know their limits .  
dhanyo’smi
