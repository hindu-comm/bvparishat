There are a total of 43 views of Apaurusheyatva complied by Pandit  
Madhusudan Ojha-explained by his student Pt. Motilal Sharma in his  
Upanishad-vijnana-Bhashya-Bhumika, volume 1. Summary is that, though  
the mantras are perceived by human Rishis, they are detached from  
bhautika world and further there is unification of thought as  
explained in Brahma-sukta. Thus, Veda mantras are free from human  
bias, errors, temporary customs, difference of language etc. which are  
found in human beings.  
There is apaurusheya apaurushatva also. Mind receives informations  
through 5 Jnanendriyas and directs 5 karmendriyas. Both inputs-outputs  
are through 5 pranas. But there are 2 more pranas which are beyond  
perception-Rishi and Paroraja Prana. Thus, 7 pranas have been stated  
from 7 tongues of Agni. 2 of them being beyond senses, normally we  
count 5 pranas only. There are 7 tongues of Agni for intake  
(lelayamana) and 7 outgoing flames (Archi). Thus, we count a total of  
14 Jihva of mind in Shanti-patha (Agni jihva manavah-- mind  
fluctuations are tongues of Agni, manvah =14 as a number). DEtailed

\> \> An Avadhoota was asked:  
\> \> #In what sense the Vedas are apourusheya?  
\> \> He answered:  
\> \> The Vedas are apourusheya NOT in the pourusheya sense;  
\> \> they are apourusheya in the apourusheya sense.  
\> \> #What does it mean?  
\> \> All pourusheya sense is and comes from the pourusheya senses;  
\> \> apourusheya sense comes ONLY through apourusheya sense!  
\> \> #Then, just as there is Divyam chakshu there is Divyam Shrotram!  
\> \> It is Chakshu as well as Shrotram. Hence, Rishi is Mantra Drashta.
