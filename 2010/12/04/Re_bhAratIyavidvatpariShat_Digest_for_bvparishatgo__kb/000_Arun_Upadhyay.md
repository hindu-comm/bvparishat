I was afraid that Gita will not be accepted by many as part of mahabharata. Muhurtta has a sense of repeatition. Muhur-muhuh = repeatedly. On Ghatika is literally time for a ghati (water pot) to be emptied. Its repeatition, i.e 2 Ghatika is 1 muhurtta.= 48 minutes.

Its another meaning is auspicious moment for various types of works. In that sense also, it comes repeatedly whenever the combination of Vara (day), date (tithi) and Nakshatras occurs. I do not think in terms of grammatical derivation, but seek the link between various meanings as parallel to methods of science. Grammatical derivations also are parallel to chain of thought and pronunciation etc-Arun
