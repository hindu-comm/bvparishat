Namaskar, although Pratisakhyas, siksas and grammarians have tried to explain udatta, anudatta etc. a number of terms require explanation. I think upadrava is also the same particularly in the context of the Samaveda which was sung in a number of ways noted or not noted by grammarians.( sahasradhaa SaamavedaH -- Patanjali). It may be related to the Desi style of recitation which later changed to gharanas in Music. Upadrava may be a flowing sound which goes on in the recitation irrespective of the number of the maatras 1,2,3 etc. It may have some resemblence to plutaanupluti which is a flowing sound. In the context of adhikara, Patanjali has noted it with the jumping of the frogs. But in the song, the context may be different which are not marked or less marked in the manuscripts of the Samaveda. Weeping for long long time and sound was taught in Siappa, a weeping custom at the time of death which is living even today. Flowing recitation in the context of the Samaveda cannot be ruled out. It may be corroborated from the traditional treatises to some extent. It is only a conjecture and not a full thesis. comments are subject to correction and search. Regards, spnarang
