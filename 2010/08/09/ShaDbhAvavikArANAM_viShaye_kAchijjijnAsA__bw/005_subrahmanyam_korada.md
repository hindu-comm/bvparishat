Answer to the first question , i.e. **jayate -asti** and **asti-jayate**, the difference in thesequence between **Nirukta** and **Tantra** , is already offered by **Bhaskararaya** in his commentary,

viz. **Setubandha, on NityAsodasikArnava (VAmakesvaratantrArgaatah) in 6th VisrAma,** **under MudrAnirvacana** on the following **Sloka**s --



**cidAtmabhittau visvasya prakAsAmarsane yadA I.......**

**kriyAsaktistu visvasya modanAddrAvanAttadA** I



**'ayam bhAvah - visvasya hi sadbhAvavikArA yAskAdibhih pariganitA asti jAyate vardhate viparinamate'paksIyate nasyatIti I tesu prathamah sattArUpo bhAvavikAro visvasya sArvakAlikah I pralayakAle'pi bIjabhAvena visvasya sattvAt I tatasca anye pancaiva vikArAscikIrsitAh ....' .**



The last sentence in the above text has to be construed as ' **tata eva anye panca vikArAh** **cikIrsitAh** ' , i.e. **evakAro bhinnaakramah .**



The following explanationmay be useful - **Patanjali** under
**'tadastyAstyasminniti matup** '

(Panini 5-2-94) rules - **na sattAm padArthao vyabhicarati** , i.e. **'sattA'** is always there forany **'thing'** . Obiously , **sattA is there in all BhAvavikAras** and the same is expressed by **asti / bhavati / vidyate** .



This is the reason why **Vaiyakaranas** hold that there cannot be a sentence without a verb

\- **astirbhavantIparah aprayujyamAno'pyasti (Pani - 'anabhihite' 2-3-1)** .



**Naiyayikas** oppose this -- n**kriyArahitam na vAkyamastItyAdikastu prAcAm pravAdah** **niryuktikatvAdasraddheyah** (**Sabdasaktiprakasika - SArthakasabde vAkyanirUpanam**) .



**SakArya - AsatkArya -- SankarAcArya** in his **Bhasya** on **BrahmAnandavallI** of **TaittirIyopanisad** says that '**asat**' means ' **avyAkrtarUpena , nAmarUpabhedAbhAvena**

**brahma astIti** ' -- **asanneva sa bhavati , asadbrahmeti veda cet, asti brahmeti cedveda,** **santamenam tato viduriti .**



One thing should be noted - asat means **'tirobhAvena sattA ' (SAmkhyadarsanam) .**



In **Vedanta Brahma is UpAdAnakAranam (SamavAyikAranam - Nyaya, Prakrti - SAmkhya**, **Anu - Vaisesikam**) . Although the universe is created (**Vivarta/ParinAma**) **Brahma** will also be there in **KAranarUpa** unlike **'mrttikA and ghata'** , i.e. **kUtasthanityam** .



The conclusion is that whether **SatkArya or AsatkArya , SattA** is there and **'asti**' can be there in the biginning.



As has already pointed out ' **BhAva'** cannot be there from '**AbhAva**' . In **KAryAdhikarana**

(**SUtrabhAsyam 4 th AdhyAya**), while refuting the **MImAmsakas**' argument that there will be

**PratyvAya** (not mentioned in **Vedas**) in case
**'nityanaimittika-karmas'** are not performed,

**BhagavatpAda** says - there cannot be ' **bhAv**a ' from **'abhAva**' . The same is there in

**BrhadAranyakopanisadbhAsya** also .



**'Nityanaimittikaireva kurvAno duritaksayam' (Manu**) - one should perform **Nitya** and **Naimittika** - is the **SiddhAnta** .



**Panini + Katyayana** also support**SatkAryavAda - sambhUte (Pani4-3-41), kosAddhan**-- **vikAra iti vaktavyam**(**Pan4-3-42**).
