विद्वद्भ्यो नमः\|

Here is a passage which gives some more details as for as the bālāparādha is concerned in the text Prāyaścittasamuccaya of Trilocanaśivācārya, which is again from the Mohacūḍottara. Further this texts adds this is for the initiated (samayadīkṣā) persons.

अशीतिर्यस्य वर्षाणि बालो वाप्यूनषोडशः। प्रायश्चित्तार्धमर्हन्ति स्त्रियो वा व्याधिपीडिताः॥ तत्रापि च परिक्लेशं ज्ञात्वार्धार्धं प्रकल्पयेत्। देशं कालं वयः शक्तिं जातिं भक्तिं क्रियाक्रमम्॥ सुविचार्य प्रदातव्यं नोपवासो रुजान्विते। प्रायश्चित्तासमर्थस्य पितृभ्रात्रादिबान्धवैः॥ तद्विभज्य विधातव्यं द्विगुणं तु परैर्जनैः।

One who is of 80 years \[of age\] or a child of below 16 years deserve

half of \[any given\] expiation, or \[also\] ladies and those afflicted by disease.

  

And even in those cases, \[the guru\], considering their difficulties, may prescribe

a quarter \[of any given expiation\].

  

Having well considered place, time, age,ability, caste, devotion and \[the difficulty of\] the ritual sequence, he should enjoin

\[expiatory rites\]. There should be no fasting for one who is ill.

  

\[The expiation\] of person who is not able to perform a prāyaścitta should be divided amongst his father, brothers and other relatives. \[If it is shared\] amongst others \[who are not relatives\], then itshould be doubled.

  

Dr. Sathyanarayanan, R.
