namo vidvadbhyah  
  
There cannot be anything like ' **Gamakasamasa**' . Most of the people think that it is **SamAsa only due to gamakatva**  
but the fact is that there is ' **sApeksatve'pi gamakatvAt
sanAdyantadhAtuvrttih, krdvrttih ' etc. .  
**  
The entire discussion revolves around ' **samarthah padavidhih' (Pan 2-1-1)** .  
  
Some general norms -  
  
1. **Naityasabdikas** hold that **Sabda is nitya** , i.e. both **Vakya and SamAsa** are there in usage , they are not to be **produced by Sastra - rAjnah purusah, rAjapurusah .  
**  
2. KAryasabdikas argue that Sabda is KArya , i.e. the samAsa has to be produced as optional from VAkya - rAjnah purusah  
optionally besomes rajapurusah .  
  
Panini follows KAryasabdavAda and says 'samarthah padavidhih' .  
  
Earlier to **Panini** people followed the **dictum - parArthAbhidhAnam vrttih (I pblished a book, viz Four Vrttis in Panini , 1996).  
**  
**Vrtti and Padavidhi are synonyms** . So **padavidhi is Vrttipaksa** and the other **(KArya) is Avrttipaksa .**  
  
There are **four Vrttis (tatra vrttih caturdhA - Nagesa in VrttivicAra, ManjUsA) or five (Deeksita**) .  
  
**Panini** injects **SAmarthyam** (capacity to denote the intended meaning) in **samAsa** etc.  
  
**SAmarthyam** is of two types - **EkArthIbhAva (in samAsa etc.) and VyapeksA ( in Vakyam) .**  
  
**NaiyAyikas accept VyapeksA in both .  
**  
Even for **Vaiyakaranas VyapeksA precedes EkarthIbhAva . 'Samarthah' of Panini means both .  
**  
**Raja and purusa** have got a **sambandha** , i.e. sevyasevakabhAva, which keeps them bounden .  
  
Two (each time) different words (in samAsa) having different meanings (of a Vakya) unite to offer a single meaning and this is called **EkArthIbhAva** -- **rAjnah purusah / rAjapurusah .  
**  
**Both VAkya and SamAsa should denote the same meaning - is the condition .**  
  
Take an example - bhAryA rajnah puruso devadatasya . Here the word rAja does not have SAmarthya and therefore no  
samAsa can be effected  
  
**Mahat kastam sritah** - at this stage there cannot be a **SamAsa** between **kastam and sritah** . Why ?  
  
Here enters **KAtyAyana -- savisesanAnam vrttirna , vrttasya vA visesanayogo na , aguruputrAdInAm** --  
  
**mahat is a visesana of kasta , i.e. kasta is savisesanam , therefore there cannot be a SamAsa .  
**  
There is a **SamAsa** - **kastasritah** . Here the word **kasta is 'vrtta' i.e. in vrtti** . So it cannot have a **Visesanam, i.e. mahat**  
**kastasritah is wrong ( mahat kastam mahakastam , tat sritah mahAkastasritah ) .  
**  
We come across a **NyAya** in mostly **Darsana** treatizes - **padArthah padArthena anveti , na tu tadekadesena** . The origin of this **NyAya** is - **vrttasya visesanayogo na** .  
  
In the case of - ' **devadattasya guruputrah** ' the norm , **vrttasya visesanayogo na** , does not apply .  
  
In the above case the **vigraha (vibhinnatvena grahah jnAnam** ) is - **devadattasya guroh putrah** . Obviously **'devadattasya'** is a **Visesanam** of the **vrttapada, 'guru**' , nevertheless there can be a **SamAsa** . How ? **PrayuktanAm idam anvAkhyAnam -** there is the usage and the intended meaning is being conveyed without a hitch .  
  
Such are called - **asamarthasamAsas**.  
  
**MahAbhAsyam** - na vaktavyam , vrttistarhi kasmAnna prApnoti ( mahat kastam sritah ) ? agamakatvAt , iha samAnArthena vAkyena bhavitavyam samAsena ca ..... etasmAddhetorbrUmah agamakatvAt iti , na brUmah apasabdah syAditi , **yatra ca gamako bhavati , bhavati tatra vrttih , tadyathA -devadattasya gurukulam , devadattasya guruputrah , devadattasya dAsabhAryeti** .  
  
Take an example **(MAgha 1-4)** -  
  
**navAnadho'dho brhatah payodharAn samUdhakarpUraparAgapanduram I  
ksanam ksanotksiptagajendrakrttinA sphutopamam bhUtisitena sambhuna** II  
  
Here **'sphutopamam**' falls under **'sApeksatve'pi gamakatvat samAsah** , as the word **'sphuta**' has got **'apeksA'(AkAnksA)** with **'sambhu**' and not **'upamA**' -- **sphutasambhUpamam ityarthah .  
**  
Then why **Panini** did not make a **Sutra** for this . He does not say everything with **VAcyamaryAdA** rather thru **VyangyamaryAdA ( VyanjanA in Sutras** ) --  
  
**asUryalalAtayoh drsitapoh** (Pan 3-2-36) - example - **asUryampasyA rAjadArAh** . Here the **VAkyam** will be - sUryam(api)  
na pasyanti , i.e**. Prasajyapratisedhah** .  
  
Within the **SUtra** there is an **asamarthasamAsa - sudanapumsakasya (anapumsakasya =napumsakasya na bhavati).  
**  
Also **'asrAddhabhojI brAhanah**' (srAddham na bhunte, as one has to **recite GAyatrI 1000 times** )  
  
**GamakatvAt sAdhutvam** is applicable in **SanAdyantadhAtuvrtti** aslo - **BhAsyam** under - ' supa Atmanah kyac '(Pan3-1-8) -  
  
yadi tarhi savisesanAnAm vrttirna vrttasya vA visesanam na prayujyate iti ucyate 'mundayati mAnavakam' atra Vrttih na  
prApnoti , evam tarhi amundAdInAmiti vaktavyam ...tattarhi vaktavyam ... na vaktavyam , vrttistarhi kasmAnna bhavati ? agamakatvAt .... yatra ca gamakam bhavati, bhavati tatra vrttih tadyathA **mundayati mAnavakam** iti .  
  
Here **NAgesa** explains the **gamakatvam** - mundayati iti prayoge **maundyasya kriyArUpatvena kArakAmse nityasApeksatayA gamakatvamiti bhAvah** .  
  
**Gamakatvam in Krdanta** - under 'jAterastrIvisayAdayopadhAt'(Pan4-1-63) , while commenting on the word 'sarvabhAk',  
Kaiyata says 'lingAni sarvAni bhajatIti sarvasabdasya lingApeksatve'pi gamakatvAt 'bhajo nvih' iti nvipratyayah .  
  
**Vrttisamudesa of PadakAnda of VAkyapadIyam (47-49**) --  
  
**sambandhisabdah sApekso nityam sarvah prayujyate I  
svArthavatsA vyapeksAsya vrttAvapi na hIyate II  
samudAyena sambandho yesAm gukulAdinA I  
samsprsyAvayavA,ste ca yujyante tadvatA saha II  
abudhAn pratyupAyAsca vicitrAh pratipattaye I  
sabdAntaratvAdatyantabhedo vAkyasamAsayoh II**  
**  
The meaning of the term 'gamaka' -- Nagesa under 'jAterastrI..' says - bodhakatvAdityrthah , abodhakatvameva hyasamarthye vrttyaprAptau bIjam .**  
  
Under samarthasUtra (**sudanapumskasya) Nagesa** says - **asAmarthye'pi jnApakAt** kvacitsamAsa ityeva bhasyAllAbhena ..  
  
I rather think that **'gamakatvam' means ' vyanjakatvam' , i.e. vyanjanayA bodhyatvam .  
**  
Where is the **PramAnam** ?  
  
**Patanjali** had had the following under SamarthasUtra - **sabdapramAnakA vayam** **,yacchabda Aha tadasmAkam pramAnam, sabdasceha sattAmAha - ayam dandah , astIti gamyate .**  
  
**Kaiyata** says thus - yatrAnyat kriyApadam na srUyate tatrAntarangatvAt **astikriyaiva pratIyate** i  
  
**PratIyamAnam is VyanjanayA bodhyamAnam - 'pratIyamAna punaranyadeva vastvasti vAnIsu mahAkavInam , - AnandavardhanAcArya .**  
  
**Nagesa** did not say - **vAcyamaryAdayA bodhakatvam** . since the intended meaning is not at all being retrieved thru  
**AbhidhA** it will be **VyanjanA** only .
