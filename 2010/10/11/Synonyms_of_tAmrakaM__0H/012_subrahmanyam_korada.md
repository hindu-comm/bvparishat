namo vidvadbhyah



The quotation is confusing --



'tvantAthAdi na pUrvabhAk' - is the ParibhAsA at the outset of Amarakosa -- if ' tu ' is at the end

or 'atha' at the beginning it (the word) does not have any connection with the earlier word .

PUrvam bhajate iti pUrvabhAk (bhajo nvih , Pa 3-2-62 , sarvalopI pratyayah ) .



'cittam tu cetah ' - here the word 'cittam' is a synonym of 'cetah' etc. .

'atha tAmrakam sulbam' - here 'tAmrakam' cannot be a synonym of 'ArakUtah' .



'Tu' and 'atha' are taken by Amarasimha as 'boundary markers' .  
  
It should be ' ArakUtah ' as otherwise the meter is disturbed . If a word like 'ArAkUtah ' is found

elsewhere then the answer will be - api mAsam masam kuryAt chandobhangam na kArayet .



Therefore the quotation should start with - atha tAmrakam ... .



dhanyo'smi
