The explanation is very logical and can also relate to the fact that the makara glyphic is often a composite glyph: ligaturing the trunk of elephant with the snout of the crocodile on sculptures in Bharhut and many other sites. karibha was a gloss for the elephant. Ligaturing was a very common orthographic style of rendering composite glyphics in Indus script.
