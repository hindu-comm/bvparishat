The Department of Sociology at the National University of Singapore seeks to fill a tenure-track position at the Assistant Professor level, with an initial 3-year contract. Candidates should have a Ph.D. in anthropology or a closely-related discipline, by the time of appointment. We will give preference to candidates with a strong research interest in visual anthropology in addition to other fields of gender, environment, kinship or development studies. Evidence of previous teaching experience and engagement with these areas either at undergraduate or graduate levels is preferred. Exceptionally strong candidates with research interests in other fields of anthropology will also be considered. Appointees would be expected to teach and develop multi-disciplinary undergraduate and graduate modules in relevant areas, supervise postgraduate students and build up the research profile of the Department. Our Department is ajoint Department of Sociologyand Anthropology, comprising about thirty faculty members in diverse fields, specialising in sociological and anthropological research on Asian societies.The Departmentattracts graduate students internationally and we have a rapidly developing postgraduate programme in which students have the option to complete a Ph.D. in either Sociology or Anthropology.  
  
NUS emphasizes excellence in both teaching and research, with opportunities forsubstantial research funding. Remuneration and benefits are highly competitive.  
  
We are looking for candidates who can begin in July 2011.  
  
Applications should include aletter of interest, curriculum vitae, a research and teaching statement, two article-length samples of research writing, evidence of teaching effectiveness, and names and contact details for three references.
