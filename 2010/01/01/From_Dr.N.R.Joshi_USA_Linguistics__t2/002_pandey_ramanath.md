Namo vidvadbhyah

The origin of Vyakarana is there in Vedic Literature. The following  
text is available in Gopathabrahmana --

Omkaaram prcchaamah, ko dhaatuh, kim praatipadikam, kim  
naamaakhyaatam, kim lingam, kim vacanam, kaa  
vibhaktih, kah pratyayah, kah svara upasasrgo nipaatah, kim  
vaiyaakaranam, ko vakaarah, ko vikaary, kati maatrah, kati varnah,  
katyaksarah, katipadah, kah samyogah, kim  
sthaananaadaanupradaanaanukaranam .

In Maitrayaniyasamhita and Aitareyabrahmana there is discussion  
regarding six and seven Vibhaktis. It may be noted that - there are  
only five Vibhaktis , i.e. prathamaadvitiiyaatrtiiyaasasthiisaptamyah,  
in Srautasampradaya.

The term Vyakaranam itself is from Veda -- naamaruupe vyaakaravaani,  
drstvaa ruupe vyaakarot satyaanrte  
prajaapatih etc.. Therefore, Vyakaranam means - separation of  
(saadhu) Sabdas from Asaadhusabdas .  
Gauh is saadhu whereas gaavii, gonii, gotaa, gopotalikaa etc.(used in  
the same sense) are apasabdas.

Vyaakriyante asaadhusabdebhyah saadhusabdah prthakkriyante anena iti  
(karane lyut by 'krtyalyuto bahulam'  
-Panini) Vyakaranam.

Vyakaranam is not Grammar, it is a rough translation. Vyakaranam  
includes Grammar but not vice versa.

In Rktantra it is stated that Brahma was the first author of  
Vyakaranasastra -- brahmaa brhaspataye provaaca,  
brhaspatirindraaya,indro bharadvaajaaya, bharadvaaja rsibhyah, rsayo  
braahmanebhyah.

For more details in this regard please refer to Paspasahnika of  
Mahabhasya , Nirukta etc.
