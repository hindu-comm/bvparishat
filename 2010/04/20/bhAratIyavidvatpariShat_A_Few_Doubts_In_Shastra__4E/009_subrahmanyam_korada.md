namo vidvadbhyah



nan has got two meanings -- paryudaasa and prasajyapratisedha.



paryudasa means exception and in this case the nan will have connection with the uttarapada .



prasajyapratisedha means having the prasanga of (refering to) something and restricting the same.

in this case thenan will have connection with the kriyaa .



there is a conspicuous difference between both the meanings - in paryudaasa there will be only one vakyam - ekavaakyam . in prasajyapratisedha there will be bhinnavaakyataa -- vaakyabhedah .



this vaakyabheda is not the dosa mentioned by mimaamsakas- sambhavatyekavaakyatve vaakyabhedastu nesyate (vakyaadhikaranam - slokavaartikam of kumarilabhatta) . that aplies if two things are injuncted

by a single vaakya -- patanjali says at times - suutram tu bhidyate, yathaanyaasamevaastu - it means

there will be vaakyabhedadosa .



The following verse is popular --



paryudaasassa vijneyah kriyayaa saha yatra nan I

prasajyapratisedho'yam yatrottarapadena nan II



here are examples from Veda, Vyakarana and Loka --



Paryudaasah -



veda - abraahmanah somavikrayii -- braahmanatvavyatiriktajaatimaan somavikrayii - here following the norm (nyaaya/paribhasaa) - nanivayuktam anyasadrse tathaahyarthagatih ( nanyuktam and ivayuktam

apply in another but similar thing as the meaning is understood like that) - it means braahmanabhinnah

braahmanasadrsah but not a stone or something ( na hi abraahmanamaanayetyukte lostamaaniiya krtii bhavati -- mahaabhaasyam).it is a single sentence - ekavakyam - so paryudaasa.



vyaakaranam - aato'nupasarge kah (panini 3-2-3) - if upasargabhinna is upapada, akaaraantadhaatu will get ka-pratyaya . here nan got conection withuttarapada and it is ekavaakya - therefore paryudaasa .



loka - abraahmanam aanaya - braahmanatvajaativyatiriktah tatsadrsah tam aanaya . here nan has got

connection with uttarapada and it is ekavaakyam - so paryudaasa .



Prasajyapratisedha --



veda - braahmano na hantavyah , naanrtam vadet



vyaakaranam - akartari ca kaarake samjnaayaam (panini 3-3-19, ghan) -- in a samjnaa ghan will be there, but in kartrkaaraka it does not come - samjnaayaam kaarake ghanpratyayah syaat , kartrkaarake

ghan na syaat . here the nan in the samaasa 'akartari' is construed with kriyaa - syaat . therefore it is bhinnavaakyam . so this is prasajyapratisedha .



loka - asuuryampasyaa raajadaaraah - raajadaaraah suuryam(api) na psyanti (suuryabhinnam pasyantiiti naarthah) . this is an asamarthasamaasa - see 'samarthah padavidhih' Pani 2-1-1 , mahaabhaasyam -

acceptable to panini (also sudanapumsakasya , Pani 1-1-42 , here either will do - see kaiyata ).
