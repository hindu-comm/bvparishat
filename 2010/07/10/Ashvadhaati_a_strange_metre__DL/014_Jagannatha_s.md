MattebhavikrIDitam is generated from Shardulavikridita. Two Laghus are put in this metre in the place of first Guru. This is very famous vritta in Old Kannada literature. But its first usage is found in Sanskrit. It is in Aihole inscription(poet of which is Ravikirti) from which we know that, Harshavardhana(patron king of Banabhatta) was defeated by second Pulikeshin.

This is the verse of that inscription (in Mattebhavikridita).



वरदातुङ्गतरङ्गभङ्गविलसद्धंसावलीमेखलां

वनवासीमवमृद्नता सुरकरप्रस्पर्धिनीं संपदा।

महता यस्य बलार्णवेन सहसा संछादितोर्वीतलं

जलदुर्गं स्थलदुर्गतामिव गतं तत्तत्क्षणे पश्यताम्।।  
  
Mattebha does differ from Mattebhavikriidita.
