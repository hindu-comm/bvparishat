namo vidvadbhyah



No doubt , Panini offeres some sutras like - 'ghanajabantAh' etc to know the Linga of some Sabdas

following the Pratyaya .



The Siddhanta in Vyakarana is -- lingam asisyam lokAsayatvAt lingasya . It means depend upon 'NAmalingAnusAsanam' , (Sista)lokavyavahAra, usages in Kavyas etc to know Linga of a particular Sabda .



On the other hand, under 'sarUpAnAm ekasesa ekavibhaktau' (Pani 1-2-64) Patanjali takes up the discussion

-- the essence is this - it is not possible to follow the Laukika definition , i.e. stanakesavatI nArI ... (here kesa

means 'bhagam'). Therefore a svaKrtAnta should be followed ( in other words SAmkhyadarsanam ) . If there

is Vrddhi of Gunas, i.e. SabdasparsarUparasagandhAh, then there will be Pumlinga if HrAsa then StrIlinga

and if SAmAnyam then Napumsakalinga ( this is what is called - sAmAnye napumsakam in commentaries).



Kim karoti - is correct as when the Linga is not known there will be Napumsaka (more in Mahabhasya in adhyaya

-4 , Ekasrtih svarasarvanAma and Napumsaka is LingasarvanAma )



Who will know the above aspects? Yogins . Therefore we have only one resort - to follow the usage .



Here is Hari (LIngasamuddesa - Padakanda - Vakyapadiyam) --



bhAvatattvavidah sistAh sabdArthesu vyavasthitAh I yadyaddharme gatamiti tattallingam pracaksate II



AvirbhAvastirobhAvah sthitiscetyapyapAyinah I dharmA mUrtisu sarvAsu lingatvena nYavasthitAh II



ApAyitvam means pratiksananasvaratvam .



That'swhy Miiamsakas argue that the word 'pasunA' in 'pasunA yajeta' means a masculine pasu only (i.e pumstva is vivaksita).  
  
BhusanakAra says Linga is Sabdanistham . But under 'svamornapumsakAt' (Pani7-1-33) Patanjali says -

na hi napumsakam nAma sabdo'sti . And there will be problem with - pumyogAdAkhyAyAm (4-1-48) .



Linga is Arthanistha according to Vyakaranam .



Vaisesikas claim that 'pumstva' etc are Jatis . So we have - idam vastu , ayam padArthah, iyam vyaktih .



Although Utpatti and VinAsa are not there in case of Cit and Purusa , following the ' upAdhisamsarga'

there will be Linga .
