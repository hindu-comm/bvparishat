Thanks, Sri Ajit-ji.

  

Yes, the same text or different texts of itihasas and puranas have different versions of the same story or different stories involving the same character.

  

Both Dr Aravinda Rao and Dr Ramesh Rao can be expected to know such basics.

  

The issue is perspective or interpretation here.

  

--------------------------------------------------------

  

Coming to the theme of 'violence' in Mahabharata, Dr Ramesh Rao avare, the nuanced understanding of ahimsaa- himsaa of the Vedic worldview that is presented in Mahabharata baffles many, particularly those coming with a modern / western baggage.

  

Adiparva, where this Parashu Rama story occurs as part of पर्वसंग्रहपर्व , has stories of sarpayaagaby Uttanka, killing of snakes by Ruru, beating of Saarameya, the divine puppy by Janamejaya'sbrothers Bhimasena and Ugrasena apart from this story of Parashu Rama.

  

In each of these stories, there is an ending with a message against violence, anger and revenge.

  

Ending Parvas of Mahabharata themselves are full of such anti-war sentiment.

  

In BhagavadyaanaParva, Yudhishthira gives a long speech against war and vengeful attitude.

  

अहिंसा परमो धर्मः एषधर्मःसनातनः gets repeated several times in the text.

  

But the same text, eulogises Veerataa , Veerasvarga etc.

  

This combination of pro war and anti war approach in the same text baffles a superficial observer not trained in the nuances of Vedic world view.

  

Modern world is full of such impatient , superficial observers that would like a clear position toward one of the extremes, violence or non-violence, it should be black and white, very clear binary.

  

That said, the story of Parashu Rama in पर्वसंग्रहपर्व too ends in advice against violence and viramaNa of the violence by the pitrah of Parashu Rama.
