\<\<The Madhvas may have got this idea of Svatantra and Paratantra Satya from this differentiation of Reality. Hari is Svatantra. Everything other than Hari is Paratantra>\>

  

Let us look at the \*source texts\* of the ontological classification of भगववान्-आचार्यपूज्यचरण to see where his idea of ontology is draw from.

  

He draws it from the Vedas & its allied literature which he fondly calls as सदागम.

  

Metaphysical Independence (स्वतन्त्र) and bestowing eight fold metaphysical determinations (सृष्टि-स्थिति-लय-नियमन-ज्ञान-अज्ञान-बन्ध-मोक्ष) upon dependents (परतन्त्र - both sentients & insentients put together) is the mark of Brahman and He is \*सदागमैकविज्ञेय\*.

  

The gradation of reality into metaphysical independence and dependence is based on the immortal teachings of the Upanishads viz., सत्यस्य सत्यम् and नित्यो नित्यानाम्.

  

The above ontological conception and relation between स्वतन्त्र and परतन्त्र is not not make believe at all. It is a true relationship of आधिपत्य and अधीनत्व.

  

The Brahman's sway over eternals and non-eternals is absolute.  

  

Where doesभगववान्-आचार्यपूज्यचरण draw this relationship calledआधिपत्य and अधीनत्व from? It is from the RigVeda (10.112.9) which he quotes in his metrical commentaryअनुव्याख्यानon the Vedanta Sutras and I quote it below,

  

^^सत्ताप्रधानपुरुषशक्तीनां च प्रतीतयः । प्रवृत्तयश्च ताः सर्वा नित्यं नित्यात्मना यतः । तथा नित्यतया नित्यं नित्यश्क्त्या स्वयेश्वरः । नियामयति नित्यं च \*न ऋते त्वत्\* इति श्रुतेः^^ ।।अनुव्याख्यान।।अन्यत्राभावादिकरणम्।।

  

The full text of the famous भगवती श्रुति is :न ऋते त्वत्क्रियते किं चनारे महामर्कं मघवञ्चित्रमर्च । (ऋग्वेद-१०.११२.९)

  

"त्वत् ऋते आरे किञ्चन न क्रियते, (हे) मघवन् - महाम् चित्रम् अर्कम् अर्च" ।।

  

Nothing takes place without reference to Brahman.

  

Metaphysically speaking, Being and becoming ofeternals and non-eternals is because of Him and He has complete and absolute sway over them.

  

The ontological conception : स्वतन्त्रमस्वतन्त्रं च प्रमेयं द्विविधं मतम् is thus grounded in the Vedic milieu is the firm and inviolable view or शास्त्र-दृष्टि ofभगववान्-आचार्यपूज्यचरण.

I think your argument for reality of the pot made from clay is based on Transactions and Utility Value. You do activities like carrying water in the pot and the pot is useful. I don't deny that. I wrote about Vyavaharika Satya and Pratibhasika Satya earlier in this thread. The pot is useful and has Vyavaharika Satya only. This is Relative Truth but not Absolute Truth. Why?

  

Take a person from another culture to visit you. He has not seen any pot before because in his culture they do not use pots. When he sees your pot he will see it is made of clay. Because he will not know its use he will say 'This is Clay only with a form and name'. What has happened? Your pot is viewed as having no reality other than clay. What happened to the transactions you do with pot and its utility? They are Relative Truths because when you change the environment they lose all meaning. They are real only in a particular environment of time and place. Therefore they are Relative Reality or Vyavaharika only.

  

The Upanishad does not explain Rajjusarpafor pot because the Rajjusarpa is PratibhasikaSatya and the pot is Vyavaharika Satya. I have explained this earlier. The main point I want to make is both Vyavaharika and Pratibhasika Satya are not Absolute Reality. The only Absolute Reality is Brahman and everything other than Brahman is not Absolutely real.

  

The Madhvas may have got this idea of Svatantra and Paratantra Satya from this differentiation of Reality. Hari is Svatantra. Everything other than Hari is Paratantra.
