kevaloham is a sentence. kevalah+ aham . It is not a vesheshaNapurvapada karmadhaaraya samaasa.

  

kevalaaham makes it vesheshaNapurvapada karmadhaaraya samaasa. It is in that case that we can see kevalaaham as a category of aham.

  

In kevaloham aham is uddes'ya, kevalah is vidheya.

  

kevalah refers to that which is only one, without any other.

  

The sentence kevaloham means I am that which is only one that is.

  

It does not refer to any sub-category of aham.

  

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  

In Vedanta, aham is an inner world entity , a functional category, and it is used as aham-kaara = sense of aham , sense of I etc.

  

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  

In VyakaraNa, in aarthika granthas like Vakyapadiyam, the aham and other self-reflexive expressions are analyzed in detail for their self-reflexive nature.
