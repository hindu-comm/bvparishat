Dear Scholars,

  

I am happy to share that I have completed the compilation of my first work in Sanskrit, theKuśāgrakāvyamuktāvalī (कुशाग्रकाव्यमुक्तावली), comprising fourśatakas (शिवकुटुम्बशतकम्, नैष्काम्यशतकम्, नव्यनीतिनिलयः, रसिकविनोदः) and two long compositions (सूर्यसुप्रभातम् and नर्मदालहरी). I have supplemented this work of 500 verses with English and Hindi translations and more than 100 footnotes to elucidate the meaning and context.

  

I am also planning to add a trilingual, autobiographical introductory essay (मम संस्कृतजीवनम् – My Sanskrit Journey) to illustrate just one instance of how interest in Sanskrit can be cultivated by a novice. I am now looking for a publisher for this work.

  

Below I am sharing some verses fromरसिकविनोदः with their English translations:

  

अलौकिकगृहचरितम्  
  
एका भार्या कविकुलगता मन्त्ररूपा द्वितीया-  
च्छागाकारस्त्वपरतनयः पत्रकारः कनिष्ठः।  
तातो नाभिस्थलकुवलयं वाहनं मानसौकाः  
स्मारं स्मारं स्वगृहचरितं वृद्धकायो विधाता॥1  
  
The first wife (Sarasvati) lives among the poets while the second (Gayatri) takes the form of a Mantra. One son (Daksha) has the face of a goat while the youngest one (Narada) is a journalist. With a lotus born from Sri Vishnu’s navel as his father and a swan as his vehicle, Brahma turned old contemplating on his domestic life.  
  
एका भार्या स्वपितृनिलयं विद्रुता तापभीत्या  
च्छाया तस्या वसति सहितं मन्दमाताऽसिताङ्गी।  
एकः पुत्रो भुवनभयदो मृत्युदेवो द्वितीयः  
स्मारं स्मारं स्वगृहचरितं दग्धदेहो दिनेशः॥2  
  
Afraid of heat, his wife (Sandhya) fled to her father’s place. Her dark shadow (Chhaya) stays but she’s mother to the dull one (Shani). The first son (Shani) causes fear to the world while the second one (Yama) is the Deva of death. Thus, contemplating on his domestic life, Surya Deva got burnt.  
  
नक्तं सेव्या विविधदयिताः सप्तविंशः क्रमेण  
पुत्रश्चैकोऽपि स विजनितः स्थाणुभूषैककार्या।  
अङ्के नित्यं चरति हरिणो लाञ्छनाभासकारी  
स्मारं स्मारं स्वगृहचरितं निष्प्रभस्तापहारी॥3  
  
Twenty seven wives (Nakshatras) must be served sequentially every night. His only son (Budha) is ill-born and only occupation is adorning an old stump (Shiva). A deer roams in his lap creating the impression of a defect. Thus, contemplating on his domestic life, Chandra Deva lost his luster.  
  
भर्ता शेषे स्वपिति सततं लोकचिन्ताविमुक्तः  
पुत्रो वृद्धो मदपि तरुणश् चापरोऽनङ्ग एव।  
वासः पद्मेऽहनि न डयते वाहनं कौशिको मे  
स्मारं स्मारं स्वगृहचरितं द्रव्यभूता हि लक्ष्मीः॥4  
  
“My husband, free from the worries of the world, always sleeps on the serpent Shesha. My first son (Brahma) is older than me while another one (Kamadeva) is youthful but formless. My residence is in a lotus and my vehicle, the owl, doesn’t fly during the day.” - Thus, contemplating her domestic life, Lakshmi turned into physical wealth.  
  
तातः क्षारो मथितजलधिर् वारुणी सोदरा मे  
भ्राता चन्द्रो विकृतवदनः कालकूटोऽनुगामी।  
बन्धुच्छेदप्रभवममृतं चानुजं द्वन्द्वबीजं  
स्मारं स्मारं स्वकुलचरितं द्रव्यभूता हि लक्ष्मीः॥5  
  
“My father is the churned and salty ocean. My sister is liquor. My elder brother is the blemished Chandra while poison itself followed after my birth. My younger brother is the nectar of immortality, born out of a fight among brothers and the root of conflicts.” - Thus, contemplating her lineage, Lakshmi turned into physical wealth.  
  
बाल्यादेकः समरविजयी देवसेनाधिकारी  
पुत्रः पूजाप्रथमपदवीमागतो मे द्वितीयः।  
वासो नित्यं जनकनिलये स्वामिना सार्धमेवं  
स्मारं स्मारं स्वगृहचरितं पूर्णकामा भवानी॥6  
  
"My first son (Skanda), even in his childhood, emerged victorious as the commander of the Deva army. My second son (Ganesha) attained the right to be worshipped first in any Puja. I always reside in my father's house (Himalaya) along with my husband" - thus contemplating her domestic life, Devi Parvati was fully satisfied.  
  
एकः पुत्रो यदि गजमुखः षण्मुखश्च द्वितीयः  
पञ्चास्यश्च स्वयमपि वृषो वाहनं व्यूढदेहः।  
यस्यावस्त्रे वपुषि फणिनश्चाश्रिता भूतसङ्घा  
भिक्षार्थं स स्मरति दयितामन्नपूर्णां भवानीम्॥7  
  
If one's first son has the mouth of an elephant, the second son has six mouths, one himself has five mouths, one's vehicle is a sturdy bull, one's body is covered with serpents with many hoods, and one's dependents are the hosts ofbhūtas, then one remembers one's beloved, the goddess Annapūrṇā.  
  
कस्त्वं रामश्चरति स वने राघवस्तात वन्दे  
सीते नाहं दशरथसुतो लक्ष्मण क्षेममीयाः।  
तस्य भ्राता रिपुदमन किं शार्ङ्गभृद् गच्छ युद्धे  
मैथिल्यैवं प्रतिहतवचाः पातु वो रामचन्द्रः॥8  
  
देवी सीता - कः त्वम्? (Who are you?)  
श्रीरामः - रामः। (I am Rāma)  
सीता - चरति स वने। (But he, Rama, the black deer, roams in the forest)  
श्रीरामः - राघवः। (No, I am Rāghava)  
सीता - तात! वन्दे। (O father Daśaratha, I bow to you)  
श्रीरामः - सीते! न, अहं दशरथसुतः। (No, Sītā, I am the son ofDaśaratha)  
सीता - लक्ष्मण! क्षेमम् ईयाः। (O Lakṣmaṇa, may you prosper)  
श्रीरामः - तस्य भ्राता। (No, I am his brother)  
सीता - रिपुदमन! किम्? (O śatrughna, what is it?)  
श्रीरामः - शार्ङ्गभृत्। (No, I am actually the wielder of theśārṅga bow)  
सीता - गच्छ युद्धे। (Oh, then go to the battlefield)  
एवं मैथिल्या प्रतिहतवचाः रामचन्द्रः वः पातु। (May Sri Rāma, who has thus been rendered speechless by Maithilī protect us.)
