मन्मनोगतम् उक्तम् पुनर् एव तत्रभवता :-) ।  
  
To put the cold fact simply: The only people capable of translating to (or from) Sanskrit are people bilingual in Sanskrit and deshabhASha-s. To the extant that they are kept in the "busy-work" of translating from sanskrit, they won't be translating to sanskrit. It approaches a zero sum game.

  

The main (or almost - only) reason for continuing to stay put in deshabhASha-s is to counterbalance the influence of our enemies there. ("अथ चेदानीं केचन दुराग्रह-ग्रहिलाः स्वदेशीया विदेशीयाश् च संस्कृत-देशभाषयोः परस्परं वैरं कल्पयन्तः सर्वा अपि देशभाषाः संस्कृत-शून्या विदधानाः सन्ति।" इति यथा शशिनोक्तम्, कृष्णशास्त्रिवर्येण स्वलेखे न परिगणितम्।)

  
This threat perception should be balanced by ऋण-प्रज्ञा towards संस्कृतवाङ्मयम्, so that prolific expansion of the latter (a long-term good) is not neglected.
