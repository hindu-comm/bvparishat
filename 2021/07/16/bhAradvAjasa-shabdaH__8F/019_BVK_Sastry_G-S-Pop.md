The following information may be helpful in this regard:



1. On \< पैंग्यस्. \> : In ‘brahma-yajna’ part of five yajnas ( pancha-maha-yajnas) prescribed for the ‘ vaidika-brahmana’, in ‘rishi-tarpana’ part, there are two names:

पैङ्ग्य- - महा पैङ्ग्य.



2. On \< पैंग्यस्. - स – add on - like the सगोत्र by compounding – taddhita pratyaya (belonging to the same / same –similar- equivalent group- lineage) rule : तेन सहेति तुल्ययोगे- सहस्य सः \> In the split ‘ ‘ goes with ‘gotra’ as ‘sa-gotra’.

The duplication / deletion of ‘sa’ in pronunciation in places like ‘ bharadawaja/ paingya is due to slack pronunciation.

In other places, ‘ kaashya-sagotra / vishamitra-sagotra’ , the ‘sa’ stays clear.



This way it seems easier and practically simple and convenient in ‘ karma-kanda’ to have grammatically correct utterance of name and gotra’s .

The ‘ taddhita-pratyayas’ have a wide range of meaning coverage; and so are the ‘samasa’.

Relevant rules that may get connected / explored for ‘ competence of pratyaya- samasa – prakriya’ is for grammarians to cover and justify the usage:
