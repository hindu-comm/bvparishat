****Dr. P. N. Ravindra, MD, Ph.D., is an Associate Professor, at the Centre for Consciousness Studies, Dept. of Neurophysiology, National Institute of Mental Health and Neurosciences (NIMHNAS, Institute of National Importance), Bengaluru, Karnataka. Formerly he was the Professor and Head of Dept. of Physiology and Vice Principal, Gadag Institute of Medical Sciences, Gadag, Karnataka, and the Hon. Director, Swami Vivekananda Studies, Karnatak University, Dharwad.

  

A neurophysiologist by specialized training has a passionate interest in integrating Indian psychological aspects pertaining to Yoga (meditation) with neuroscience with a special focus on understanding neuroscientific aspects of meditation and its effect on sleep & cognition.  
  
He has many peer-reviewed publications in national and international journals of repute. His work was recognized with a National award from the Association of Physiologists and Pharmacologists of India. He conducts seminars/workshops at various institutions and organizations on various contemporary subjects with integrating neuroscience with Indian psychology and philosophy with special reference to Swami Vivekananda. He is a national faculty in conducting courses on sleep and sleep medicine. Has traveled extensively for lectures and workshops in various countries. He is visiting faculty at Svyasa Bangalore.  
  
As a founder Hon. Director of Swami Vivekananda study center, Karnataka University Dharwad, a curriculum for a course personal and professional skill development an open elective course. The curriculum is based on the integration of neuroscience, yoga, Indian psychology principles. It was largely focused on the personality development of an individual. He is also a fellow of Mind and Life Institute.
