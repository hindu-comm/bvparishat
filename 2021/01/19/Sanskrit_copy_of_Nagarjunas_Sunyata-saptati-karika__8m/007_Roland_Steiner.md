It is actually a re-translation into Sanskrit from Tibetan (by Acarya  
Sempa Dorje, if I remember correctly). As far as I know, there is no  
Chinese translation of the Śūnyatāsaptati. According to Chr. Lindtner,  
the text was originally composed in the āryā metre (like the  
Vigrahavyāvartanī and the Pratītyasamutpādahṛdayakārikā).
