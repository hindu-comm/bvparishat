1. Mahabharata and Ramayana are included for the conference. (the second para of the conference note has : “the texts of itihasas like Mahabharata and Ramayana which are included in Puranas(for example, by Rajasekhara in Kavyamimamsa)” )

2. The following sub-themes are indicative but not exhaustive:

1. Vedangas, Darashanas and other Shaastras including jyotisha and vaastu in Puranas

2\. Contents related to Contemporary Knowledge Systems such as Psychology, Astronomy, Geography, Governance, Public Finance, Correctional Administration, etc. in Puranas

3. History related discussion of Puranas: The issue of viewing Puranas and itihasas as accounts of the past, Puranas as sources of contemporary history writing, Historical vs Puranic and Aitihaasic view of the past etc.

3\. Contents related to the sciences/shaastras of fine and performing arts and poetics in Puranas

4\. Contents related to perspectives/understandings/movements such as environmentalism in Puranas

5\. Knowledge and perspectives expressed as stories in Puranas

or interpretation of stories as indirect expressions of knowledge and perspectives

6\. Methodological issues in the study of knowledge systems in Puranas

 3. Size of the papers can be around 1500 to 2000 words. Longer papers are accepted for

 publication. But such papers need to be edited to 1500-2000 words size for the

 presentation purposes. Unnecessary long introductions, restating commonly well known

 points etc. are discouraged. Crisp, to the point presentations are encouraged.
