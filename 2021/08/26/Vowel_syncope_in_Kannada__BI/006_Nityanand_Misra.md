Now we come to the most important part, the topic of the thread. Firstly, what do you mean by northern and rustic dialects? What do you mean by southern dialect? Do you have a source/citation to back up your claim? I ask because multiple published sources seem to say otherwise. As perZvelebil (1970) the three major regional dialects of Kannada are Dharwar (North Canara), Mysore/Bangalore (old Mysore state), and Mangalore (South Canara). Of these, it is the **Mysore dialect** in which the dichotomy between colloquial and written standards is more pronounced than that in the Dharwar dialect as per Mahapatra, Padmanabha, McConnell and Verma (1989, see [here](https://books.google.co.in/books?id=yU8nq-C6wnoC&pg=PA240&lpg=PA240)). Mahapatra *et al.* point out that it is this Mysore dialect which has become more popular with films and radio using it. D. N. Shankara Bhat (2001) also says the vowel deletion takes place in the Mysore dialect of Kannada. Is that a *northern* or *rustic*dialect? I do not think so.
