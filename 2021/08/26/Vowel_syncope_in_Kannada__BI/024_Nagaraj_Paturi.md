In[phonology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phonology "Phonology"),**syncope**([/ˈsɪŋkəpi/](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English"); from[Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language "Ancient Greek language"):[συγκοπή](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CF%83%CF%85%CE%B3%CE%BA%CE%BF%CF%80%CE%AE#Ancient_Greek "wikt:συγκοπή"),[romanized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization_of_Ancient_Greek "Romanization of Ancient Greek"):**sunkopḗ**,[lit.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Literal_translation "Literal translation") 'cutting up') is the loss of one or more sounds *from the interior of a word*, especially the loss of an unstressed vowel. It is found in both[synchronic and diachronic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synchrony_and_diachrony "Synchrony and diachrony")analyses of languages. Its opposite, whereby sounds are added, is[epenthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epenthesis "Epenthesis").
