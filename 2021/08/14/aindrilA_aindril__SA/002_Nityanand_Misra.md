Thank you, Korada Garu. No doubt there are many ways to derive the word, if one wants to. For example, in addition to the above, we can go for इन्द्रस्यापत्यं पुमान् ऐन्द्रिः, ऐन्द्रिरस्त्यस्या इति ऐन्द्रिला (सिध्मादित्वात् लच्) or ऐन्द्रिम् लातीति ऐन्द्रिला (कपिलिकादित्वत् लत्वम्) on lines of words likeश्रीलः and श्रीला. But my question is different. Does the word ঐন্দ্রিলা in Bangla come from Sanskrit? If not, we do not need to look into Sanskrit sources for the root. And if it does, there must be some reference to the word ऐन्द्रिला in Sanskrit texts. Hence my question (repeated): Does anybody know a reference to this word (or a character named Aindrilā) in Sanskrit texts?

  

PS: After I posted, I came to know that the reputed dictionary Samsad Bangla Obhidhan (সংসদ বাংলা অভিধান, Shailendra Biswas, Calcutta: 1955) does list the word ঐন্দ্রিলা and gives the same meaning, viz. বৃত্রাসুরের কন্যা (“daughter of Vṛtrāsura”). So what was the source of Biswas? Probably the word is used in Bangla literature? As Vṛtrāsura being a character originally present in Sanskrit texts, is it possible the name ঐন্দ্রিলা occurs in some Bangla version/adaption of a Sanskrit text (like the Ramayana of Krittivasa)?
