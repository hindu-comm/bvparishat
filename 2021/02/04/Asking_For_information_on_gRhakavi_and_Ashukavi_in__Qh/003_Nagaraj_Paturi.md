Venerable monk Sri WelipitiyeIdananda-jishared with me the following link that respected Dr R Ganesh-ji shared with him :

  

<https://www.prekshaa.in/extempore-poetry-sanskrit-ashu-catu>

  

The words used in the link indicate that the focus is on आशु and चाटु

  

Classifications provide there are

  

Classification -I (Rajashekhara) :

  

1\. सारस्वत

2\. आभ्यासिक पौरस्त्य

3\. औपदेशिक

  

 Classification -II (Rajashekhara) :

1\. काव्यविद्यास्नातक

2\. हृदयकवि

3\. अन्यापदेशी

4\. पौरस्त्य

5\. घटमान

6\. महाकवि

7\. कविराज

  

Classification -III (Rajashekhara):

1\. गृहकवि

2\. सभाकवि

  

Here is where we find गृहकवि

  

The article gives the definitions of the two as follows :

  

गृहकविone who composes poetry leisurely, without external constraints

  

*सभाकवि*one who composes poetry in public, with external constraints

  

Classification IV:

  

1. असूर्यंपश्य

2. निषण्ण

3\. प्रायोजनिक

  

There is a separate section on आशुकविता

  

So to treatगृहकवि and आशुकवि together is not in line with the material in the article.
