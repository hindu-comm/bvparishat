Dear Vishal Ji,

  

Thank you for your article. I would like to share an anecdote that can perhaps add to your research.  

  

I spent a few days digitizing our family tree during the beginning of the lockdown. It dates back nearly 500 years.

  

Our family tree starts with someone named Pandit Kesho Gopal ji whosename is mentioned in the Guru Granth sahib (Page no 923). The inscription on the top-left corner of the family tree states that he served as one of the spiritual guides (upAdhyAya) of the sikh Guru Sri Amardas Ji.

  

I attach an image for yourreference.  

  

The verse reads -

  

अंते सितगुf बोिलआ मै िपछै कीरतनु क\`रअ\< िनरबाणु जीउ ॥ **केसो गोपाल पंिडत** सिदअ\< ह\`र ह\`र कथापड़िह पुराणु जीउ ॥ ह\`र कथा पड़ीऐ ह\`र नामु सुणीऐ बेबाणु ह\`र रंगु गुर भावए ॥ िपंडु पतिल िक\`रआ दीवाफु ल ह\`र स\`र पावए ॥ ह\`र भाइआ सितगुf बोिलआ ह\`र िमिलआ पुरखु सुजाणु जीउ ॥ रामदास सोढीितलकु दीआ गुर सबदु सचु नीसाणु जीउ ॥५॥(page 923)  

  

What I found interesting is that the interpretation of the verse that includes our ancestor's name has been completely modified to make it mean something else. This I discovered on asking a very dear Sikh friend as to the interpretation of the verse that includedour ancestors' name. I include the interpretation document as well.

  

Thus furthering your point.
