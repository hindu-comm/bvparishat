I think formations like these  
are possible because of the following rule (which allows genitive as an  
option to instrumental):  
  
tulyārthair atulopamābhyāṁ tṛtīyānyatarasyām \| Pāṇini 2.3.72 \|  
  

This rule doe not apply in the case of इव as explained by Nyasa:  
  
अर्थग्रहणं पदान्तरनिरपेक्षया ये तुल्यार्थतामाहुस्तत्परिग्रहार्थम्। तेन  
पदान्तरसान्निध्येन द्योतका इवादयो निवर्तिता भवन्ति-- गौरिव गवय इति,  
यथा गौस्तथा गवय इति।  
  
It applies only to the cases, where the words themselves directly mean  
equal. सदृशः, समः etc. So इव and others द्योतक-s are excepted.  
  
I do not remember any Vartika, optionally acceptable for तृतीया,  
"पृथग्विनानानभिस्तृतीयान्यतरस्याम्" makes the Instrumental optional  
with these specific अव्यय-s. variable with द्वितीया, and पञ्चमी.
