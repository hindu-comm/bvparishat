Further evidence from Purāṇas that Hastināpura is on the banks of Gaṅgā and not Yamunā.  
  
विष्णुपुराणम्  
शतानीकादश्वमेधदत्तो भविता॥ ४.२१.५॥  
तस्मादप्यधिसीमकृष्णः॥ ४.२१.६॥  
अधिसीमकृष्णान्निचक्षुः॥ ४.२१.७ ॥  
यो गङ्गयाऽपहृते हस्तिनापुरे कौशाम्ब्यां निवत्स्यति॥ ४.२१.८ ॥  
Verse 4.21.8 talks about King Nicakṣu living in Kauśāmbī when Hastināpura will be submerged by Gaṅgā. The inference is that Gaṅgā, not Yamunā, flows near Hastināpura.  
  
पद्मपुराणम्  
ततोऽहमागमं पुण्यं हस्तिनापुरमुत्तमम्।  
यत्र श्रीपतिपादाब्जजाता गङ्गा सरिद्वरा॥ ६.२०८.४४ ॥  
In 6.208.44, the the Brāhmaṇa says - *Then I went to the sacred and foremost city of Hastināpura, where there is (=flows) Gaṅgā, which is born from the lotus-feet of the Lord the is the best among the rivers.* This is a direct statement stating Gaṅgā flows where Hastināpura is.  
  
श्रीमद्भागवतम्  
लाङ्गलाग्रेण नगरमुद्विदार्य गजाह्वयम्।  
विचकर्ष स गङ्गायां प्रहरिष्यन्नमर्षितः॥ १०.६८.४१ ॥  
जलयानमिवाघूर्णं गङ्गायां नगरं पतत्।  
आकृष्यमाणमालोक्य कौरवाः जातसंभ्रमाः॥ १०.६८.४२ ॥  
The above verses describe Balarāma dragging Hastināpura into the Gaṅgā. Inference to be made again that the river Gaṅgā flows next to Hastināpura.  
  
Satyanārāyaṇa Dāsa Ji has already mentioned the narrative of King Śantanu and Gaṅgā Devi.  
  
As Satyanārāyaṇa Dāsa Ji has translated works of Jīva Gosvāmī, he may explain better the Kramasandarbhaḥ on Bhāgavatam 1.19.6. Prima facie, it does not say that Parīkṣit sat on the banks of Yamunā, but states that the river is Gaṅgā only.
