Vasudeva (I)--of the family of Yayāti; a son of Deva-

mīḍha (also Śūra) and Māriṣā.

  

Refer:

  

\[Footnote: 1) Bhā. IX. 24., 23-45; Br. III. 61. 23; 71. 146, 160-1, 174; M.

44\. 72; Vā. 86. 28; 96. 144, 159-161, 198; 98. 94; Vi. IV. 14. 19.\]

  

<http://www.sanskrit-lexicon.uni-koeln.de/scans/PUIScan/2014/web/webtc/indexcaller.php>  

  

\(i\) Vasudeva was the son of Śūrasena, and the husband

of Devakī, the father of Śrī Kṛṣṇa, the brother of

Kuntī, and the minister of Ugrasena. It was Vasudeva

who sent the priest Kaśyapa to Śataśṛṅga to perform

the purification ceremonies such as naming etc. of the

Pāṇḍavas. (M.B. Ādi Parva, Chapter 123, Dākṣiṇātya-

pāṭha).
