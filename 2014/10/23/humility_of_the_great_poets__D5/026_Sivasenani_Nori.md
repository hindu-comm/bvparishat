I don't think that this is a stuti by Sankaracarya. Three reasons: Firstly, this stotra is not found in the Stotras included in the Complete Works of Sankaracarya published by Vani Vilas Press with the blessings of the Sringeri Matha. Secondly, the Stotra itself says that the author is more than 85 years old at the time of writing this stotra, whereas Sankaracarya is known to have stopped his dehayatra at quite a young age:

  

परित्यक्ता देवा विविधविधसेवाकुलतयामया पञ्चाशीतेरधिकमपनीते तु वयसि।  
इदानीं चेन्मातस्तव यदि कृपा नापि भवितानिरालम्बो लम्बोदरजननि कं यामि शरणम् ॥५॥  

  

Finally, the sense of the stotra cannot fit what is known about Acarya. For instance, nothing in the first verse is appropriate:

  

न मन्त्रं नो यन्त्रं तदपि च न जाने स्तुतिमहोन चाह्वानं ध्यानं तदपि च न जाने स्तुतिकथाः।  
न जाने मुद्रास्ते तदपि च न जाने विलपनंपरं जाने मातस्त्वदनुसरणं क्लेशहरणम् ॥१॥  

  

How can the commentator of Upanishads not know Devimantra? How can the sthaapaka of Sricakras not know Yantra? How can the writer of so many stutis and other works not know stuti of Devi? How can a Sishta like Sankaracarya not know the poojaavidhaana involving aavaahana, dhyaana etc.? How can the आलयः of Sruti, Smriti and Puraana not know stutikathaas? How can the proponet of Srividyaa not know the mudras of Devi?
