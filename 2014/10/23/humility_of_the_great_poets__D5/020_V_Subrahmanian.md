    Here are some sentiments expressed by the devotee in the Shivanandalahari

    of Shankarabhagavatpada:

    Verse 14 of Shivanandalahari:

    प्रभुस्त्वं दीनानां खलु परमबन्धुः पशुपते

    प्रमुख्योऽहं तेषामपि किमुत बन्धुत्वमनयोः ।

    त्वयैव क्षन्तव्याः शिव मदपराधाश्च सकलाः

    प्रयत्नात्कर्तव्यं मदवनमियं बन्धुसरणिः ॥

    O Lord of creatures! Are You not the Almighty as also the greatest friend of
    the miserable, of whom I am the chief? Is there not, then, a close kinship
    between us two? Therefore, O You bestower of good, You should forgive all my
    transgressions and work for my salvation even if it be fraught with
    difficulty.  For this is the test of close relationship everywhere.

    Here the devotee says the darkness of his ignorance is extremely 
    thick ...

    एको वारिजबान्धवः क्षितिनभोव्याप्तं तमोमण्डलं
    भित्त्वा लोचनगोचरोऽपि भवति त्वं कोटिसूर्यप्रभः |
    वेद्यः किन्न भवस्यहो घनतरं कीदृग्भवेन्मत्तम-
    स्तत्सर्वं व्यपनीय मे पशुपते साक्षात् प्रसन्नो भव ||५८ ||

    Here the hard-hearted nature of the devotee is submitted: 
     
    एष्यत्येष जनिं मनोऽस्य कठिनं तस्मिन्नटानीति मद्-

      रक्षायै गिरिसीम्नि कोमलपदन्यासः पुराभ्यासितः

      नो चेद्दिव्यगृहान्तरेषु सुमनस्तल्पेषु वेद्यादिषु

      प्रायः सत्सु शिलातलेषु नटनं शम्भो किमर्थं तव ॥

    Here is one from the Shivabhujangastotram:

    पशुं वेत्सि चेन्मां तमेवाधिरूढः

    कलङ्कीति वा मूर्ध्नि धत्से तमेव ।

    द्विजिह्वः पुनः सोऽपि ते कण्ठभूषा

    त्वदङ्गीकृताः शर्व सर्वेऽपि धन्याः  ॥ १२ ॥

    O Sharva, if you think I am an animal (jāḍyam), have you not chosen a bull
    for your vehicle?  If I am a sinner, you have placed the moon on your

    head (Chandra had sinned by coveting his guru's wife). If you think
    I am a liar (double-talk), you have made the two-tongued snake

    your necklace. If all these, the bull, the moon and the snake,
    are blessed by Your association, why not me, with all these
    defects? 

    Here is a short conversation from the Mādhavīya shankara vijaya

    Towards the end of His human role, Shankara Bhagavatpada
    was questioned by His Paramaguru Gaudapadacharya:

    कच्चित्सर्वां वेत्सि गोविन्दनाम्नो हृद्या विद्या संसृदुद्धारकृद्या ।

    कच्चित्तत्त्वं तत्त्वमानन्दरूपं नित्यं सच्चिन्निर्मलं वेत्सि वेद्यम् ॥

    कच्चिन्नित्याः शत्रवो निर्जितास्ते कच्चित्प्राप्ता सद्गुणाः शान्तिपूर्वा: ।

    कच्चिद्योगः सधितोऽष्टाङ्गयुक्तः कच्चिच्चित्तं साधु चित्तत्त्वगं ते ॥

    [ '' Do you fully know the delightful teaching of Govindapada
    which saves one from transmigratory existence? Have you realized

    the Truth, which is eternal, blemishless, of the nature of existence,

    consciousness and bliss and which should be known?  Have you conquered
    the constant foes (lust, anger, greed, pride, delusion and jealousy)? Have you
    acquired good qualities such as tranquility? Have you accomplished

    the eight-limbed yoga? Is your mind absorbed in the Supreme 
    Consciousness?]

    इत्याचार्यवर्येण तेन प्रेम्णा पृष्टः शङ्करः साधुशीलः ।
    भक्त्युद्रेकाद्भाष्पपर्याकुलाक्षो बध्नन्मूर्धन्यञ्जलिं व्याजहार ॥

    यद्यत्पृष्टं स्पष्टमाचार्यपादैस्तत्तत्सर्वं भो भविष्यत्यवश्यम् ।

    कारूण्याब्धेः कल्पयुषमत्कटाक्षैर्दृष्टस्याऽऽहुर्दुर्लभं किन्नु जन्तोः ॥

    [On being so questioned lovingly by the excellent Advaitic preceptor,

    Shankara of noble conduct reverentially joined his palms above

    his head and, with his eyes filled with tears owing to a surfeit of
    devotion, said 'Whatever has been clearly asked by Your Holiness
    will certainly come to pass.  What indeed is difficult of
    attainment for a being on whom have fallen the side-glances

    of an ocean of mercy, Yourself?]

    At the end of his brahmasūtravṛttiḥ, Sri Sadashivendra Saraswati says:

    जडः क्वाहं बालः क्व च गहनवेदान्तसरणि-

    स्तथाप्याम्नायार्थं परमशिवयोगीन्द्रकृपया ।

    विजानन्व्याख्यानं व्यरचयमहं वेदशिरस-

    स्तदेतत्क्षन्तव्यं मयि सदयदृष्ट्या बुधजनैः ॥
