[[Nityanand Misra	2014-06-30, 06:14:11 [Source](https://groups.google.com/g/bvparishat/c/1qe0Fja2vo4)]]





\[Words which stand for deities and others which stand for auspiciousness – all those are never to be criticized\] – from the viewpoint of लिपि (वर्ण) or even from the viewpoint of गण.

I.e., even if such words violate the guidelines used in poetics for लिपि (वर्ण) and गण, they are not to be criticized.

मल्लिनाथ cites this verse (originally said to be from भामह but I could not trace this in my edition of काव्यालङ्कार) in both किरातार्जुनीय and शिशुपालवध.

लिपितः means from the viewpoint of वर्ण. The शब्दकल्पद्रुमः says लिपिः, स्त्री, लिप् + “इगुपधात् कित्” (उणा० ४ . ११९) इति इन् . स च कित् . लिखितवर्णम्. As per वर्णशास्त्र (as well as तन्त्र), each वर्ण has its own significance, its own बीजत्व, and stands for different meanings. E.g. वकार is the बीज of अमृत, hence considered very auspicious for beginning a work. अष्टाध्यायी (वृद्धिरादैच्) begins with वकार and so does the रामचरितमानस (वर्णानामर्थसङ्घानां रसानां छन्दसामपि).

गणतः means from the viewpoint of गण. As per गणशास्त्र, each of the eight गणs have their own देवता and फल –

मो भूमिः श्रियमातनोति य जलं वृद्धिं र चाग्निर्मृतं सो वायुः परदेशदूरगमने त व्योम शून्यं फलम्।

जः सूर्यो रुजमादधाति विपुलं भेन्दुर्यशो निर्मलं नो नाकश्च सुखप्रदः फलमिदं प्राहुर्गणानां बुधाः।

There are further specifications in poetics, like it is auspicious to begin a work with मगण of three heavy syllables. Examples are (1) The RCM again, which begins with वर्णाना... – वर्णानामर्थसङ्घानां रसानां छन्दसामपि (2) My Guru’s श्रीभार्गवराघवीयम् which begins with सीतारा... – सीतारामयशोमञ्जुमुक्तामोदमुदं मुदे. Similarly, a work should not be begun with a जगण, unless it is followed by a भगण or has the name of a deity (I do not have the references with me now, but should be easy to find them, Dr. Ganesh may know better.)

Now both किरातार्जुनीय (श्रियः कुरूणामधिपस्य पालिनीम्) and शिशुपालवध (श्रियः पतिः श्रीमति शासितुं जगत्) begin with the वंशस्थ metre – which has जगण followed by तगण – जतौ तु वंश्स्थमुदीरितं जरौ. But as they both begin with श्री – name of the supreme goddess, due to the auspicious name, the defect from the viewpoint of गणशास्त्र (and लिपिशास्त्र/वर्णशास्त्र/तन्त्र) is cast away.
