The form पिनाकधृक् cannot result from धृञ् धारणे by applying any of क्विन् or क्विप्.



If we apply क्विप् in case of पिनाकं धरति by क्विप् च (पा॰सू॰ ३.२.७५), ह्रस्वस्य पिति कृति तुक् (पा॰सू॰ ६.१.६९) will apply resulting in तुगागम, which will make the प्रातिपदिक and final form as “पिनाकधृत्” and not “पिनाकधृक्”. “पिनाकं धरतीति पिनाकधृत्”.



If we allow for application of क्विन्, then ह्रस्वस्य पिति कृति तुक् (पा॰सू॰ ६.१.६९) will not apply but the प्रातिपदिक will be अजन्त “पिनाकधृ”. Then by क्विन्प्रत्ययस्य कुः (३०६, ८.२.६२) which ordains कुत्व of final अल् (अच् or हल्) one will get पिनाकध्क् which will lead to संयोगान्तस्य लोपः (पा॰सू॰ ८.२.२३) causing elision of final क् resulting in पिनाकध् which will read to पिनाकत्/पिनाकद् – I have not come across such forms.



The broader question is if पिनाकधृक् can be formed from धृञ् धारणे in “any way”. That needs more thought. First it needs to be inferred if it can be formed without using पृषोदरादित्व argument or assuming an appropriate औणादिक suffix following Mahabhashya’s कार्याद्विद्यादनूबन्धमेतच्छास्त्रमुणादिषु.

For that we need a कृत् suffix which satisfies the following –

1.  

    The suffix is कित्/गित्/ङित् so that there is no वृद्धि or गुण in धृ

    

2.  

    After अनुबन्धलोप, the suffix should be left with a single consonant
    from चवर्ग. Why not कवर्ग or श्? Since by लशक्वतद्धिते (पा॰सू॰
    १.३.८) they are इत् in a non-तद्धित suffix.

    

3.  

    The suffix is not पित् to avoid तुगागम

    

I cannot recall such a suffix in Ashtadhyayi, but it may be the उणादि Sutras. If it does not exist in the उणादि Sutras also, it may be assumed following कार्याद्विद्यादनूबन्धमेतच्छास्त्रमुणादिषु.



Or one may use पृषोदरादीनि यथोपदिष्टम् (पा॰सू॰ ६.३.१९) and say that तकार in पिनाकधृत् becomes ककार in पिनाकधृक्.



If an authoritative source (e.g. Nirukta or a Puranic verse) says that पिनाकधृक् comes from धृञ् धारणे or explains it as पिनाकं धरतीति पिनाकधृक्, it may be accepted. If that is not the case, then it is not advisable to resort to बाहुलकत्व/पृषोदरादित्व/ऊह्य औणादिक प्रत्यय et cetera. Bhattoji Diksita says in the प्रौढमनोरमा – “बाहुलकं त्वगतिकगतिः” (प्रौ.म. ३) – such बाहुलक means are the resort of those left with no option. बाहुलकत्व/पृषोदरादित्व/ऊह्य औणादिक प्रत्यय are okay if no Paninian solution exists, but if a Paninian solution exists, then resorting to these arguments is not preferable.
