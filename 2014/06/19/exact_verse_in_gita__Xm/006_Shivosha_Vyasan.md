Dear Scholars,

In my childhood, I had seen the so-called "gItA sAra" starting with "jo huA acchA huA.." which made me doubt- "then if some theft, murder, waris done- is it good too?" If yes- then for whom? the doer or the victim?

If "parivartan saMsAr kA niyaM hai"- then whatabout that which is stable? What will a human-being rely on? Will gItA just say about saMsAra and leave the paramAtmA who is viSvavyApI?



Then I learnt not even one statement out of that list- was true to gItA spirit.(God knows who prepared it- and when and why? whether it is based on gItA at all or not; when someone says- something is gItA "sAra" why will people have to search for the sources and mostly fail to find the statements..?This confusion created is enough beared. Enough damage done.



gItA is one of India's (or world's)greatest books ever produced.. inspiring generations together- all over the globe. I think now its time to come out with real gItA sAra with Sanskritquotations- (and asimple translation in English and Hindi) so that when one quotes some line, one is damn sure- it is fromgItA. There will be surely some lists prepared by individuals ( I heard about one prepared by Ramana Maharshi with 50 quotes, could not get it..)or some traditional institutions, but definitely notreaching to common public who are away from them.. and surprisingly, these meaningless lines like "jo huA acchA huA"reach one and all, creating a lot of confusion!!



Scholarsrealated toSanskrit and people from other streams, who have a habit of reading gItA, will certainlyhave their own favorite quotes from the divine book. I have prepared a list of my own choice- I could select 21statements which inspired me - (looking just upto 6th chapter- I would like to add others too.)



Can others in the forumcome up with their lists too, so thatthe oft-repeated and inspiredquotes be picked, and prepared as a list of "true gItA sAra". Some 11 or 15 or21(or less or more)- and release in the WorldSanskrit Conference orsomebig event related to Samskrita Bharati, where, common(I mean- unrelated to Sanskrit in anyway)people come- and make the listpublic, so that ordinarypeople (who can only know gItA by name and not spirit) can come out with real quotes when needed?

Ifthe esteemed members of the forumaccept on the statement as "sAra" level (undoubtedly gItA has each line (700+ \*2 lines)complete in itself.. but for general people such "sAra statements" will help), then it is as good as approved.
