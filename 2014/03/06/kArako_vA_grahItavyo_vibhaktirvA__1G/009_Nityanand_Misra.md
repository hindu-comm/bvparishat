It might be useful to see how different commentators have interpreted the वाक्यपदीय verses. Here is a Gujarati translation by J M Shukla (1984, L D Institute of Indology, Ahmedabad, page 313) -

<http://www.new.dli.ernet.in/scripts/FullindexDefault.htm?path1=/data14/upload/0007/714&first=358&last=358&barcode=99999990316884>


Prima facie the verses talk about शक्ति and that is how Shukla has translated. I do not know what "Karaka relation" in Prof. Deshpande's translation stands for, if the phrase is used for the word शक्ति or something else.

It does not appear that the verses or the translation by Shukla or Prof. Deshpande talk about same case ending (समानविभक्ति). Shukla talks about शक्ति and कालप्रत्यय, Prof. Deshpande talks about Karaka relation and Gillon talks about case - they do not seem to be the same. The same कारक can have different case (विभक्ति) depending on which rule of Panini is applied.

One more thing, I do not think there is any सम्प्रदानत्व in तां प्रणम्य तस्यै \[मदुदन्तम्\] आख्याय. In तस्यै \[मदुदन्तं\] आख्याय (= आ + ख्या + ल्यप्), I believe तस्यै is कर्म as the root आ + ख्या used here in the meaning of कथने, just like आख्यन्मुनिस्तस्य शिवं समाधेः (Bhattikavya 1.19). Roots used in the sense of कथने are द्विकर्मक, like ब्रू (e.g. माणवकं धर्मं ब्रूते) –

दुह्याच्पचिदण्ड्रुधिप्रच्छिचिब्रूशासुजिमथ्मुषाम्।

कर्मयुक्स्यादकथितं तथा स्यान्नीहृकृष्वहाम्॥

अर्थनिबन्धनेयं सञ्ज्ञा (VSK 539)

आ + ख्या is synonymous with ब्रू, so in तस्यै मदुदन्तमाख्याय, तत् should get the कर्मसंज्ञा by अकथितं च (PS 1-4-51). Then why the चतुर्थी? One explanation is क्रियार्थोपपदस्य च कर्मणि स्थानिनः (PS 2-3-14) which governs when a कर्म gets चतुर्थी -तां बोधयितुं मदुदन्तमाख्याय becomes तस्यै मदुदन्तमाख्याय as the verb बुध् + णिच् is not stated in the sentence.
