> > Maybe, but Hanumān being Śiva himself is taken for granted in the > Rāmānanda Sampradāya. >
> 
> > I remember once in 2009 a disciple asked my Guru if there was any > evidence for the same in the Vālmīkīya Rāmāyaṇa, Gurudeva had said > that there is a hint in this verse from Sundarakāṇḍa where Hanumān is > thinking to Himself – >
> धिगस्तु राजसं भावमनीशमनवस्थितम्। ईश्वरेणापि यद्रागान्मया सीता न > रक्षिता॥ VR 5-55-17 ॥ >
> Where the second line can be interpreted as यत् रागात् मया ईश्वरेण > (=शिवेन) अपि सीता न रक्षिता, since Īśvara is one of the names of Śiva. > So मया ईश्वरेण can be explained as “by me, who is Śiva”. Of course > other interpretations like “by me, who is capable” are also possible > here. >
> Now coming back to Rāmānanda Sampradāya, Gosvāmī Tulasīdāsa has > unequivocally stated Śivatva of Hanumān at tens of palces in his > works. While there is not direct statement and only subtle hints in > the Rāmacaritamānasa, here are some of the references from other works > of Tulasīdāsa – >
> **1. Hanumāna Cālīsā ** >
> शङ्कर स्वयं केसरीनन्दन। HC 6 । >
> A Pāṭhabheda, which is currently popular and used by Gita Press, goes > शङ्करसुवन केसरीनन्दन. However, this does not appear to be correct > since the Awadhi/Braja word सुवन/सुअन (coming from षु > प्रसवसैश्वर्ययोः) means “son” and the same word शङ्करसुवन is used by > Tulasīdāsa in the Vinayapatrikā for Ganeśa (शङ्करसुवन भवानीनन्दन, VP > 1-1). >
> **2. Dohāvalī** >
> Two couplets state that Śiva became Hanumān: >
> जेहि सरीर रति राम सों सोइ आदरहिं सुजान। रुद्रदेह तजि नेहबस वानर भे > हनुमान॥ D 142 ॥ >
> जानि राम सेवा सरस समुझि करब अनुमान। पुरुषा ते सेवक भए हर ते भे हनुमान॥ > D 143 ॥ >
> 
> > 
> > **3. Vinayapatrikā** >
> The section on Hanumatstuti (VP 25 to 36) contains Padas devoted to > Hanumān. There are at least ten places where names or adjectives of > Śiva are used to describe Hanumān – >
> रुद्र अवतार (VP 25.3), महादेव (VP 26.1), कपाली (VP 26.1), > शाकिनीडाकिनीपूतनाप्रेतवेतालभूतप्रमथयूथयन्ता (VP 26.7), वानराकारविग्रह > पुरारी (VP 27.1), हर (VP 27.2), रुद्राग्रणी (VP 27.3), जटाजूटधारी (VP > 28.2), वामदेव (VP 28.5, also used for VP 8.3 and VP 12.1), मनमथमथन (VP > 29.3), शूलपाणी (VP 29.5). >
