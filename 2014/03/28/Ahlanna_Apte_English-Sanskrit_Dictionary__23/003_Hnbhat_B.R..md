I could not find the word आह्लन्न, in VS Apte's dictionary as such, but only variant for ह्लादित, ह्लन्न for the past participle of the root ह्लाद् :

  

ह्लाद् hlādह्लाद् 1 Ā. (ह्लादते, *ह्लन्न or ह्लादित) 1 To be glad or delighted,* rejoice.-2 To sound.-3 To be pleasant; ह्लादते तनयस्पर्शः L. D. B. -Caus. (ह्लादयति) To gladden, delight.  

  

As explained by Shivakumari, it is variable form for the verb, ह्लाद

दयति) To gladden, delight.  

  

ह्लादित seems to be incorrect.ह्लन्न or ह्लादित, when derived from ह्लादी सुखे च.

  

आह्लन्न is the example given for the participle suffix, which differenciates the form ह्लाद अव्यक्ते शब्दे, which gives the participle form ह्रादित। and not ह्लादित variable with ह्लन्न. This is possible only both are recorded as ह्लाद् or ह्राद्
