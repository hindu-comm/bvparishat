The verses refer to a numismatic event in the history of Kashmir where an old currency (Balāhata, Bālāhata or a "currency struck by force \[of the current king\]") was replaced by a new currency Dīnnāra by the heir-apparent Torāmāṇā.

English Translation by Ranjit Sitaram Pandit (1935 Indian Press Limited, Allahabad):

Hiraṇya and Torāmāṇā, his two sons, enjoying sovereignty and the privilege of heir-apparent respectively, thereafter afforded gratification of the land. 102. Having forbidden the abundant coins struck by the brother, which was improper, Dinnāras struck in his own name were put in circulation by Torāmāṇā. 103.

तदनन्तर हिरण्य तथा तोरमाण नामके उसके दो पुत्र राजा तथा युवराज बनकर सुन्दर शासन द्वारा प्रजावर्गकी रक्षा करने लगे। १०२। राजा तोरमाणने बालाहत नामक प्राचीन सिक्कों का प्रचलन बन्द करके अपने प्रभावसे दीनार नामका सिक्का चलाया। १०३।

Shastri gives the reading हिरण्यतोरमाणाख्यं which does not appear to be correct since व्यधत्ताम् is in the dual (वि धा लङ् प्रथमपुरुष द्विवचन)

Here is the Anvaya (by me) of the two verses

अथ साम्राज्ययुवराज्यत्वभाजने हिरण्यतोरमाणाख्यौ तत्सुतौ क्षितेः रञ्जनं व्यधत्ताम्।

तोरमाणेन असमञ्जसं बलाहतानां प्राचुर्यं विनिवार्य स्वाहताः दीन्नाराः संप्रवर्तिताः॥

Interesting facts to note -

1) असमञ्जसम् is used as as indeclinable adverb (=unbecomingly or improperly)

2) आहत (आ + हन् + कत) literally means "struck", but hear in बलाहत and स्वाहत means minted.

3) Sitaram Pandit's translation of बलाहत/बालाहत as "coins struck by the brother" appears to be incorrect, unless बल/बाल was another name of हिरण्य. Either बलाहत/बालाहत was the name of the currency struck in the name of a \[possibly former\] king named बल/बाल. Or बलाहत could also mean "currency struck by force \[of the reigning king\]". Commentaries may be referred to get a better idea.
