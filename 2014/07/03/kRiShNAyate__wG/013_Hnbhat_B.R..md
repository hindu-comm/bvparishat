The above is a general commentary without taking into consideration of the meaning in total.

  

In the present case, Mr. Nityananda, has explained the problem correctly and gave his own grammatically correct Anvaya, but not much convincing in the meaning.

  

Anyhow, the commentator Jivananda Vidyasagara, without giving any clues, explains the meaning in general as we can see in the web pages, the verb related with कर as its object and अङ्गार as the agent of the verb.

  

![Inline image 1](https://groups.google.com/group/bvparishat/attach/9b159931813dcda4/image.png?part=0.1&view=1)

  

And one more irregular usage of णिच् after कृ in स्वार्थे, which is meant for चुरादिगण. without the causative meaning in the present case
explained as नैव कुर्वीत।

  

And he takes the reading सख्यं प्रीतिं चापि न कारयेत् = न कुर्वीत.

  

करं कृष्णायते = हस्तम्, संस्प्रष्टुरिति शेषः। कृष्णायते = कृष्णं करोति; मलिनीकरोतीति यावत्।

  

Again the same problem, कृष्णं करोति = कृष्णयते, कृष्णयते would be the causative णिच् suffix would give, as "प्रातिपदिकाद् धात्वर्थे बहुलमिष्ठवच्च" prescribes णिच् in the above sense. The क्यष् mentioned in Vacaspatya, by "लोहितादिडाज्भ्यः क्यष् ३।१।१३" modified by "लोहितडाज्भ्यः क्यष्"लोहितडाज्भ्यः क्यष् वचनम्, भृशादिष्वितराणि। यानि लोहितादिषु पठ्यन्ते तेभ्यः क्यङेव, अपरिपठितेभ्यस् तु क्यषेव भवति। in काशिका.

This would give the meaning, अकृष्णः कृष्णो भवति, कृष्णायते/ति and the agent would be करः or अङ्गार। And the करम् has to be taken with the already finished sentence as object, उष्णो दहति चाङ्गारः करम्, शीतः कृष्णायते (स्वयमङ्गारः)। as suggested by Mr. Nityananda, which is neither figurative as suggested by Mr. Murthy nor feasible by the meaning suggested by the वैरं or प्रीति, or सख्य or प्रीति, which is related with दहति and कृष्णायते as the result.
