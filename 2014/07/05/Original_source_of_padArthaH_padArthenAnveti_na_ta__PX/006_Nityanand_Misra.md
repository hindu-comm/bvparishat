My curiosity arose from this: नागेश starts this comment with भाष्यकारो न्यायं दर्शयति – “अगमकत्वादित्यादिना” ….  
  

Now दर्शयति may be taken to mean “shows” or “explains”. If the former meaning is taken, then the न्याय is from भाष्य and इदम् probably refers to the भाष्य. If the latter meaning is taken, then न्याय is from the वार्त्तिक and इदम् probably refers to the वार्त्तिक.

As per your interpretation, इदम् refers to the वार्त्तिक.

I later checked S D Joshi’s translation (Univ of Poona, 1968). While he does not give a complete translation of Nagesa’s comment, he interprets that Patanjali’s principle of गमकत्व is different from the वार्त्तिक. Please refer Note (41) on second and third pages of attached PDF. Following Joshi’s interpretation, the principle of गमकत्व (which is explained by Nagesa भाष्यकारो न्यायं दर्शयति – “अगमकत्वादित्यादिना”) in भाष्य, being different from the वार्त्तिक, would be what Nagesa refers to as इदम्.

Would be interesting to see if चारुदेवशास्त्री has explained this. Or if any other translators of महाभाष्य or उद्द्योत have shed light on this.
