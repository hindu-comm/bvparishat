Hare Krishna.  
Respected sirs,  
  
Thankyou very much for providing some references. This shall be very helpful.  
  
In Ramayana in a chapter in the second canto, which primarily consists  
of Rama addressing Sita as he tries to dissuade her from following him to  
the forest, he does address her as "Sita," "Maithili," and "Bhamini." So in  
that instance he is actually addressing her by her proper name.  
  
Also in Srimad Bhagavatam 8.7.37 Lord Siva addresses His wife as BhavAni.  
  
Are these the cases of dharma-vyatikrama? These conversations seem to  
be in normal course and not some special case like parasurama in which  
some special adjustments might have needed.  
  
Thankyou,
