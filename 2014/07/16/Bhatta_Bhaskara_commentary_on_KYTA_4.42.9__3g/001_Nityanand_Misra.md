> > Dear Shri Misraji!  
>   
> > 
> > There are two patha-s in TA, called the Dravida patha (DP) and Andhra > patha (AP).  
> Today, they are more or less synonymous with SriVaishnava and Smarta > patha-s.  
> The former being pracina, is followed by Bhatta Bhaskara Bhashya (BBB) > and the latter by Sayana Bhashya.  
> > 
> > The correspondence in TA is as follows:  
> > 
> > There are only 8 prasnas in DP, while it is divided into 10 prasnas in > AP.  
> > 
> > Prasnas 1-3 : no difference in sequence, though in content one > \[last\] anuvaka is more in TA 2 in AP, plus some patha-bhedas.  
> > 
> > Prasna 4 in DP is pitr-medha, which is 6th in AP. You have seen this > numbering and hence did not find the bhashya. Prasna 4 in DP has only > 12 anuvakas, while 7th prasna has 42 anuvakas. That is pravargya > prasna.  
> > 
> > Prasnas 5 and 6 are Taittiriyopanishad in DP, while they are split as > four prasnas, i.e, 7, 8, 9 and 10th in AP. In the last, there are over > 20 anuvakas added in AP.  
> > 
> > Prasna 7 - Pravargya mantras -> 4th in AP.  
> > 
> > Prasna 8 - Pravargya Brahmana -> 5th in AP.  
> > 
> > Thus, the mantra you are interested in, is to be seen at 7th prasna of > BBB.  
> > 
> > It is there in page 91 of Vol. III of TA published by Mysore edition > in 1902.  
>   
> > 
> > For quick reference, the short text of the BBB for 4.42.9 is:  
> > 
> > त्वम् **इन्द्रस्य गृहोऽसि सगुः साश्वः** । तं त्वां **प्रपद्ये** > प्रविशामि । किं बहुना - **यत्** वस्तु **मे अस्ति तेन** सर्वेण **सह** > ।।  
> >
