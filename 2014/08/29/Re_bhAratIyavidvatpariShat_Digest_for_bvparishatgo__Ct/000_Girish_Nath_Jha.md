Dear all

  

You are invited to a talk at the Sanskrit center of JNU on 3rd Sept at 4pm.

Prof Himanshu Pota from the University of New South Wales, Canberra,  
Australia is giving a talk in the center on the topic "Sanskrit  
Learning and Computational Linguistics". The schedule of the talk is  
as follows -  
  
Topic: "Sanskrit Learning and Computational Linguistics"  
Speaker : Prof Himanshu Pota, University of New South Wales, Canberra, Australia  
Day, Date & time: Wednesday, 3rd Sept, 4-5 pm  
Venue:Committee Room (first floor),Special Center for Sanskrit Studies, JNU,  
  
Abstract of the talk :  
----------------------------  
The talk will focus on the interaction between Sanskrit learning and  
the role played by computational linguistics. Although the beauty of  
the Panini grammar is overwhelming, a language is its usage and not  
just the grammar. Computational linguistics can enable Sanskrit  
learners and users to comprehend the usage and given them the full  
force and majesty of Sanskrit to express themselves.  
  
The talk will move from the ancient to the modern. Someone has said,  
"Not Sanskrit from the point of view of scholarship but a Sanskrit, a  
Sanskrit - how to put? - that opens the door to all Indian languages."  
Computational linguistics can use the wonderful framework of Sanskrit  
and extend it to modern Indian languages to lend them strength so that  
languages like Hindi can hold on their own in the worldwide family of  
languages.  
  
  
About the speaker  
--------------------------  
Hemanshu R. Pota received B.E. from Sardar Vallabhbhai Regional  
College of Engineering and Technology, Surat, India, in 1979, M.E.  
from the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore, India, in 1981, and  
the Ph.D. from the University of Newcastle, NSW, Australia, in 1985;  
all in Electrical Engineering.

  
He is currently an associate professor at the University of New South  
Wales, Canberra, Australia. He has held visiting appointments at the  
University of California, Los Angeles; the University of Delaware;  
Iowa State University; Kansas State University; Old Dominion  
University; the University of California, San Diego; and Centre for AI  
and Robotics, Bangalore. He has published 106 journal papers and 250  
international conference papers. He has attracted over $2M in  
externally reviewed research grants. Eleven PhD students and 8  
master’s students have completed their degrees under his supervision.
