Apart from the etymological meaning given for the word 'upaniṣat' (in the introduction to the Kaṭhopaniṣat bhāṣya that you have quoted here), there is another (several) instance/s of the term occurring in the Bṛhadāraṇyaka upaniṣat itself. For example:  
  

बृहदारण्यकोपनिषद्भाष्यम्।पञ्चमोऽध्यायः।पञ्चमं ब्राह्मणम्।मन्त्रः३

य एष एतस्मिन्मण्डले पुरुषस्तस्य भूरिति शिर एकं शिर एकमेतदक्षरं भुव इति बाहू द्वौ बाहू द्वे एते अक्षरे स्वरिति प्रतिष्ठा द्वे प्रतिष्ठे द्वे एते अक्षरे तस्योपनिषदहरिति हन्ति पाप्मानं जहाति च य एवं वेद ॥ ३ ॥  
  

For this term 'upaniṣat' occurring here, the Bhāṣya gives the meaning: rahasya abhidhānam' 'secret name'.  

  
bhāṣyam:  
तस्यास्य व्याहृत्यवयवस्य सत्यस्य ब्रह्मण उपनिषत् *रहस्यम् अभिधानम्*, येनाभिधानेन अभिधीयमानं तद्ब्रह्म अभिमुखी भवति लोकवत् ;  
  

This term occurs again in the very next mantra and there too the bhāṣyam gives the above meaning: 'secret name'.  
  

Also, in this very upaniṣat this word 'upaniṣat' occurs in yet another place:  
  

In this bhāṣya piece for the Br.Up. mantra 2.1.20 conclusion and beginning/introduction to Br.up.mantra 2.2.1 we have the occurrence of this word 'upaniṣat' in the bhāṣyam thrice:  
  

The Br.up. 2.1.20 mantra itself uses the word:  
  

तस्योपनिषत् - सत्यस्य सत्यमिति....  
  

For this the bhāṣyam is:...तस्मात् परस्यैव ब्रह्मणः सत्यस्य सत्यं नाम उपनिषत् परा\|  
  

The standard translation is: Therefore the highest secret name of the 'Truth of truth' belongs only to the Supreme Brahman. p.220 of Swami Madhavananda.  

  
तेषां कार्यकरणात्मकानां भूतानां सत्यत्वनिर्दिधारयिषया ब्राह्मणद्वयमारभ्यते सैव उपनिषद्व्याख्या । कार्यकरणसत्यत्वावधारणद्वारेण हि सत्यस्य सत्यं ब्रह्म अवधार्यते । अत्रोक्तम् [‘प्राणा वै सत्यं तेषामेष सत्यम्’ (बृ. उ. २-१-२०)](http://advaitasharada.sringeri.net/php/format.php?bhashya=Brha&page=02&hval=%E2%80%98%E0%A4%AA%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%B0%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%A3%E0%A4%BE%20%E0%A4%B5%E0%A5%88%20%E0%A4%B8%E0%A4%A4%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%AF%E0%A4%82%20%E0%A4%A4%E0%A5%87%E0%A4%B7%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%AE%E0%A5%87%E0%A4%B7%20%E0%A4%B8%E0%A4%A4%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%AF%E0%A4%AE%E0%A5%8D%E2%80%99%20%28%E0%A4%AC%E0%A5%83.%20%E0%A4%89.%20%E0%A5%A8-%E0%A5%A7-%E0%A5%A8%E0%A5%A6%29#BR_C02_S01_V20) इति ;तत्र के प्राणाः, कियत्यो वा प्राणविषया *उपनिषदः* का इति च — *ब्रह्मोपनिषत्*प्रसङ्गेन करणानां प्राणानां स्वरूपमवधारयति — पथिगतकूपारामाद्यवधारणवत् ॥  
  

In the translation of Swami Madhavananda, all the occurrences are given the meaning: 'secret name'.   
  

I pointed out the above just to add another dimension to the search of the original poster, Smt Usha Sanka. I have known from some sources that Madhva too gives the meaning 'rahasya nāma' for the word 'upaniṣat' occurring in the above context. It may also be noted that the dhātvartha based explanation to the term appearing in the Kathopanishat introduction (already presented by a member), the above 'secret name' meaning does not appear/is not covered.
