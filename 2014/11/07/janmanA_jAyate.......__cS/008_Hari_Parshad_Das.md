kṣamyatām\| purāṇa-kartā śrī-vyāsadevaḥ \| āstika-dṛṣṭyā sa na tu pramattaḥ na cerṣyādi-doṣa-dūṣitaḥ \| yadi janmanā jāyate śūdraḥ iti skānde vyāsa-kṛtaṁ vākyam apramāṇam tadārdha-kukkuṭīya-nyāyena sarveṣv api vyāsa-kṛta-purāṇeṣv aprāmāṇya-doṣa-bhayaḥ sambhaviṣyati \| yadi prakṣiptaḥ tarhi 'janmanā jāyate śūdraḥ' iti na tu 'īrṣyāsūyā-doṣa-dūṣitaḥ' \|  
  

yathā mahābhārate śānti-parve —  
  
"na viśeṣo ’sti varṇānāṁ sarvaṁ brāhmam idaṁ jagat  
brahmaṇā pūrvasṛṣṭaṁ hi karmabhir varṇatāṁ gatam"  
  

iti bhṛgvoktiḥ labhyate.  
  

yadi mahābhārata-vākye 'pi bhavān aprāmānyaṁ manyase tarhy aitareya-brāhmaṇe \[2:19\] kavaṣasya karmaṇā jāti-parivartanetihāsaḥ labhyate —  
  

**\[Aitareya-brāhmaṇa quote\]**  

ṛṣayo vai sarasvatyāṃ satram āsata. te kavaṣam ailūṣaṃ somād anayan. dāsyāḥ putraḥ kitavo 'brāhmaṇaḥ kathaṃ no madhye 'dīkṣiṣṭeti. tam bahir dhanvodavahann — atrainam pipāsā hantu, sarasvatyā udakam mā pād īti. sa bahir dhanvodūḷhaḥ pipāsayā vitta etad aponaptrīyam apaśyat. pra devatrā brahmaṇe gātur etv iti, tenāpām priyaṃ dhāmopāgachat. tam āpo 'nūdāyaṃs, taṃ sarasvatī samantam paryadhāvat tasmād dhāpy etarhi Parisārakam ity ācakṣate, yad enaṃ Sarasvatī samantam parisasāra.  
  
te vā ṛṣayo 'bruvan — vidur vā imāṃ deva, upemaṃ hvayāmahā iti. tatheti. tam upahvayanta, tam upahūyaitad aponaptrīyam akurvata. pra devatrā brahmaṇe gātur etv iti, tenāpām priyaṃ dhāmopāgachann upa devānām. **\[End of Quote\]**  
  

***Āṅgla-bhāṣānuvādaḥ*** — The sages were seated on the banks of the Sarasvati for a soma sacrifice. They drove away Kavaṣa Ailūṣa saying — “He is a dāsī-putra (son of a maid-servant), a cheat and a non-brāhmaṇa. How can he sit between us initiated folks?” They drove him out to a desert. They said — “Let him be killed by thirst. Let him not drink the waters of the sarasvatī.”  
  
Kavaṣa, driven out by the sages and afflicted by thirst envisioned the Aponaptrīya Sūkta of the Ṛg-veda \[10:30\], beginning with ‘pra devatrā’. Thus, Kavaṣa attained the abode of the waters. The demigods also attained prosperity due to Kavaṣa. Then the waters of the river Sarasvatī flowed all around Kavaṣa.  
  
The place where the river surrounded Kavaṣa is known even today as Parisāraka. The sages said — “He knows the gods. Call him to our sacrifice.” They called him and all of them together performed the aponaptrīya sacrifice to attain the gods of divine waters. **\[End of Translation\]**  
  

asmin prasaṅge adīkṣitasya dāsī-putrasyāpi tasminneva-kṣane brāhmaṇatva-prāptiḥ dṛśyate. ataḥ janmanā jāyate śūdraḥ iti na tu 'īrṣyāsuyā-doṣa-dūṣitaḥ'. janmanā jāyate śūdraḥ iti mata-bheda-mātraḥ.  
  

samprati mahītale eko 'pi janmanā kṣatriya-cakravartī samrāṭo nāsti. ata eva jāti na tu janmanaiva. karmaṇāpi jāti-lābhaḥ śāstreṣu dṛśyate. atra vālmīki-gautama-ṛṣyaśṛṅgādy itihāsa-udāharaṇāni śāstreṣu pramāṇāni santi.
