Here Prof. Korada takes the meaning of  
1) असत्यम् as सत्यशब्दवाच्यं ब्रह्म इति नास्ति किञ्चिद्वस्तु. English = There is nothing called Satyam ...  
2) अप्रतिष्ठम् as धर्मस्यापि नास्ति प्रामाण्यम्. English = Dharma is also meaningless \[??\].  
3) अनीश्वरम् as ईश्वरः इत्यपि अनृतमेव. English translation is missing.  
  

These interpretations are all questionable, since all the three words are नञ्समास, and are descriptions of जगत्. The verse says that as per the आसुर जनs, the जगत् is असत्यम्, अप्रतिष्ठम् and अनीश्वरम्. In the commentaries, the समासs are all taken as नञ्बहुव्रीहि (the only option, see below), so that the terms असत्यम्, अप्रतिष्ठम् and अनीश्वरम् become attributes of जगत्.  

  

Objection 1: How can असत्यम् be interpreted as सत्यशब्दवाच्यं ब्रह्म इति नास्ति किञ्चिद्वस्तु? As a नञ्तत्पुरुष, असत्यम् means (न सत्यम्) or "non-Satyam" or "that which is not Satyam". As नञ्बहुव्रीहि,असत्यम् means (नास्ति/न विद्यते सत्यं यस्मिन् तत् असत्यम्) "that in which there is no Satyam". There is no other समास possible here. Then how can असत्यम् mean "there is nothing called Satyam". **What is the Vigraha which gives this meaning and as per which Panini Sutra?** अब्राह्मण can mean either a non-Brahmin (नञ्तत्पुरुष) or "without a Brahmin" (नञ्बहुव्रीहि), but can अब्राह्मण mean "there is nothing called Brahmin".  
  

Objection 2: Same objection applies to अप्रतिष्ठम्. Here also the only possibility is नञ्बहुव्रीहि (नास्ति/न विद्यते प्रतिष्ठा यस्य/यस्मिन्) = that of which/in which there is no प्रतिष्ठा. As प्रतिष्ठा is feminine and अप्रतिष्ठम् is neuter gender so नञ्तत्पुरुष is not possible. The meaning "धर्मस्यापि नास्ति प्रामाण्यम्" cannot be obtained by नञ्बहुव्रीहि. **What Vigraha is being used and what is the Panini Sutra for the same**? How can प्रामाण्यम् come in the meaning when it is not in the original?  
  

Objection 3: Same for अनीश्वर. ईश्वर being masculine and अनीश्वरम् being neuter, नञ्तत्पुरुष is not possible. The only possibility is नञ्बहुव्रीहि again (नास्ति/न विद्यते ईश्वरो यस्य/यस्मिन्) = "that in which/of which there is no ईश्वर". Prof. Korada comments ईश्वरः इत्यपि अनृतमेव (There is no ईश्वर). This meaning cannot be obtained by नञ्बहुव्रीहि. **What Vigraha is being used and what is the Panini Sutra for the same**? How can अनृत come in the meaning when it not in the original.  
  

Objection 4: Prof. Korada's Anvaya is also not clear to me. His commentary interprets the words असत्यम् and अप्रतिष्ठम् without associating them with जगत्, which sounds a bit strange - is he taking multiple sentences where only one is needed? That goes against संभवत्येकवाक्यत्वे वाक्यभेदस्तु नेष्यते. The Anvaya as per my understanding is ते (आसुराः जनाः) आहुः - जगत् असत्यम् अप्रतिष्ठम् अनीश्वरम् अपरस्परसंभूतम् कामहैतुकम्, अन्यत् किम्?

Objection 5: (I pointed out earlier also). Meaning of स्त्रीपुरुषयोः संयोगः अविच्छेदेन गच्छति is unclear. Whence, where and how does the union of female and male "go" (गच्छति)?  
  
Objection 6: The natural meaning of किमन्यत् should be "what else \[is this world\]" (rhetorical question). The meaning here is "nothing else is cause of the world"? Where is the words कारणम् in the verse?  
  

Objection 7: Prof. Korada takes अपरस्परसंभूतम् as अर्शआद्यजन्त (or as बहुव्रीहि). Such an interpretation means it qualifies जगत्. Then how can कामहैतुक be taken with अपरस्परसंभवः. If अपरस्परसंभूतम् is taken as अर्शआद्यजन्त, then अपरस्परसंभूतम्, then the maxim पदार्थः पदार्थेनान्वेति न तदेकदेशेन prevents us from taking कामहैतुकम् to qualify अपरस्परसंभवः. In बहुव्रीहि, अपरस्परसंभूतम् stands for अन्यपदार्थ and so कामहैतुकम् cannot qualify अपरस्परसंभवः. As per Prof. Korada, the compound अपरस्परसंभूतम् means "brought about (संभूत) by continuity \[of union\]", then "continuity \[of union\]" being already compounded, how can that be qualified by a non-compounded word - the meaning "continuous union \[of males and females\] caused by lust" goes against principles of समर्थः पदविधिः.

Objection 8: "Dharma is also meaningless" is a mistranslation of धर्मस्यापि नास्ति प्रामाण्यम्. The correct translation of धर्मस्यापि नास्ति प्रामाण्यम् is "There is no authority/evidence of Dharma also". प्रामाण्यम् and meaninglessness are not the same.
