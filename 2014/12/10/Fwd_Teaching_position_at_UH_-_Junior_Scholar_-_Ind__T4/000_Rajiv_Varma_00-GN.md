The Department of Comparative Cultural Studies at the University of Houston invites applications for Instructional Assistant Professor of India Studies. The Department combines degree programs in Anthropology, Religious Studies, India Studies, Global and International Studies, and Liberal Studies.



**We seek a junior scholar whose area of study is Indian cultural traditions in global contexts. Areas of specialization may include religion, literature, art and architecture, history, and/or social structures. Preference will be given to applicants who have experience in language instruction and are qualified to teach Hindi. We encourage applicants to demonstrate how their research and teaching will strengthen the interdisciplinary and comparative objectives of our Department and the India Studies Program.**



This position does not carry tenure but is renewable long-term. Instructional faculty in the Department of Comparative Cultural Studies typically teach three courses per semester. Salary range is $40,000 to $45,000. Applicants must hold the Ph.D. degree.



Please send curriculum vitae, statement of research agenda and teaching philosophy, and record of teaching experience by December 1, 2014, to Dr. Lois Parkinson Zamora, Department of Comparative Cultural Studies, University of Houston, Houston, TX 77204-5020. Materials can also be emailed to [dtu...@central.uh.edu]() with the subject line: India Studies Position.



The University of Houston is an Equal Opportunity/Affirmative Action employer. Minorities, women, veterans, and persons with disabilities are encouraged to apply.
