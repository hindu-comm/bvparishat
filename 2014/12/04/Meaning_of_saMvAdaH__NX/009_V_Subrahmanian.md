In the upadesha panchakam said to be of Shankaracharya, there is this upadesha:  
  

बुधजनैर्वादः परित्यज्यताम् (the last pāda of the third stanza)  
  

There are other meanings too for the word 'samvāda':  
  
<http://www.spokensanskrit.de/index.php?script=HK&beginning=0+&tinput=%E0%A4%B8%E0%A4%82%E0%A4%B5%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%A6%E0%A4%83&trans=Translate&direction=AU>  
  

agreement, concord, concurrence, assent as per the above page.  
  

In the Bhāmatī gloss for the Bhāsya of Shaṇkarāchārya, (BSB 2.2.42 where the bhāgavata/pācharātra doctrine is discussed) we have:  
  

अन्यत्र वेदाविसंवादात् यत्रांशे विसंवादः स निरस्यते ....  
  

Hence, samvāda in this context means 'agreement with the vedanta' and 'visamvāda' is 'disagreement/discord with the vedanta.'
