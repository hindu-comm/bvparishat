Thanks for the context. With the context –

stūyate’cyuta kiṁ tava may be translated as"O Acyuta, is it possible for your \[Prabhāva\] to to be praised \[by me\]".

stūyate tava kiṁ tadā maye be translated as"then, is it possible for your \[Prabhāva\] to to be praised \[by me\]".

Śrīdhara Svāmī's interpretation of stūyate as stotum śakyate follows the rule PS 3-3-131 vartamānasāmīpye vartamānavadvā. Otherwise with śak, one would get the liṅ lakāra (stūyeta instead of stūyate) by the rule PS 3-3-172 śaki liṅ ca which ordains liṅ or kṛtya suffixes in combination with the root śak. The usage is very similar to the usage kiṁ varṇyate in the Adhyātmarāmāyaṇa. Here isAdhyātmarāmāyaṇe'pāṇinīyaprayogāṇāṃ Vimarśaḥ (3.29).

(२९) किं वर्ण्यते

यत्पादपङ्कजपरागपवित्रगात्रा भागीरथी भवविरिञ्चिमुखान्पुनाति।  
साक्षात्स एव मम दृग्विषयो यदास्ते किं वर्ण्यते मम पुराकृतभागधेयम्॥

– अ.रा. १-५-४५

अहल्या स्वकीयं भाग्यं प्रशंसति।किं वर्ण्यतेइति। अत्रवर्णयितुं शक्यतेइति वर्तमान­सामीप्याल्लट्।
