DLi is an initiative from Goverment of India. Because of copyright laws, the texts are all out of copyright and therefore not sources for current information, but rather useful for history and background.  
  
The **Million Book Project** (or the **Universal Library**), was a book digitization project, led by Carnegie Mellon University School of Computer Science and University Libraries.^(\[1\]) Working with government and research partners in India (Digital Library of India) and China, the project scanned books in many languages, using OCR to enable full text searching, and providing free-to-read access to the books on the web  
  
COPYRIGHT POLICY  
The copyright policy is adopted for DLI, is as per the Indian copy Right Act 1957. Materials which are free of copyright as per the Indian Copyright Act 1957 have been scanned for DLI. However, in case of a possible error in copyright checking, if the author or publisher sends a written request for removal, such a request will be validated and complied with.  
Following works are included in the DLI:  
o Free of copyright restriction, available for use by anyone  
o Expired copyright  
o Dedication to the public  
o Works “born public,” e.g. works of the Indian Government  
o Public Domain works.
