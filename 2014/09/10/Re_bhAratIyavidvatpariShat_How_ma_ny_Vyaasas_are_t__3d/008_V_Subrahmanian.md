One can also consider terms such as ''śārīraka-mīmāmsā' (for 'uttara mīmāmsā') and 'karma mīmāmsā' (for pūrva mīmāmsā). Here is a quote from Shankaracharya's Brahmasūtra bhāṣya preamble:  
  
यथा चायमर्थः सर्वेषां वेदान्तानाम्, तथा वयमस्यां *शारीरकमीमांसायां* प्रदर्शयिष्यामः । *वेदान्तमीमांसाशास्त्रस्य* व्याचिख्यासितस्येदमादिमं सूत्रम् —  
  

The terms finally mean: 'Enquiry into the nature of the dweller-of-the-body' (śārīraka) and 'Enquiry into the nature of works (karma). Shankara has also used the term 'vidhi-kāṇḍa' in the BSB to denote the pūrva bhāga of the veda.
