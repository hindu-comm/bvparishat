Yes I think they are the Visaya, since Patanjali says uccārya hi varṇān āha (MBh Paspasahnika). Anything that is an object of pronunciation must be a Visaya for hearing. What Hari is talking about is that Varnavibhaga in a Pada is Aupadhika, one cannot say a Pada consists of several Varnas, that is only perfunctory and appears so when the Pada is pronounced in Dhvani, actually Pada is not decomposable into Varnas.

In the Mahabhashya, Patanjali says Dhvani is Guna of Sabda.  

evam tarhi sphoṭaḥ śabdaḥ dhvaniḥ śabdaguṇaḥ . katham . bheryāghātavat . tat yathā bheryāghātaḥ bherīm āhatya kaḥ cit viṃśati padāni gacchati kaḥ cit triṃśat kaḥ cit catvāriṃśat . sphoṭaḥ ca tāvān eva bhavati . dhvanikṛtā vṛddhiḥ . dhvaniḥ sphoṭaḥ ca śabdānām dhvaniḥ tu khalu lakṣyate \| alpaḥ mahān ca keṣām cit ubhayam tat svabhāvataḥ
