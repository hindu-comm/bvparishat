One should totally respect the Bhakti tradition and sayings from Nāradapurāṇa are greatly revered. Again, discussion on what path leads to self realization/ultimate goal is an age old debate within theistic schools of Indian philosophy. But yet, all that depends on what knowledge tradition one follows (यो यो यां यां तनुं भक्तः; ज्ञानयोगेन साङ्ख्यानां कर्मयोगेन योगिनाम् ।। etc.). I heard that Sri Chaitanya
proposed bhakti as the fifth and ultimate puruṣārtha.  
  

Next, your comment, \<प्रवर्तसे or वर्तसे? The former has one syllable too many (nine syllables in the second foot).\>. Agree. On a lighter note - आस्माकीनां नैयायिकेषां अर्थने एव तात्पर्यः :-) The prose and verse (from prastāvanā of adhyātmarāmāyaṇe'pāṇinīyaprayogāṇāṃ vimarśaḥ) written about Udayana in spotless Sanskrit is really refreshing.
