निहत is obtained from नि + √हन् + क्त, with क्त signifying the object of action. The root नि + √हन् has many meanings. One of the meanings is “to strike or hit (with a weapon)”. निहन्ति = प्रहारं करोति. An example of this usage is तानेव सामर्षतया निजघ्नुः (रघुवंश ७-४४). Here मल्लिनाथ explains निजघ्नुः as प्रजह्रुः (they struck or hit).

निहत here can be explained in the same sense. ततस्त्वां निहतं दृष्ट्वा = Then on seeing you being struck or hit (with Vajra, even though the strike had no effect), ….
