Yes, as per the Chapter 236 (aptly titled Tāmasaśāstrakathanam) of the Uttarakhaṇḍa of the Padmapurāṇa, these are Tāmasa Śāstras. In addition to enlisting Tāmasa Śāstras, the chapter also classifies 18 Purāṇas and 18 Smṛtis into Sāttvika, Rājasa and Tāmasa. No surprises, the Padmapurāṇa itself is listed as a Sāttvika Purāṇa. :)



The context is important. The Uttarakhaṇḍa of the Padmapurāṇa establishes nothing but Bhakti, and so anything that leads away from Bhakti of the Lord is considered Tāmasa. The chapter from where the above verses are begin with Pārvatī beseechign Śiva thus -



तामसानि च शास्त्राणि समाचक्ष्व ममानघ।

संप्रोक्तानि च तैर्विप्रैर्भगवद्भक्तिवर्जितैः॥ ६-२३६-१ ॥



You may read the full chapter page 3190 onwards (page 936 onwards) in the edition published by MUM here

<http://is1.mum.edu/vedicreserve/puranas/padma_purana_6uttara.pdf> 



I do not think staunch Advaitins like you would consider chapter 236 of Padmapurāṇa's Uttarakhaṇḍa as authentic, since it contains some rather unflattering verses like below (just a few verses after the verses cited above) -



दैत्यानां नाशनार्थाय विष्णुना बुद्धरूपिणा।

बौद्धशास्त्रमसत्प्रोक्तं नग्ननीलपटादिकम्॥ ६-२३६-६ ॥

मायावादमसच्छास्त्रं प्रच्छन्नं बौद्धमुच्यते।

मयैव कथितं देवि कलौ ब्राह्मणरूपिणा॥ ६-२३६-७ ॥

अपार्थं श्रुतिवाक्यानां दर्शयल्लोँकगर्हितम्।

कर्मस्वरूपत्याज्यत्वमत्रैव प्रतिपाद्यते॥ ६-२३६-८ ॥

ब्रह्मणोऽस्य स्वयं रूपं निर्गुणं वक्ष्यते मया।

Needless to say, the verses are considered authentic by Vaiṣṇavas and cited in many Vaiṣṇava works. Vijñānabhikṣu has also quoted the verse calling Māyavāda as Asacchāstra twice in the Vijñānāmṛtabhāṣya on Brahmasūtra.

Jaimini Śāstra, yes the Tāmasaśāstrakathanam calls it Tāmasa.



मदाज्ञया जैमिनिना पूर्वं वेदमपार्थकम्।

निरीश्वरेण वादेन कृतं शास्त्रं महत्तरम्॥ ६-२३६-१२ ॥



Vyākraraṇa, no. After the above verse, the discussion moves to Purāṇas. Not even the most biased scholar would like to Vyākraraṇa as Tāmasa. It is perhaps the only Śāstra honoured by scholars of all hues –



निघण्टुना विना वैद्यो विद्वान् व्याकरणं विना।

अनभ्यासेन धानुष्कस्त्रयो हास्यस्य भाजनम्॥
