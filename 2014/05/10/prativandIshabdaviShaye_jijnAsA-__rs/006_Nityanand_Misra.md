The word is प्रतिबन्दी. And not प्रतिवन्दी.

प्रतिबन्दी is one of the five (or six) types of Tarkas.

As per S. M. Srinivasa Chari (1988), Tattvamuktākalāpa, Motilal Banarsidass, ISBN 9788120802667, p. 93, the five types are आत्माश्रय = self-dependence, अन्योन्याश्रय = reciprocal dependence, चक्रक = circular reasoning, अनवस्था = infinite regress, प्रतिबन्दी = counter-argument.

And the word is indeed listed by Apte as Dr. Bhat pointed out.

प्रतिबन्दी seems to come from बद् (बन्द्) स्थैर्ये. Monier Williams and Westergaard list both बद् and बन्द् in the same meaning. The derivation is same – using “सुप्यजातौ णिनिस्ताच्छील्ये” (पा.सू. ३.२.७८).

प्रति + √बन्द् + णिच् = प्रतिबन्दिन्। विभक्तिकार्ये प्रतिबन्दी।
