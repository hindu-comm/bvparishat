In the true spirit of enquiry, it can be seen that a question that was posed to know the source of a quotation/concept has grown into going into the meaning of the quotation/concept and the various disciplines the concept finds place in. The Ayurveda instance is shown here.
Earlier the idea contained in the māṇḍūkyopaniṣad was presented. Here is yet another place where this idea is stated:  
  
The Kaṭhopaniṣad Mantra 2.1.10:

  
यदेवेह तदमुत्र यदमुत्र तदन्विह ।

मृत्योः स मृत्युमाप्नोति य इह नानेव पश्यति ॥ १० ॥

  
यत् what एव indeed इह is here तत् that अमुत्र is there यत् what अमुत्र is there तत् that अनु इह alone is here. मृत्योः from death स he मृत्युम् death आप्नोति attains यः whoever इह here नाना इव as though different पश्यति sees.

  
*What is here, the same is there and what is there, the same is here. He goes from death to death who sees any difference here. ‘*
