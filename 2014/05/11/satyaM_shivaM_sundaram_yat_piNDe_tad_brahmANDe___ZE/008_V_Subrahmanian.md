Regarding the pinDa/brahmANDa question, here is a reference:  
  

The Yogakundalini Upanishad says:  

  

पिण्डब्रह्माण्डयोरैक्यं लिङ्गसूत्रात्मनोरपि .  
स्वापाव्याकृतयोरैक्यं स्वप्रकाशचिदात्मनोः .. ८१..

  

<http://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_upanishhat/yogakund.itx>

  

piNDabrahmaaNDayoraikya.n li\~Ngasuutraatmanorapi .  
svaapaavyaakR^itayoraikya.n svaprakaashachidaatmanoH .. 81..  

  
  
This is Upanishad Brahmendra Yogin's commentary on the referenced verse (1:81)  

  

कथं पुनः ब्रह्ममात्रमवशिष्यत इत्युक्तम् ? व्यष्टिसमष्टिविभागसिद्धविश्वादिजीवत्रयभेदसत्तादित्याशंक्य,

यदि सर्वत्र भेदं मन्यसे तदा व्यष्टिसमष्टिप्रपञ्चतदारोपापवादाधारविश्वादितुरीयान्तस्य

विराडादितुर्यान्तेनैक्यं विचिन्तयेदित्याह – पिण्डेति ।  
ऐक्यं विश्वविराडैक्योपलक्षणार्थम् ।

भेदसापेक्षतदैक्यगतसविशेषताऽपाये ब्रह्म निष्प्रतियोगिस्वमात्रमवशिष्यते इत्यर्थः ॥  
  

The concept of 'that which is in the brahmnANDa, macrocosm, is in the pinDAnDa, microcosm,' is well known through all the upanishads, especially in the mANDUkya. The pancha bhutas that constitute the macrocosm is found in the jiva's body/mind complex too. The pralaya, etc, that happen in the macrocosm happens in the jivasharira too in the form of waking, sleep, etc. For the microcosmic, vyaShTi, jiva triad, vishwa, taijasa and prAjna, we have the corresponding macrocosmic, samaShTi, virAT, Hiranyagarbha and Ishwara.
