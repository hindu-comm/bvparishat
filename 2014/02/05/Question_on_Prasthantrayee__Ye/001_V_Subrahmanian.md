The term prasthAnatraya is used to denote the three: shruti prasthAna, smRti (bh.gItA) prasthAna and sUtra prasthAna (the brahmasutras). The sUtraprasthAnam is also called nyAyaprasthAnam since the sutra-s employ nyAya, logic, to determine the purport of the upaniShadic/smRti passages. The three are called 'paths' that lead to the knowledge of Brahman which is the pratipAdyam in the three 'texts'.
