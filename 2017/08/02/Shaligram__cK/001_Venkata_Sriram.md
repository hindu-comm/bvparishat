There are 2 views w.r.t to this. Infact, it is a fossil of a particular insect that got turned into stone after several thousands of years in river. 

  

A per a a great telugu scholar & gemologist who was also poet says that it is called as सालग्रावः (स=with; अलि = insect ; ग्रावः =stone).

  

Another version is that :गण्डक्याः उत्तरे तीरे गिरिराजस्य दक्षिणे । क्षेत्रं तु विष्णुसान्निध्यात् सर्वक्षेत्रोत्त्मुत्तमं ॥

  

The northern banks of River Gandaki was considered to be very sacred owing to the presence of 'shaala' trees (shaala is viSNu swarupa).

Owing to the conglomeration / cluster of these shaala trees, the sacred place around the Gandaki river was called 'shaala-graama'. The stones

associated with this place is calledशालग्राम शिला (शालोपलक्षितः ग्रामः शालग्रामः)
