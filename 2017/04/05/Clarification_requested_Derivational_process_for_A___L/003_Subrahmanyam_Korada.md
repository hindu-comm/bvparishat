नमो विद्वद्भ्यः

  

1." could any of the**स्वार्थिकप्रत्यय**s be used to derive the word अवश्यकम् (in the sense of आवश्यकम्), knowing that the word has been accepted into mainstream vernaculars " -----  

  

स्वार्थिकाः means निरर्थकाः ( see उद्योत under ’ पङ्क्तिर्विंशति .....’ पा 5-1-58 ) -- because they simply denote the meaning of प्रकृति only ---

  

अवेः कः - पा 5-4-28 -- अविरेव अविकः ।

  

On the other hand the term आवश्यकम् is in भाव / कर्म ।

  

\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*

  

2." So potentially, we can have a Sanskrit base अवश्य with the addition of this affix 'ka' to form a भावनाम avaśyaka, which gives the same meaning as आवश्यकम्" ---------------

  

  

It is not possible to certify the words in vernacular languages , which are produced  by adding some प्रत्यय or the otheron
संस्कृतप्रातिपदिकs .

  

There is good discussion by Patanjali under 'तदस्य तदस्मिन् स्यादिति ’ पा 5-1-16 ---

  

Panini inserted the word ' इति ’ , simply to suggest -- if the native speaker understands the meaning in case such a शब्द is employed .

  

so शिष्टलोक is प्रमाणम् ।

  

  

3."ps: Regarding impropriety of the usage of the word**आवश्यकता**as per the न्याय - उक्तार्थानाम् अप्रयोगः, I would like to bring to your kind attention that**हितोपदेशः**(संधि-कथा 8) has the word used, in the sense of obligation, so:

अथ भगवता क्रुद्धेन वरदानस्यावश्यकतया विचारमूढयोः पार्वती प्रदत्ता "
--------

  

वरदानम् is a कर्म - अवश्यंकर्म आवश्यकम् - आवश्यकस्य भावः - आवश्यकता ।

  

वुञ् and त्व / तल् - both cannot be in भाव ।

  

वुञ् in कर्म and त्व् / तल् in भाव is okay .

  

भाव is जाति ( DNA ) .
