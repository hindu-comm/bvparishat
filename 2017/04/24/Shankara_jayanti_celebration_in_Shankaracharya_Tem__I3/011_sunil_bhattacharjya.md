Dear friends,  
  

I wish to clarify that I am not an official spokesperson of the Kanchi Kamakoti Math, but form my historical studies I found that the Kanchi Kamakoti Math has traced the Guruparampara to Adi Shankaracharya, who lived in the 5th-6th century BCE. Adi Shankara visited Kashmir about one hundred years before the start of the reign of king Gopaditya. It appears that the Kanchi Kamakoti Math was referring to the Rajatarangini verse 1.341, which is given below :  
  
ज्येष्ठेश्वरं प्रतिष्ठाप्य गोपाद्रावार्यदेशजाः ।  
गोपाग्रहारान्कृतिना येन स्वीकारिता द्विजाः ॥ १- ३४१ ॥  
  

I am also attaching the translation and notes on this by Ranjit Sitaram Pandit in his book"*Rajatarangini*: The Saga of the Kings of Kashmir".  
  

Regards,  

Sunil KB
