> > Adi Shankara visited Kashmir about one hundred years before the start > of the reign of king Gopaditya. It appears that the Kanchi Kamakoti > Math was referring to the Rajatarangini verse 1.341, which is given > below :  
>   
> ज्येष्ठेश्वरं प्रतिष्ठाप्य गोपाद्रावार्यदेशजाः ।  
> गोपाग्रहारान्कृतिना येन स्वीकारिता द्विजाः ॥ १- ३४१ ॥  
> > 
> > I am also attaching the translation and notes on this by Ranjit > Sitaram Pandit in his book"*Rajatarangini*: The Saga of the Kings of > Kashmir". >
> 
> > 
> > 

  

So, as per the English translation, गोपाद्रि is also गोपकार, on which Jyeshtheswara was installed. If both Jyeshtheswara mandir and Sankaracarya mandir are not different, then Kanchi matha must have referred to this context. Thanks for this extract!
