2. Shastri, Shanti Bhikshu. An incorrect reading in Siddhantakaumudi, *Indian Linguistics,* 15, 1954



3. Palsule, G.B. A survey of the pre-Paninian grammatical thought in the matter of the verbal root, *Indian Linguistics*,18, 1957, pp. 116-139



4. Palsule, G.B. A brief account of the different dhatupathas, *Indian Linguistics, Sir Ralph Turner Jubilee volume I,* 1958 pp. 103-133



5. Ayachit, M.S. Ganapatha : a critical study, *Indian Linguistics*, 22, 1961 pp. 1-63

6. Joshi, S.D. Verbs and nouns in Sanskrit, *Indian Linguistics*, 23, 1962 pp. 60-63

7. Pandit, M.D. Some linguistic principles in Panini’s grammar, *Indian Linguistics*, 24, 1963 pp. 50-69



8. Pandit, P.B. Sanskritic clusters and caste dialects, *Indian Linguistics*, 24, 1963 pp. 70-80



9. Mishra, Vidya Niwas. Panini’s grammar as mathematical model, *Indian Linguistics,* 25, 1964 pp.157-178



10. Shastri, Kapil Deo.Bhartrhari’s discussion on the relation between the Upamana and Upameya in his Vakyapadiya, *Indian Linguistics*, 25, 1964, pp. 229-233
