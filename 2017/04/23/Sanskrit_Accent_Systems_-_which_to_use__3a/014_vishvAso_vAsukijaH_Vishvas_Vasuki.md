Fromgrassman-sanskrit-german

### agni

agní, m., 1) das Feuer, als das bewegliche (aj) aufgefasst, 2) der Gott des Feuers. Vgl. jātávedas, vēśvānará, tánūnápāt, apām nápāt, nárāśáṅsa, hótṙ, dūtá, viśpáti u. s. w., und die Zusammensetzungen indrā-agní u. s. w.

### agni

अग्नि ag-ní, m. fire;

conflagration;

god Agni.

Frommacdonell

### hotR

होतृ hó-tṛ, m. \[sacrificer: √ 1. hu\] priest, chief priest (with whom in the oldest Vedic period the Adhvaryu only is associated);

in Br. the first of the four chief priests (the term in its widest sese also being employed to embrace these four priests together with all their assistants);
