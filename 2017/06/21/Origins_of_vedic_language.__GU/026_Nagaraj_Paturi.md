[[Nagaraj Paturi	2017-06-25, 13:14:18 [Source](https://groups.google.com/g/bvparishat/c/GU1zGYtjoh4)]]



Thanks, Sri Shrikant Jamadagni-ji for concurring with my view.

  

Let us take Sri Achyut Karve-ji's

  

"Nay it is we alone who can protect it because all the conditions for its protection are only available in our land as it was born and brought up here."

  

particularly

  

"it was born and brought up here."

  

This is the general heartbeat of most of the Indians.

  

Let us split this into two parts:

  

1\. It was born here.

  

2\. It was brought up here.

  

For #1, Sri Shrikant Talageri-ji provides a whole book-full of evidences and arguments, mostly internal evidences from the Vedic texts. Sri Achyut Karve-ji's argument is that the substrata of Indian languages is the evidence for Indian origin.

  

To this argument of Sri Achyut Karve-ji, those who support the origin outside India may say this supports brought up not birth in India.

  

Thus brought up in India from as early as the date of occurrence of the substrata of other Indian languages can not be disputed even by those who identify these substrata.

  

The point is that the Vedas and Sanskrit , unlike the Classical Greek and Latin, are living , living among us as our life breath. For us, the focus is understanding them as part of our life. In view of this , any old or new discovery about their birth inside or outside India are insignificant for us.
