Kindly redraft your statement. I think the analogy is not correct.
Sanskrit having its origin in here tells us that there is a long tradition of aboriginal dialects which provided the material for the evolution of Sanskrit. (Sanskrit did not evolve from nothing) and that Sanskrit helped the evolution of many a regional dialects into full blown formal languages like Marathi,Hindi, Kannada etc. When we are speaking of Sanskrit having its origin in the Indian subcontinent we mean that in addition to being a contributor of the numeral system to the world India has also helped the world formalize a language which can then be used for storing and passing knowledge for future generations.
