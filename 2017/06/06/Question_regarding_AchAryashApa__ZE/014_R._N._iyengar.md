Dear Members,

  

"ācāryaśāpadagdha" perhaps refers to the story of Shankaracharya and his curse at Kalaty against the local Bahmins.

![Inline image 1](https://groups.google.com/group/bvparishat/attach/68b20b2f89e8/image.png?part=0.5&view=1)  

But the practice of cremation near the house appears to be a very ancient practice. Traditional writers and Dharmashastra texts do not take historical approach in their writings mix ups are quite common. PT srinivasa Iyengar in his "History of the Tamils" refers to five peculiar traits of Kerala Nambutiri Brahmins. This he has taken from
"Brahmins of Malabar" by Raja Ramavarma (JRAS 1910. 625-639). This latter paper is very well written without being judgmental, since what outsiders considered अनाचार was सदाचार for the locals. Ten traits are listed. I am abridging them. If any one is interested I can share the full paper as it is a good reference

![Inline image 3](https://groups.google.com/group/bvparishat/attach/68b20b2f89e8/image.png?part=0.3&view=1)  

  

![Inline image 4](https://groups.google.com/group/bvparishat/attach/68b20b2f89e8/image.png?part=0.6&view=1)  

  

![Inline image 6](https://groups.google.com/group/bvparishat/attach/68b20b2f89e8/image.png?part=0.4&view=1)  

![Inline image 7](https://groups.google.com/group/bvparishat/attach/68b20b2f89e8/image.png?part=0.1&view=1)  

  

Ramavarma does not attribute no.9 above to Shankaracharya, although he refers to the Acharya in many places. My interest is item no.10, of Solar reckoning of Time. Whatever may be the reason behind such a practice, it goes back to the Vedic period. Based on the above discriminating characters PTS Iyengar rightly argues that Nambutiris must be the most ancient of the Vedics to have migrated to South. No wonder their traits appear odd (to be dubbed asअनाचार )if the historical dimension is ignored.

  

Thanks

  

RNI
