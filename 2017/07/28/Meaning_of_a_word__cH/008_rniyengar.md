First, let me thank Prof.Paturi, Mishraji, Vishnuji and Abhyankarji for taking interest to respond to my question.

In the context of the text (Naarada-shilpa) I am studying, the word does not mean or refer to a tree. The text knows "Kovidaara" among trees. It is quite possible in some languages Koviraala=Kovidaara, but here it is an artificial construction related to "Gopura".

Prof.Paturi: I am going thro' the book by Stell Kramrisch you have cited. Many thanks! This may give some further leads. The 'viraala' of elephants, lions etc depicted are generally hollow. I wonder whether this word (whether Oriyan or not) is an extension of the Sanskrit
'virala' which indicates a sense of spatial separation i.e.hollowness.
