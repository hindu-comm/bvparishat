This is most likely correct. As per the *National Register of Medicinal Plants* (IUCN Nepal, ISBN 9789291440481), p. 96, the *kovidara* is known as *koiralo* in Nepali. *Koiralo* sounds very similar to *Kovirala*, with the *v*sound elided and the terminal *a* changing to *o*. So very likely that *koiralo* in Nepali has come from *kovirala* in Prakrit, which in turn has come from*kovidara*in Sanskrit.

  

On the same page, the plant's scientific name is given as *Bauhinia variegata L*. It is known as *kachnar*(कचनार) in Hindi. The word is कचनार used in a वंशस्थ metre in प्रियप्रवास by अयोध्या सिंह उपाध्याय ‘हरिऔध’, one of the first poems to use Sanskrit metres in Hindi (Khari Boli) poetry.

  

अनार में औ कचनार में बसी ललामता थी अति ही लुभावनी।

बड़े लसे लोहित-रंग-पुष्प से पलाश की थी अपलाशता ढकी॥
