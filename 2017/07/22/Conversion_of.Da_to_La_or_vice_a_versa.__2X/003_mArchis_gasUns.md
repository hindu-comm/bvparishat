> > According to Śikṣās and Prātiśākhyas, both ड and ळ are mūrdhanya or > retroflex sounds. While ड is described as a stop (sparśa), ळ is > described by some texts with the term duḥspr̥ṣṭa. In modern phonetic > terminology, this may be described as a flap sound, in contrast with a > stop sound. They both involve touching the top of the oral cavity > with the reversed tip of the tongue, but the nature of touch is > different. For ड it is a firm touch (sparśa) while for ळ it is a > flap. The R̥gveda-Prātiśākhya ascribes the change of intervocalic ड to > ळ to an authority named as Vedamitra, and not to Śākalya, and yet the > Śākalya Saṃhitā of the RV seems to have followed this view of > Vedamitra. The views ascribed to Śākalya by Pāṇini are not fully > followed in the Śākalya Saṃhitā as it has come down to us, indicating > that the reciters of the Śākalya tradition, over time, adopted > practices from other teachers. >
