Thanks, Paturiji, for pointing to this page explaining ळ as a retroflex lateral flap. I don't want to add to complications, but for those who may be interested in knowing the origin of retroflexes in Sanskrit within the framework of Indo-European linguistics, there is a theory proposed by a Russian linguist Fortunatov (1848-1914) where he suggests that the Indo-European combinations of l+dental consonant show up in Sanskrit as a retroflex consonant (for example: IE l+t \> Skt ṭ).
Fortunatov's theory has been debated, rejected, reformulated by many other linguists since the original publication, but his theory suggests a possibility for explaining the variability of ड and ळ in the dialects of ancient Indo-Aryan.
