[Gandaberunda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gandaberunda "Gandaberunda"), the Karnataka state emblem, flanked by red maned yellow lion elephant Sharabha

<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharabha>  

The[Government of Karnataka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_Karnataka "Government of Karnataka"), the[University of Mysore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Mysore "University of Mysore")and the[Karnataka Soaps and Detergents Limited](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karnataka_Soaps_and_Detergents_Limited "Karnataka Soaps and Detergents Limited")(KSDL-an industrial unit owned by the Government of Karnataka) have adopted Sharabha, with modifications and also appropriate justifications, as their emblem or logo.^([\[33\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharabha#cite_note-33))

In Karnataka Soaps and Detergents Limited logo, Sharabha is depicted in the form of a body of a lion with the head of an elephant to represent the virtues of wisdom, courage and strength.^([\[34\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharabha#cite_note-34))The Royal Emblem of Mysore has also been adopted by the University of Mysore as their logo too. This logo displays Gandabherunda flanked on either side by the lion-elephant Sharabha - stronger than the lion and the elephant and defender of uprightness, surmounted by a lion.^([\[35\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharabha#cite_note-Univer-35))
