> > The following guidelines are applicable to authorsfor achieving > decorum, acceptability and uniformity in the conduct of the seminar. >
> 
> > 
> > (1) The papers shall be neatly typed in double space, in Windows > Unicode font formatand submitted in MS Word & PDF also, set to A4 > size paper. The papers can written in Samskritam, Tamil, Telugu, > Kannada, Malayalam, Hindi & English. Words of Samskritam origin have > to be used as such without translations. Samskritam verses must be > given in Devanagari script only. >
> 
> > 
> > (2) The abstracts shall be restricted to one page of A4 size. >
> 
> > 
> > (3) The papers shall be in-line with the objective title of the > seminar - the importance, influence of Samskritam language on the > other three - music, dance & drama. The interdependencies of the > latter three also can be discussed with Samskritam as the base. >
> 
> > 
> > (4) The authors shall focus and bring out the richness of Samskritam > language and its peculiar usage or uniqueness if any in the texts or > lyrics of music. Also how learning the language has improved the > performances can be discussed. References shall be given to all the > texts, ( published or unpublished )papers & articles, books, etc.. >
> 
> > 
> > (5) The papers shall not be of a general descriptive type but be > authoritative on the subject with proper backing from the established > procedures / published texts. The music/dance/drama that is in > practice today shall be referred to. >
> 
> > 
> > (6) The authors shall refrain from raking up controversies, disputes, > slanderand other types of unpleasantness. Personal vendetta against > personalities ( living or dead ), undesirable criticism and expression > of displeasure against anyone is not allowed. If the paper contains > any of the above, it shall be summarily disqualified, at any stage. >
> 
> > 
> > (7) The seminar is not a forum to express personal opinions such as > (a)liking for one thing causing dislike for another(b) degradations, > derisivecomparisons,(c) pinpointing defects, etc.,ofany subject > or personalities. Hence such expressions in the papers or utterances > during the presentation are to be strictly avoided. >
> 
> > 
> > (8) The organisers reserve the right to record the proceedings and > use it for any use as may be deemed fit by them. >
> 
> > 
> > (9) The decision of the organisers is final and binding on all > participants in all respects. >
