My impressions about Holi in the Royal City of Mysore:I grew up in Mysore city.The festival is observed in Mysore no doubt, but not celebrated like Deepavali or Navaratri. At least during 50's,60's and even 70's playing Holi by throwing coloured water and syringes was
considered raunchy in the elite Agraharas. Not all Hindu communities participated in this as a public activity. I remember the Kannada poem quoted by Prof.Paturi which urchins from the bordering roads around the agraharas would shout for collecting money for buying firewood. The royal community (Urs) sprayed colours among themselves in the streets where they were concentrated. Vasantotsava was certainly a listed festival in all the temples but not necessarily for the Holi-day. Burning of the pyre of wood was a speciality in the Shiva temples, particularly the Trinayaneshwara temple in the palace complex. As I moved over to IISc Hostels in Bangalore as a student in 60's-70's the distinction between observing a festival and celebrating one started becoming clear. North Indian students did publicly celebrate but they were warned not to force those who do not like to join them. There was no religious fervor or sentiment that was apparent in this no holds barred celebration. Others just tolerated Holi colours as a nuisance. In fact many of the girl students from Tamil Nadu would just go home for 2-3 days perhaps to escape the event. It was only afterI spent a few years in U.P. I got to understand the link of Holi to the Radha-Krishna legend. I did participate, that is, permit others to throw colours on me! I was told that on Holi one forgives the mistakes of others and even enemies are supposed to become friends. A laudable ideal indeed! In brief my impression is in the Mysore region Holi is related to the Puranic Kaama-dahana episode widely known all over India. Physical participation of the Hindu society in public (across the spectrum of castes and beliefs) may be around 20-30% unlike in Deepavali when the participation surely reaches 99% or even more.

Delayed Holi Greetings!

RN Iyengar
