Dear friends,  
  

There is another story, linking Holi with Holika, the aunt of Prahlada, who wantd to burn Prahlada alive and in that process she got burnt and not Prahlada, though Holika had a boon that she would not be burnt by fire. In some vaishnava families in the eastern India, a bonfire is lighted, on the night beore the Holi-day, in such a way that a hair of a goat can be burnt, where the goat symbolized Holika.
