पादसंवाहने वज्री  

केशसंवाहने फणी ।

  
अहो भाग्यं पुरन्ध्रीणां  
दधिसंमथने रविः ।।  
  

Oh what fortune of ladies! They have Indra to polish their feet, the hooded Snake-god to do their hair and the Sun-god to churn yogurt.  
  
  
अहो भाग्य महिलाओं के! पादसेवन के लिए वज्री (इन्द्र), बाल सवारने के लिए फणी (नाग), दही मथने के लिए रवी (सूर्य)!  
  
This subhashita plays on puns in two languages Sanskrit and Marathi. The meanings of these three words are different in both languages:  
वज्री : Sanskrit- Indra; Marathi- pumice stone  
फणि : Skt. Hooded snake; Mar- a comb  
रवि : Skt- Sun; Mar- a Churning stick.

  
  
Harshada Sawarkar  
University of Mumbai.
