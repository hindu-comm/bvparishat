Dear Sankara ji,

  

One of my friends shared a beautiful verse whose source is unknown:



कूरगाय कृतं त्रासं

पालनेति गवां प्रियं

अंबलिद्वेषिणं वन्दे

चिन्तकाय शिवप्रदम्



कूरगाय कृतं त्रासं (कुत्सित: उरग: कूरग: ( तस्मै ) कूरगाय) (one who had frightened the serpent kAliya);पालनेsति गवां प्रियं (one who is fond of cattle)चिन्तकाय ((चिन्तां करोति इति चिन्तक : (तस्मै) चिन्तकाय( (to those who always meditates him)शिवप्रदं (giver of prosperity)बलिद्वेषिणम् (the enemy of the king Bali)अं (to Lord Krishna) (the alphabet “a” is Krishna vide His identity in Vibhuti Yoga)

वन्दे = I prostrate



I prostrate Krishna who frightened Kaliya, who is fond of cattle, who bestows prosperity to His devotees and who is the enemy of Bali



However, in telugu, the words :kuragAya means vegetables ; pAlu means milk ; nEti means ghee; ambali means rice grueland chintakAya means tamarind nut



Regs,

Sriram
