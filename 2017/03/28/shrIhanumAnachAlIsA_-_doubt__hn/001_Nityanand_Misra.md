1\) On औ/और/अरु 

Both और and अरु are used in Awadhi, examples from the Manasa

**और** एक तोहि कहउँ लखाऊ (१.१६९.३)

धीरज धर्म मित्र **अरु** नारी (३.५.७)

औ is not seen in Manasa (probably less common in Awadhi) but seen in the Suradasa's Sura-sagara (Braja)

कहौं कहा कछु कहत न आवै **औ** रस लागत खारौ री

In the Chalisa verse, the reading has to be either औ or अरु since both हाथ बज्र औ ध्वजा बिराजै and हाथ बज्र अरु ध्वजा बिराजै add up to 16 matra-s. With और, there will be 17 matra-s and the chaupai metre will be violated.  

  

2\) On साजै/छाजै

साजै \[=सजाता है\] is from Sanskrit सज्ज्

छाजै \[=शोभित होता है\] is from Sanskrit शोभ् -> छज्ज् -> छज्जइ -> छाजै

Although साजै \[GP\] is the more popular reading, I have heard छाजै \[Mahaviri\] in several traditional performances. Among popular renditions, the one by S P Balasubramaniam uses छाजै.
