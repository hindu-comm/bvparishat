A. R. stands for Anarghyarāghava.

Bg. „ Bhagavadgītā (Bombay Edition).

Bh. „ Bhartrihari II denoting Nitiśataka and HI Vairāgya- śataka (Bombay Edition).

Bk. „ Bhattikāvya.

C. ,, Chānakyaśataka.

Dk. „ Daśakumāracharita, I denoting the first part, and II the second and the Arabic figure the number of the story (Bom. Edition).

G. M. „ Ganaratnamahodadhi.

H. „ Hitopadeśa, the Arabic figures denoting the four parts in their order (Bombay. Edition).

H. C. „ Harshacharita.

K. „ Bānā’s Kādambari (Bombay Edition), Arabic figure signifying the number of the page.

Kāv. „ Kāvyaprakāśa.

Ki. „ Kirātārjuniyam.

Ku. „ Kumārasambhava (Calcutta Edition)

M. „ Mālavikāgnimitra (Bombay Edition).

Māl. „ Mālatimādhava (Bombay Edition).

Mb. „ Mahābhārata.

Mbh. „ Mahābhāshya.

Me. stands for Meghadūta.

Mk. „ Mrichchakatika.

Mn. „ Mudrārākshasa.

Mv. „ Mahāvīracharita.

Na. „ Naishadha.

Nig. „ Nāgānanda.

P. „ Panchatantra, the Roman figure denoting the number of the Tantra, and Arabic figure the number of the story (Bombay Edition).

P. R. „ Prasannarāghava.

R. Raghuvans’a (Bombay Edition).

Rām. „ Rāmāyana.

Ratn. „ Ratnāvali (Calcutta Edition).

S’. „ Śakuntalā (Bombay Edition).

S’. B. „ Śānkarabhāshya.

S’i. „ Śiśupālavadha.

S. K. „ Siddhānta Kaumudi.

S. M. „ Śānkarāchārya’s Mohamudgara.

Su. „ Subhashitaratnākara.

U. „ Uttararāmacharita (Calcutta Edition).

V. „ Vikramorvaśīyam (Bombay Edition).

Vār. „ Vārtika of Kātyāyana.

Vās. „ Vāsavadatta.

V. B. „ Viddhaśālabhanjika.

Ve. „ Venisamhāra (Calcutta Edition).

Y. „ Yājnavalkya, where II denotes the Vyavahāra chapter.
