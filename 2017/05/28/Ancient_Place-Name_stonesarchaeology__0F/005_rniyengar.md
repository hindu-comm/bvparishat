Namaste!

Thanks to Sri Murthyji and Prof.Paturi for their response. Yes, my search is very specific. I am looking for before 15 century name boards or नामलक्ष्मof towns or agraharas or settlements. Banavasi is mentioned in several inscriptions easily searchable in the volumes of online Indian Antiquary. I have searched many of these volumes. They are generally about charities, legal documents, etc. too long to have been of guidance to travellers. It is possible the early archaeologists in India did not consider standalone stones with only a few marks to be important. Or such name stones have deteriorated in time to be recognized as such. I have looked into Indian Epigraphy by R.Salomon (The University of Texas Center for Asian Studies 1998) and works by several Indian and western authors. Importance of inscriptions to understand toponyms and geography is well recognized. I learn that the location of Lumbini and Kapilavastu was identified in modern times with the help of inscriptions only. But my curiosity is to know the artefacts used by people in ancient period to recognize or locate cities and villages in their own region or kingdom.

I understand that there are some ancient stones in Tamilnadu that indicate the local Place Name and sometimes even distance to nearby settlements. This is mentioned in the book "Measurement System in Karnataka" by Jagadish (Publ Dir.Arch & Museums Hospet, 2005 p 96). It is possible this statement is reinterpretation of another reference.
There are of course many mile stones and name indicating hand-posts in dilapidated condition as we travel along forest regions, away from modern cities all over the country. These are relatively modern or if ancient (\< 1500 AD) this has to be verified. That is why I mentioned ancient cities and scripts.

There is a road side inscription containing a few names of sculptors near Mahabalipuram

<http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/chennai/Mamallapuram-inscriptions-escape-by-the-skin-of-teeth/article14404780.ece>

I am looking for similar or better ones that could have been place name indicators. I will look into Shamba Joshi’s book as suggested by Murthyji and also ASI publications. I have seen a few issues of the journal of the Place Name Society of India, but so far no success. 

best regards

  

RNI
