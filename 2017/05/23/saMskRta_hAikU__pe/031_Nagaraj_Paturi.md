The number of lines being three is common between Gaayatree and Haikoo.

  

The number of syllables per line are uniform in Gaayatree. Non-uniform in Haikoo.

  

Total number of syllables is even in Gaayatree. Odd number in Haikoo.

  

Gaayatree is a member of a group of meters like Anushtubh each of which has uniform number of syllables per line.  Haikoo is not like that.

  

Gaayatree is recited in a particular Vedic style of music. Haikoo is recited in a prose-like style.

  

Modern Haikoos are considered to be part of free verse. Gaayatree is a meter.

  

Gaayatree has a sacred Vedic association. Zen Buddhist philosophy is said to be behind Haikoo. But no sacrality associated (attributed / felt by the composers or audience)  

  

Gaayatree is believed to have emerged as part of mantra dars'ana. Haikoo is not believed to be such a revelation.  

  

Known Gaayatree verses are believed to encapsulate a huge world of content in them. Haikoos are expected to be poems with short form and great content.
