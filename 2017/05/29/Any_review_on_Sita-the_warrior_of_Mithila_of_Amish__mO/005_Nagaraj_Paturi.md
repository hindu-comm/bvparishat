The view of Sita presented by AadaraNIya Shrikant Jamadagni-ji is what makes her stand alongside all pativratas all of whom are daring
courageous, fearless, confident women who stood their ground and threatened their adversaries with dire consequences for trying to disturb them from their chosen path and held perspective. What is Savitri known for? She chose Satyavan on her own, no one could convince her to give up the idea. She dared to follow Yama of whom all mortals are dead afraid. She could argue about Dharma with the epitome of dharma and please him to the extent of getting boons from him in admiration. Anasuya was able to change the forms of the all supreme trinity. Sakuntala dearingly argues with Dushyanta in the open court of that emperor. Sita's fearless admonition of Ravana while being the captive in the own territory of that trilokabheekara asura is comparable to the dharmaveera of all these pativrata characters. These are examples that establish that dharmaveera need not necessarily be in the form of yuddhaveera. But it is nevertheless Veera. That is the point.
