[[K S Kannan	2017-05-05, 23:01:46 [Source](https://groups.google.com/g/bvparishat/c/OQ2EKSWuJdg)]]



kṛpaṇa has multiple senses.

  

The sense of the miser is of course the most famous.

*dātā laghur api sevyo,*

*na kṛpaṇo mahān api samṛddhyā\|*

*kūpo'ntas svādu-jalaḥ*

*prītyai lokasya na samudraḥ\|\|*

*  
*

It means the poor in

*dvijebhyo bāla-vṛddhebhyo*

*kṛpaṇebhyo hy adāpayat*

*  
*

It means distressed in

*pālyāśca kṛpaṇā janāḥ*

*  
*

It means pitiable in

*kṛpaṇe patatriṇi*

*  
*

It means despicable in

*aṅgīkṛtam utsṛjan kṛpaṇavat*

*  
*

It means lacking discrimination in

*kāmārtā hi prakṛti-kṛpaṇāḥ*

*  
*

It means one in indigent circumstances in

the second occurrence in

*āditaḥ kṛśa-vṛttir yaḥ kṛpaṇo na sa rāghava\|*

*mahātmā vyasanam pṛapto dīnaḥ kṛpaṇa ucyate\|\|*

*  
*

It means a source of worry/sorrow in

*duhitā kṛpaṇam param*

*  
*

It means servility in

*vittavatsu kṛpaṇām vṛttim vṛthā mā kṛthāḥ*

*  
*

It means one who departs from this world without knowing the *akṣara* principle in

*etad akṣaram aviditvā ...praiti, sa kṛpaṇaḥ*

*  
*

*kārpaṇya* is pitted against *brāhmaṇya* too

*yad-aprabodhāt kārpaṇyam,*

*brāhmaṇyam yat-prabodhataḥ.*

*  
*

*kārpaṇya* is pitted also against *māna* in

*manasvī mriyate kāmam kārpaṇyam na tu gacchati*

*  
*
