Dear list members,  
  
Shri Achyut Karve's question on post-Paninian formulations, inspires the following question on pre-Paninian formulations:  
  
Traditionally, we have all heard about "nava-vyakaranas" that Hanuman had learned from the Sun. Ravana and more recently, Sri Vedanta Desikar are also said to have been nava-vyakarana vettas. What are these nava-vyakaranas? Surely, Panineeyam cannot be one of the nava-vyakaranas, as one would expect the account from Ramayana to predate Panini (see this list - <https://www.quora.com/What-are-the-most-amazing-and-lesser-known-facts-about-Ravana/answer/Srinivasan-PS-2>).
Does any scripture mention the names of all these nine Vyakaranas?  
  
Thanks,  
MK
