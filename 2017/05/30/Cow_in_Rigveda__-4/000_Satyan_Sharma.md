Here is a verse from Rigveda:

श्रोणामेक उदकं गामवाजति मांसमेकः पिंशति सूनयाभृतम् ।  

आ निम्रुचः शकृदेको अपाभरत्किं स्वित्पुत्रेभ्यः पितरा उपावतुः ॥ ऋग्वेद 1.161.10

  

The components श्रोणाम्-गाम्, मांसम् and शकृत् seem to point directly to an animal. It also seems that the मांसम् is connected to the श्रोणाम्-गाम् (lame/cripple cow) as is the शकृत्, which makes it look like an economic activity once a cow cannot produce milk. Acharya Sayana does not give any other interpretation to this verse other than interpreting श्रोणाम् asशोणम् and connecting it withउदकम् to use it asरुधिरम्.

  

I would like the respected members to shed some light regarding any other interpretation of this verse.
