Namaskārā vidvadbhiḥ,

  

Can anyone enlighten me with the exact connotation of the grammatical concept, 'nipāta'?

The 'cādi' list in the Gaṇapāṭha includes the vowels too. Should the vowels enumerated in the Pratyāhārasūtras be also considered as nipātas? I heard people saying that the vowels in the Pratyāhārasūtras do not fall within the scope of Sandhi due to the rule "nipāta ekāj anāṅ". I wanted to confirm if this is true.

I had an idea that the words designated as 'nipātas' must be meaningfully used as padas in sentences.  

The Kāśikā as well as the Gaṇapāṭha say-- "upasargavibhaktisvarapratirūpakāś ca nipātāḥ". I do not fully understand if it is vibhaktisvaraor vibhaktiand svara. How can be a vibhakti or a vibhaktisvara called a nipāta?

  

Here is the Kāśikā:
