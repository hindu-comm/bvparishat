Dear Scholars,

  

I had a question on rules for writing down unfolded words while attempting an *anvaya*.

  

The reason for my confusion is different text sources do it differently, and I wanted to know what is the standard way of doing this, the rules for this, and whether this is documented in any work?

  

While the verse itself can be written in different ways, during the *anvaya*, when the verse is unfolded, what is the standardized process to write those unfolded words?

  

Particularly, I need to know when to use the *anusvāra* and when to use *ṃ.* Do rules of *Sandhi* dictate this even in the *anvaya*? Or is it the case that dictates this? (Accusative with an *ṃ*, and so on?)

  

For example, the verse is:

  

धृत्वा पदस्खलनभीतिवशात्करं मे

यारूढवत्यसि शिलाशकलं विवाहे।

सा मां विहाय कथमद्य विलासिनि द्या-

मारोहसीति हृदयं शतधा प्रयाति।।



dhṛtvā padaskhalanabhītivaśātkaraṃ me

yārūḍhavatyasi śilāśakalaṃ vivāhe।

sā māṃ vihāya kathamadya vilāsini dyā-

mārohasīti hṛdayaṃ śatadhā prayāti।।

  

Is the *anvaya* as follows:

  

विलासिनि या विवाहे पदस्खलनभीतिवशात् मे करम् धृत्वा शिलाशकलम् आरूढवति असि साअद्य माम् विहाय कथम् द्याम् आरोहसि इति हृदयम् शतधा प्रयाति।

vilāsini yā vivāhe padaskhalanabhītivaśāt me karam dhṛtvā śilāśakalam ārūḍhavati asi sā adya mām vihāya katham dyām ārohasi iti hṛdayam śatadhā prayāti

  

Or as follows?

  

विलासिनि या विवाहे पदस्खलनभीतिवशात् मे करंधृत्वा शिलाशकलं आरूढवति असि साअद्य माम् विहाय कथम् द्यां आरोहसि इति हृदयं शतधा प्रयाति।

  

vilāsini yā vivāhe padaskhalanabhītivaśāt me karaṃ dhṛtvā śilāśakalaṃ ārūḍhavati asi sāadya mām vihāya katham dyāṃ ārohasi iti hṛdayaṃ śatadhā prayāti.



Or should it be something else, done using some other rules?
