Dear Ajit Ji,

  

  You have brought out some of the practical difficulties in collecting manuscripts to prepare critical editions. In 1980s, itt took me more than 10 years to collect 20+ manuscripts of the Śaunakīya Caturādhyāyikā before preparing and publishing its critical edition. It involved a lot of traveling across India, seeking help from numerous scholars/administrators, and exchanges with manuscript collections in Eurpoe. I don't think that circumstances havechanged significantly today. Many manuscript libraries are reluctant to share their manuscripts even for scholarly purposes, and many want exorbitant royalties for copying manuscripts. There needs to be some governmental intervention in making the manuscript photographs available for research purposes. You may have a better understanding of the current situation in India, than me. I would like to know if things have changed for the better.

  

Madhav M. Deshpande

Professor Emeritus

Sanskrit and Linguistics

University of Michigan

\[Residence: Campbell, California\]
