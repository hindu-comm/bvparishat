You are invited to join us for a roundtable conversation on Hindu Eco-Dharma for the Age of Climate Change, presented by the Hinduism and Ecology Society at the 2018 Parliament of World's Religions

  

Saturday, November 3, 2018 at 12:15-1:45pm EDT

Parliament of the World's Religions, Toronto, Ontario

Room 714B-Metro Toronto Convention Centre.

  

Our esteemed panel will share with you explorations into these vital questions:  
  
1. What is Hindu Eco-Dharma?  
2. How does Hindu Eco-Dharma help us to confront the wicked challenges of climate change?  
3. Who and where are the communities practicing Hindu Eco-Dharma for radical ecological empowerment?  
  
Our roundtable includes:  
-Frederique Apffel-Marglin (Professor Emeritus of Anthropology-Smith College, MA)  
-Rita D. Sherma (Director of The Center for Dharma Studies, Graduate Theological Union, Berkeley, CA, Chair, GTU Sustainability 360)  
-Kusumita P. Pedersen (Professor Emerita of Religious Studies at St. Francis College, NY, Board of Trustees, Parliament of the World's Religions)  
-David L. Haberman (Professor of Religious Studies at Indiana University)  
-Christopher Key Chapple (Doshi Professor of Indic and Comparative Theology, and Director, Master of Arts in Yoga Studies, Loyola Marymount University, CA)  
-Christopher P. Miller (Clinical Professor of Yoga Studies,Loyola Marymount University, CA)

-Gopal Patel (Director, The Bhumi Project)  
-Mat McDermott (The Bhumi Project, Hindu American Foundation)  
-Gauranga Dasa (Director, Govardhan Eco-Village, India)  
-Yachneet Pushkarna (CEO, Social Initiatives, Govardhan Eco-Village, India)  
  
-Christopher Fici (Moderator) (Director, Hinduism and Ecology Society)  
  
The Hinduism and Ecology Society, under the auspices of The Oxford Centre for Hindu Studies and The Bhumi Project, is a community for co-creative fellowship and scholarship within the field of Hinduism and Ecology. The Hinduism and Ecology Society builds upon the work of foundational scholars in the field to provide rich, fertile soil for the study and practice of Hinduism in a ecological vein as we enter into the climatic challenges and opportunities of the 21st Century.
