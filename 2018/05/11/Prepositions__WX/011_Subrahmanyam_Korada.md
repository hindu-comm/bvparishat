नमो विद्वद्भ्यः

  

Preposition is akin to विभक्तिप्रत्यय । In some languages it will be postposition --

  

in London (Eng) -- London ni (Japa)

  

Prepositions are seen in a वाक्यम् \|

  

Deictic Elements --

  

Deictic elements are words (including prepositional phrases) which are useful in connecting sub-sentences in a

Discourse (महावाक्यम्) ।

  

The semantic relations of different sub-sentences (अवान्तरवाक्यानि) and paragraphs are maintained by these paradigms

and thus the cohesion across the entire Discourse is secured..

  

Deictic elements of Conjunction ---

  

Addition --

  

and , and also , not , and .... not , or , or else , furthermore , alternatively , by the way , incidentally , that is , in other words ,

thus , likewise , in the same way , on the other hand , by contrast .

  

Adversative --

  

yet , though , only , but , however , even so , all the same , in fact , actually , conversely , on the other hand , instead , on the contrary , rather , at least , I mean , or rather , in any case , anyhow .

  

Casual --

  

so , then , therefore , consequently , on account of this , in consequence , with this in mind , for , because , under the circumstances,

in this respect etc .

  

Temporal ---

  

then , next , just , then before that , hitherto , in the end , finally , in conclusion , first ..... then , at first , originally , formerly ...

now , meanwhile , until /then , at this moment , to conclude etc.

  

Others ---

  

now , of course ,well , surely , after all , any way .

  

In Sanskrit certain pairs of words / corelatives ( नित्यसंबद्धाः ) and certain terms are employed to maintain the एकवाक्यता ( सङ्गति)

across a महावाक्यम् --

  

यत् - तत् , यदा - तदा , यः - सः , येन - तेन , यस्मत् - तस्मात् etc .

  

तु , यत्तु , यच्च , यतः , अतः , तस्मात् , अत एव , च , ततः , अथ , तथाच , न च , अपि च , किन्तु , कथमिति चेत् , पुनः , वस्तुतः , परे तु , अपरे तु ,

इतरे , विशेषेण तु , नैव , न हि , एवम् , एवमपि , इति चेन्न etc.

  

  

I have discussed this aspect in *Theories of Language : Oriental and Occidental* ( pp 44 and 107, 108 ) .
