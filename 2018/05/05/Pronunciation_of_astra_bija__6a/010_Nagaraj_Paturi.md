[[Nagaraj Paturi	2018-05-17, 17:38:04 [Source](https://groups.google.com/g/bvparishat/c/6atRTQx_oGo)]]



-   Vedic had
    a[voiceless](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voiceless_bilabial_fricative "Voiceless bilabial fricative")
    [bilabial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voiceless_bilabial_fricative "Voiceless bilabial fricative")
    [fricative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voiceless_bilabial_fricative "Voiceless bilabial fricative")(ᳶ\[ɸ\],
    called*upadhmānīya*) and a[voiceless velar
    fricative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voiceless_velar_fricative "Voiceless velar fricative")(ᳵ\[x\],
    called*jihvāmūlīya*)—which used to occur when the
    breath*visarga*(अः) appeared before voiceless labial and velar
    consonants respectively. Both of them were lost in Classical
    Sanskrit to give way to the simple*visarga*- upadhmaniya occurs
    before*p*and*ph*, jihvamuliya before*k*and*kh*.
