Prof. Paturi,

  

Sorry for the late response, but like you I was traveling. I think I understand where you are coming from. Tamil paNDitas are rarely acquainted with Hindi, so there might be a difference there. I agree that vedAGgas are very important, but it’s difficult to get the right pronunciation by reading these texts, primarily because there is no single text which captures all rules, and the accepted texts sometimes contradict each other.

  

Going to a trained person and listening is easier and has been the mode of operation. But you are right in pointing out the chicken and egg problem, how to recognize the truly trained person from a poseur or incorrectly trained person if you already did not know how the Veda is to be recited. This is especially a problem for the diaspora.  I have usually had a good experience from scholars trained in the Sringeri or Kanchi mathas. But I have experienced people claiming to have gone to these schools whose recitation skills were quite bad. It’s a tough problem.
