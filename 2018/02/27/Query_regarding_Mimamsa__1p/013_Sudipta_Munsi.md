> > In jyotiShToma, which is the prakriti yAga - षड्विंशति अस्य वङ्क्रयः. > This says the number of rib bones of the sacrificial animal is 26.
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > Let us consider the case of the ashvamedha, a vikriti yAga.
> > 
> > 

> 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > +D - taking the rule प्रकृतिवत् विकृतिः कर्तव्या, this mantra > (षड्विंशति अस्य वङ्क्रयः) >
> 
> > 
> > has to be chanted during the sacrifice of the horse in the ashvamedha > also. >
> 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > -D however, the horse has 34 rib bones. So the prayoga vidhi for > ashvamedha says चतुर्त्रिंशत्येव ब्रूयात् , prohibiting the use of the > previous mantra and to useचतुस्त्रिंशद्वाजिनो देवबन्धो mantra > instead. >
> 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > +D there are oxen and goats also sacrificed in the ashvamedha. Should > the vikriti rule hold in their case also? No. The prayoga vidhi for > them saysनचतुस्त्रिंशति इति ब्रूयात्षड्विंशतिरित्येव ब्रूयात्. >
> 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > Thus, there is first a codanA (प्रकृतिवत् विकृतिः कर्तव्या), then > there is a special rule for vikriti, then a counterexception for other > parts of the vikriti.
> > 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > This is quoted as an example of pratiprasava. D is reinstated by > pratiprasava, but accommodation is made for the special rule of -D in > the case of the horse, otherwise that vidhi will lose any scope for > application. >
