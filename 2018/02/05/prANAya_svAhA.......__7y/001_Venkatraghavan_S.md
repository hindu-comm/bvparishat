> > In addition to the Taittiriya AraNyaka, this also occurs as an anga > karma called prANAgnihotra to the vaishvAnara upAsana between sections > 5.11-5.24. >
> 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > This upAsana is conveyed in the form of a story where the guru, the > king of kekaya country, ashvapati, teaches his five disciples the > vaishvAnara vidyA, an upAsana of virAT.
> > 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > After teaching the main upAsana, the prANAgnihotra is also taught as a > subsidiary karma to be performed along with vaishvAnara upAsana. >
> 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > This is called agnihotra because the mouth of virAT is compared to > AvahanIya agni in the main upAsana (which is the agni in which > agnihotra is performed), and here the upAsaka offers food to virAT via > his own mouth. Thus with the five Ahutis, the five prANas and through > them, the entire universe is satisfied. The mechanism of transfer of > tripti, or satisfaction, from the individual to universal is > beautifully described in sections 5.19-5.23. >
> 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > A stuti of the upAsana occurs in 5.24, where it is said that a person > who does this upAsana is feeding the whole world. All his sins are > burnt away. Like a child dependent on the mother, the entire world is > dependent on him. An interesting application of nahi nindA nyAya also > occurs here, where it is said that only a person who performs this > prANAgnihotra is really doing agnihotra. >
> 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > Whereas, the person who does the nityAgnihotra and not this upAsana is > like the person who offers an Ahuti in bhasma, ashes, ie it is not > efficacious.
> > 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > Again, this is a case of nahi nindA, the intention is to only glorify > the upAsana, not to say that the regular agnihotra is ineffective.
