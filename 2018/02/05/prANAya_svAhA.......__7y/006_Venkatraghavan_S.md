Namaste Subbuji,

The brahmaNe svAha mantra is not explicitly mentioned in both the prANAgni portions of the vaishvAnara vidyA occurring in the ChAndogya portions I quoted earlier, nor in the taittirIya AraNyaka portion you mentioned earlier. I think there must be a smriti basis for its inclusion which needs to be looked into. (perhaps to match the naivedya mantras?)

  

The clue for the interpretation of brahmaNe svAha could be from the bramaNi ma Atma amritatvAya mantra which occurs immediately after the Ahutis to the panca prANa in the taittirIya AraNyaka.

  

BhaTTabhAskara and SAyaNAchArya treat the word Brahman as parabrahman. That is, the offering is made to Brahman.

  

BhaTTabhAskara says:

इदानीमनेनान्नहोमेन मे आत्मा शरीरं ब्रह्मणि एव स्थितम् \| तच्च मम अमृतत्वाय भवति \| यद्वा - अमृतत्वाय ममात्मा ब्रह्मणि तिष्ठतु अनेनान्नपरोक्षेण \| यद्वा - ब्रह्मणि अन्ने मम शरीरं स्थितम् तदधीनस्थितिकत्वात् \| तस्यामृतत्वाय इदमन्नं जुहोमि \|

sAyaNAchArya says:

एताभि: पञ्चाभिराहुतिभिरमृतत्वाय मोक्षाय मे मदीय आत्मा जीवो ब्रह्मणि परमातमन्येकी भवत्विति शेष: \|
