On the lines of the various quotes already presented here, yet another one is:

  

प्रत्यहं प्रत्यवेक्षेत नरश्चरितमात्मन:  
किन्नु मे पशुभिस्तुल्यं किन्नु सत्पुरूषैरिति \|\|

Every day one should introspect one's own behavior and see what are characteristics (in me) that resemble with animal ('pashu') and what are the characteristics that resemble with the qualities of a noble person. ('satpurusha').  
(A simple way of implementation given for any individual to brighten the inherent noble qualities present in the human mind!)

प्रतिदिन आदमी को अपने चरित्र को देखना चाहिए, कौन ऐसी चीजें हैं जो मुझ में पशु जैसी हैं और कौन ऐसी हैं जो महान पुरुषों जैसी हैं \|\|  
(यह प्राकृतिक तरीका है अपने आप के चरित्र को देखने का !)

  

The verse places a very high standard, with virtually no choice for a man to constantly work for: a nara has to be satpurusha. Anything less than that will only mean that he is pashu.
