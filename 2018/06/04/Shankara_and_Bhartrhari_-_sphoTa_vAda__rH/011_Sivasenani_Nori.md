Dear Sri Venkatraghavan

  

That the world emanates from Sabda is agreeable to Vedantins. It is regarding the nature of Sabda that the dispute is. According to Vaiyaakaranas, it is Sphota, whereas it is varNajAti according to Mimamsakas. The Vaiyaakarana's refutation of varNavAda is more detailed than what is given in Devataadhikaranam of Vedantasutras. The basic issue for the VaiyaakaraNa is that Sabda is a whole, undifferentiated one and the division into varNas etc. is only for convenience - it is mithya; so, how can it be real?

  

While establishment of Advaita does not require refutation of Sphotavada, refutation of Sphota indeed amounts to refutation of Sabdabrahman - at least as the VaiyaakaraNas put it (for the Vedantins since there is nothing other than Brahman, Sabda is also Brahman, as indeed is said in many Vedic sentences) - since the nature of Sabda is now established as Varnajati, which cannot be said to be encompassing all that Brahman is.

  

Regards

Senani
