I thought I would share some observations to the potential new researchers.

  

1. You should define your own intellectual interest and look for a path forward.

  This is done by meeting people and exchanging ideas. To frame up a track

  for life is an important step for any researcher.

  

2. The process in 1 might take one or more years of thinking and wandering.

  However if you get rooted, you would be driven to your goal. You may not

  get the right opportunity, but you must be clear in mind.

  

3. In case you want to do fundamental work, train yourself also in the sciences

  and mathematics. India's foundation is in analysis, but the notes of the scholars

  do not exist. You would do revolutionary work by thinking hard on the analytic

  foundations of Indian cosmology, speech science, yoga, health science and music..

  

4. See that you are studying or working at least fourteen hours a day.
Keep a

  daily journal of your work to create discipline. Start writing articles on interest

  every two months and let it be read by your friends and peers.
Develop

  regularity.

  

Travel without a plan could be a waste. It would be like going to market to spend

money without knowing what to buy. "Know yourself" as has been said in the past!
