The name 'लक्ष्मीः' occurs in the Vishnu Sahasra Nama (943) for which Shankara has alternatively commented as 'आत्मविद्या' and cited a smriti 'आत्मविद्या च देवि त्वम्' इति श्रीस्तुतौ.

  

There are also name such as 'devataa' in the Chandogya upanishad to refer to Brahman: सेयं देवतैक्षत ... apart from Sat and Atma. Here we get all the three genders referring to Brahman, which itself is neuter.

  

The Bhagavadgita 10.34 has the Lord listing a few feminine words as He himself: कीर्तिः श्रीर्वाक्चनारीणांस्मृतिर्मेधा धृतिः क्षमा 
where Shankara comments: कीर्तिः श्रीः वाक् चनारीणांस्मृतिः मेधा धृतिः क्षमा इत्येताः उत्तमाः स्त्रीणाम् अहम् अस्मि,…
