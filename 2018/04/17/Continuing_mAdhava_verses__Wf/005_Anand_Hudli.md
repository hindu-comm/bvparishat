> > माधवरसकाव्यं भो रसिकाः पिबतान्वहम् ।  
> पीत्वा पश्यत किं चित्रं द्वापरतां गतः कलिः॥

O Rasikas (those who appreciate poetry)! Drink the poetry of Madhava every day. Having drunk it, behold the wonder! Kali Yuga has turned into Dvapara.



> 
> > 
> > नगरीयं व्रजीभूता क्रीडति यत्र राधया।  
> माधवो मधुपो नित्यं गोपीमुखाम्बुजस्य च॥  
> > 
> > 

  

This city has turned into Vraja (Vrindavana), where Madhava, who is the bee enjoying the (nectar) of the lotus faces of the Gopis, plays eternally with Radha.



> 
> > 
> > अद्य मे जनिता शंका कोऽयं काव्यरसप्रदः।  
> माधवमानवो वाऽथ तत्स्थितव्याससूनुर्वा (भागवतोत्तमः शुकः)॥ >
> 
> > 

A doubt has arisen in me today. Who is this poet showering the Rasa of poetry (on us)? Is he the human Madhava or Vyasa's son Shuka, the best of the Bhagavatas, situated in him (human Madhava)?
