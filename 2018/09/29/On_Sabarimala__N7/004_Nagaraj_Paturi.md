The Wikipedia page cited by Sri Kalyan-ji says,

  

> 
> > After the installation of the temple, it was mostly unreachable for > about three centuries. In the 12th century, a prince of Pandalam > Dynasty, called Manikandan, rediscovered the original path to reach > Sabarimala. He had many followers with him, including the descendants > of the Vavar (a Muslim warrior whom Manikandan defeated) family. This > Prince is considered an Avatar of Ayyappa, and is believed to have led > a pack of Tigers to his Palace with Vavar and then later disappeared > to the Sabarimala temple. The temple was then > renovated^([\[16\]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabarimala#cite_note-16)). >
> 
> > 
> > In 1821 AD, the kingdom of Pandalam was added > to[Travancore](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travancore "Travancore"). > 48 major temples including the Sabarimala temple were also added to > Travancore. The idol was erected in > 1910^([\[17\]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabarimala#cite_note-17)). >
> 

The sentence The idol was erected in 1910 should be taken as"The present idol was erected in 1910" . Otherwise, it leads to the idea that the temple meant in " the temple was renovated" needs to be taken as a temple without idol, which is a misnomer.
