I guess the point is it is difficult to test a guru by any shishya.

that verse

  

  
परीक्ष्यैव गुरुः शिष्यं शिष्यो अपि गुरुमव्रजेत् \|  
अन्यथा नरकायैव प्रायश्चित्तं गुरोस्तथा \|\|

  

found by Krishna Ji comes close.

  

I guess this word parIkshya can be taken be taken as rigorously evaluating or testing to whatever level it can be done.

The whole point is a Shishya may not be mature enough to really test a Guru.

  

My question was approximate. the word Testing which I used is somewhat close to "parIkshya".

How else can one test a guru? one can check references of the guru. or ask a few questions and see if answers consistently convince that this is the person to learn from

  

These shlokas quoted by Sri Subramanian Ji definitely gives some guidelines for a person to understand how to look for a guru.

  
सिद्धं सत्संप्रदाये स्थिरधियमनघं श्रॊत्रियं ब्रह्मनिष्ठं

सत्वस्थं सत्यवाचं समयनियतया साधुवृत्त्या समेतम् \|

दम्भासूयादिमुक्तं जितविषयगणं दीर्घबन्धुं दयालुं

स्खालित्येशासितारं स्वपरहितपरं देशिकं भूष्णुरीप्सेत् \|\| (7.2) (*Nyasa-vimsathi*1)
