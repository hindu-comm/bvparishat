[[Nagaraj Paturi	2018-09-14, 09:09:30 [Source](https://groups.google.com/g/bvparishat/c/Mo3awJv-kEM)]]



The stories in the text of Srimad Bhagavata Purana being much older than the date given by modern scholars to it and those stories being prevalent in north India much earlier than that date can not be disputed.

  

These modern scholars might be talking about the date of composition of the extant text of the book.

<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliodorus_pillar#Inscriptions>  

  

There are two inscriptions on the pillar.^(\[*[specify](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources "Wikipedia:Citing sources")*\])

The first inscription describes in[Brahmi script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brahmi_script "Brahmi script")the situation of Heliodorus and his relationship to the[Shunga Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shunga_Empire "Shunga Empire")and the[Indo-Greek Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Greek_Kingdom "Indo-Greek Kingdom").

Devadevasa Va\[sude\]vasa Garudadhvajo ayam  
karito i\[a\] Heliodorena bhaga  
vatena Diyasa putrena Takhasilakena  
Yonadatena agatena maharajasa  
Amtalikitasa upa\[m\]ta samkasam-rano  
Kasiput\[r\]asa \[Bh\]agabhadrasa tratarasa  
vasena \[chatu\]dasena rajena vadhamanasa

^([\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliodorus_pillar#cite_note-SW-2))

This Garuda-standard of[Vāsudeva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/V%C4%81sudeva "Vāsudeva"), the God of Gods  
was erected here by the devotee Heliodoros,  
the son of Dion, a man of[Taxila](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taxila "Taxila"),  
sent by the Great[Yona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yona "Yona")King  
Antialkidas, as ambassador to  
King Kasiputra Bhagabhadra, the Savior  
son of the princess from[Varanasi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Varanasi "Varanasi"), in the fourteenth year of his reign.

^([\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliodorus_pillar#cite_note-8))

The second inscription on the pillar describes in more detail the spiritual content of the faith supported by Heliodorus:

Trini amutapadani‹\[su\] anuthitani  
nayamti svaga damo chago apramado  
  
Three immortal precepts (footsteps)... when practiced  
lead to heaven:[self-restraint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damah "Damah"), charity, consciousness

[Richard Salomon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_G._Salomon_(academic) "Richard G. Salomon (academic)")gives a similar but slightly different translation:^([\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliodorus_pillar#cite_note-SW-2))

> "This Garuda-pillar of Vãsudeva, the god of gods, was constructed here > by Heliodora, the Bhãgavata, son of Diya, of Takhkhasilã, the Greek > ambassador who came from the Great King Amtalikita to King Kãsîputra > Bhãgabhadra, the Savior, prospering in (his) fourteenth regnal year. > (These?) three steps to immortality, when correctly followed, lead to > heaven: control, generosity, and > attention.^([\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heliodorus_pillar#cite_note-9))
