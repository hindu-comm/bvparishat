Dr. Ravi Khangai's query is about source of the verse

  
  
"Koi ho Nrip hame ka hani  
Ham to chakar Ram ke  
Ab kahe ko hoge Nare ke mansabdar"  
  

This verse is not correct. It is a combination of two different verses from different sources under different contexts. The first line "Koi ho Nrip hame ka hani" is found in Sriramcharitmanas by Goswami Tulsidas. Ayodhyakanda 15.3. But it is incorrectly quoted in your mail. The correct version is

Kou NRip hou hamahi ka hani, cheri chhaDi ab hob ki rani.  
कोउ नृप होउ हमहि का हानी. चेरी छाड़ी अब होब कि रानी (रामचरितमानस 2/15 / 03) \[Manthara to Kaikeyi\]  
  
The second part is also incorrect  

Ham to chakar Ram ke  
Ab kahe ko hoge Nare ke mansabdar"  
  

Correct verse  
Ham chakar raghuvir ke, patau likho darbar हम चाकर रघुवीर के, पटौ लिखो दरबार।  
Ab Tulsi ka hoinge, nar ke mansabdaar अब तुलसी का होइंगे, नर के मनसबदार ।।  
  
The author of these lines is again Goswami Tulsidas.  Shri Managa Dev Tripathi (in this thread) has wrongly attributed these lines to Shri Maithili Sharan Gupt.  
  
Gupt ji had quoted this Tulsidas verse on the dedication page of his magnum opus 'Saket'.  
  
To the best of my knowledge, thisverse "Ham chakar raghuvir ke" is not found in any book by Goswami ji. But it is quoted in almost all of his biographies. Context: It is said Emperor Akbar invited Tulsidas ji to be one of his 'Mansabdars' and Nav Ratna. Goswami ji politely declined the offer by telling him "  
Ham chakar raghuvir ke, patau likho darbar हम चाकर रघुवीर के, पटौ लिखो दरबार।  
Ab Tulsi ka hoinge, nar ke mansabdaar अब तुलसी का होइंगे, नर के मनसबदार ।।  
i.e. I am serving Shri Ram, as a writer in HIS court. Tulasidas cannot work now under a human being.
