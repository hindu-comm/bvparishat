Dear Sri Rishi Goswami-ji,

  

Your post is a digression from the theme of the thread.

  

Let us be careful about the nuances of the comparisons and connections between Ancient Indian knowledge and the knowledge in the modern sciences.

  

1\. A certain modern science idea is similar to an idea in ancient Indian knowledge or vice versa

  

is different from

  

2\. A certain theory (a certain explanation to an intriguing phenomenon) , a certain solution to a problem in modern science was already known in ancient Indian knowledge systems.

  

is different from

  

3. A certain modern science idea is an *influence of* an idea in ancient Indian knowledge; A certain theory (a certain explanation to an intriguing phenomenon) , a certain solution to a problem in modern science is an influence of a theory /solution in ancient Indian knowledge systems   

  

is different from

  

4. A certain modern science idea is a direct borrowing from an idea in ancient Indian knowledge;A certain theory (a certain explanation to an intriguing phenomenon) , a certain solution to a problem in modern science is a direct borrowing from a theory /solution in ancient Indian knowledge systems.

  

This thread is titled as  

  

"Impact of ancient India on Modern science"  

  

Hence only cases coming under 3 & 4 above fit in this thread.
