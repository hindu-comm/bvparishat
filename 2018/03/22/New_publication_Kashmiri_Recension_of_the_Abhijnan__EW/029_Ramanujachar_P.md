> Respected Prof. Nagaraj ji,  
> Namaste !  
> I have downloaded the book by Prof. Romila Thapar & have gone through > the same. Thank you for your suggestion. It presents a new direction > to analyze a narrative, which may have been retold / reworked upon in > different ages by different authors and artists. In the preliminary of > the book she says – “ the narrative, I have chosen focuses on a young > woman, Shakuntala. That each variant of the story created at different > historical period projects her differently, not only allows a > historical view on the story, but also introduces a gender > perspective.” Her intention is to demonstrate that it is a viable > activity ( = area ) which will direct us to many unexplored dimension > of both our past and our present. No doubt, this is a commendable > approach.  
> But if I or any textual critic has to say anything regarding this > type of study, it would be that, it needs caution – Any literary / > cultural / historical criticism ( or study ) should be preceded by > “Textual Criticism” . So, whatever has been said by Prof. Romila > Thapar is, of course very interesting & thought provoking, but it may > be misleading. In the absence of a critical text, any literary / > historical study will give you temporary and unsteady results / > conclusions. Because a text of Abhijnaana-s’akunatalam ( Ed. By M. R. > Kale & S. K. Belvelkar ), which was utilized by her, is not a > dependable one!!! ( Those are not critical editions of any recension. > )  
> Till date no-body has full & correct ( i. e. objectively and logically > proven ) picture of “a journey of the text” of this drama. For the > first time, I have brought out the critical edition of fifth and the > oldest Kashmiri recension. During my research work I have suggested > the chronological development / creations of all these five > recensions. ( According to my findings, Kaashmiri recension comes > first, Maithili stands second ( in the time of Chandra Gupta-2 ), > third one is Bengali ( 12th century ). Dakshinatya and Deva-nagari > recensions, which provides a short and shorter text, come fourth and > fifth only 15th & 16th centuries. ) After completing this task, I > have also prepared a comparative study of all texts of these five > recensions in a very big volume. ( Abhijnaana-shakuntala-naata.kasya > panca-ranagaa-vrittayaH. With full texts of all five recensions. Here, > any reader will be able to see the chronological changes in words, > sentences ( dialogues ), verses, stage-directions, all variants, all > omissions, all interpolations, changes in the names of characters, > their costumes, make-ups, acting on the stage etc, etc.) After this > detailed and meticulous comparative study, I have venture to > “Reconstruct” a full text of this drama. “Reconstructed text” means > which should be nearer to the original and at least oldest one. So, > once again, I would like to say that No Literary study is final, until > it is based on Critical edition.
