. 1- अल्पाक्षरं असंदिग्धं सारवत् विश्वतोमुखम्।

अस्तोभं अनवद्यं च सूत्रं सूत्र विदो विदुः॥(Vayu Puraan)

It has fewest possible syllables (अल्पाक्षरं), It is meaningful (सारवत्), It has a wide or universal application (विश्वतोमुखम्), It is flawless (अनवद्यम्), It has no superfluous words or breaks (अस्तोभम्). It flows smoothly, it is clear and leaves no room for doubt (असंदिग्धम्).

Answer- Sage Jaimini clearly stated as Kala,

not mentioned Bhaaga or Amsa , so no place for doubt (असंदिग्धम्).

Otherwise ha should have been said as Bhaga OR Amsa !

श्लो॥ विकळानां कळा षष्ट्या तत् षष्ट्या भाग उच्यते।

 तत् त्रिंशता भवेद्राशिः भगणो द्वादशैव ते ॥

2-It is a cardinal rule that a sutra has to be understood from vyanjana or what is unsaid rather than what is patently obvious.  

Answer- This is wrong, because Vyanjana will be used in poetry, not in Sastra

  

3- कलादि as कलया: आदय: अंशास्तै: as per bahuvrihi samaas (this is a different technique in samskrit where we are not breaking words but getting proper interpretation while considering as 1 word, bahuvrihi samaas gives the hidden meaning of that word called as Aparoksh) where आदि means commencement, starting with etc. hence before kalaa ie minutes degrees is to be considered ie kindly start from beginning ie degrees follows by minutes etc and अधिकः says that which is highest will be aatmakaarak.

3- Actual Bahuvrihi is like this- above compound is wrong to convey real meaning. correct compound given below. see this

संपत्करी संस्कत व्याख्यानं- कळादिभिरित्यत्र कळा आदिभिः \[कळा-विकळा\] एषां ते, कळादयः तैः कळादिभिः इति बहुव्रीहिः वाच्यः ,तदा भाग इत्यर्थः
नायाति ।
