**How to Study Indic Sources: The Anatomy of Indic Scientific Texts**

  

**Course Designed by**:

Prof. Shrinivasa Varakhedi

*Well known Nyaya scholar,Professor and Dean Academic Affairs, Karnataka Samskrit University,Dean, Chinmaya Viswavidyapeeth, Veliyanad, Kerala,Board of Studies member, School of Vedic Sciences, MIT ADT University, Loni Kalbhor, Pune*

  

**Medium of Instruction:**

Mix of Samskrit and English. Using English translations for key Indian concepts can cause much confusion and is studiously avoided.**  
**

**  
**

**Prerequisites:**

10+2 or equivalent, Ability to read and understand conversational Samskrit, basic

familiarity with its grammar. Will conduct a short 2-day course on conversational Sanskrit for those who do not have any knowledge of Sanskrit.

  

**Certification:**

Attendance and online exam  

  

**Objective:**

To understand the purpose, structure, methodology and classification of Indic scientific treatises andto elucidate how to study them.

  

**Value Proposition:**

India had a rich scientific tradition that helped it remain a developed country for millennia. Some ofits insights are being discovered to be highly relevant even today – in mathematics, psychology andmanagement, and wellness sciences to name a few. This course helps modern seekers quickly rampup on how to study Indic texts, so they can explore their modern relevance and innovate.

  

**Target Audience:**

Students and professionals trained in modern scientific discourse, and interested in study of Indicshaastra texts.

  

**Expected Outcome:**

-   Ability for independent study of Indic shaastra texts to explore
    contemporary relevance.  
-   Ability to navigate Indic texts for concept search and knowledge
    mining applications.  
-   Ability to paraphrase Indic shaastra content to novice
    mainstream-educated audience.  

  

  

**COURSE CONTENT**

  

**Objective:**

To enable the participants to understand the purpose, structure, methodologyand hierarchical classification of Indic sources and to elucidate how to study them.

  

**Content:**

1.  Indian world view:

-   What is world according to Indic perspective -   Purpose of life (Puruṣārthas – the purpose that Indic literature
    meet)  
      

Indic knowledge sources:

Aṣṭādaśa-vidyāsthānas

Darśanas

Classical literature

-   History vs literature -   Rasa as the primary purpose -   Maṅgalāśaṁsana - Message/teaching promoting optimism

Technical literature  

Commentarial tradition and reading the original work  
  

Transmission methods from oral to written:

Oral tradition is the watermark for written tradition (human centric rather than papercentric knowledge tradition)

Methods of paraphrasing a text (śruti-liṅga- vākya-prakaraṇa- samākhyā)

Interpretation of a text - Tātparya-nirṇaya (upakrama-upasaṁhāra- abhyāsa-apūrvatā-phalam-arthavāda- upapatti)

Structural hierarchy and purpose - Sūtra - Bhāṣya - Vyākhyā - Ṭīkā - Ṭippaṇī - Prapaňcikā -Saṅgraha - Kārikā - Vṛtti - Vārtika - Prakaraṇa - Vāda - Khaṇḍana etc.

Features in Indic sources

-   Anubandha-catuṣṭaya -   Maṅgalācaraṇa -   Uddeśa-lakṣaṇa- parīkṣā -   Vāda-paddhati -   Nyāya-vākya -   Dṛṣṭānta -   Laukika-nyāya -   Śāstrīya-nyāya -   Adhikaraṇa-racanā -   Discussions on etymology, grammar, apparent contradiction, mild
    apprehension(mandā śaṅkā)  
      

Scientific literature:

-   Soft sciences – dealing with mind, soul, psychology -   Hard sciences – pertain to world and worldly endeavour -   Gleanings of Indic ‘scientific’ sources (Mathematics, Āyurveda,
    Siddhauṣadha etc.) -   Tantrayukti - manuals/tools prescribing how to study the text – e.g.
    Tantrayukti forAmarakoṣa, Āyurveda, Arthaśāstra, Jyotiṣa etc.  
      

Potential Project topics –

-   Maṇḍala and aṣṭaka structures of Ṛgveda -   Vedāṅga structures -   Link between Saṁhitā and Āraṇyaka sections of Yajurveda -   Structure of Āgama texts -   Structure of Tantra texts -   Structure of Mahābhārata -   Structure of Purāṇas -   Structure of Mahākāvyas -   Caraṇavyūha - Vedalakṣaṇa
