I guess your query is with respect to people. If not, I do know of a description that fits the bill. In the Bhavishyapurana, Uttaraparva, 161st Adhyaya, we find the following about Kapilagau:

  

दशेह कपिलाः प्रोक्ताः स्वयमेव स्वयंभुवा \|

यो दद्याच्छ्रोत्रियस्यैव वै स्वर्गं गत्वा स मानवः \|\|28\|\|

विमानैर्विविधैर्दिव्यैर्दिव्यकन्याभिरर्चितः \|

सेव्यमानस्तु गन्धर्वैर्दीप्यमाना यथाग्नयः \|\| 29 \|\|

सुवर्णकपिला पूर्वा द्वितीया गौरपिङ्गला \|

आशा चैव तृतीया स्यादग्निज्वाला चतुर्थिका \|\| 30 \|\|

पञ्चमी जुहुवर्णा स्यात् षष्ठी च घृतपिङ्गला \|

सप्तमी श्वेतपिङ्गा स्यादष्टमी क्षीरपिङ्गला \|\| 31 \|\|

नवमी पाटला ज्ञेया दशमी पुष्पपिङ्गला \|

एता दशा समाख्याताः कपिलाश्च वसुन्धरे \|\| 32 \|\|

  

Having described the ten types of Kapilagau based mainly on their colour (not withstanding the name, the most common colour in this sub-species seems to be black!), both the cows and the effect of donating them is then described till the end of the chapter called kapilaadaanamaahaatmyam. Some of these verses are found in some versions of Mahabharata as well (One instance: Mahabharata in Telugu script edited by P. P. S. Sastry, Ramaswami Sastrulu & Sons, Madras, 1933 -
Asvamedhikaparvan, adhyaaya 110).
