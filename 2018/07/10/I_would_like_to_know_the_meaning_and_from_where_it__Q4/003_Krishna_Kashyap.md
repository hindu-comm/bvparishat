Thanks Deshpande Ji, L Srinivas Ji,

  

I was wondering if there is any yogic meaning in this term ghatasarpa.

  

the classical view seems to be "serpent in a ghata" or like Deshpande ji mentioned about the bacterium. in this literature reference, it is used as a method to kill someone using a snake in a pot.

  

<https://books.google.com/books?id=ilxM6LKFLZwC&pg=RA2-PA479&lpg=RA2-PA479&dq=ghatasarpa+prayogena&source=bl&ots=84_1cb9Axi&sig=DrednJ17V-qXkuW0lBLt23MiEUQ&hl=en&sa=X&ved=0ahUKEwi5poX8y5ncAhWPKHwKHc5GCIgQ6AEIKzAA#v=onepage&q=ghatasarpa%20prayogena&f=false>  

  

However,

  

In hatayoga there are 4 stages arambha, ghata,parichaya, nishpatti.

  

someone indicated to me that in stage ghata, the kundalini starts to awaken. Since kundalini is allegorically related to a snake due to the movement, ghata-sarpais probably related to kundaliniin this state of ghata.

  

Just thought of sharing.

  

However, I do not know any reference in any work to indicate that this theory is valid.
