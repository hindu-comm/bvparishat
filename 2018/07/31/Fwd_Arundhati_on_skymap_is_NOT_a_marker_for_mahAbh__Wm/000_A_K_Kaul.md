6.2.88 Kisari Mohan Ganguly's translation:She, O king, who is celebrated over the three worlds and is applauded by the righteous, even that constellationArundhatikeepeth her lordVasiṣṭhaon her back.

Source:<http://ancientvoice.wikidot.com/mbh:arundhati>

  

I agree with Narahari Achar. The mention of Arundhati's position on the skymap DOES NOT indicate a contemporary event related to MBh episodes, but simply a recounting of turbulent omens.

  

Nilakantha Oak has wasted his astronomical excursus trying to date the event of Arundhati moving ahead of Vasiṣṭhato date the Great Epic events. Arundhati's rise on the celestial sphereis certainly a skaymap observation, BUT, has no bearing on the date of MBh events. Arundhati moving ahead CANNOT be reckoned as a date marker for MBh.
