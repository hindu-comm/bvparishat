Thanks Shankara ji,

  

I went through the page you had linked above. I also read the Sankshepashariraka II.182 verse cited therein:

  

![](https://groups.google.com/group/bvparishat/attach/7c5c25aeede77/image.png?part=0.3&view=1)  

![](https://groups.google.com/group/bvparishat/attach/7c5c25aeede77/image.png?part=0.2&view=1)  

  

In the above account, we are reminded of the famous verse of the Valmiki Ramayana, Yuddha kaanda:

  

  
[https://sa.wikisource.org/s/65y](https://sa.wikisource.org/s/65y)  

  

इत्युक्तो लोकपालैस्तैः स्वामी लोकस्य राघवः ।   
अब्रवीत्रिदशश्रेष्ठान् रामो धर्मभृतां वरः ।। ६.१२०.११ ।।   
  
*आत्मानं मानुषं मन्ये रामं दशरथात्मजम् ।*   
यो ऽहं यस्य यतश्चाहं भगवांस्तद् ब्रवीतु मे ।। ६.१२०.१२ ।।   
  
इति ब्रुवन्तं काकुत्स्थं ब्रह्मा ब्रह्मविदां वरः ।   
अब्रवीच्छृणु मे राम सत्यं सत्यपराक्रम ।। ६.१२०.१३ ।।   
  
भवान्नारायणो देवः श्रीमांश्चक्रायुधो विभुः ।   
एकशृङ्गो वराहस्त्वं भूतभव्यसपत्नजित् ।। ६.१२०.१४ ।।   
  
अक्षरं ब्रह्म सत्यं च मध्ये चान्ते च राघव ।   
लोकानां त्वं परो धर्मो विष्वक्सेनश्चतुर्भुजः ।। ६.१२०.१५ ।।  

  

And all the other verses that follow these are of the nature of portraying the svarupa of Rama.

  

From the above, I think the reference in the Sanksepashariraka is a pointer to the VR and not necessarily the YV. If Mainkar was referring to the YV, there is not any clinching evidence for matching it with the Sank.Shaa.. If there are any words like 'sankalpita ajnanam' ,etc. in the YV in the particular episode of Brahmaa - Raama dialogue, then maybe there is a suggestion in the SS for the YV.

  

I request others here to please make an assessment of Mainkar's proposition. There are two more commentaries to the SS that I have not checked for this verse.

  

Thanks and warm regards

subrahmanian.v
