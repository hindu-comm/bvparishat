  Here I would like to present the comment of Bohtlingk on P.6.4.103 and the corresponding sutra हियासामः (स॰कं॰8.3.12). For SKA sutras, pl. click:<http://ashtadhyayi.com/ska/>

  

अङितश्च (पा॰6.4.103)

Aber auch dann, wenn diese Personalendung kein stummes ङ् hat.

रारन्धि, यन्धि, युयोधि. Nach 3,4,88 kann हि im Veda पित् sein; ist es पित्, so ist es nach 1,2,4 nicht ङित्. Da es den angeführten Beispielen nicht ङित् ist, erfährt die Wurzel keine Schwächung, wird sogar verstärkt. Trotz पित् Oxytona !



In English translation:

Also when this personal ending has no ङ् marker.

रारन्धि, यन्धि, युयोधि. By P.3.4.88 (वा छन्दसि), हि in Veda can be पित्; if it is पित्, it is not ङित् as per P.1.2.4 (सार्वधातुकमपित्). Since the cited examples are not ङित्, the root does not experience weakening, is even strengthened. Despite पित् Oxytona!

  

Here, it is not clear how aपित् suffix can be oxytone (उदात्त). As perअनुदात्तौ सुप्पितौ (P.3.1.4), it has to beअनुदात्त, unless a separate rule is specifically prescribed that in spite ofपित्, it will beउदात्त.

  

महाभाष्य, काशिका, न्यास and पदमञ्जरी do not say anything about it.  

Regarding clarification, I wrote a letter dated 3rd June 1998 toa Vedic scholar Dr Ravi Prakash Arya. He replied as follows:

"You are correct in your observations. ... Bohtlingk's inference of oxytone पित् is based on examples and not that he could understand Panini. In the present case the accent rule has to be supplemented in order to be more specific and precise."  

  

Under स॰कं॰8.3.12 (हियासामः), the suffix हि (and hence its substitute धि) has been prescribed as उदात्त. For detailed discussion, pp.13-14 of the article published in June 2000 issue of वेदवाणी are attached.
