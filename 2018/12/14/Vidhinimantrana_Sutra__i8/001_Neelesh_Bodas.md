Namaste,

  

I have tried to explain Padamanjari below. Nyaas is more or less the same (explains only 3 pakshas). Hope this helps. Please beware that this is my interpretation (and could be far from being correct). Happy to learn / discuss further.

  

=========================================

  

अयं विध्यादिः प्रकृत्यर्थः, प्रत्ययार्थः, प्रकृत्यथेविशेषणम्, प्रत्ययार्थविशेषणं वेति चत्वारः पक्षाः ।

  

There are four ways of interpreting the first word of the sutra - विधिनिमन्त्रणामन्त्रणाधीष्टसम्प्रश्नप्रार्थनेषु ।

  

Paksha 1 : प्रकृत्यर्थ - Does this word represent meaning of the verb?

Paksha 2: प्रत्ययार्थः - Does it represent the meaning of the pratyay (that is, does it represent the कर्ता / कर्म / भाव as mandated by prayoga? )

Paksha 3 प्रकृत्यथेविशेषणम् - Is it used as an adjective of the verb to which the lakaar is attached?

Paksha 4 प्रत्ययार्थविशेषणम् - Is it used as an adjective to the meaning of the lakaar pratyay?

  

The first three pakasha have been refused as follows -

==========================================

  

तत्राद्ये पक्षे विध्यादिषु वर्तमानाद्धातोरित्यर्थः स्यात्, ततश्च यत्र प्रकृतिरेव विध्यादीनाचष्टे तत्रैव स्यात्---विदध्यान्निमन्त्रयेत, आमन्त्रयेत, अधीच्छेत्, संप्रच्छेत्, प्रार्थयेतेति;

  

In the first paksha, we will be able to apply this lakaar pratyay to only six dhatus, corresponding to these six meanings - विधि, निमन्त्रण, आमन्त्रण, अभीष्ट, सम्प्रश्न, प्रार्थना.

  

तत्रापि प्रकृत्यर्था भावकर्मकर्तारश्चेत्येतावदवगम्येत, न प्रकृत्यर्थव्यतिरेकेणापरं विध्यादिकम् ।

  

And even there, the application is limited to the verbs that directly represent these six meanings, and not for the verbs that represent these six meaning using negation or other qualifiers (because the vidhi-etc must characterize the actual dhatu, and not its visheshana).

  

यथा चेच्छार्थेभ्यः "विभाषा वर्तमाने" इत्यत्र लिङि इच्छेदिति लडादयश्च न स्युः; अनेन लिङा बाधितत्वात् ।

  

For example, in the previous sutra 3.3.160, (in the paksha where लट् is not used) will not giveइच्छेत् (because it does not represent any of these six meanings).

  

**Thus first paksha is refuted.**

=====================================

  

द्वितीये नाप्राप्तेषु भावकर्मकर्तृृषु विध्यादयोऽर्था निर्द्देश्यमानास्तेषां बाधकाः स्युरिति लिङा कर्त्रादीनामभिधानं न स्यात्, द्विवचनबहुवचनानुपपत्तिश्च, विध्यादीनामेकत्वात् ।

  

In the second paksha, where the vidhi etc are expected to represent either the karta or the karma or bhava, the lakaar will fail to represent the actual karta / karma present in the sentence. Moreover, we cannot do dvivachan and bahuvachan forms of this lakaar, since the “assumed karta” (vidhi etc) only makes sense in ekavachan. That’s definitely not correct.

  

**Thus the second paksha is refuted.**

=====================================

  

तृतीये तु यत्र प्रकृतिरेव विध्यादीनाचष्टे तत्र न स्यात्---विदध्यान्निमन्त्रयेतेति, न ह्यत्र प्रकृत्यर्थादन्ये तद्विशेषणभूता विध्यादयः सन्ति ।

  

In the third paksha, these six words are considered adjectives of the verb. In this case, the lakaar can only be used for the verbs where one of these six words can be used as additional qualifier for that verb. This means that we cannot use the lakaar for the verbs whose meaning is one of these six terms,because there won’t be a qualifier (adjective) there which would represent one of these six terms. This would make the forms like विदध्यात्, निमन्त्रयेत् etc incorrect.

  

  

**Thus the third paksha is refuted.**

========================================================

  

चतुर्थे तु विध्यादीनां कर्त्रादीनां च स्पष्ट एव भेद इति दोषाभाव इति तमेवाश्रयति---विध्यादयश्चैत इति ।

  

In the fourth paksha, the six terms are considered to represent the visheshana of the pratyartha. That is, they just qualify the karta / karma etc with some addional information. This way, there is a clear distinction between the karta (e.g. देवदत्तः) and the pratyayartha (e.g. आमन्त्रितः). **There is no dosha here and hence this paksha is accepted.**

**  
**

\- Neelesh
