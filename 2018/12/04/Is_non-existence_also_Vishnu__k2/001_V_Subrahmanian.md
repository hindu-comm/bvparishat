Bh.G.9.19:

  



  

तपाम्यहमहं वर्षं निगृह्णाम्युत्सृजामि च।  
अमृतं चैव मृत्युश्चसदसच्चाहमर्जुन॥ १९ ॥

  

9.19 O Arjuna, I give heat, I withhold and pour down rain. I am verily the nectar, and also death, existence and nonexistence.  

तपामि अहम् आदित्यो भूत्वा कैश्चित् रश्मिभिः उल्बणैः। अहं वर्षं कैश्चित् रश्मिभिः उत्सृजामि। उत्सृज्य पुनः निगृह्णामि कैश्चित् रश्मिभिः अष्टभिः मासैः पुनः उत्सृजामि प्रावृषि। अमृतं चैव देवानाम् , मृत्युश्च मर्त्यानाम्। सत् यस्य यत् सम्बन्धितया विद्यमानं तत् , तद्विपरीतम् असच्च एव अहम् अर्जुन। न पुनः अत्यन्तमेव असत् भगवान् , स्वयं कार्यकारणे वा सदसती ये पूर्वोक्तैः निवृत्तिप्रकारैः एकत्वपृथक्त्वादिविज्ञानैः यज्ञैः मां पूजयन्तः उपासते ज्ञानविदः, ते यथाविज्ञानं मामेव प्राप्नुवन्ति ॥ १९ ॥

  

I Myself am sat, existence- the effect which has come into being in relation to its cause; and its opposite, asat, nonexistence. \[Nonexistence: the cause which has not become manifest as the effect possessing name and form, It cannot be admitted that the effect has absolute existence, for the Upanisad says, 'All transformation has speech as it basis, and it is name only' (Ch.6. 1. 4). Nor can it be said that the cause has absolute non-existence, for there is the text,'[৷৷.by](http://xn--i8ba.by) what logic can the existent come verily out of nonexistence? But surely,৷৷.all this was Existence, one without a second' (op. cit. 6.2.2).\] It is not that the Lord is Himself absolutely nonexistence; nor are effect and cause (absolutely) existence and nonexistent (respectively).  

  

In BG 13.12 too we have this pair:

  

ज्ञेयंयत्तत्प्रवक्ष्यामियज्ज्ञात्वामृतमश्नुते।  
अनादिमत्परंब्रह्मनसत्तन्नासदुच्यते॥ १२ ॥

  

13.13 I shall speak of that which is to be known, by realizing which one attains Immortality. The supreme Brahman is without any beginning. That is called neither being nor non-being.

  

In the commentary Shankara says:यद्धिइन्द्रियगम्यंवस्तुघटादिकम्,तत्अस्तिबुद्ध्यनुगतप्रत्ययविषयंवास्यात्,नास्तिबुद्ध्यनुगतप्रत्ययविषयंवास्यात्।इदंतुज्ञेयम्अतीन्द्रियत्वेनशब्दैकप्रमाणगम्यत्वात्नघटादिवत्उभयबुद्ध्यनुगतप्रत्ययविषयम्इत्यतः‘नसत्तन्नासत्’इतिउच्यते॥

  

That which is perceptible, like a pot, can be either 'sat' (when a pot has been produced, it is spoken of 'the pot is'.) or 'asat' (when the pot is destroyed/or not produced yet, it is spoken of 'it is not.'). Brahman is neither of these since the possibility of 'is' and 'is not' in the above sense does not arise at all.

  

This seems to be the best fit for the MB verses, since it also says:
असच्च सच्चैव च यद्विश्वं *सदसतः परम्* \| It is both 'is' and 'is not' and beyond these two categories.

  

Thus, the existence of a pot and its non-existence, both belong to creation. Brahman is what all is creation. It is beyond these categories too since these categories inhere only 'in' creation; Brahman transcends creation. \[Van Buitenen's translation does not include this segment of the verse: सदसतः परम् in clear terms, as it appears to me.\]

  

Even though the BG 9.19 commentary does mention the cause, unmanifest as effect, is asat, and the effect, manifest, is sat, yet, the negation of absolute non-existence, for asat, is also mentioned. This rules out asat as non-existence of Brahman.

  

  

Prashnopanishat 2.5:

एषोऽग्निस्तपत्येष सूर्य एष पर्जन्यो मघवानेष वायुः।  
एष पृथिवी रयिर्देवःसदसच्चामृतंच यत् ॥ ५ ॥

[भाष्यम्](http://advaitasharada.sringeri.net/display/bhashya/Prashna?page=2&id=PR_C02_V05&hlBhashya=%E0%A4%B8%E0%A4%A6%E0%A4%B8%E0%A4%9A%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%9A%E0%A4%BE#bhashya-PR_C02_V05)

कथम् ? एषः प्राणः अग्निः सन् तपति ज्वलति। तथा एषः सूर्यः सन् प्रकाशते। तथा एषः पर्जन्यः सन् वर्षति। किञ्च मघवान् इन्द्रः सन् प्रजाः पालयति जिघांसत्यसुररक्षांसि। किञ्च, एषः वायुः आवहप्रवहादिभेदः। किञ्च, एषः पृथिवी रयिः देवः सर्वस्य जगतः सत् मूर्तम् असत् अमूर्तं च अमृतं च यत् देवानां स्थितिकारणम् ॥

In the Prashnopanishat too occurs this pair: sat and asat. Here Shankara's commentary is: sat = formed. asat = devoid of form.
