O the highly distinguished Nārāyaṇa! Your questions are extremely difficult \[to answer\]. \[Yet,\] I, being one who delights in the doctrine of Pāṇini, would like to give you the answers.

> लोहिताच्च मणौ सूत्रे स्वार्थे कननुवर्तते। कनं विहाय रक्तार्थे कना > लोहितकस्त्विति।।२।।  
> > 
> > 
> > 

  
प्रायशः प्रत्ययेनार्थोऽङ्गस्येषद्विकृतो मतः। अपवादोऽस्ति त्वर्थे स्वे प्रत्ययो मुनिना हितः॥ २ ॥  
  
It is understood that in most cases, the meaning of the Aṅga (base) is slightly transformed or modified by the Pratyaya (suffix). However, a suffix ordained by the seer Pāṇini in Svārtha (own sense) is an exception. Such a suffix does not modify the meaning of the base.  


> 
> >  style="color:#000;background-color:#fff;font-family:lucida console,sans-serif;font-size:12pt"> >
>  style="color:rgb(0,0,0);font-size:16px;font-family:lucida console,sans-serif;background-color:transparent;font-style:normal"> >
> 
> >  style="color:rgb(0,0,0);font-size:16px;font-family:lucida console,sans-serif;background-color:transparent;font-style:normal"> >
> संशीतिः स्वार्थशब्देऽत्र मणीनां रङ्गभेदतः। माणिक्ये लोहितो > रङ्गस्स्वार्थस्तन्मयतेति वा?।।३।।  
> > 
> > 
> > 

  
इति स्वार्थस्य सिद्धान्तोऽनङ्गार्थविकृतिर्यतः। लोहितात्कन्मणौ स्वार्थे रक्तो लोहितको मणिः॥ ३ ॥  
  
This is the concept of Svārtha (own sense), by which there is no change in the meaning of the Aṅga (base). By the aphorism *lohitānmaṇau* (5-4-30), the suffix *kan* is in Svārtha from the base *lohita* when it means the gem ruby. Therefore *lohitaka* means the red gem, i.e. ruby. One of the meanings of *lohita* is ruby, and when the base has this meaning, the meaning is unchanged by the suffix *kan*.

> > 
> >

पुँल्लिङ्गे हि मणिः प्रोक्तो लोहितिका स्त्र्युदाहृता। लोहितिका मणिर्न स्याद्वर्णेऽनित्ये च कन् हितः॥ ४ ॥  
  
The word *maṇi* has been verily uttered in the masculine gender while *lohitikā* is said to be a woman (i.e. it is feminine). \[Therefore\] *lohitikā* may not mean a *maṇi*, for the suffix *kan* is \[also\] ordained \[from the base *lohita*\] in the sense of a temporary colour by the aphorism *varṇe cānitye* (5-4-31). So by 5-4-31, *lohitikā* means she who is temporarily red as in a woman red-faced from anger. In addition, by the aphorism *rakte* (5-4-32), *kan* is ordained from the base *lohita* in the sense of dyeing. Therefore *lohitikā* also means she who is dyed red, as in a red Sari.

> >

अङ्गं गुणी गुणो प्रत्ययोऽङ्गप्रत्यययोः श्रुतौ। स्वार्थत्वं लोहितेऽङ्गेऽस्ति न मणावर्थबोधने॥ ५ ॥  
  
Of the the Aṅga (base) and the Pratyaya (suffix), the Aṅga is the one endowed \[with a change in meaning by the Pratyaya\], and the Pratyaya endows \[the Aṅga by a change in the latter's meaning\] - these two \[concepts\] are heard. The Svārtha-hood is in the Aṅga *lohita,* it is not in the perception of its meaning as the ruby.

> अयं लोहितकः कोपादियं लोहितिका तथा। स्वार्थता लोहिते वाऽत्र पूरुषे वा > स्त्रियामथ?।।६।।  
> > 
> > 
> > 

  
अतः लोहितकः कोपादतो लोहितिका तथा। स्वार्थताऽङ्गे हि रक्ते सा पूरुषे न स्त्रियां च न॥ ६ ॥  
  
Thus, \[when saying\] he is red from anger (*lohitakaḥ kopāt*), and thus, \[when saying\] she is red from anger (*lohitikā kopāt*), the Svārtha-hood is in the Aṅga standing for red-colour (i.e. *lohita*) , it is neither in the masculine nor in the feminine.

I am neither wise, nor high-minded, nor is my effort \[in understanding Pāṇini\] great. Whatever I know well, is verily borne out of eating the leftovers of my Guru.
