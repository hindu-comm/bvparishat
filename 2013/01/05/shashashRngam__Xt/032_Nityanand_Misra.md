This is not completely true. पद is a परम्परागतसंज्ञा in the case of first defintion (1-4-14 सुप्तिङन्तं पदम्) only and a शास्त्रीयसंज्ञा in case of other three aphorisms (1-4-15 नः क्ये, 1-4-16 सिति च, 1-4-17 स्वादिष्वसर्वनामस्थाने). पद as the शास्त्रीयसंज्ञा is limited to Panini's system and is like specific mathematical notation which has no meaning outside Paninian system. In all the above mentioned Sutras pertaining to पद, any वर्णसमुदाय which satisfies one of the four definitions is intended. In fact many many a time an अङ्ग gets the पद noun in Paninian system. This is clear from forms where जश्त्व takes place by झलां जशोऽन्ते (8-2-39), a rule which operates at end of the पद, before the पदसंहिता between the अङ्ग and the सुप् takes place. An example of such form is षण्णाम् whose derivation is as follows  
  
षष् -> ष्णान्ता षट् (1-1-24) -> षट् संज्ञा -> स्वौजसमौट्छष्टाभ्याम्भिस्ङेभ्याम्भ्यस्ङसिभ्याम्भ्यस्ङसोसाम्ङ्योस्सुप्‌ (4-1-2) -> षष् आम् -> स्वादिष्वसर्वनामस्थाने (1-4-17) -> पद संज्ञा of षष् -> झलां जशोऽन्ते (8-2-39) -> जश्त्व -> स्थानेऽन्तरतमः (1-1-50) -> डत्व -> षड् आम् -> षट्चतुर्भ्यश्च (7-1-55) -> नुट् आगम -> आद्यन्तौ टकितौ (1-1-46) -> आद्यागम -> षड् नुट् आम् -> षड् न् आम् -> ष्टुना ष्टुः (8-4-41) -> न पदान्ताट्टोरनाम् (8-4-42) -> अनाम्नवतिनगरीणामिति वाच्यम् (वार्त्तिक) -> षड् ण् आम् -> यरोऽनुनासिकेऽनुनासिको वा (8-4-45) -> प्रत्यये भाषायां नित्यम् (वार्त्तिक) -> षण् ण् आम् -> षण्णाम्  
  
In the above process, it is the षष् (प्रातिपदिक अङ्ग) which gets the पदसंज्ञा. If this did not happen, we would not see forms like षण्णाम् or षट्सु. If you disagree with the above derivation, please provide the alternate solution.

In Panini's system, अर्थ is not limited to a पद, even धातु, प्रत्यय and प्रातिपदिक have meaning as is evident from Acharya's Sutra अर्थवदधातुरप्रत्ययः प्रातिपदिकम् (1-2-45). But धातु and प्रत्यय are not पद. प्रातिपदिक can be called a पद or a भ only in very specific conditions - one example given above. Therefore it is not an अन्वर्थसंज्ञा in the strict sense.

If you mean अङ्ग by "base", then the above should read "are taken as the base", and not "are not taken as the base". चैत्रः and पचति are mostly not taken as the base. There are exceptions like पचतितराम् or पचतितमाम् where पचति is the base before the घ affixes (तरप्तमपौ घः 1-1-22).

Strongly disagree. Just like the synthetic noun भ, defined in यचि भम् (1-4-18) and used in rules like भस्य (6-4-129), did not lead to any chaos, any synthetic noun in place of पद would be just fine. If Panini said सुप्तिङन्तं फम् instead of सुप्तिङन्तं पदम् (1-4-14) and replaced occurrences of पद by फ in the other Sutras, e.g. समर्थः पदविधिः (2-1-1) into समर्थः फविधिः, et cetera, the end result will not change. A संज्ञा in Panini's system is like a token in lexical analysis of a regular language or variable name in progamming code - the end result is the same no matter what the token or variable name is.

No doubt he is a वैतण्डिकः but from the text you quoted, it appears to me Sriharsa is clarifying Panini's nomenclature rather than refuting it. Here is my attempt at translating the same - your quotes in red, my translation in italics. I fail to see any refutation in the quotes (I do not have the original text or context though). Please explain why you see it as a refutation.  
  
सुप्तिङन्तं पदमिति केचित् , वर्णा विभक्त्यन्ताः पदमित्यन्ये। .... अपशब्देऽपि पदलक्षणं गतमिति। ...  
*As per some (Panini included), that which ends in a सुप् or तिप् is a पद. As per some others, syllables ending in a विभक्ति are a पद. ... Thus, even an अपशब्द has the characteristics of a पद.*  

  
तस्मात् पाणिनिना आचार्येण शब्दसिद्ध्यर्थे पदसंज्ञेयं रुत्वादिविद्ध्यनुरोधेन अपशब्ददशायामन्यैव कृता नदीसंज्ञावत् , न तु लौकिकपदव्यवहारसिद्ध्यर्थम् ।  

*Therefore, in the situation of an अपशब्द, Acharya Panini has coined this पदसंज्ञा in accordance with the rules like रुत्व, similar to other संज्ञाs like the नदीसंज्ञा, and not for the accomplishing the usage of पद in the world.*

  
  
अन्यथा दाक्षीनन्दनोदीरितनदीसंज्ञाप्रत्यभिज्ञायां पाथः प्रार्थयमानः काननस्थलीमलीकाभिमानी भवानीहेत।  

*Otherwise while perceiving the नदीसंज्ञा uttered by the son of Dākṣī (Pāṇini), water being worshiped, a river-goddess in a forest area may be intended.*
