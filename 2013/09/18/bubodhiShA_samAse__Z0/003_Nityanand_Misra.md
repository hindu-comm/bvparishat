“हरौ” is not the विग्रह of अधिहरि. अधिहरि is a नित्यसमास formed by PS 2-1-6. नित्यसमास has either no लौकिकविग्रह, or it has what is called an अस्वपदविग्रह. प्रायेणाविग्रहो नित्यसमासः प्रायेणास्वपदविग्रहो वा (LSK on PS 2-1-6). Here in प्रायेणाविग्रहो, the लौकिकविग्रह is meant, since the अलौकिकविग्रह (grammatical विग्रह) always exists for all समासs.

When looking for समसन, an apples to apples comparison is needed - which means comparing the समास with the स्वपदलौकिकविग्रह or अलौकिकविग्रह. Since “अधिहरि” has neither लौकिकविग्रह nor a स्वपदविग्रह (as it is a नित्यसमास), it can be compared with only the अलौकिकविग्रह to see if there is समसन or not. One cannot compare “अधिहरि” with “हरौ” to say there is no समसन, “हरौ” only shows the meaning of “अधिहरि” but is not the स्वपदलौकिकविग्रह. The अलौकिक विग्रह of अधिहरि = अधिहरि + सुँ (-> लुक्) is “हरि ङि + अधि सुँ (-> लुक्)”. As “अधिहरि + सुँ (-> लुक्)” + has only one विभक्ति (which undergoes लुक् by PS 2-4-82 अव्ययादाप्सुपः) compared to “हरि ङि + अधि सुँ (-> लुक्)” which has two विभक्तिs (one of which undergoes लुक्) - there is definitely समसन in अधिहरि - there is one विभक्ति in place of two, with the first विभक्ति undergoing elision by PS 2-4-71 सुपो धातुप्रातिपदिकयोः following प्रातिपदिक nomenclature by PS 1-2-46 कृत्तद्धितसमासाश्च.

The अस्वपदलौकिकविग्रह can be shown as either हरौ अधिकृत्य = अधिहरि (which was the practice before Bhattoji Diksita, for example see KV on PS 2-1-6 which says स्त्रीष्वधिकृत्य कथा प्रवर्तते अधिस्त्रि) or as हरौ = अधिहरि (which is how Bhattoji Diksita and shows the अस्वपदविग्रह, which LSK follows). However, as mentioned before the comparison of अस्वपदलौकिकविग्रह to समास is not an apples-to-apples comparison, and hence it cannot be used to infer the presence of समसन.

The topic is addressed in detail on pages 18-19 (अव्ययीभावसमासप्रकरणम्) in part four of the भैमी commentary (2007, Fourth edition) on the LSK by पण्डित भीमसेन शास्त्री. The book can be viewed online [here](http://www.scribd.com/doc/75389492/Laghu-Siddhanta-Kaumudi-IV-Bhaimi-Vyakhya-Samas).

> > 

  

In कृष्णसर्पः, both कृष्ण and सर्प words leave their meanings partially (i.e. no independent meaning) and combine to give the special meaning of “black snake.” This is the वृत्ति here. Just like in case of राजपुरुषः, one cannot use adjective “ऋद्धस्य” of “राजन्” to say “ऋद्धस्य राजपुरुषः”, similarly one cannot say “अत्यन्तः कृष्णसर्पः” to mean “a very black snake.” “अत्यन्तः” separately can no longer modify “कृष्ण” only, but will rather modify the “परार्थ” which is “black snake.” This aspect is also addressed in the भैमी commentary (same volume as above, pages 10 and 11 in the केवलसमासप्रकरणम्).

The भैमी is considered by many to be the finest commentary on the LSK, and my Gurudeva recommends the same for self-study.
