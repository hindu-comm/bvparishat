\> here ke in water , sava -corpse drona -crow , pandava- jakle  
  

I believe the more standard form (which I have seen cited in different texts), is  

  
केशवं पतितं दृष्ट्वा पाण्डवाः हर्षनिर्भराः \|  

रुदन्ति कौरवाः सर्वे हा हा केशव केशव \|\|  
  
This is said to be one of the difficult verses of the महाभारत, and is explained like क उदके शवं मृतशरीरं पतितं दृष्ट्वा पाण्डवाः मीनाः हर्षनिर्भराः, रुदन्ति कौरवाः काकाः सर्वे, etc. I don't see how द्रोण would be as appropriate, and perhaps रुदन्ति कौरवाः सर्वे is likewise more apt to set up the conundrum.
