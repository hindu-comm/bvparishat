After going through the very 'fundamental question' in the discussion I felt I have some questions to ask Shri Anil Kumar.

Is physical quantification possible in case of SOUL?  

As this is a question about the SOUL what the modern science speaks of this aspect?

Do they aspect the SOUL as the life principle?

As far as the function of our Karma is concerned it is in the form of latent impressions in the CHITTA.

When we believe in the KARMA theory we have to accept the theory of Rebirth also.  

As the SOUL is invisible and not perceivable so also the KARMA and its effects. No branch of physical science may prove it. Not even the modern branches of psychology have satisfactory answers to this question.  

To understand this in a perfect manner to a possible extent we have to rely upon Upanishadic and Ayurvedic Texts.  

1.Of all the upanishads GARBHOPANISHAD is the best source to explain this subject in a convincing manner on Indian grounds of Knowledge. I shall try to upload Garbhopanishad this evening.,

2. Karma according to Mahabharata always follows the beings in all avasthas and at all times. Here is a beautiful sloka of Mahabharata.

शयनं चानुशेते हि तिष्ठन्तं चानुतिष्ठति।  

अनुधावति धावन्तं कर्म पूर्वकृतं नरम़् ॥

Just as a basic assumption is necessary to solve the Algebraic problems in case of Karma or Atman also a basic assumption that "these two exist beyond any physical quantification" is necessary.  
Here are some more citations from Charaka Samhita which may satisfy the present context proving the invisible effect of Karma.

  

१. भूतैश्चतुर्भिस्सहितस्सुसूक्ष्मे मनोजवो देहमुपैति देहात़् ।

कर्मात्मकत्वान्न तु तस्य दृश्यं दिव्यं विना दर्शनमस्ति रूपम़्॥

               - इति चरकप्रतिसंस्कृते अग्निवेशतन्त्रे शारीरे अध्या.२

२. न शक्यश्चक्षुषा द्रष्टुं देहे सूक्ष्मतमो विभु:।

दृश्यते ज्ञानचक्षुर्भि: तपश्चक्षुर्भिरेव च॥

 - इति सुश्रुतसंहितायां शारीरे ५अध्याये  

३. पञ्चमहाभूतसमवाय: पुरुष:। स एव कर्मपुरुष: चिकित्साधिकृत:।

  

४. सर्वभूतानां कारणमकारणं सत्त्वरजस्तमोलक्षणमष्टरूमखिलस्य जगत: सम्भवहेतुरव्यक्तं नाम। तदेकं बहूनां क्षेत्रज्ञानामधिष्ठानं समुद्र इव औदकानं भावानाम़्।  

आयुर्वेदशास्त्रेषु असर्वगता: क्षेत्रज्ञा: नित्याश्च।

त एते अनुमानग्राह्या: परमसूक्ष्माश्चेतनावन्त:।
