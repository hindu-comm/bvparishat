हीरकम् २ नीलमणिः ३ पद्मरागः ४ मुक्ता ५।  

  

one of the navanidhi-s or treasures of Kubera.

  

So अञ्जनाभं धनञ्जयरथभूषणं धनम् **is the correct intrerpretation of the idea. reflecting the treasure itself is the नीलमणि makes the waiting at the verandah of greedy lords for getting alms futile as explained already by Srimallalitaji.

  

वितर्दिका ---  

  

f\. (said to be fr. √ तृद्) a raised and covered piece of ground in the centre of a house or temple or in the middle of a court-yard , verandah , balcony &c R. Ra1jat. ( L. also °तर्दी , °तर्द्धी and °तर्द्धिका)  

  

The word दुरासिका - the sitting in the outer veranda, of the rich lords looking for their grace of donation is reflected in this सुभाषित attributed toज्ञानानन्त by विद्याकर in his सुभाषितरत्नाकर---

*  
*

*यद् वक्र्त्रं मुहुरीक्षसे न धनिनां ब्रूषे न चाटून् मृषा*

*नैषां गर्वगिरः शृणोषि न पुनः प्रत्याशया धावसि ।*

काले बालतृणानि खादसि सुखं निद्रासि निद्रागमे

तन्मे ब्रूहि कुरङ्ग कुत्र भवता किं नाम तप्तं तपः ॥२२०८॥

  

The same idea of दुरासिका is reflected in the first half of the verse.
