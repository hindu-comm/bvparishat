I did not have any doubts in the first reading with दुरासिका, which is धात्वर्थनिर्देशे ण्वुल्, from the verb आस उपवेशने prefixed with or without गतिसमास with दुर् or प्रादिसमास. Precisely, दुष्ठा आसिका - आसनम्। which makes the picture clear. कपर्दिकायै - वराटिकायै is more open.

  

Anyhow I too do not have any printed source. Hope somebody will check and will find any one of the variants already you have met and the same will be the position as all could be explained in one way or other unless there is any critical edition which is based on original manuscripts readings compared. Even then, excepting grammatical or scribal errors, the descretion of the Editor would be the selected reading and the other variants given to the readers to judge the selection.

  

The idea of दुरासिका is more clear in this verse positively described in the similar expression on the whole even in the locative द्वारि or in compound द्वार- waiting at the door or दुरीश्वर-s.

  

निद्राति स्नाति भुङ्क्ते चलति कचभरं शोषयत्यन्तरास्ते

दीव्यत्यक्षैर्न चायं गदितुमवसरो भूय आयाहि याहि ।

इत्युद्दण्डैः प्रभूणामसकृदधिकृतैर्वारितान् द्वारि दीनान्

अस्मान् पश्याब्धिकन्ये सरसिहरुचामन्तरङ्गैरपाङ्गैः ॥

  

nidraati snaati bhunkte calati kacabharam shoshayatyantaraaste

diivyatyakshairna caayam gaditumavasaro bhuuya aayaahi yaahi \|

ityuddandaih prabhuunaamasakrdadhikrtairvaaritaan dvaari diinaan

asmaan pashyaabdhikanye sarasiharucaamantarangairapaangaih\|\|

  

quoted in *Kuvalayananda* (source not traced).
