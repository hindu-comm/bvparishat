Sorry for not being clear enough in my post. You are correct, the dvitva of त् is by अनचि च (refer the table below). However, धात्त्रंश and other examples are to be understood as derived from the whole Prasang (इको यणचि to यणो मयो द्वे वाच्ये), and not necessarily from the final Varttika in the Prasang. Bhattoji has given full derivation of सुध्युपास्य with four forms, and the rest only as examples at the end of the Prasang, whose full derivation is to be understood.
