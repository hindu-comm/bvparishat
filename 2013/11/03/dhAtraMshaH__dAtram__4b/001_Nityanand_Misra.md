Actually Bhattoji has read two Varttikas together in the commentary on 8-2-23 संयोगान्तस्य लोपः - the first is यणः प्रतिषेधो वाच्यः and the second is यणो मयो द्वे वाच्ये (with the specific interpretation which has यणः in genitive and मयः in ablative case). The following examples are to be understood as examples of the entire Prasanga (beginning with इको यणचि and ending with संयोगान्तस्य लोपः) and not just of the Varttika यणो मयो द्वे वाच्ये.

Especially amongst the examples

-   In case of सुध्युपास्य/सुद्ध्युपास्य/सुध्य्युपास्य/सुद्ध्युपास्य -
    four forms are Sadhu because both Varttikas apply optionally in the
    case सुध्य् उपास्य. This is clearly stated by Bhattoji.  
-   In case of धात्रंश/धात्त्रंश - two forms are Sadhu as the first
    Varttika applies optionally (see A2 below). The second does not
    apply as duplication of र् is not to be understood due to its
    निमित्तत्व in अचो रहाभ्यां द्वे  
-   In case of लाकृतिः - only one form is Sadhu since none of the
    Varttika applies.

A2: Commentaries differ here as you have already shown. However, the authority here is Patanjali who under 8-4-47 अनचि च clearly lists both वाक् and वाक्क् as correct forms. If दीर्घादाचार्याणाम्‌ was नित्य, second form would not have been Sadhu. Patanjali says वाक्क् is Sadhu, which proves that 8-4-52 दीर्घादाचार्याणाम् is not नित्य as per Bhashyakara. Therefore, even though various commentators may differ on scope of दीर्घादाचार्याणाम्‌, it is to be interpreted as optional ("In the view of \[some\] Acharyas") by those who take Bhashyakara as Pramana.

Commentaries are to be respected, but when the interpretation in a commentary contradicts Bhashya, the interpretation is to be discarded - so says my Guru.
