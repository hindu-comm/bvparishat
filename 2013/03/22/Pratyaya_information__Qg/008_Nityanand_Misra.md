If you understand Hindi, you may refer the third volume of **Aṣṭādhyāyī Sahajabodha**(Kṛdantaprakaraṇam) by Mahāmahopādhyāyā Mātājī Puṣpā Dīkṣita, arguably one of the foremost scholars of Sanskrit grammar in the world today. The work is available on scribd -

  

<http://www.scribd.com/doc/220668527/Astadhyayi-Sahajabodha-III-Dr-Pushpa-Dixit>  

  

Mātājī was trained by several stalwarts, including AbhinavapāṇiniĀcāryaRāmaprasāda Tripāṭhī of Vārāṇasī, Guru of many current day authorities on Sanskrit grammar including Dr. Rāma Nātha Śarmā (author of English translations of Aṣṭādhyāyī and Kāśikā) and Jagadguru Rāmānandācārya Svāmī Rāmabhadrācārya (author of Sanskrit commentaries on Aṣṭādhyāyī inGadya and Padya).
