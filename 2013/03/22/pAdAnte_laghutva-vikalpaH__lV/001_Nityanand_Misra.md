Wrong. Awadhi prosody has different rules than Sanskrit, Sanskrit rules are not applicable to Awadhi Dohas. In Awadhi, a Laghu before a Samyukta in a different word is not considered Guru in Prakrit. E.g.  
  
E.g. राम ब्रह्म परमारथ रूपा from the RCM must parse into 16 instants (half of a chaupai). The म in राम is Laghu even though followed by a Samyukta ब्र as it is a different word. Otherwise this will give you 17 instants and Tulsidas's metre would break. RCM is replete with many such examples - please see <https://sites.google.com/site/nmisra/metresinrcm>**  
  
**संयुक्ताक्षर (दो या अधिक व्यञ्जन जिनके बीच कोई स्वर न हो यथा क्य, ज्ञ, क्ष, र्त्स्न्य, इत्यादि) के पहले आनेवाले लघुवर्ण को गुरु माना जाता है। इसके कुछ अपवाद हैं जैसे प्र ह्र ब्र और क्र। प्राकृत में यह तभी होता है जब एक पहले का वर्ण और संयुक्ताक्षर एक ही शब्द में आएँ। यथा मायामनुष्यं में नु गुरु है (संस्कृत), परन्तु जब ते राम ब्याहि घर आए (२.१.१) में राम का म लघु है (प्राकृत, भिन्न शब्द)।  
  
The first and third quarters of of Doha have 13 instants which must parse as 6-4-3. चन्दन (4) विष (2) व्यापत (4) नहीं (3) so that चन्दन विष is 6 instants, व्यापत is 4 and नहीं is 3. Doha also has involved rules of त्रिकल (segments of three instants) and दुकल (segments of two instants) and it is not that easy. If you take विष as three instants then 6-4-3 parsing would break, breaking the rhytm of the singer or reciter.  
  
Best way to learn Awadhi prosody is to learn how to sing the RCM verses.
