Suppose we take प्रौढमनोरमा sentences for instance: चुलुम्पादीनामिति वक्ष्यते (I am not remembering whether शब्दरत्न going into the details of this or not) here we will get the Varttika: कास्यनेकाचइति वक्तव्यं चुलुम्पाद्यर्थम् etc.  

Or आचारादप्रधानत्वाल्लोपश्च बलवत्तरः। ( प्रौढमनोरमा) whereas we get separate Vaarttikas in the Mahabhashya while the Sivasutra expalnations in the Paspasa as: आचारात् then its explanation and followed by अप्रधानत्वात् explanation etc. So whether you follow the Vaiyakaranasiddhantakaumudi, Laghusidhantakaumudi orders of corresponding sutras it will be good to give Sivasutra numbers (if applicable) or Ashtadhyayi order numbers to the Mahbhashya quotations and corresponding commentary hints, partial notes or the verses given in Kashika etc. which will be useful to follow. Otherwise, it will not complete in their sense, even Linganusasanam also will play the roles. For cross references we can have many books reference order.  

Laghusidhantakaumudi does not cover all sutras, but Vaiyakaranasiddhantakaumudi covers all of it.

In case of Mahabhashya, ajabhakshitam deprives from those sutras for explanation.

I am worried about the popularity of the प्रौढमनोरमा and शब्दरत्न, especially the portions after the Avyayibhavanta is not al all available in the market. Chowkhamba groups stopped its printing of what is called the Uttaraardha even with the Hindi Translation for marketing.  

So, I think citing Panini sutras, Vaiyakaranasiddhantakaumudi order and other commentary text reference order all are useful.
