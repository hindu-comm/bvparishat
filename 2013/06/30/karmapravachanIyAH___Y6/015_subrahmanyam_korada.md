1. I have still not understood why theअनीयर्‌प्रत्ययis to be related to the past? (कर्मप्रवचनीयःइतिकर्म प्रोक्तवन्तः?)

earlier the same word (in some other example)expressed क्रिया - ex प्रादेशं विलिखति - here वि means विमाय (having measured)

but now the क प्र does not express any क्रिया - this is what is meant by भूते अनीयर्\|

  

2. What is retiring(विनिवर्तनम्‌)of aक्रिया?

--that means it is not being used - it has disappeared after establishing the संबन्ध between two Karakas --

  

ex. राज्ञः पुरुषः = राजसंबन्धी पुरुषः , it means there is some संबन्ध - स्वस्वामिभावरूपः between राजा and पुरुष - it is so because राजा पुरुषं पोषति। Here the क्रिया - पोषति, having generated the said संबन्ध , disappeared/retired from प्रयोग ।

  

यत्र क्रिया अश्रूयमाणा तत्रापि स्वस्वामिसंबन्धः अवगम्यते ।

  

It is between two Karakas .

  

Now let us see a संबन्ध between a क्रिया and a कारक --

  

मातुः स्मरति = मातरं स्मरति इत्यर्थः। Here either जन्यजनकभाव or निमित्तनिमित्तिभाव is the संबन्ध between माता and स्मरणम् ।

But if the क्रिया -स्मरति , is आश्रूयमाणा , ie not employed , then the said संबन्ध cannot be understood - so the क्रिया should not retire.

What is a preposition ? -- in London (English preposition) - London ni (Japanese post position)

Generally the term is used to denote विभक्त्यर्थ । So it is not advisable to go for translation --

आ मुक्तेः संसारः - here आ means प्राक्

शकल्यस्य संहिताम् अनु प्रावर्षत् - here अनु is द्योतक of लक्ष्यलक्षणभावसंबन्ध ।

In such cases kp cannot be called either a preposition or adverb - the स्वभाव of Sanskrit is different .

(answer to 'a' - no)

-- this kp does not suggest any संबन्ध(such as लक्ष्यलक्षणभाव) , nor does it denote any संबन्ध ,

nor does it get any क्रिया thru आक्षेप -- in the example प्रादेशं विलिखति - the वि would fetch/attract मानधातु - विमाय - although it(मानधातु) is not there in the example - this is क्रियाक्षेप।

संबन्धस्य तु भेदकः = it will indicate the संबन्ध।

a. (Page 291, para 3) It is in this sense that Punyaraja (VP, II, 199:317) defines KP as a cause (hetu) which distinguishes a particular relatum (sambandhavisesa) additionally brought (upajanita) to a particular action (kriyavisesa). (Page 291, para 3)



Kindly explain this with reference to VP-199.

---199स चोपजातः संबन्धो विनिवृत्ते क्रियापदे ।

   कर्मप्रवचनीयेन तत्र तत्र नियम्यते ॥

one may take (since there is तृतीया - कर्मप्रवचनेन) kp as a हेतु but RN Sharma's commentary does not express the actual meaning of the verse - also what and where is this उपजनित ? which goes against the original उपजात ?

Meaning - the relation that is born, while the क्रिया retired , is fixed in respective क्रिया by kp.



b. (Page 292, para 2) This special relationship is recognised as hetuhetumadbhava 'the relationship between the cause and the causal agent.'



How is it different from karyakaranabhava?

--

roughly both are the same here - संहितापठनम् कारणम् , वर्षरूपकार्यस्य ।

  

c. (Page 292, para 2)The terms nipata and karmapravacaniya will now share the samjnasamavesa by themselves.



What is samjnasamavesa?



There is one condition - आकडारादेका स्ंज्ञा(1-4-1) - till 'कडाराः कर्मधारये’(2-2-38) there should be only one संज्ञा । As a result the kp cannot have the other संज्ञा - निपातः (प्राग्रीश्वरान्निपाताः1-4-56) , simultaneously --

  

having both the संज्ञs simultaneously in a single thing is संज्ञासमावेश।

संज्ञयोः, समावेशः=सहावस्थानम्
