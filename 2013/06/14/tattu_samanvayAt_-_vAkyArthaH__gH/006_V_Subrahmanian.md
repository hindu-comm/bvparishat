Namaste Sir,  
  
This exercise was very interesting. Before seeing your post, I was discussing this matter with a scholar and he, appreciating your question, pointed out the following sentence from the मीमांसापरिभाषा (p.22 of the nirnayasagar edition):  
  
// तत्र निन्दार्थवादस्य विेधेयान्यनिन्दाद्वारा विधेयप्राशस्त्यपरत्वम् । ’अश्रुजम्..’ इत्यादिरजतनिन्दाद्वारा च विधेयभूतरजतदाननिषेधप्राशस्त्यपरत्वे विरोधाभावात् । //  
  
We have 'yama' in patanjala system, shama, dama, uparati, titikShA in vedanta where actually there is a pratiShedha (asteyam, satyam (asatyatyAgaH), aparigrahaH, ahimsA), antarindriya, bahirindriya nigrahaH, etc. All these 'don'ts' are actually the subject matter of great appreciation, praise, for these exercises are extolled in the shAstra and a person practicing these 'don'ts' is the uttamAdhikArin, dhIraH.  
  
पराञ्चि खानि व्यतृणत्स्वस्व्यंभूस्तस्मात्पराङ्पश्यति नान्तरान्तरात्मन् ।  
कश्चिद्धीरः प्रत्यगात्मानमैक्षदावृत्तचक्षुरमृतत्वमिच्छन् ॥ (कठोपनिषत् )
