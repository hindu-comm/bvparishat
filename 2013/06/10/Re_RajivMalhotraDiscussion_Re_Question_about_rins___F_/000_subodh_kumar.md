Sir,

I am extremely thankful to you for correcting my misapprehension about RIN in Vedas. You are absolutely correct in referring to Rig Ved 6.61.1. This mantra very significantly points out how Saraswati enlightens the mind her ward to be conscious and recognise the debts he owes in life and repay them .

The poetical construction of the Ved mantra is very beautiful.

"इयमददाद्रभसमृण्च्युतं दिवोदासं वध्र्श्वाय दाशुषे \|या शश्चन्तमाचखादावसं पणि ता ते दात्राणि तविषा सरस्वति \|\| ऋ.6.61.1

  

If today we are unable to recognise the bounties showered on us by Nature, our teachers ,our parents by our ungrateful irresponsible conduct only mother wisdom of Saraswati can bring about the change by providing us with enlightened perception to be grateful of the Environment, our elders teachers parents and the entire society for the debts we owe to them and in our turn perform our duty to repay those debts by proper behaviour towards all of them.

> > There is one indirect reference to the concept of pitṛṛņa in the > Sarasvatī sūkta of Ŗgveda (6:61.1), which refers to the \`gift' made > by the River Goddess Sarasvatī to King Vadhryaśva. According to > Skandasvāmi (he wrote a commentary on the Nirukta), Vadhryaśva was > denied entry to heaven because he had no son. He then offered worship > to Sarasvatī and by her grace he had a son who was named Divodāsa. > King Vadhryashva thereafter could enter heaven. Divodāsa is therefore > described in this sūkta as ṛņacyuta (ṛņavimocaka) i.e. one who > delivered \[his father\] from the debt \[ṛņa\].
