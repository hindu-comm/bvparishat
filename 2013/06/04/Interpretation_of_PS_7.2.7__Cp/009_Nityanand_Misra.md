But in the extract from शेखर above, नागेश contradicts काशिका, कौमुदी and your earlier interpretation (mine too) by saying हलादि applies to अत् by considering आदि as पूर्ववृत्ति.  
  
One of the two interpretations is wrong. I believe नागेश is on the wrong foot here.  
  
The two interpretations will lead to different forms from इजादि अनेकाच् धातु which have अकार as the second (or later) अच्. Such धातुs are very rare in the धातुपाठ (maybe नागेश had only the नामधातुs in mind?). I could find only one -  
  

उध्रस् उञ्छे (धा.पा. 1524) – उध्रस्नाति  

As per काशिका/कौमुदी's interpretation of 7-2-2, the अकार after रेफ will not take the वैकल्पिक वृद्धि, and so the form should be औध्रसीत् (similar प्रक्रिया as अकणीत्, with आट् आगम instead of अट् आगम and the additional rule ६-१-८७ आटश्च accounting for the वृद्धि एकादेश in case of आ + उध्रसीत्).  
  
As per नागेश's interpretation of 7-2-2, since the the अकार after रेफ follows a हल्, the वैकल्पिक वृद्धि निषेध will be optional even though the अङ्ग is अजादि. Then two forms will be generated - औध्रसीत्/औध्रासीत्  
  
So it all comes down to औध्रासीत् - is this form seen in any शिष्टप्रयोग? I could not trace any use on Google.  
  
The editions of धातुरत्नाकर and माधवीयधातुवृत्ति I refer do not have this root. The बृहद्धातुरूपावली gives only औध्रसीत्. Would be interesting to know if other DHatu books have this root.
