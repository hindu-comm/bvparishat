The अङ्ग includes everything between the मूलप्रकृति (धातु/प्रातिपदिक) and प्रत्यय in consideration, including any अन्तागम to मूलप्रकृति, विकरणs (शप्, श्ना, श्नम्, etc), any additional प्रत्ययs introduced between मूलप्रकृति and प्रत्यय (e.g. between a धातु and तिङ्, additional suffixes like णिच्, सन्, यङ्, च्लि may be introduced) and both आद्यागम and अन्तागम of the additional प्रत्ययs (e.g. वलादि इट्).  
  
However, the अङ्ग does not include आद्यागम to the मूलप्रकृति, since by definition it is before the मूलप्रकृति, and not between मूलप्रकृति and प्रत्यय.  
  
This becomes clear when one considers the complete प्रक्रिया of अकणीत्/अकाणीत्. if the definition of अङ्ग included the आद्यागम to धातु (अट्), then the अनिष्ट form आकणीत् would be ,generated.  
  
The विवक्षा is कण्, कर्तरि प्रयोग, सामान्यभूत, प्रथमपुरुष, एकवचन. First the प्रक्रिया of the two साधु forms (I am showing the main steps only) - it does not matter whether अट् is introduced earlier or later into the प्रक्रिया, as वृद्धि of the अङ्ग is अन्तरङ्ग and will happen before the आगम combines with the अङ्ग.

The इष्ट and अनिष्ट illustrate that the हलादि अङ्ग does not become अजादि (which we know by definition) on introduction of the आद्यागम अट्, and an undesired side-effect if it were to become so.
