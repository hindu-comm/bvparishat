Not recognizing the Brāhmaṇas as Veda is an Arya Samaji tenet and practice unknown to Indian tradition at least since Patañjali who recommends *sarahsya* Vedic study that includes the Upanişads. The Śaunakīya Atharvaveda Saṃhitā respectfully utters the name *jyeṣṭháṃ *(*y**é*) *br**āhmaṇaṃ *(*vidús*) (vs.10.7.17) ‘higher Brāhmaṇa’ meaning the Upanişads. (see p.xxi Introd. Vol.3 Paippalāda-Saṃhitā,2011) That means recognition as authority by the Saṃhitā itself. ‘Derecognition’ against so ancient a tradition is unacceptable.
