My compliments to Sri Misra for a most ingenious explanation of the Sloka. What is all the more commendable is that in the first pAda, it isSriramavatara which is referred to; Srikrishnavatara, in the second pAda; and Srimahavishnu as antaryAmin in the third pAda- in effectcovering the two most important avataras and one ofthe most important gunas.Also the name Vishnu is most appropriate in the fourth quarter, as the earlier three descriptions become explanations of the omnipresence of Srimannarayana as Vishnu. What is most impressive is the familiarity of the vimarSaka with Ramayana that he could pick the correct Sloka out of 24,000. Evenmore astounding is the reference to MedinIkoSa. The exactverse relied upon is given below, as a tribute tothe original scholarship of Sri Misra:



तश्चौरामृतपुच्छेषु क्रोडे म्लेच्छे च कुत्रजित् ।

अपुमांस्तरणे पुण्ये कथितः शब्दवेदिभिः ॥



Sri Misra's command over VyakaraNa is more well known; still to use तसु उपक्षये (माधवीयधातुवृत्तिः gives a variant - "प्रक्षेपे इति मैत्रेयः" - but I am sure the desired meaning can be teased with even that reading) isquite anintellectual feat. Overall an astounding example of scholarship.

It is notvery important -for the main point is that we have a brilliant and original explanation ofa difficult verse- but it bears a clarification.My original statement was:



"I believe that if we follow the path pointed out by him, every interpretation of the verse would be similarly defective."



"Similarly" was interpreted as "with sapekshatvadosha". It is one interpretation. However, another interpretation - and my intent - easily obtains: "शास्त्रोक्तरीत्या", by the application of other Sastras. That this is indeed so becomes clear, by the phrase "Let me explain" that immediately follows introducingmy reasons given at length later. Linking up the "dosha" unearthed by Sri Misra to the desire of Sri Sharma to have a defect-free interpretation, I discussed the various doshas as per Alankara Sastra and ended with the assertion that only Srimannarayna is defect-free. If Iintended to limit myself to sapekshatva, there would be no need for me to discuss the other doshas and in fact extend defectiveness to all states of all entities bar the Purushottama. So,I reiterate my stand - everyinterpretation of the verse (by the way the verse itself has been changed; but I do not belabour that point because if one sufficiently exerts oneself, even that reading of thethird padacan be made to mean Vishnu) would be similarly defective, if finding defects is to be our aim. However, we should not search for defects, for if we do,they can be found everywhere, except in Srimannarayana. As to whatdefect is there in Sri Misra's interpretation, it has been made clear by Sri Misra himself (especially, mark the words - "शब्दबलात्कारश्चानिवार्य एव", "तं विना न सम्भवेत्" - in the same vein as what I asserted).



प्रश्नः - कथमत्र द्रविडप्राणायामः प्रस्तूयते शब्दबलात्कारश्च क्रियते?  
उत्तरम् - अत्रभवन्तः प्रसङ्गं न जानन्ति. इदं पद्यं चित्रप्रकरणे "कूटानि" विभागे विद्यते. कूटपद्यविषये द्रविडप्राणायामः शब्दबलात्कारश्चानिवार्य एव. प्रथमे कूटपद्येऽपि "केशवं पतितं दृष्ट्वा" इत्यत्र "केशवं" इत्यस्य "जले मृतकं" इतयर्थोऽपि द्रविडप्राणायामसहितशब्दबलात्कार एव. तं विना पद्यस्यार्थः न सम्भवेदिति.  
  
As to what is the dosha in such text-torturing as per Alankarasastra, it is best not discussed, for we ought to celebrate gunas, not berate doshas. The celebration of guna in the present context is this: Till the present interpretation, most of the explanations on BVPinvolved sapekshatva (which makes the particular element 'ripu' in the compound 'ripugarbha' in the third pada, unfit, असमर्थ, for compounding) - Sri Misra came with an explanation without involving sapekshatva, and that is the guna. As he rightly said, whether in his approach (or in the earlier approaches), the round-about-nessis pretty much inevitable.
