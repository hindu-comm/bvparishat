Yes, it is the Natyasastra refers Pingala as on of Bharata's sons:

कौत्सं ताण्डायनिं चैव पिङ्गलं चित्रकं तथा। बन्धुलं भल्लकं चैव मुष्ठिकं सैन्धवायनम्।।NS.१.२९।।

But the Agnipurana gives many references on the term pingala having different contextual meanings. The proper name Pingala is here in the following:

मतङ्गः पिङ्गलश्चैव हरो वै धातृसञ्ज्ञकः। शङ्कुकर्णो विधानश्च श्रीकण्ठश्चन्द्रशेखरः॥Agni Purana. 85. 12॥  

Here Pingala appears to be the synonym of the Lord Agni on the context of the Pratishthakalashodhana.  

But how do we correlate the term pingala as the author of the Chandasshastra?

<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pingala>

I hope Dr. P. Ramanujan of CDAC Bangalore, who worked on the Vedic Metres, can comment on this authentically.

Now, the Natyashastra's Pingala, the Agnipurana's Pingala, the Chandasshastra's Pingala are really confusing. Only the historians can say according to the composition periods of Ch.Sh., NSh. and Ag.Pu etc.  

You may search in Google if you want the verse quotations etc. with different case endings of पिङ्गल in Devanagari, like: पिङ्गलं, पिङ्गलस्य, पिङ्गले, पिङ्गलात् etc. Here is an example with पिङ्गलेन:   

छन्दस्तु भगवता **पिङ्गलेन** अष्टाध्याय्यात्मकं प्रणीतं is from the Bhattadipika.
