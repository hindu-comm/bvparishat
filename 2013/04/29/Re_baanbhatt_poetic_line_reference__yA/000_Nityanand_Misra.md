I read about a legend which goes that when बाण knew he would die soon, Kadambari was unfinished. He called his two sons, both poets, to decide who will carry forward his work. When asked to describe a tree devoid of leaves which Bana could see from his deathbed, one of the sons said शुष्को वृक्षस्तिष्ठत्यग्रे and the other said नीरसतरुरिह विलसति पुरतः. Bana decided in favour of the son who spoke later. You may refer Three Novels from Ancient India: Dandin's Dashakumāracharita, Subandhu's Vāsavadattā, Bānabhatta's Kādambarī (1982) by Vishwanath S. Naravane, ISBN 9780706913477 for more on this.

  

In my childhood, I heard the same narrative from a Samskrita scholar but with बाण and his two sons replaced by विक्रामादित्य, वैय्याकरण and कालिदास where the वैय्याकरण insisted that he was a greater scholar than कालिदास and

  

I do not know where the legends originate from.
