Sir,

The technical terminology attributed to any particular branch of study is applicable to that context, as the terms have many meanings contextually. If we take the term अन्तरम् a neuter term:  

अन्तं राति ददाति इति अन्तरम्। अन्त+रा+कः+अम्। उपपदसमासः।  

Amara suggests the meanings: अवकाशः। अवधिः। परिधानम्। अन्तर्धानम्। भेदः। तादर्थ्यं। अवसरः। मध्यम्। अन्तरात्मा। छिद्रम्। आत्मीयः। विना। and बहिः। where we are not seeing the meaning सदृशम् required for the rule: 1.1.50.

At the same time, a commentator to Amara named Bharata suggests:

सदृशे यथा- हकारस्य घकारोऽन्तरतमः। Here he takes the combination of अन्तर and तमप् to have the meaning सदृशम् to attribute आदेशः।  

We have the lexicon-Medini's meaning suggestion: सदृशम्

Panini uses the term अन्तरम् in another rule: 1.1.36 अन्तरं बहिर्योगोपसंव्यानयोः।

Here two types of meanings are to be suggested for the term अन्तर:  

1\. बहिर्योगे-अन्तरे गृहाः, अन्तराः गृहाः। नगरबाह्याश्चाण्डालादिगृहा उच्यन्ते। Meaning: "outskirts of city". (Not mentioned by Amara).

The Balamanorama says: उपसंव्यानम्=अन्तरीयं वस्त्रम्।  

2\. उपसंव्याने-अन्तरे शाटकाः, अन्तराः शाटकाः। उपसंव्यानं परिधानीयम् उच्यते, न प्रावरणीयम्। Meaning: "under garment" (Mentioned by Amara)

But in the case of the rule: 1.1.50, neither of these meanings are required at all. In the Mahabhashya, whenever he uses आन्तर्यम्, (read MB of the rule: 1.1.50) the meanings: "one of the same class" "one related to", "one resembles to" are intended, although the other lexical suggestions are sidelined for the different contexts. In the case of a substitution: i.e. हकारस्य घकारोऽन्तरतमः। with the rule: 1.1.50, apart from any lexical suggestions, the meaning of सदृशतम "the most resembling syllable to the substituent is to be substituted with." is dominant in the context of the particular system of study. So, the number of lexical meaning suggestions has nothing to do with.  

More specifically, the Mahabhashya says: यत्र अनेकविधं आन्तर्यं तत्र स्थानतः एव आन्तर्यं बलीयः यथा स्यात्। So, the dominance among the resemblances for the locations of utterance of syllables has been preferred.  

And the Medini's view also from this grammatical point of view. Bharata also suggested accordingly: सदृशे यथा- हकारस्य घकारोऽन्तरतमः। The location of pronunciation of the syllables ह and घ (the क-group) is most homogeneous (अन्तरतम) which is not necessarily to be accommodated in general lexicons rather than a grammatical lexicon.

Regards,

Narayanan
