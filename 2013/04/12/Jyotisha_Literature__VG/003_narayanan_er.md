Respected Professor Iyengar,

I agree that the etymology of the term: Lagadha is not relevant to the context, not because of it's being a proper name only. It has also nothing to do with the queries of Dr. Mishra. Personally I have an inclination to grammar and etymology, and as far as Sanskrit though very little I understand, most of the proper names apart from nouns are also deserved for derivation. No terminology should be left underived. As we had been taught by the way:

नाम च धातुजमाह निरुक्ते व्याकरणे शकटस्य च तोकम्।  
यन्न पदार्थविशेषसमुत्थं प्रत्ययतः प्रकृतेश्च तदूह्यम्।।

(Noun is from root: says the Nirukta and so says Sakatayana grammar. If something is not revealed from any peculiar meaning, then guess from root and suffix). If we say नामन् it covers nouns, all types of names including proper names, place names, number names etc. and all are derivable. Roots and suffices are guessed, the terms popular first are grammatically justified then. By the way we justified the terms like: Agastya=अगं विन्ध्यं स्त्यायति स्तभ्नाति वा। स्त्यै संघाते। आतोऽनुपसर्गे इति कः।, Pulastya=पुल महत्त्वे। क्विप्। पुलं महत्त्वं स्त्यायति स्तभ्नाति वा। स्त्यै संघाते। आतोऽनुपसर्गे इति कः। Pulinda=पुल महत्त्वे। कुणिपुल्योः किन्दच्। उणा० ४। ८५। and then the term (proper name-Varahamihira) Varaha=वरान् आहन्ति इति दुर्गः। कृत्० ४। ३। ४९। हन् + डः। then Mihira=मेहयति सेचयति मेषजलेन भूमिमिति। मिह सेचने। इषिदिमुदिस्विदिच्छिदिभिदिमन्दिचन्दितिमिमिहीति। उणा० १। ५२। किरच्। सूर्यः।

At the same time it should not be wise to derive the non-Sanskrit proper names in the way of Sanskrit derivations. But I thought that as Lagadha being an ancient name like Agastya, Pulastya, Pulinda and Varahamihira etc. could be derivable. Such derived terms are supposed to take नामन् status after being a praatipadika and take the nominal suffix, which is essential in Sanskrit noun inflexion for 7X3=21 forms, having endings, gender, case and number. The process to the stem status is simple:

1\. Root \> Group Infix \> Verbal affix \> Verb  

2\. Verb \> Primary affix \> Stem \> Nominal affix \> Noun/Name

Due to this approved designed process it is true that all Nouns/Names are derived from Roots.  

Thanks and regards,

Narayanan
