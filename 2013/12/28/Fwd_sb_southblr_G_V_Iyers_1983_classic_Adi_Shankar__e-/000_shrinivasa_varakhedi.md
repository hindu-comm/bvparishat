> > I recently watched a digitally restored 1983 classic Samskrtam film by > the late G. V. Iyer, "Adi Shankaracharya" on DVD. Published in this > format by the National Film Development Corporation (NFDC), the film > is visually very sharp. Excellent photography, artistically pleasing > narration, and brilliant casting make this a delight to watch. (I am > not sure if this is the first-ever Samskrtam film, or the latest, or > only.)  
>   
> Narrating the story of one of India's foremost thinkers and > integrators who, in the short span of 32 years, achieved so much is a > daunting task. This is done very well and in Samskrtam. The English > subtitles are barely adequate (and too slipshod at some points) but > still reasonably allow one to gather the gist of the dialogue.  
>   
> Sarvadaman D. Banerjee as the adult Shankaracharya is a perfect fit > for the role. Of course, the original was probably somewhat > darker-complexioned and not quite so well-built. :)  
>   
> Particularly well-handled are the 'miraculous' stories (legends) of > Shankaracharya's life. The episode of the chaandaala obstructing the > path of the Acharya is one of the most sublime sequences in the > film.  
>   
> Music direction, as was the practice with G V Iyer, is by that > redoubtable genius Dr M Balamuralikrishna.  
>   
> Though the singing is superb, there are some glaring mistakes in the > words of some of the hymns (example: the 3rd line of the 1st verse of > the gorgeous hymn, thotakaashtakam - when the dumb unlettered > youngster is moved to mellifluous poetry at the joy of meeting his > guru, Shankaracharya). How such mistakes stayed in the film is a > mystery to me, but they do jar.  
>   
> At a mere Rs. 199 (paise nil!), this DVD is very accurately labeled > "collector's edition" on the package. This is a great gift to give > people - inexpensive but a treasure of our culture and art.
