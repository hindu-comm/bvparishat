Namo Gītāyai



Today is Gītā Jayantī, Vikrama 2070. Śrī Tulasī Pīṭha Sevā Nyāsa, Chitrakoot, is pleased to release the second edition of the book **श्रीगीतातात्पर्य **(**Śrīgītātātparya**) by our Gurudeva, Jagadguru Rāmānandācārya Svāmī Rāmabhadrācārya.



The release of this second edition comes exactly 28 years after the release of the first edition of the work, which happened on the day of Gītā Jayantī, Vikrama 2042 (December 22, 1985). At the time of publication of the first edition, Gurudeva was a Virakta Rāmanandī Sādhu known as Prajñācakṣu Ācārya Rāmabhadradāsa, and the first edition was published with this name.



The book is free to download and read, and a copy is attached (the work can also be downloaded from [this link](http://jagadgururambhadracharya.org/works/sgt/JR2013Srigitatatparya.pdf)). Due to lack of time, we have not been able to provide a Unicode HTML version, but it will be available soon on our official website. A print version of this edition is expected to be out next year.

***About Śrīgītātātparya***

  

Śrīgītātātparya is a 40,000-word essay in Hindi, with small Saṁskṛta portions interspersed between. The essay was spontaneously dictated by Gurudeva over three days in August 1985. The work was published by the Shri Raghav Sahitya Prakashan Nidhi, Haridwar, in the same year. The book was released in December 1985. All the 2,000 copies published were sold out in the next few years and the work subsequently became unavailable. In 1988, Gurudeva was anointed as Jagadguru Rāmānandācārya and he later authored a 1,200-page bilingual commentary in Saṁskṛta and Hindi on the Bhagavadgītā, titled Śrīmadbhagavadgītāsu Śrīrāghavakṛpābhāṣyam, which was published in 1998. After the publication of the comprehensive commentary, the older minor work Śrīgītātātparya was slowly forgotten. We obtained an old and damaged copy of the book earlier this year, and digitized it for republication. Gurudeva heard some extracts of the book he had composed 28 years ago, and added some small portions with new explanations and Bhāvas. The book was then typeset using XeLaTeX, and the same being released today.

  

The essay is divided into three parts called Unmeṣas. The first Unmeṣa arrives at Śaraṇāgati as the Tātparya (essence) of the Bhagavadgītā using the evidence of the six indicator marks (Ṣaḍlinga - Upakramopasaṁhāra, Abhyāsa, Apūrvatā, Phala, Arthavāda and Upapatti) of the Mīmāṁsakas. The second Unmeṣa arrives at Śaraṇāgati as the Tātparya of all the 18 chapters of the Bhagavadgītā. The third Unmeṣa is a critique of Tātparyas different from Śaraṇāgati by various philosophers, political leaders, and saints.

***Some features of the work***

-   Refined, erudite, poetic and at the same time easy-to-understand
    language, in both Saṁskṛta and Prākrṭa (Hindi) portions -   Mellifluous poetic compositions in Saṁskṛta and Prākrṭa interspersed
    in between the prose portions -   Many original Yuktis (arguments) supported by various scriptures and
    arguments -   In-depth explanations of many Saṁskṛta words, concepts, and phrases
    using astonishing Vyutpattis and Paninian processes -   Strong emotion of Bhaktirasa coming from and resulting in the
    Bhāvātireka of a Bhagavadbhakta -   Around 600 citations drawing from more than 75 scriptures and works
    of Sanātana Dharma, starting from Ṛgveda till works of the 20th
    century -   Review of interpretations and views on the Bhagavadgītā from many
    different commentaries and works authored by scholars and saints of
    various traditions -   Praise by Jagadgurus of three different Vedānta schools -   Praise and benediction by the Parama Guru (Guru's Guru) and the
    Sadguru of the author -   Introduction by the Vyākaraṇa Guru of the author
