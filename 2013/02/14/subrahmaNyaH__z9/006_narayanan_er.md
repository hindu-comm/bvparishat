Dear Shri. Bhattacharya,  
The term सुब्रह्मण्यः is popular with Somayaaga (the plant juice ingredients of soma-haoma prefer to havis-ghee is dominant here). सुब्रह्मण्यः is the reciter of the Saaman chantings when the Agnishtoma is to be performed. It is used in masculine gender.  
अग्नीनां स्तोम अग्निष्टोमः। तत्र साममन्त्रकर्तृषु उद्गातृगणे उद्गाता, प्रस्तोता, प्रतिहर्ता, सुब्रह्मण्यः।  
Among the Udgaatr groups of the Saamavedins सुब्रह्मण्यः is the fourth reciter in order. The others are Udgaataa, Prastotaa and Pratihartaa are in order.  
Now we have the term in feminine gender too: सुब्रह्मण्या। a particular recitation of certain mantras by the उद्गातृ priests. Panini, the grammarian, puts an aphorism: न सुब्रह्मण्यायां स्वरितस्य तूदात्तः (1.2.37) as he uses the term in feminine gender. He denies the Vedic optional monotone and the Udaatta is dominant over Svarita. सुब्रह्मण्या is a गदतिः or गद्या is in prose form (In Nigada there is neither foot division nor ardharca-half a verse) not having any metrical feature. Here गदतिः in feminine gender has been attributed by another feminine सुब्रह्मण्या, a particular recitation of certain prose mantras unbroken in one stream by the उद्गातृ priests like: सुब्रह्मण्योम् इन्द्रागच्छ हरिव आगच्छ मेधातिथेर्मेष वृषणश्वस्य मेने गौरावस्कन्दिन्नहल्यायैजार कौशिकब्राह्मण गौतमब्रुवाण श्वः सुत्यामागच्छ मघवन्। etc. So, because of it starts with सुब्रह्मण्या it is called सुब्रह्मण्या। Then a series of अनुदात्त starts since मघवन्।
