There are two rulings in respect of taachila suffixes. One is relating to णिनिः, as per3-2-78 सुप्यजातौ णिनिस्ताच्छील्ये which restricts सुप् as Upapada, for having णिनि AND even Kashika, excepts उपसर्ग as उपपद, other than सुबन्त ---

  

अजातिवाचिनि सुबन्त उपपदे ताच्छील्ये गम्यमाने धातोः णिनिः प्रत्ययो भवति। उष्णभोजी। शीतभोजी। अजातौ इति किम्? ब्राह्मणानामन्त्रयिता। ताछ्हील्ये इति किम्? उष्णं भुङ्क्ते कदाचित्। सुपि इति वर्तमाने पुनः सुब्ग्रहणम् उपसर्गनिवृत्त्यर्थम्।  

  

So what Prof. Korada wanted to say, other than those required by taking णिनि are restricted to popular usages, as I said some authentic usages like अध्यात्मरामायण, and collected by Vartika:

  

उत्प्रतिभ्याम् आङि सर्तेरुपसङ्ख्यानम्। उदासारिण्यः। प्रत्यासारिण्यः। साधुकारिणि च। साधुकारी। साधुदायी। ब्रह्मणि वदः। ब्रह्मवादिनो वदन्ति।  

  

in Kashika. This makes ताच्छील्ये णिनि ONLY to restricted popular रूढिप्रयोग and rules out प्रवाचिन् as ताच्छील्ये णिनि. Now one can ask who can decide whether a word is रूढ or not, which is a question deserving million dollar.

  

Now, there is another rule specifically for ताच्छील्य, and other meanings, the suffixes ordained up to and including क्विप्(\*३,२.१७७)

  

आ क्वेः तच्छील-तद्धर्म-तत्साधुकारिषु># । । PS_३,२.१३४ ।  

  

which makes any suffix between३,२.१३४ -३,२.१७७ in all the three meanings generally assigned to them in this rule, in addition to कर्तरि and other requirements, णिनि appears among these also, but only specified verbs, and not general one, with Upasarga:

  

\<प्रजोरिनिः># । । PS_३,२.१५६ । ।\<जि-दृ-क्षि-विश्रि-इण्-वम-अव्यथ-अभ्यम-परिभू-प्रसूभ्यश्च># । । PS_३,२.१५७ । ।  

  

and no more instances of णिनि. So प्रवाचिन् is not acceptable as साधु unless I can understand otherwise it is a authentic usage like अध्यात्मरामायण or वाल्मीकिरामायण. Probably this meant by रूढप्रयोग by Prof. Korada.

  

Now, coming to प्रवच, though पचाद्याकृतिघण, and also णिनि for ग्रह्यादिः, both are कर्तरि, And not ग्रह्यादिः which takes णिनि. And, under including प्रवच in पचादि, only प्रवक्तीति प्रवचः, will be sufficient to convey the meaning for अच्, is कर्तरि only and the form प्रवचिन् is redundant.

  

In भावघञ्, there is another problem घञ् or घः,

  

चजोः कु घिण्ण्यतोः॥ ७।३।५२ requires, कुत्व, and one can get from प्रवच् in भाव, only प्रवाकः and not प्रवाचः with घञ्. or प्रवच as both are घित् प्रत्यय-s, it warants कुत्व. So with अत इनिठनौ, will not give प्रवाचिन् or प्रवचिन्.  

  

  

Now coming to the original question, whether प्रवचिने is साधु, is definitely असाधु as for grammatical form, unless it is a रूढप्रयोग from some authentic source. And he has not asked for any alternative word to convey the meaning he has given which is coined or otherwise, any source for the word प्रवचिने which supposes a word प्रवचिन्. He had to clearly place the question to avoid a lot of exercise unwarranted by the question and undue comments.

  

सत्यं च स्वाध्याय-प्रवचने च, etc. are usages popular for प्रोपसृष्टवच् धातु.

  

Any such usage as प्रवचिने with some authentic source, need to be attended with laborious exercise to justify it, if one hesitates to accept it as अपाणिनीय or and आर्ष. So Swamiji, has is requested to give the source of the usage he has found as प्रवचिने in the sense he has given in English:'to one who has expounded'? before asking for साधु or असाधु or whether it is coined.

  

And finally, any grammarian freely make use of the the words of Mahabhashya if one says they not used, न च एते उपलभ्यन्ते । :

  

  

"उपलब्धौ यत्नः क्रियतां । महान्हि शब्दस्य प्रयोगविषयः । सप्तद्वीपा वसुमती त्रयः लोकाः चत्वारः वेदाः साङ्गाः सरहस्याः बहुधा विभिन्नाः --- एकशतम् अध्वर्युशाखाः, सहस्रवर्त्मा सामवेदः , एकविंशतिधा बाह्वृच्यम्, नवधा आथर्वणो वेदः, वाकोवाक्यम्, इतिहासः, पुराणम्, वैद्यकम् इति। एतावान्शब्दस्य प्रयोगविषयः । एतावन्तं शब्दस्य प्रयोगविषयं अननुनिशम्य सन्ति अप्रयुक्ताः इति वचनं केवलं साहसमात्रम् ।  

  

for the sake of argument. The same Patanjali, rejects many other forms, though possible applying the related rules of Panini, as अनभिधानात्. So it is a real question, whether a usage is रूढिशब्द or not. It can be argued in either way.
