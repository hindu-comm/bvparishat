Respected Scholars,  
I express my deep sense of gratitude to all those learned members of  
this group for enlightening me on the usage of Hari.h O.m. In fact, this  
discussion did reflect on the background of the usage and its adoption  
in society and Institutions. At the some time scholars seem divided on  
the usage of Hari & Hara suffixed with o.m, and some felt the need of a  
Yugapuru.sa to start the new usage - Hara.h o.m, which is not  
conventional. My intention was not to differentiate Hari or Hara, rather  
I was exploring the possibility of that unconventional usage.  
Furthermore, this discussion extended to the topic of the usage of  
Hari.h o.m at the end of Vedic chanting, which seems to be interesting.  
With regards,  
Anil Kumar Acharya  
  
  
  
Dr. Anil Kumar Acharya  
Asst. Prof. in Sanskrit  
Ramkrishna Mahavidyalaya  
Kailashahar,  
Tripura (North)
