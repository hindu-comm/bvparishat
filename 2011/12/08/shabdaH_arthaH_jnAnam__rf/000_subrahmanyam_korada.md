I came across the differentiation of *çabda, artha,* and *pratyaya* in Yoga Sutras of Patanjali (3.17*çabdärthapratyayänäm itaretarädhyäsät*...).

As Yoga accepts the Sphota theory of meaning, does it mean that the same differentiation is accepted in Grammar as well?

  

If we translate '*pratyaya'* as the concept in the mind and '*artha'* as the manifestation of that concept in reality outside of us, would that be correct? Would the following be correct from that point of view?

  

While the word (*çabda*) stands for the set of phonemes (or the symbol) used to express/represent an entity, sense (*artha*) is the referent of that representation in reality. The notion of that representation in the mind is termed *pratyaya*. It is to be noted here that even for abstract concepts, there is a sense which is distinct from notion. For instance, the notion of an ‘arrangement’ of furniture in a room is distinct from the manifestation of that arrangement we see in actuality. However, the idea of ‘arrangement’ itself is something which is abstract.

  

I would be greatly obliged if you could give me some feedback on this.

According to Paniniyasiksa the things are first put together with बुद्धि (intellect) by अन्तःकरणम्( आत्मा) and then the same अन्तःकरणम्
puts the मनस् ( mind )on the job of विवक्षा(वक्तुम् इच्छा - a desire - to say / employ a शब्द) --  
  
आत्मा बुध्या समेत्यार्थान् मनो युङ्ते विवक्षया !  
  
The Bauddhartha (imaginary thing) is acceptable to Vyakarana , Nyaya , Vedanta etc. . Under उपदेशे’जनुनासिक इत् (1-3-2) Patanjali, while answering a question - who is Devadatta ? says -  
अङ्गदी कुण्डली व्युढोरस्को वृत्तबाहुः ईदृशो देवदत्तः  
Here the term ईदृशः , according to PradIpa and Udyota , means the thing , which flashes thru the mind and an outside thing similar to that .  
Under हेतुमति च (3-1-26) Patanjali says -  
  
सतो बुद्धिविषयन् प्रकाशयन्ति (they express things that are there in the mind ) , which clearly support बौद्धार्थ .  
  
A glance at Mahabhasya under स्थानिवत् (1-1-56) , पङ्क्तिर्विंशति (5-1\`-59), तदस्यास्त्यस्मिन् (5-2-94) etc would substantiate the concept .  
  
So far as Panini is concerned , two Sutras are pronounced for प्रातिपदिक -- अर्थवदधातुरप्रत्ययः प्रातिपदिकम् (1-2-45) and कृत्तद्धितसमासाश्च (1-2-46) . The word समास in the second Sutra , while it is already  
Scovered by the first Sutra , is meant to include words like शशशृ!ङ्गम् , गगनकुसुमम् etc. , which express things that are imaginary (बौद्धः) rather than real / tangible ( स्पर्शयोग्यं वस्तु ) .  
Since the thing denoted by the latter word , i.e. शृङ्गम् , is there the image of शशशृङ्गम् can be had -- explain Kaiyata and Haradatta under स्त्रियाम् (4-1-3) .  
  
**Therefore , unless the image of a thing is there in the बुद्धि (mind) , it is not possible to have it outside and even when the thing is not available outside one can have the image in बुद्धि .  
  
**The author of Nyayasutras , Gotama , compiled a Sutra to explain the above point - बुद्धिसिद्धं तु तदसत् (4-1-50) . Pundit Subrahmanyasastri , the author of शाब्दतरङ्गिणी , argues that the above Sutra does not support बौद्धार्थ .  
Vacaspatimisra , the author of BhamatI , also advocates the above concept under जन्माद्यस्य यतः (1-1-2) - चेतनो हि नामरूपे बुद्धावालिख्य घट इति नाम्ना कंबुग्रीवादिना रूपेण च बाह्यं घटं निष्पादयति !  
Advaitavedantins use the term तुच्छ्म् ( अप्रामाणिकम् /mean) to denote शशसृङ्गम् , कूर्मरोम etc. .  
  
The followers of Madhavacarya call it , i.e. शशशृङ्गम् , असत् - कालसामान्यसम्बन्धि !  
  
Laugakshibhaskara of Nyayasiddhantamanjariprakasa , explains the argument of Nastikas and refutes the same -- शशशृन्ङ्गाद्यलीकम् असत्ख्यात्या सिद्ध्यति ! सा च असत्ख्यातिः नोपपद्यते , ज्ञानविषयस्य अस्तित्वव्याप्यतया असत्ख्यातिविषयत्वस्य सन्निकृश्टभेप्रतियोगितावच्छेदकत्वासिद्धेरित्याहुः (p 17) .  
  
As far as the Nastikas are concerned the falsehood of शशशृङ्ग etc. is secured thru असत्ख्याति . The same is not possible .  
  
Sankaracarya discusses बौद्धार्थ under - शब्द इति चेन्नातः प्रभवात् प्रत्यक्षानुमानाभ्याम् (1-3-28) -- सभूरिति व्याहरत् , स भूमिमसृजत् ... तै ब्रा 2-2-4-2) - इत्येवमादिका भूरादिशब्देभ्य एव् मनसि प्रादुर्भूतेभ्यो भूरादिलोकान् सृष्टान् दर्शयति !  
AcArya quotes the following Manusmruti in support of the above Sruti --  
  
नामरूपं च भूतानाम् कर्मणां च प्रवर्तनम् !  
वेदशब्देभ्य एवादौ निर्ममे स महेश्वरः !!  
सर्वेषां तु स नामानि कर्माणि च पृथक् पृथक् !  
वेदशब्देभ्य एवादौ पृथक्संस्थाश्च निर्ममे !! ( Manu 1-21)  
  
Appayyadikshita , the author of Nyayaraksamani , suports the बौद्धार्थ of the above श्रुति -- शिल्पी यथा शिल्पशास्त्रवचनेभ्यो मरुत्वताम् .  
  
Udayanacarya of Nyayamanjari , concludes that the word शशशृङ्गम् is
अपार्थकम् /unmeaningful - one of the 22 निग्रहस्थानानि .  
  
  
For Bauddhas and Jainas everything is विज्ञानम् (imaginary) - सर्वं विज्ञानम् विज्ञानमिति सर्वे पदार्थाः विज्ञानाकारा एव न बाह्याः !  
  
Kanada compiled a Sutra - अभूतं नास्तीत्यनर्थान्तरम् (9-1-9) -
NyayakandalI explains the meaning .  
  
According to Samkhyadarsana शशशृङ्गम् is not available due to
अत्यन्ताभाव and this is explained in Matharavrtti.  
  
Patanjali in Yoganusasanam calls it वैकल्पिकज्ञानम् -- शब्दज्ञानानुपाती वस्तुशून्यो विकल्पः (1-9) .  
  
Kumarila in Tantravartika says that although the real thing is not there Sabda can generate cognition (ज्ञानम्) --  
  
अत्यन्तासत्यपि ह्यर्थे ज्ञानं शब्दः करोति हि !  
प्रमाबध्या ’प्रमा तत्र ह्यतः प्रामाण्यनिर्णयः !!  
  
In Vakyapadiyam Hari at many places discusses Bauddhartha --  
  
अत्यन्तमतथाभूते निमित्ते श्रुत्युपाश्रयात् !  
दृश्यते’लातचक्रादौ वस्त्वाकारनिरूपणा !! (1-130)  
  
In Alamkarasastra etc the Bhava etc is dependent on Bauddhartha only .
