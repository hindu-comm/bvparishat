The point in question is why to analyze the correct and incorrect usages, according to a fixed standard of usage.One of our earliest rhetoricians, who (probably was a co-author of काशिका, a known commentary on अष्टाध्यायी only next to महाभाष्य) sums up the practical need of analysis of merits and demerits in the language in written lanuage (i.e. poetic language):

  

सौन्दर्यमलंकारः।१।

स दोषगुणालंकार-हानादानाभ्याम्।।२।

(स त्वलंकारो दोषहानात्, गुणालंकारादानात्।)

शास्त्रतस्ते।।३।।

............शास्त्रतो हिज्ञात्वादोषान् जह्यात्। गुणालंकारांश्चाददीत।

  

The gist of above quote portion is that by a शास्त्र one should avoid faulty usages and use embellished language with merits. This holds good mutatis mutandis to the practical use of Grammar standardization of the language in use. This was the purpose of my quoting the relevant portion too.

It has been discussed already earlier, as to whether the knowledge of correct word is beneficiary or its usage, and answered affirmatively. If the knowledge correct usage accredits धर्म, one would know incorrect words also distinguish them through his knowledge of grammar, and would be credited with अधर्म due to knowing them. So this objection beautifully solved here, even accepting this argument, a man digging a well would be covered with mud and dust also during his toils, he would quench his thirst first and could wash away the mud and dirt when he gets the water. In the same way, even though the knowledge of incorrect usages may accrue अधर्म, while analyzing correct and incorrect usage of words, he can easily thwart away the अधर्म so accrued, by the धर्म he gets with his knowledge of correct usages.
