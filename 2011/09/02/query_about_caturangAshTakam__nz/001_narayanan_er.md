Dear Sree Sajeevkumar,

I can hardly have any idea of chess. But the term Caturangam in neuter gender is indicating dicing-अक्षक्रीडाविशेषः, as well as a military troop having four types of groups of elephant, horse, chariot and armed men. As far as the modern chess pieces are concerned, king, queen, rook, bishop, knight and pawn are displayed on table. Regarding caturanga-चत्वारि अङ्गानि यस्य तत् चतुरङ्गम् is the derivative sentence-something which having four elements. The Ramayana takes the term for military troop. The Mahabharata story is famous for dicing and Yudhishthira asks about the types of dicing to Vyasa:  

अष्टकोष्ठ्याञ्च या क्रीडा तां मे ब्रूहि तपोधन!।  
प्रकर्षेणैव मे नाथ! चतूराजी यतो भवेत्॥

So, there are two types of play-octo-columnous and quadro--columnous.  

  

Vyasa replies:

अष्टौ कोष्ठान् समालिख्य प्रदक्षिणक्रमेण तु।  
अरुणं पूर्वतः कृत्वा दक्षिणेहरितं बलम्॥

.................................................................  

सिंहासनं चतूराजी नृपाकृष्टन्तु षट्पदम्।  
काककाष्ठं बृहन्नौका नौकाकृष्टप्रकारकम्॥  
घाताघाते वटी नौका बलं हन्ति युधिष्ठिर!।  
राजा गजो हयश्चापि त्यक्त्वा घातं निहन्ति च॥  
अत्यन्तं स्वबलं रक्षेत् स्वराजा बलमुत्तमम्।  
अल्पस्यारक्षया पार्थ! हन्तव्यं बलमुत्तम्॥  
नौकायाश्चत्वारि पदानि अश्वस्याष्टौ पदानि इत्याधिक्यमश्वस्य॥

  

Protecting the King (राजा) is important. It is clear from these verses that the types of movements of various pieces like knight (हयः-horse), rook (रथः, नौका-chariot) bishop (गजः-elephant), pawn (बलम्-armed force) etc. Chess experts can explain more. A work called the tithyaditattva may be a part of the Mahabharata.

  

It don't know about the work called Caturangashtakam. a8 h8 squares makes 64 squares in chess, like a pictorization of an anushtup sarvatobhadra verse in Sanskrit.
