Respected Scholars,

The Abhijnanasakuntala of Kalidasa speaks about one's rights for his livelihood. A fisherman was caught by King's men and being questioned. Fisherman says that he is managing his family by catching fish by the net. King's men remark on him over his "great profession". Fisherman replies:  

सहजे किल जे विणिंदिदे ण हु दे कम्म विवय्यणीअके।

पसुमालणकम्मदालुणे अणुकंपामिदु एव्व सोत्तिए।। (अभि. शा.६.१)  

(It is not to be avoided even if any condemned activity which is hereditary . A Vedic expert is soft with compassion on the animal while he is brutal on its killing.)  
Kalidasa is conveying the message that killing is condemned everywhere but if it is hereditary then it can hardly be avoided. The compassion to animals are revealed through the chantings too. Fisherman feels: Are they be compassionate while an animal is being brutally slaughtered? For Srotriyas sacrifice has been hereditary, slaughter is a part of it and cannot be avoided. Fishing is too a killing can hardly be justified but a hereditary activity. Kalidasa might have had against of all sorts of slaughter. The pishtapasu (dummy) might be a later phenomenon.  
कौशिकसूत्रम्-  
(५,८\[४४\].७) य ईशे पशुपतिः पशूनां \[२.३४.१\] इति हुत्वा वशां अनक्ति शिरसि ककुदे जघनदेशे।  
(५,८\[४४\].२८) यत्ते क्रूरं यदास्थितं तच्छुन्धस्व। इति अवशिष्टाः पार्श्वदेशे अवसिच्य यथार्थं व्रजति।  
(५,८\[४४\].३०) ओषधे त्रायस्वैनं स्वधिते मैनं हिंसीः \[६.१५\] इति शस्त्रं प्रयच्छति।  
"Let the harshness to be passified", "O herbs, protect it and don't kill it" these are compassionate sentences too.

The Dharmashastra texts upholds that animal killing in a sacrifice cannot be treated as killing, because it is for universal welfare. But commercial killing is not appreciated.  

विष्णुस्मृतिः-  
यज्ञार्थं पशवः सृष्टाः स्वयं एव स्वयंभुवा।  
यज्ञो हि भूत्यै सर्वस्य तस्माद्यज्ञे वधोऽवधः। । ५१.६१ । ।  
न तादृशं भवत्येनो मृगहन्तुर्धनार्थिनः।  
यादृशं भवति प्रेत्य वृथा मांसानि खादतः। । ५१.६२ । ।  
ओषध्यः पशवो वृक्षास्तिर्यञ्चः पक्षिणस्तथा।  
यज्ञार्थं निधनं प्राप्ताः प्राप्नुवन्त्युच्छ्रितीः पुनः। । ५१.६३ । ।

यज्ञार्थेषु पशून्हिंसन्वेदतत्त्वार्थविद्द्विजः।  
आत्मानं च पशूंश्चैव गमयत्युत्तमां गतिम्। । ५१.६५ । ।

The advaitashataka (<http://sa.wikisource.org/wiki/%E0%A4%85%E0%A4%A6%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%B5%E0%A5%88%E0%A4%A4%E0%A4%B6%E0%A4%A4%E0%A4%95%E0%A4%AE%E0%A5%8D>) speaks about relevance of non-killing.
