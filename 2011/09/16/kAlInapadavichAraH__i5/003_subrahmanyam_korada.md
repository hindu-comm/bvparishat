kAlika - kAlIna  
  
generally the word ' kAlIna ' is seen across NyAya works . It is an apas'abda .  
  
It should be ' kAlika ' following 'kAlAt.t.ha\~n' (Pan4-3-11) . It also means kAlavAcakAt . So dainika.h , mAsika , sAmvatsarika.h , pAks.ika.h etc .  
  
On the word 's'as'vat ' also it is applicable - ' yues.Am ca virodha.h
s'As'vatika.h ' (Pan2-4-9) .  
  
Since the usage of 'kAlIna' is very much there Apte must have made the entry .  
  
' vis'rAma' - is another such S'abda . But I think it is right according to CAndravYAkaran.am --  
vis'rAmam labhatAmidam ca s'ithilajyAbandhamasmaddhanu.u (S"Akuntalam-7) , In MAlatImAdhavam (1) also .  
  
'ingAlam' (angAra.h) - is a PrAkr.tas'abda used by S'rIhars.a ( vitenuringAlamivAyas'a.h pare , Nais.adham -1) and by AppayyadIks.ita in SarvasiddhAntales'asamgraha .  
  
Haradattamis'ra in PadamanjarI (beginning) says that there are 'apas'abdas' in KAvyas and PurA.nas . N  
  
Nirankus'A.h kavaya.h , NaiyAyikAs'ca ?  
  
dhanyo'smi
