ANu, ParamANu, AakAsha etc.



Respected Scholars, Namaskar!



Many thanks to Dr. S. Korada, Dr.D. Bhattacharya, , Dr.H. N. Bhat, Dr. Narang and other scholars who contributed their valuable information to my questions. Special thanks to Dr. BVK Sastry who interacted with me from many years. As usual the posting of Prof. S. Korad is full of many references. I convert them in Devanagari script to understand Sanskrit words correctly. The question of using ANu and ParamANu for modern concepts of electrons and atoms are in limbo now because the ancient Indianconcepts differ considerably.



Now I will explain why I raised the question about AakAsha, GuNa and Shabda. Modern quantum mechanics reports that when the spin of one fundamental particle is changed, the spin of the other particle (entangled states) away from it also undergoes spin change.This is very important experiment. It shows that there is sometning unknown that connects two particles. This is also described as "non-locality". It is indirectly suggesting that what we call vacuum (I will use here Aakaasha) is not a vacuum. Perhaps vacuum has some properties all of which are not known yet. There is something called vacuum oscillations in quantum mechanics. On this background I wanted know the property (GuNa)of AakAsha described in the ancient Indian books.



I would like to go from concrete concepts to abstract concept if needed. I find it difficult to derive mundane scientific particles starting from the creation of the MahAbhootas by the GOD.Prof. Korada referred to the condition prior to creation of AakAsha -"MahAbhootas are created by TanmAtras and AakAsha is filled with ShabdaTanMaatras". Although I have vague idea about the meaning of the word TanmAtra, I cannot figure out the meaning of Shabda TanmAtra.



Now I will tell my meaning. Sound pulse (one short word-shabda)) is attenuated after it is produced. The same way electromagnetic pulse (shabda)created in space also gets attenuated. The content of air attenuates Dhvani pulse. What is there in space that attenuates electromagnetic pulse? This attenuation of shabda after its production (both concepts) are incorporated in single Sanskrit word shabda. This is one GuNa of AakAsha. AakAsha has other GuNas. One meaning of GuNa is threads (like on bolt). Does AakAsha have threads? Yes!It has to do with Euclidean space and no-Euclidean space. Now comes the question of the origin of the word AakAsha. There are words like PrakAsha, San'kAsha, AvakAsha. There is root Kaash. Where does this root come from? Thanks. N.R.Joshi.
