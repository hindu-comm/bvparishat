namo vidvadbhyah  
  
**CArvAkamatakha.n.danam** is done (**mostly indirectly** , i.e. without mentioning the name '**cArvAka**) in **NyAyakusumAnjali , Kha.n.danakha.n.dakhAdya** etc and commentaries thereupon .  
  
For example the following verse of **CArvAkas** is refuted in **MahAbhA.sya and MImAmsA (S'AbarabhA.syam** etc.) by offering the meaning of the .**Rk (.Rgveda 10-106-6)**--  
  
**trayo vedasya kartAro bha.n.dadhUrtanis'AcarA.h I  
jarbhrIturpharItyAdi pa.n.ditAnAm vaca.h sm.rtam II**  
  
The **.Rk** in question --  
**s.r.nyeva jarbharI turpharItU naitos'eva turpharI parpharIkA I  
udanyajeva jemanA maderU tAme jarAyvajiram marAyu II  
**  
**DharmakIrti in NyAyabindu** refutes the argument of **CArvAkas that 'vyApti**' is not possible to accept and therefore there is no **AnumAna** --  
**kAryakAra.nabhAvAdvA svabhAvAdvA niyAmakAt I  
avinAbhAvaniyamo dars'anAnna nadars'anAt II  
**  
**AvinAbhAva (vyApti) can be known thru either kAryakAra.nabhAva or tAdAtmya .  
**  
In 6th and 7th **Skandhas of BhAgavata** the **CArvAkas / NAstikas** are described as having '**vairabhakti'**  
  
All the **Astikadars'anas** , including the commentaries are refutations of **CArvAka** as they propose what was unacceptable to the rivals .  
  
dhanyo'smi
