> Thanks for the reply. Is यमळ exclusively used to indicate twin > offsprings alone? Is त्रिकम् an exclusive term for indicating > triplets or does the word have application in other contexts too? For > example we can say शमादिषट्कम् .  

  

The words यम, यमल, यमज are synonyms of pairs by usage they are restricted twins, pairs of two. And it has no extended sense of numbers, though it can be use to denote the pair synonymously युग्म, युगल also. as in the usage:

  

त्वदीयभृत्यावथ काश्यपात्तौ सुरारिवीरावुदितौ दितौ द्वौ।

सन्ध्यासमुत्पादनकष्टचेष्टौ यमौ च लोकस्य यमाविवान्यौ॥१७॥



Generally this is used to denote twins (pairs of two) than to denote numeral value according to the context. As also it is clear with:

  

"नारदोक्तिपरामर्शी*यमलार्जुनभञ्जनः*। धनदात्मजसंघुष्टो नन्दमोचितबन्धनः ॥ ३९ ॥

  

वत्सवाटचरोऽनन्तो धेनुकासुरमर्द्दनः। तृणीकृततृणावर्तो*यमलार्जुनभञ्जनः*॥३१-३५॥

  

etc. used to denote pairs of trees too by extension of the word. I don't think there is any equivalent of "triplets" 3 or more twin children in Sanskrit. All we get त्रयम्, त्रिकम्, त्रयाणां वर्गः - the group of three, which if you want, one can use it for 3 or more twins without numerical value as used in technical texts:

  

*पञ्चक*-*त्रिक*-चतुष्क-शतेन in Lilavati and other mathematical texts.
