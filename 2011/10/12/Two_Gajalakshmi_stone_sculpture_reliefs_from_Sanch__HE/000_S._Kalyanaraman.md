## WEDNESDAY, OCTOBER 12, 2011

### Two Gajalakshmi stone sculpture reliefs from Sanchi and Bharhut

I present below two comparable two stone reliefs showing Gajalakshmi from Sanchi and from Bharhut. I hope Art historians will be able to figure out the differences in representations of pratimaa.  
![](https://ci4.googleusercontent.com/proxy/fZDvsa-ByNC4sSW8XunLzT7yN1ewjMHirT6E0vBxmKuOeMChOb9K0-POmuTp9GGr7jfZxn1Fv7XOuQOOUqC7zYjto0avqwjApbftIrIhl6q-=s0-d-e1-ft#http://www.hindu.com/fline/fl2418/images/20070921505506607.jpg)Lakshmi being lustrated by elephants, East Gateway. Also interpreted as Queen Maya, mother of Prince Siddhartha. From the earliest Sanchi stupa and the Bharhut stupa onwards, one of the earliest deities seen in Indic art is Lakshmi, who represents the abundance and riches of nature.
