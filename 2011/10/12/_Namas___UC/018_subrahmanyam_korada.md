namo vidvadbhyah  
  
**namas -- avyayam only or subanta too ?  
  
PUrvapaks.a.h - since ' namas ' is listed under ' svarAdinipAtam avyayam ' (Panini 1-1-36) it is an Avyayam .  
  
SiddhAnta.h - since ' namas' is sattvavAcI (also ) , due to its ' dhAtusambandhe anekas'aktidars'ana ' it is subanta also .  
  
Laghu / Guru - s'abdendus'ekhara.h -- mahasAm namasAm iti prayogastu sattvavAcakatve bodhya.h .  
  
n.ama = prahvatve ( s'abde ca - Ks.IrasvAmI) - BhvAdi -- namati , nanAma , nantA , namsyati , namatu , namet , namyAt , anamsIt ,  
  
namra.h ( namikampidhUpo ra.h 3-2-167) , nemi.h (nemitumbAram - S'AbarabhAs.yam ) .  
  
sAks.Atprabhr.tIni ca ( 1-4-73) paks.e gatisamj\~nA - namaspuraso.h gatyo.h (8-3-40) - satvam - namaskr.tya .  
  
sarvadhAtubhya asun (Un.Adi) -- namas .  
  
Panini offered two SUtras - svarAdinipAtam avyayam , (prAgrIs'varAt nipAtA.h 1-4-56) cAdayo'sattve (1-4-57) --  
  
- you can put ' svarAdi ' under 'cAdi ' why to have a separate list ? (Patanjali)- both are NipAtas !  
  
cAdInAm vA asattvavcanAnAm nipAtasamj\~nA , svarAdInAm puna.h sattvAvacanAnAm asattvavacanAnAm ca ( here 'vA' means 'eva' , but not 'anyatarasyAm ' / 'vibhAs.a' -- dhAtunipAtAvyayAnAm anekArthatvAt ) .--  
  
-- cAdis , if and iff denote 'asattva' ( adravya) only then they are NipAtas in Panini ( S'abdakaustubha etc.) - lodham nayanti pasu manyamAnA.h -- pas'u = samyak ( dravya - pas'Un tAn cakre vAyavyAn -- R.gveda and Yajurveda) .  
  
What is 'sattvam' / dravyam ( s.adl. + tva , Aun.Adika.h ) - sIdanti
jAtigun.akriyA asmin iti sattvam .  
  
idam , tat - iti sarvanamaparAmars'ayogyam vastu dravyam .  
  
Here is VAkyapadIyam - PadakA.n.da - BhUyodravyasamuddes'a - 3 --  
  
[vastUpalaks.an.am](http://vastUpalaks.an.am) yatra sarvanAma prayujyate I dravyamityucyate so'rtho , bhedyatvena vivaks.ita.h II  
  
So following Vivaks.A as Vis'es.yam , even JAti , Gun.a and KriyA also are fit to be considered as Dravyam .  
  
ata iti yat pratIyate tad dravyam - Niruktam ( ata.h is a form of 'idam') .  
  
svasti vAcayati , sva.h pas'ya , sva.h pas'yanti , svar Arohaya etc. - in these cases we find ' anekakArakas'aktiyoga' and the same is a
proof to show that each one is a sattvam (lin'gasamkhyAnvayitvam) .  
  
AvyayIbhAvas'ca ( 1-1-40) - is to show that in cases like - unmattagangam and lohitagangam , it will be in Napumsakalinga only .  
  
Some Avyaya is VibhaktyarthapradhAnam - uccai.h , nIcai.h , another is KriyApradhAnam - hiruk , pr.thak , still some are neither -- sva.h pas'ya etc.  
  
More discussion is there under 'taddhitas'cAsarvavibhakti.h' 1-1-37) -- note - the Pondicherry book on S'Abdabodha registeres only two under 'Vibhakti' - subantam and tin.antam , but Taddita is also called Vibhakti .  
  
By and large since the word 'namas' is listed under 'svarAdi' it can be sattvavAci / subanta and asattvavAci/ avyayam .
