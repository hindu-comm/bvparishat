नमांसि भूयांसि /

With regard to the Paa.ninian rule " sphaaya.h sphii ni.s.thaayaam (6.1.22)" we have " sphiita" and "sphiitavat" but " sphaati" and not " sphiiti "in case of "ktin".The dictionary of Monier Williams gives an example of "sphiiti" from Kalha.na's Raajatara\*ngi.nii.Can we justify the use of "sphiiti" within the framework of Paa.nini or any other school of grammar ? Or this is an exceptional use ? The word " sphiiti" is current as a tatsama word in any number of our languages.
