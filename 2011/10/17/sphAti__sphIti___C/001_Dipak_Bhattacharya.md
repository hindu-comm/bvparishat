Dear Friend,  
  

There are many examples of Vedic examples not tallying with Pāṇini’s rules. It has come to the notice of scholars that though Pāṇini (3.1.35) rules against the periphrastic perfect in ‘Mantra’, the forms *gamayāṃ cakartha* and *gamayā́ṃ cakāra* occur in AVP 18.65.10 and AVŚ 18.2.27 respectively. Pāṇini 3.3.123 allows *udaṅka* but AVP 8.12.11 has *idaṅka*. The Aitareya Brāhmaṇa’s *niniyoja* (for *niyuyoja*) has no Pāṇinian sanction.

We have to accept the forms occurring in the Vedas.
