The information on ळ and ळ्ह as intervocalic variations of ड and ढ will be found in the Ṛkprātiśākhya 1.21 (1.51, 52 in M.D.Shastri’s edition) and also in Ṣaḍguruśiṣya’s commentary on Kātyāyana’s Sarvānukramaṇī on RV.1.28. According to the RP Vedamitra held the place of articulation of ḍ and ḍh to be the root of the tongue and the palate. The verse cited by Ṣaḍguruśiṣya is अज्मध्यस्थडकारस्यळकारंबह्वृचा जगुः। अज्मध्यस्थढकारस्यळ्हकारं च यथाक्रमं ।। Thus वीळु and मीळ्हुषे but वीड्वङ्ग.
