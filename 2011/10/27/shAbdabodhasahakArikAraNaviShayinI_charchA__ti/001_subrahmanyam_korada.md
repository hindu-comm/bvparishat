namo vidvadbhyah  
  
**AkAn'ks.Adi --  
  
 kr.s.n.ayujurvede  yujUms.i  pras'lis.t.apat.hitAni . tatra na j\~nAyate kiyadekam yaju.h iti -- is.etvA UrjetvA vAyavastha etc.  
Then in order to fix the parimAn.a of a yajus , Jaimini offered a
sUtra in yaju.h pariman.Adhikaran.a ( 2-1-14-46) --  
  
arthaikatvAt ekam vAkyam sAkAn'[ks.am](http://ks.am) ced vibhAge
syAt ( if there is arthaikya / prayojanaikya and if the parts , when separated , are found to be wanting - it is a vAkyam .  
  
When there is ekavAkyatA one should not split a sentence - it becomes a dos.a called ' vAkyabheda ' --  
sambhavatyekavAkyatve vAkyabhedastu nes.yate (vAkyAdhikaran.am - s'lokavArtikam .  
  
If there is arthaikyam / ekavAkyatA among the parts it is a vAkyam .  
  
VAkyam = yaju.h .  
  
Notice - Jaimini did not insert the term ' padam ' in the sUtra ( I discussed this extensively in my book MahavAkyavicAra.h and concluded that the same is the definition for both a vAkya and a mahAvAkya ) .  
  
Here is TantravArtikam of KumArilabhat.t.a(same sUtra) --  
  
yajurbhedam ca muktvaitad vAkyaikatvasya
[laks.an.am](http://laks.an.am) I  
mantrabrAhman.alokes.u vaktavyam mkvopayujyate II  
  
Therefore , following BhavabhUti - [r.s.In.Am](http://r.s.In.Am) punarAdyAnam vAcamartho'nudhAvati , we have to find out what actually Jaimini meant .  
  
KumArila asserts that the same is applicable in mantra, brahman.a and
loka and it is vAkyaikavAktatvalaks.anam .  
  
EkArthabodhakatvam in vAkya is thru padaikavAkyatA and in a mahAvAkya it is thru vAkyaikavAkyatA (among avAntaravAkyas) .  
  
If AkAn'ks.A is said what about yogyatA and Asatti ? they are implied - how can one understand a sentence without these factors ?  
  
AkAn'ks.A is put to rest whereas yogyatA lives on - caitra.h --
caitra.h kim karoti ? pacati .  
  
It is in later works that scholars quote the trio - SAhityadarpan.am (2) , Tarkasamgraha etc .  
  
Panini compiled a similar sUtra -(abhij\~nAvacane lr.t. ,3-2-112) vibhAs.A sAkAn'ks.e (3-2-114) - lr.t. paks.e lan' -- smarasi kr.s.n.a
yat kas'mIres.u vatsyAma.h tatra saktUn pAsyAma.h / smarasi kr.s.n.a yat kas'mIres.u avasAma tatra saktUn apibAma .  
  
This ' abhij\~nA' later became ' pratyabhij\~nA ' (also abhij\~nAnam) .  
  
This above sUtra is a proof to exhibit the analysis of a mahAvAkya by Panini .  
  
The As.t.AdhyAyI is based on mahAvAkya - not a single application can be carried out without this concept .  
  
In nyAya (nyAyamahAvAkya) - parArthAnumAnam depends on mahA vakya .  
  
tattvamasi etc are mahAvAkyas following arthagaurava and not s'abdagaurava as in the case of mahAkAvya . In fact, mahAkAvyam and mahAvAkyam are synonyms .  
  
KumArila elaborates (TantravArtikam 1-3-13-24)--  
  
svArthabodhe samAptAnAm an'gAn'gitva vyapeks.ayA I  
vAkyAnAm ekavAkyatva punassamhatya jAyate II  
  
In every s'Astra upades'A is thru mahAvAkya only ( TantravArtikam 3-4-4-13) --  
  
sarvAn.yeva hi s'AstrAn.i svaprades'Antaraissaha I  
ekavAkyatayA yuktam upades'am pratanvate II  
  
... vis'es.en.a tu vyAkaran.e , tatra hyekaikasmin
prAyen.As.t.AdhyAI vyApriyate ...  
  
It was gan'ges'opAdhyAya (11 AD)who for the first time attributed the three kinds of s'Abdabodha - dhAtvartha of vaiyAkaran.as , bhAvanA of mImAmsakas and prathamAnta / dhAtvartha / nipAtArtha etc of naiyAyikas .
