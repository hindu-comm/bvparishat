Dr Bhat has quoted the VArtikam rightly --  
  
the first point is whether the word 'rAs.t.ra' is used in the sense of (present) 'des'a' . Even if it is taken for granted what will be the PrakriyA --  
  
[rAs.t.re](http://rAs.t.re) bhava.h / jAta.h etc. - rAs.t.AvArapArAd ghakhau - ghapratyaya.h - iyAdes'a.h (AyaneyInIyiya.h phadhakhachaghAm pratyayAdInAm - 7-1-2) - rAs.t.riya.h (in the sense of 'rAjasyAla' it can be 'rUd.ha") .  
  
Then samAsa with antar by 'antar aparigrahe'(1-4-65) - antar iti avyayam samarthena samasyate , sa.h gatisamjn\~as'ca bhavati - antArAs.t.riya.h .  
  
But there is a niyama -- vr.ttivigrahayo.h samAnArthakatvam --  
  
rAs.t.rayo.h (rAs.t.rAnAm) madhye - antArAs.t.ram - then the Taddhita , i.e. 'gha' cannot be effected .  
  
Here the word 'antar' has got 'sAmarthyam' with 'rAs.t.ra' but not
'rAs.t.iya' .  
  
Therefore it will become an 'asamarthasamAsa' (sud.anampusakasya' - Panini) .  
  
So Prof Dvivedi must have thought of this problem and ruled out .
