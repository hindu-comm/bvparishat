**" S'arabha",as I remember some description,resembled a deer but was far more powerful than a lion.In an ancient poetry in Odia literature,RAma's arrow(s'ara) is likened to a s'arabha : "s'ara-s'arabhare kale vinaas'a capala je ".'S'arabha' also denotes a young elephant(karabha),a kind of monkey,camel or deer.S'arabha is also the name of a demon.The root is 's'.r hi.msaayaam'.Often it appears as if a 's'arabha' has something to do with a dinosaur !  
Another such word is 's'alabha' coming from root 's'al' with 'abhac',meaning a kind of locust.Regards,SMMishra**
