 As one who has been active both as an author and lecturer and teacher in India, the U.S. and Europe (including the U.K.) may I share some thoughts with members of these forums?



 The longer we keep the AIT dispute alive, especially trying to refute their linguistics, the longer we give them space to keep alive their theories. What is needed now is a pro-cative approach that relgates it to a footnote in history with an alternative version that becomes the standard.



 The refutation has been done **ON SCIENTIFIC GROUNDS, WITH HARD DATA FROM ARCHAEOLOGY AND NATURAL HISTORY AND GENETICS.** Their linguistic disputes have no place in history, but they keep acting as though their brand of linguistics is the last word on history. I have faced them down in their own home base so that is not the issue.



 We need to do two things-- (1) produce an academically credible alternative; (2) design and teach courses in mainstream academia both in India and the West.



 We have started a project called **People's History of India** which will take just such an approach. The first volume will be on the Vedic Age. It will start from scratch takingthe origins of Vedic civilization at the end of the Ice Age and look at natural history and genetics and then look at the human imprint on India (based on genetics) and correletate it with the Vedic and Puranic literature.



 In effect it will look at Vedic history as an extension of natural history-- from the Ice Age to the end of the Sarasvati. The AIT will have no place in this approach to history. We plan to relegate it to a box item or Appendix explaining the pathalogical nature of this scholarship and expose its ties to Nazi ideology and British colonialism that has no basis in science or Indian tradition.  
  
 We cannot keep fighting these people worried about their reputations and positions forever. We need to be proactive.



N.S. Rajaram
