Aakaasha and Shabda



Thanks to Prof. Narang, Dr D. Bhattacharya and Dr. Kalyanraman. I agree with Prof Narang that there may be connection to the root "an". I read in the book, "Shabda-shakti-Prakashika" (English translation) that Jagadish was saying that Indian concept of "ANu" does not have Avayava (parts). So we cannot use it for modern scientific word "atom".



There is a lot of talk about science in Vedas. Many scholars repeat Panch Mahabhootas. It is said that GuNa of Aakaasha is Shabda. I never understood this. If we assume Shabda means sound, then how can sound be the "GuNa" of Aakaasha (vac cum). Sound needs material medium for to travel. Charvak did not recognize Aakaasha as Mahabhoota.I also earlier asked the etymology of the word "Shabda". My meaning of "Shabda" is a sound pulse based upon my phonemic symbolism in Sanskrit.It does not matter whether it is Dhvanyatmaka or VarNatmaka. I am always interested in AbhidhA meanings, no lakshaNa, no VyanjanA. Thanks. N.R.Joshi
