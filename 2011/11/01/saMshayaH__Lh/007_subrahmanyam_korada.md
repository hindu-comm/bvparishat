**upakrama and upasamhAra -- s'AbdA.h (brahmasUtra 1-1-4) -- bhAmatI  
  
UpakramopasamhArau ... - is from br.hatsamhitA ( not available )  
  
alpAks.aramasandigdham ..., uktAnukta ... , vAkyastham padam ... are
from [vis.n.udharmpottarapurAn.am](http://vis.n.udharmpottarapurAn.am) ( and another book) .See Bhamati 1-1-1 for a different definition of sUtram -- laghUni sUcitArthAni ...  
  
PurAn.as discuss many things -- I may discuss vyAkaran.am according to [garud.apurAn.am](http://garud.apurAn.am) someday .  
  
That is right - here s'AbdA.h means 'pUrvamImAmsakA.h' -- they take vedas'abda as a pramAn.a  independent of pratyaksa. etc. (although Patanjali says - s'abdapramAn.akA vayam - 2-1-1) and keep harping on it ( in vedas no Apta) .  
  
It should be noted that vedAnta does not have any separate parameters to analyze vAkya and mahAvAkya but totally depends on karmamImAmsA .  
  
I discussed this aspect vividly in my book - Mahavakyavicarah and take example from the same ( rather than what Bhamati offered ) -  
  
Following Jaiminisutram - arthaikatvAdekam vAkya
sAkAn'[ks.am](http://ks.am) cedvibhAge syAt ( 2-1-14-46) , one should try to get ekavAkyata , here it is vAkyaikavAkyatA as the question is related to mahAvAkya rther than vAkya , by different devices such as an'gAn'gibhAva , gun.agun.ibhAva , upakAryopakArakabhAva etc which will end up in vis'es.n.avis'es.yabhAva .  
  
In vais'vAnares.t.yadhikaran.a , vAkyaikavAkyatA is achieved following upakramopasamhArau - so s'abdabhAvanArUpA mahAvAkyadhI.h .  
  
In Taittiriyasamhita ( 2-2-5) the following text is there -- vais'vAnararam dvAdasa'kapAlam nirvapet putre jAte ,
yadas.t.AkapAlo bhavati gAyatraivainam brahmavarcasena punAti , yannavakapAlo bhavati ... yaddas'akapAlo bhavati ... , yadekAdas'akapAlo bhavati .... yaddvAdas'akapAlo bhavati ... annAda indrayAvI bhavati .  
  
Apparently this is an utpattivAkyam consisting of vaikalpikAnekasamkhyA . Here one has to refute nAmadheyatva to as.t.AkapAla etc and
establish stutiparatvam of as.t.AkapAla etc to dvAdas'akapAla
thru ekavAkyatA and the device is upakramopasamhArau , i.e. gaun.yA vr.ttyA .  
  
Jaimini offered two sUtras - gun.avAdastu , avibhAgAt vidhAnArthe
stutyarthenopapadyeran ( 1-2-1-10 , 1-4-11-20) .  
  
Sabarasvamy - santi hi dvAdas'asamkhyAyAm as.t.atvAdaya.h samkhyAvis'es.A avibhaktA.h ...  
  
Prabhakara in Brhati -- tasmAdApas's'AntA itivadupapannA stuti.h .  
  
In Tantravartika (1-4-11-22)Kumarila says --  
  
vAkyAni yAvadartham syu.h gun.ebhya.h phalakalpane I  
ekopakramasamhArAdekam cedam pratIyate II  
  
ekArthabodhakatvena , ekaphalakalpakatvena , ekopakramopasamhAratvena
cAtra vAkyaikavAkyatayA ekam mahAvAkyam samjAtam - this is tAtparyam .  
  
So all the stutivAkyas end up in ' vais'vAnaraprarocanA' , following upakramopasamhArau .  
  
Bhamati refers to this . Other examples can be offered .  
  
All six Lingas are useful in Vedanta .  
  
  
s'AbdIbhAvanA is so important for them so s'AbdA.h .  
  
The bhAvanA that is thru lin' etc. is s'AbdI , ArthIbhAvanA is prayatna / anyotpAdAnukUlo vyApAra.h .  
  
Bonus Info --Vacaspatimisra said there is no ' kus'A ' - s'abda . But it is there in Vedas (see Praudhamanorama) . astyaprayukta.h iti sAhasamAtrameva (patanjali) .
