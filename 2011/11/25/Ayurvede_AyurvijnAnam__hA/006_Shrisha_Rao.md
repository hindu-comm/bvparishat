I don't believe the description of 100 years of life (also seen in the ईशावास्य's कुर्वन्नेवेह कर्माणि जिजीविषेत शतं समाः, etc.) is meant to be taken as denoting an exact number for years of a life-span. One can also see, for example, a figure of 116 years in the छान्दोग्य:  
  
एतद्ध स्म वै तद्विद्वानाह महिदास ऐतरेयः स किं म एतदुपतपसि योऽहमनेन न प्रेष्यामीति स ह षोडशं वर्षशतमजीवत्प्र ह षोडशं वर्षशतं जीवति य एवं वेद \|\| ३.१६.७ \|\|  
  
(The phrase षोडशं वर्षशतं was interpreted as 1600 years by A.B. Keith but all Vedantic commentators are agreed that it means 116 years only.)  
  
It might thus be better to take such figures as metaphors for the fullness of a complete life-span, rather than a literal and precise indication of extent. न च परिमाणोक्त्या किञ्चित् प्रयोजनम्, as Madhva says under the BG's दिवि सूर्यसहस्रस्य, etc.
