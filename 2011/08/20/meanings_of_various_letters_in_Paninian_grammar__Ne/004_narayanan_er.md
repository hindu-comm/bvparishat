The Kashika of Jayaditya and Vamana uploaded by you in your Sanskrit World site can answer these.

छ (वृद्धाच्छः ४.२.११४), ड, (सप्तम्यां जनेर्डः ३.२.९७), and घ (दादेर्धातोर्घः ८.२.३२), are suffixes having meta-datum to each of them will elide while they function but direct towards the nature of the word to be derived.  

Both in the case of टाप् and डाप् the meta-data ट्, ड्, and प् the common in both will elide and the applied part will be आ in all these cases. Though both are पित् (प् as an eliding element) टाप् is टित् (ट् as an eliding element) and डाप् is डित् (ड् as an eliding element).

अजाद्यतष्टाप्‌ (४.१.४) is the rule for टाप्।

Here Kashika says: अजादिभ्यः प्रातिपदिकेभ्यः अकारान्ताच् च प्रातिपदिकात् स्त्रियां टाप् प्रत्ययो भवति। पकारः सामान्यग्रहणार्थः। टकारः सामान्यग्रहणाविघातार्थः। Here common / general feminine suffix is applied intact.  

डाबुभाभ्यामन्यतरस्याम्‌ (४.१.१३) is the rule for डाप्।

डाप् could be applied optionally with the feminine suffix ङीप्, where you will get an ई ending form of a word.  

Our grammarians can give more thoughts on this regard.
