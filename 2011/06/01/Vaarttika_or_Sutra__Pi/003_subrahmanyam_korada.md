Why such variations ?  
  
No doubt , both are **VArtikas of KAtyAyana .**  
  
Here and there he uses **'prAcAm samjn\~A' -**-  
  
1. **astirbhavantIparo'prayujyamAno'pyasti' (anabhihite , 2-3-1) -- bhavantI = lat.  
  
2. carkarItam ca -- carkarItam = yan'luk**  
  
The authors of **KAs'ikA have got a peculiar habit of throwing VArtikas into SUtras in a number of places .So the variation .  
**  
Here is **Haradattamis'ra , the author of PadamanjarI , under ' vr.ddhasya ca pUjAyAm'** --  
  
**vArtikakArIyam cedam sUtram I vr.ttikAren.a tu sUtres.u praks.iptam I etena uttarasUtram vyAkhyAtam .  
**  
When there is a tie between **kAs'aikA and mahAbhAs.yam** it is the latter that prevails -- **kelimara upasamkhyAnam , pacelimA mAs.A.h , paktavyA ityartha.h , vr.ttikArastu karmakartaricAyamis.yate ityAha , tadbhas.yaviruddhatvAt upeks.yam (tavyattavyAnIyara.h, 3-1-96) .**  
  
Similarly if anything is said by **PadamanjarI etc** and the same is not there in **BhAs.yam** then it is rejected .  
  
**Yathottaram munInAm prAmAn.yam ( dhinvikr.n.vyora ca, 3-1-80 )** - is applicable among Munitrayam only .  
  
But if there are usages that are covered by kAs'ikA , Bhat.t.ojidIks.ita takes them .
