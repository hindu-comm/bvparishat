7.3.118 applies only to इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s which are neither नदी nor घि, only because 7.3.117 applies to इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s termed नदी and 7.3.119 applies to इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s termed घि. Now when the question is what if 7.3.117 was not formulated? In discussing this question, the above situation will not hold as the domain of 7.3.118 will not longer be the same in the absence of 7.3.117.

  

This is the current framework  

7.3.116 ङेराम्नद्याम्नीभ्यः applies to नद्यन्त, आबन्त, and न्यन्त अङ्ग-s.

7.3.117 इदुद्भ्याम् applies to इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s termed नदी

7.3.118 औत् applies to इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s

7.3.119 अच्च घे applies to इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s termed घि

  

By the उत्सर्ग-अपवाद principle, one can further refine the domains of the rules

7.3.116 ङेराम्नद्याम्नीभ्यः applies to नद्यन्त अङ्ग-s that are not इकारान्त and उकारान्त, आबन्त अङ्ग-s, and न्यन्त अङ्ग-s.

7.3.117 इदुद्भ्याम् applies to इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s termed नदी

7.3.118 औत् applies to इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s which are neither नदी nor घि (Example of उकारान्त is not found, butइकारान्त examples are सखि andपति)

7.3.119 अच्च घे applies to इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s termed घि (neuter case is an exception, but we will ignore that for simplicity)

  

As a result of the above,

7.3.116 explains नित्य forms like कुमार्याम्, मालायाम्, वध्वाम्, सेनान्याम्

7.3.117 explains the वैकल्पिक forms like मत्याम्, धेन्वाम्

7.3.118 explains the नित्य forms सख्यौ and पत्यौ

7.3.119 explains the वैकल्पिक forms like मतौ and धेनौ; as well as the
नित्य forms like हरौ and साधौ

\[The optional forms exist because मति, धेनु, etc are optionally नदी in locative singular due to 1.4.6 ङिति ह्रस्वश्च, and optionally घि due to 1.4.7 शेषो घ्यसखि)\]

  

Now without 7.3.118 you would have

7.3.116 ङेराम्नद्याम्नीभ्यः applying to नद्यन्त, आबन्त, and न्यन्त अङ्ग-s.

7.3.118 औत् applying to नद्यन्त अङ्ग-s

7.3.119 अच्च घे applying toनद्यन्त अङ्ग-s (assuming आबन्त, and न्यन्त are not carried over) termed घि

  

In this case, 7.3.118, औत् will prevail being the परकार्य for नदी cases, giving us undesired forms like मत्यौ and धेन्वौ in locative singular (as well as undesired forms like कुमार्यौ and वध्वौ in locative singular). There is a need to restrict the application of 7.3.118 to पति and सखि alone. Furthermore, in this case, forms like हरौ and साधौ will not be achieved as there is a need to change the domain of 7.3.119 to इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s termed घि. But nowhere we have इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s available.

  

Due to the separate formation of 7.3.117 इदुद्भ्याम् and carrying only इदुद्भ्याम् but not नदी into औत्, these problems are avoided.

  

In other words, both 7.3.116 and 7.3.119 are अपवाद-s of 7.3.118 and it is for this reason that 7.3.118 applies only to इकारान्त and उकारान्त अङ्ग-s which are neither नदी nor घि.
