The book Harivamsa itself mentions the name Ascaryaparvan:

आश्चर्यपर्वमखिलं यो हीदं धारयेन्नृपः। नाशुभं प्राप्नुयात्किञ्चिद्दीर्घमायुरवाप्नुयात्॥ 113.82, हरिवंशः॥

  

We also have a commentator referring to it by that name:

इयमाश्चर्यपर्वणि दुर्बोधबोधिनी। टीका विरचिता शुभ्रा सतामास्तां मुदे सदा॥ Found at the end of Arjunamisra's commentary on the Harivamsa, as quoted in p. XIII in Vaidya, Parashuram Lakshman, Ed.,*The Harivamsa,*Vol. 1. Pune: BORI, 1969. (Critical Edition)

  

Further there is a reason for the name: Lord Krishna himself is called as आश्चर्यः in the book, as seen in three different instances:

a\. आश्चर्यश्चैव नान्य़ोऽस्ति कृष्णश्चार्यसनिधिः। सर्वेष्वाश्चर्यकल्पेषु नास्त्याश्चर्यमवैष्णवम्॥113.75॥

  

b\. Narada addressing Krishna:

आश्चर्यं खलु देवानामेकस्त्वं पुरुषोत्तम। धन्यश्चासि महाबाहो लोके नान्योऽस्ति कश्चन॥ 100.22॥

एवमुक्तः स्मितं कृत्वा प्रत्युवाच मुनिं प्रभुः। आश्चर्यश्चैव धन्यश्च दक्षिणाभिः सहेत्यहम्॥100.23॥

  

c\. Later in the same Vishnuparva., the reason why Narada calls Krishna Ascarya is revealed. While bathing in Ganga once, Narada comes across a huge tortoise (अपश्यमश्मकूटाभं कपालद्वयदेहिनम्। क्रोशमण्डलविस्तारं तावद्वृत्तं समन्ततः॥33॥) Impressed with this, Narada approaches the tortoise and says त्वमाश्चर्यशरीरोऽसि कूर्म धन्यश्च मे मतः ॥35॥. The Tortoise refuses the appellations and suggests that Ganga is Ascarya and Dhanya, as Ganga supports this huge Tortoise. So Narada approaches Ganga and praises here as AscaryA and DhanyA, but Ganga in turn refers to the ocean as Ascarya, as she herself merges in the even bigger ocean. So Narada moves on to ocean; and ocean to Earth (ocean is on Earth); Earth to mountains (mountains bear the Earth); mountains to Prajapati (Brahma is the creator of the mountains); Brahma to Vedas (लोकान् धारयन्ति), and finally, Yajnas, referred to by the Vedas as the entity fit for the titles Ascarya and Dhanya (as Vedas have Yajnas as their prayojana), who in turn say that both the words Ascarya and Dhanya properly belong to Vishnu, who is their ultimate resort:

  

अग्निष्टोमादयो यज्ञा मम वाक्यादनन्तरम्। प्रत्यूचुर्मां परं वाक्यं सर्वे यूपध्वजाः स्थिताः॥ 100.77 ॥

आश्चर्यशब्दो नास्मासु धन्यशब्दोऽपि वा मुने। आश्चर्यं परमं विष्णुः स ह्यस्माकं परा गतिः॥100.78॥
