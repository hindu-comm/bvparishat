Without loss of generality, the question may be rephrased as:

  

**What is the meant by अम् in the rule \[अकः\] अमि \[अचि\] पूर्वः \[एकः पूर्वपरयोः\] (पा॰सू॰ ६.१.१०७)****?**

  

I know of five definitions of अम् in the अष्टाध्यायी:

 अम्\[1\]is a प्रत्याहार, used in the rule पुमः खय्यम्परे (पा॰सू॰ ८.३.६)

 अम्\[2\]is the सुप् affix अम्

 अम्\[3\]is the Vedic suffix अमुँ ordained by the rule अमु च च्छन्दसि (पा॰सू॰ ५.४.१२)

 अम्\[4\]is the replacement of मिप् by तस्थस्थमिपां तान्तन्तामः (पा॰सू॰ ३.४.१०१) for ङित् लकारs

 अम्\[5\]is an आगम, used in the rules सृजिदृशोर्झल्यमकिति (पा॰सू॰ ६.१.५८), अनुदात्तस्य चर्दुपधस्यान्यतरस्याम् (पा॰सू॰ ६.१.५९) and अम् सम्बुद्धौ (पा॰सू॰ ७.१.९९)

  

So the question may be extended to cover all five of the above. Let’s broaden the question to:

**  
**

**Which of the five अम्s are intended in the rule अमि पूर्वः (पा॰सू॰ ६.१.१०७)?**

  

The प्रत्याहार अम्\[1\]can be easily ruled out: it will result in many अनिष्ट forms.

  

The affix अम्\[2\]cannot be ruled out: it is certainly meant to be here else we will not get forms like हरिम्, गुरुम् etc and will get the अनिष्ट forms हर्यम्, गुर्वम् etc (like the optional Vedic forms शम्यम्, गौर्यम् effected by वा च्छन्दसि).

  

The affix अम्\[3\]can be ruled out by the परिभाषा निरनुबन्धकग्रहणे न सानुबन्धस्य, just like the नुँट् आगम by \[अङ्गस्य आमि\] ह्रस्वनद्यापो नुट् (पा॰सू॰\~७.१.५४) is ruled out for आमुँ ordained by किमेत्तिङव्ययघादाम्वद्रव्यप्रकर्षे (पा॰सू॰ ५.४.११).

  

In case of the अम्\[4\]replacement of मिप्, I do not recall any उत्तम पुरुष एकवचन form in a ङित् लकारs which involves इ+अम्=इम्/ई+अम्=ईम्/उ+अम्=उम्/ऊ+अम्=ऊम्, unlike forms हरिम्/पपीम्/गुरुम्/हूहूम् in case of the सुप् अम्\[3\]. (For ऋ+अम्=ऋम्/ॠ+अम्= ॠम्/ऌ+अम्=ऌम्, there are no examples for the सुप् अम् also.) So the examples of स्वरसंधि in the case of अम्\[4\]replacement of मिप् would be restricted to अ+अम्=अम् and आ+अम्=आम्. The former result already accounted for अतो गुणे (पा॰सू॰ ६.१.९७) and the latter already accounted for by अकः सवर्णे दीर्घः (पा॰सू॰ ६.१.१०१). Then, what is the use of the ग्रहण of अम्\[4\]replacement of मिप् in the word अमि of the rule \[अकः\] अमि \[अचि\] पूर्वः \[एकः पूर्वपरयोः\] (पा॰सू॰ ६.१.१०७)? Nothing. The ग्रहण serves no purpose as the intended results are already available. Usually a seemingly व्यर्थ ग्रहण in Paninian system is a ज्ञापन. Nobody has interpreted such a ज्ञापन in my limited reading.Hence, my conclusion is that अमि in पा॰सू॰ ६.१.१०७ does not cover the अम् replacement of मिप्, as there is no need for it.

  

I have not had the time to think of अम्\[5\] but will do so in my next post on this thread which may take some time.
