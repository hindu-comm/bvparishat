Dear Sunil Bhattacharya mahodayāḥ,  
  
  

Not only different daṇḍas for different varṇas. They should be in these specifications like:  
.rjavas te tu sarve syur avra.naa.h saumya.dar"sanaa.h \|  
anudvegakaraa n.R.naa.m sa.tvaco .anagniduu.sitaa.h \|\|  
  

The daṇḍa should be straight, without blemishes, pleasing to the eye, not liable to alarm people, with the bark intact and undamaged by fire. I do not think for brahmacārins different numbers of daṇḍas (if you know please enlighten me)  
  

Different mekhalā, upavīta, ajina also.  
  

mekhalā:  
mau\~njii triv.rt samaa "slak.s.naa kaaryaa viprasya mekhalaa \|  
k.satriyasya tu maurvii jyaa vai"syasya "sa.nataantavii \|\|  
mu\~njaalaabhe tu kartavyaa.h ku"saa"smantakabalvajai.h \|  
triv.rtaa granthinaa-ekena tribhi.h pa\~ncabhir eva vaa \|\|  
  

muñja for Brāhmaṇa, maurvī for Kṣatriya and śaṇatānta or śaṇa for Vaiśya. If muñja grass is unavailable kuśa, aśmantaka or balvaja (alternation given only to Brāhmaṇa??). Wrap the girdle around the waist three times and make one, three or five knots.  
  

upavīta:  
  
kaarpaasam upaviita.m syaad viprasya-uurdhvav.rta.m triv.rt \|  
"sa.na.suutramaya.m raaj\~no vai"syasya-aavikasautrikam \|\|  

  

kārpāsa for Brāhmaṇa, śaṇasūtra for Kṣatriya and āvikasūtra for Vaiśya.  
  

ajina:  

  
kaar.s.narauravabaastaani [carmaa.ni](http://carmaa.ni) brahmacaari.na.h \|  
  

(black antelope) kṛṣṇājina for Brāhmaṇa, (ruru deer) raurava for Kṣatriya and (male goat) bāsta for Vaiśya.  
  
  

bhavadīyah  
  

Satyanarāyaṇaḥ
