In Tamil Nadu, the entire Mahabharata at a stretchis performed as afolk-style dance-drama and the performance is called bāradakkūttu (bhārata + kūttu) , performed for several nights. This is associated with the worship of draupadī ammāl̥ ; darmarāchar (Dharmaraja) in folk temples and rituals associated with the worship.

  

Telugu version of this culture, performance, templesand rituals is found in the Chittoor and Nellore districts of Andhra Pradesh boerdering with Tamil Nadu.

  

In Bengaluru one of the huge city-wide fares is the fare of draupadī /dharmarāja temple.
