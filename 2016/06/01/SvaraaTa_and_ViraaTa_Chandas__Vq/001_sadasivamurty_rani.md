Prof. Joshi Ji!

Namaste. Sanskrit Prosody is three fold. 1. अक्षरच्छन्दासि Where the number of syllables in a foot is of sole importance giving no importance to the length of the syllables (Guru and Laghu) and event to the Syllabic instance. All the Vedic Metres come under this class.

2\. गणच्छन्दांसि। These metres are exclusive to the Classical Prosody in Sanskrit where the Ganas य, र, त, भ, ज etc are very much important.

  

3\. मात्राछन्दांसि - These are also exclusive to Classical Prosody only where the syllabic instants are of prime importance ignoring either the number of syllables in a foot or the syllabic order of the Ganas.

  

The metres विराट् and स्वराट् are exclusively found in the Vedic Prosody.

There are certain instances where these metres विराट् and स्वराट् are recommended by Pingala Naga and other Vedic Prosodians.

According to the definitions like  "यदक्षरपरिमाणम् तच्छन्द:" fromकात्यायनमहर्षि's ऋग्वेदसर्वानुक्रमणिका and "छन्दश्शब्देनाक्षरसंख्यावच्छेद: अत्र अभिधीयते" a statement by हलायुध in his पिङ्गलनागछन्दशास्त्रव्याख्यान\|

These definitions affirm that the number of syllables in a foot are very important in identifying a meter of a Mantra.

Here Pingala and other Vedic Prosodians observe that sometimes in some mantras there may be an overall shortage or excess of one or two syllables than the required number of syllables in a particular meter. Even in such instances according to the Rshi and Daivata of the Mantra the meter should be determined to be one that is relevant to the Rshi and Daivata. That shortage is not ignored there but considered as a variety of that relevant meter.

  

Four such meters are identified. They are :

1\. निचृद् (if a syllable shortage is there than the required number)
-

2\. भुरिक़् (if two syllables shortage is there than the required number) -

3\. विराट्(if a syllable is in excess than the required number) -

4\. स्वराट् (if two syllables are in excess than the required number)

Thus one can see meters like: Nicrd Gayatri, Nicrd Ushnih, Nicrd Anusthubh, Bhurik Gayatri, Bhurik Ushnih, Bhurik Anusthubh etc

Here are the relevant Sutras from Pingala's Chandas Sastra

ऊनाधिकेनैकेन निचृद्भुरिजौ। (पिङ्गलछन्दश्शास्त्रे ३/५९ सूत्रम्)

"चतुर्विंशत्यक्षरा गायत्री एकेनाक्षरेण न्यूनेन सा "निचृद्" इति विशेषसंज्ञां लभते। एकेनाधिकेन "भुरिक्" इति। एवमुष्णिगादिष्वपि इष्टव्यम्।" - इति हलायुध:।

द्वाभ्यां विराट्स्वराजौ। (पिङ्गलछन्दश्शास्त्रे ३/६० सूत्रम्)

"।ऊनाधिक। ग्रहणमनुवर्तते। द्वाभ्यामक्षराभ्यां न्यूनाधिकाभ्यां गायत्री यथाक्रमं विराट् -स्वाराट् - संज्ञा भवति। एवमुष्णिगादिषु द्रष्टव्यम्।" - इति हलायुध:।

  

This matter can be seen in the treatises like: ऋक्प्रातिशाख्य, गार्ग्य's उपनिदानसूत्र, षड्गुरुशिष्य's वेदार्थदीपिका etc



**Prof. Rani Sadasiva Murty**
