जै.ब्रा. २.२७२  
तस्माद् अश्व एकैकं पादम् उदकन् तिष्ठति ।  
(my translation):  
Therefrom (तस्मात्), the sacrifice-ready (उदकन्) horse (अश्व:) stands (तिष्ठति), all-leg (एकैकं पादम्).  
  
Here, एकैकं can be taken as अव्यय, but पादम् is unambiguously accusative. This results in the interpretation as given above (i.e., कर्मकर्तृ made explicit by the presence of an accusative qualifying the implicit आत्मानम् / स्वयम् एव).  
  
  
P.S.  
source: https://sa.m.wikisource.org/wiki/जैमिनीयं\_ब्राह्मणम्/काण्डम्\_२/२७१-२८०  
The translation supplied is meant merely as a starting point (not production standard), scholars are welcome to verify and correct. Note: I have replaced उदंकन् with उदकन् (relating to the dual हस्त- and पाद- water offering) in the quoted text.
