नमो विद्वद्भ्यः

  

I would be grateful if you could very kindly teach me what our tradition says and characterises**esoteric**and**profane**literature.

  

                             ---- Vidvan Sati Shankar

  

This is a difficult question in Indian सनातनधर्म context .

  

Every system and school is associated with धर्म in one way or the other.

  

Even चार्वाकs take वैदिकवाक्यम् as प्रमाणम् \|

  

Let us take a survey  --

  

Esoteric is understood and appreciated by limited number of people - say ब्रह्मविद्या । In Andhra we use this term as a synonym of ' a thing impossible to do/achieve - it is not ब्रह्मविद्या । But the rest is not totally profane ( secular).

  

Then let us take ज्ञानम् and विज्ञानम् --

  

मोक्षे धीः ज्ञानम् अन्यत्र विज्ञानं शिल्पशास्त्रयोः -- अमरकोशः ।

  

(The above is a general statement - one should not go too deep - ' विज्ञानघन एतेभ्यः समुत्थाय ’ etc) \|

  

So any system that (directly or indirectly) leads to मोक्ष is esoteric and the other one can / may be profane ?

  

What is the problem ?

  

In Indian culture there is no way to escape -- even a नास्तिक cannot be stamped as अधार्मिक ( ’ धर्मं चरति ’ ठक् , न धार्मिकः अधार्मिकः) । Because भागवतम् (6&7) puts him under वैरभक्ति - eligible for मोक्ष due to hostility with विष्णु ।

  

Panini rules - ' अस्ति नास्ति दिष्टं मतिः ’ ठक् - Patanjali cracks a joke - कुंभकारे अपि प्राप्नोति ? - a कुंभकार has got the मति ’ I am a कुंभकार ’ !

  

Panini apparently does not say or suggest - what मति is अस्ति / नास्ति ।

  

Commentators say - it is परलोक । ’ नास्तिको वेदनिन्दकः ’ - मनुः ।

  

Let us take विज्ञानम् --- शिल्पम् and शास्त्रम् --

  

वीणावादनम् ( ’ तदस्य शिल्पम् ’ ठक् - वैणिकः) । अश्वशास्त्रम् , काकशास्त्रम् , गजशास्त्रम्   लोहशास्त्रम् , धनुर्विद्या , स्थापत्यम् etc.

  

But in every system/school there is धर्म associated .

  

In आयुर्वेद also there is the discussion of पञ्चविंशतितत्त्वानि etc .

  

व्याकरणम् has got two purposes - धर्म and अर्थबोधकत्वम् - but it will be अर्धजरतीयम् (only one part of the lady is liked and the rest is not).

*  
*

Why so ?

  

Indian culture stands on four pillars - धर्म - अर्थ - काम - मोक्ष ।

  

In स्वर्गारोहणपर्व of भारतम् , व्यास shouts --

  

ऊर्ध्वबाहुः विरौमि एष न हि कश्चित् शृणोति माम् ।

धर्मात् अर्थश्च कामश्च स धर्मः किं न सेव्यते ?

  

व्रात्याः - are those who care only for अर्थ and काम ।

  

One more thing - we believe in कर्मसिद्धान्त । Unless and until 'the other person'

comes with a viable alternative (till today he could not and I do not believe that he will come with one tomorrow) he also has to follow our theory. Then this life is bound with कर्म and as such there is no anything profane.

  

This is the uniqueness of Indian culture - this discussion should be an eyeopener to ' others ' . Let them first study all the 14 विद्यास्थानानि ।

  

A good question . Pl feel free .
