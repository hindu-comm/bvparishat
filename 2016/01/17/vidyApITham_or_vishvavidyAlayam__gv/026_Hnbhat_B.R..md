\> None of the Sanskrit words suggested अग्रहारः, घटिका (=synonym of अग्रहार?) suggest the meaning of u·ni·ver·si·ty (yo͞o′nə-vûr′sĭ-tē)  
\> n. pl. u·ni·ver·si·ties  
\> 1. An institution for higher learning with teaching and research facilities typically including a graduate school and professional schools that award master's degrees and doctorates and an undergraduate division that awards bachelor's degrees.  

The first is found in वाचस्पत्य, and it gives as a grain storage for use for the students.

३ स्वकर्म ब्राह्मणस्याध्ययनमध्यापनम्यज्ञो याजनं दानं प्रतिग्रहणं दायाद्यं शिलोञ्छः\|

४ अन्यच्चापरिगृहीतम् ५ एतान्येव क्षत्रियस्याध्यापनयाजनप्रतिग्रहणानीति परिहाप्य दण्डयुद्धाधिकानि ६ क्षत्रियवद्वैश्यस्य दण्डयुद्धवर्जं कृषिगोरक्ष्यवाणिज्याधिकम्  

आपस्तम्ब prescribes अध्यापन, as one of the षट्कर्म of ब्राह्मण. Modern scholars claiming ब्राह्मण by गुणकर्मविभाग, may question this division. In other words, those of Teaching profession, would be called ब्राह्मण, like Professors in University. According to Apastamba, the others are not entitled to teaching profession अध्यापन and exclusively ब्राह्मण-s.  

विश्वविद्यालय is anyhow accepted as the modern equivalent of University and there is no equal word in Sanskrit.
