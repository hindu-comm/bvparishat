Is this what you are looking for–

  

“अमोऽनुनासिका न ह्रौ” इति शिक्षोक्तेर्यण इति नोक्तम्। तेन् सँय्यन्तेत्यादौ परसवर्णनिष्पन्नानुनासिकयवलानाम् “अनचि च” इति द्वित्वं सिद्ध्यति।(लघुशब्देन्दुशेखरः संज्ञाप्रकरणम्)



I have not seen*Cidasthimālā*and other commentaries on the above and cannot say if this is Nageśa’s final or only opinion in this context, but it appears*prima facie*that Nageśa holds that*anaci ca*(8-4-47) results in duplication of*anunāsika*versions of*y*(य्),*v*(व्), and*l*(ल्) also. This duplication can only happen if the set of consonants eligible for this duplication operation includes the*anunāsika*versions of the y, v, and l. The operand for the duplication in 8-4-47 isthe set*yar*, carried from the rule*yaro’nunāsike’nunāsiko vā*(8-4-45). Therefore, the quoted extract implies that the set*yar*includes the*anunāsika*versions of of*y*(य्),*v*(व्), and*l*(ल्), which is possible if and only if one admits of*y*(य्),*v*(व्), and*l*(ल्) to stand for both the*ananunāsika*and*anunāsika*versions.

  

If my thinking is correct, it is here that*y*(य्),*v*(व्), and*l*(ल्) stand for both themselves and their respective*savarṇa*-sin the view ofNageśa.
