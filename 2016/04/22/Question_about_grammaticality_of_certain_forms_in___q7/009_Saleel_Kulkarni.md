Respected Korada Sir has used the wording “अभियुक्तानाम् उपदेशेन” in the email appended below.

That reminds me that of late the word अभियुक्त is often used in official (government’s) Hindi to mean an accused, a criminal etc. The word अभियोग is used to mean a lawsuit or a legal case in the court of law.

The meanings of the word अभियुक्त given in Apte’s dictionary are as shown below.

*p. p.***1**(*a*) Engaged oroccupied or absorbed in, applyingoneself to, intent on;**स्वस्वकर्मण्यधि-कतरमभियुक्तः परिजनः**Mu. 1. (*b*) Diligent, persevering, resolute, zealous, intent, assiduous, zealously engaged, attentive, careful;**इदं विश्वंपाल्यं विधिवदभियुक्तेन मनसा**U. 3. 30;Mu. 1. 13; Dk. 55;**अश्वावेक्षणेऽनभि-**\[Page0136-b+ 57\]**युक्ते**Mu. 3;**भवतु भूंयोभियुक्तः स्वरव्यक्तिमुपलप्स्ये**Mu. 1; Bg. 9. 22; Kàm.5. 77.**— 2**Well-versed or proficientin;**शास्त्रार्थेष्वभियुक्तानां पुरुषाणां**Ku-ma4rila.**— 3**(Hence) Learned, of ac-knowledged position; a competent judge, an expert, connoisseur, alearned person (*m.*also in thissense);**न हि शक्यते दैवमन्यथा कर्तुमभियुक्तेनापि**K. 62;**अन्येऽभियुक्ता अपि नैवेदमन्यथा मन्यंते**Ve. 2;**सूक्तमिदमभियुक्तैः प्रकृतिर्दुस्त्यजेति***ibid.***— 4**Attacked,assailed;**अभियुक्तं त्वयैनं ते गंतारस्त्वा-मतः परे***S*i. 2. 101; Mu. 3. 25.**— 5**Accused, charged, indicted; Mk. 9. 9; prosecuted; a defendant;**अभियुक्तोऽभियोगस्य यदि कुर्यादपह्नवं**Nàrada.**— 6**Appointed.**— 7**Said, spoken.

Out of the total seven meanings given in this entry, the first three are praiseful/praising, the other three (viz. 4, 6, 7) are neutral, while only the fifth one points to meanings such as an accused, charged, indicted, prosecuted, defendant, which have a negative shade.

How common is this (fifth) meaning? What is the first shade of meaning (positive or negative) that comes to the mind of a Sanskrit scholar when he hears this word?

........ **यद्यपि इतिहासपुराणादिषु अपशब्दा अपि संभवन्ति ,** **तथापि येषामेव पूर्वपूर्वव्यकरणवशेन तस्मिन् तस्मिन् काले सत्तानुसन्धानं त एव अनादयः साधवः इति इति निर्णीयन्ते ।**
