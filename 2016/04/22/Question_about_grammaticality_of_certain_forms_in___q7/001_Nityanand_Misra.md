Dear Prof. Deshpande Ji

  

Th is the first verse in the last chapter of the last *skandha* of the *Śrīmadbhagavatam*. The commentaries on the same are attached (1975, ed. Krishna Shankara Shastri, Bhagavata Vidyapeetha, Sola, Ahmedabad).

  

Most commentators explain *stunvanti* as *stuvanti* but do not comment on the non-Pāṇinian nature of the form. The *Anvitārthaprakāśikā*comments that the *śnu*in*stunvanti* is*ārṣa*and adds that a rare *pāṭha*has the reading*tanvanti* instead.

  

Again, most commentators say that the*akāra*-ending for the word*upaniṣad* is *chāndasa*, *arṣa*, or due to the requirements of prosody (*chando’nurodha*). However, in the*Anvitārthaprakāśikā,*Gaṅgāsahāya puts forward a very ingenuous Pāṇinian explanation using the rule *dvandvāccudaṣahāntāt samāhāre* (A 5.4.106). The following derivation by me is a fuller version of the explanation found in the*Anvitārthaprakāśikā*

  

अङ्गानि च पदानि च क्रमाश्चोपनिषदश्चेति अङ्गपदक्रमोपनिषदम्। ‘अध्ययनतोऽविप्रकृष्टाख्यानाम्‌’ (पा॰सू॰ २.४.५) इत्यनेनैकवचनम् (समाहारः)। ‘द्वन्द्वाच्चुदषहान्तात्समाहारे’ (पा॰सू॰ ५.४.१०६) इत्यनेन टच्।

सह अङ्गपदक्रमोपनिषदेन (वेदः) इति साङ्गपदक्रमोपनिषदः। ‘तेन सहेति तुल्ययोगे’ (पा॰सू॰ २.२.२८) इत्यनेन बहुव्रीहिसमासः। ‘सहस्य सः संज्ञायाम्’ (पा॰सू॰ ६.३.७८) इत्यनेन सहस्य सः।

तैः साङ्गपदक्रमोपनिषदैः।

  

It may be noted that one of the examples cited for ‘अध्ययनतोऽविप्रकृष्टाख्यानाम्‌’ (पा॰सू॰ २.४.५) is पदकक्रमकम्, which has the same order of पद and क्रम as in साङ्गपदक्रमोपनिषदैः.

  

Thanks, Nityānanda

  

PS: The*Anvitārthaprakāśikā*is the commentary recommended for self-study of the*Śrīmadbhagavatam*by my Guruji, who knows the entire text of*Śrīmadbhagavatam*and major commentaries on the epic by heart.
