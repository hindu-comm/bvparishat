There is a beautiful self explanatory verse on the word of "Sarva, a epithet of "Siva in a 9th century's Saiva liturgical text i.e. the "Vyomavyapaistotra" composed by Kasmirian saidhantika exegete Ramakantha Bhatta which may be useful in this context:

  

pu.mso bandhanabhayata"s"sara.na.m praaptaanv.r.noti guru sa.mstha.h\|

diik.saabhyupagamato yastu "sarva"sarvaa.stu te namastasmai\|\|(Vyomavyaapistotra, v.64)

  

commentating the verse Vedaj\~naana says:

  

ya"s"siva.h guru.h sa.mstha.h gurvadhikara.nassatvajanabhayata.h sa.msaarabhindeheto diik.saabhuuvagamata.h diik.saa.mgikaaraat "sara.na.m rak.sitaara.m sva.m praaptaat pu.msa.m paripakva.h nalanarrat rak.sati tasmai "sarva te namo.astu\|\|

  

The epithet "Sarva is included in one of the main "Saiva mantra, known as Vyomavyapimantra which is constituted of 91 words those represent various accepts of "Siva.
