Namaami Dr Joshiji.

  

'Etymology' may mean व्युत्पत्ति andनिरुक्ति both. But while translating we use 'grammar' for व्याकरण that gives व्युत्पत्ति or derivationand 'etymology' for निरुक्त that gives निर्वचन। "निरुक्तं हि कार्त्स्न्यं व्याकरणस्य"/ निरुक्त fulfills and compensates where grammar deems its weapons insufficient to arrive at the intended meaning. But how can something be left uncomprehended ? So येन केन वा निर्ब्रूयात्, संस्कारो नाद्रियेत / All that contemplated on the Vedas have difficulty, right from the days of the great BraahmaNa-s. So there are possible variables. It is also so with the post-Vedic commentators.

  

In the dictionaries "यूपः" is found derived from roots 'yu' 'yuk' and 'yU'. In UNaadi "यूयते बध्यते पशुर्येन - यज्ञपशुबन्धनकाष्ठं ".

वैजयन्ती sees it as " यूपोsस्त्री संस्कृतस्तम्भः". Here the word is in neuter, it's rare to come across. Regards.
