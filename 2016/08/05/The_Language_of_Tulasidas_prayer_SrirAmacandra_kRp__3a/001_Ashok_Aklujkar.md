The composer’s intention is not to use a different language but to use a style known as Maṇi-pravāla in which Sanskrit is combined with a regional language (Avadhī in this instance). Until we come to the modern period, India largely had a linguistic situation we can describe as a situation of classical diaglossia — one in which the classical language Sanskrit was widely understood (and revered and enjoyed) but actually employed by fewer people for ordinary communication.
