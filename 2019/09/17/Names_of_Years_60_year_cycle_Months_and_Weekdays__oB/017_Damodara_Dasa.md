\>\>\>\> \<\*Uttara aayane (Jan 14 -\*\*July 13) or Dakshin aayane (July 14 - Jan  
\>\>\>\> 13)\* \>  

\>\>\>\> As per the Vedas, the Vedanga Jyotisha, Puranas, siddhantas and even  
\>\>\>\> modern astronomy, Uttarayana starts from the shortest day of the year  
\>\>\>\> and  
\>\>\>\> Dakshinayana from the longest day of the year for Northern latitudes.  
\>\>\>\> These two phenomena, known as Winter Solstice and Summer Solstice, take  
\>\>\>\> place these days around December 21 and June 21 respectively.  
\>\>\>\> Similarly, the two Vishuvas---Vasanta Sampat and Sharat Sampat, (known  
\>\>\>\> as Vernal Equinox and Autumn Equinox) take place around March 21 and  
\>\>\>\> September 21 these days respectively.  
\>\>\>\> The shortest day of the year viz. the Uttarayana is also known as Makar  
\>\>\>\> Samkranti, the longest day of the year viz. Dakshinayana as Karkata  
\>\>\>\> Samkranti, the Vasanta Sampat viz. Vernal Equinox as Mesha Samkranti  
\>\>\>\> and  
\>\>\>\> Sharat Sampat viz. Autumn Equinox as Tula Samkranti as per all the  
\>\>\>\> Puranas,  
\>\>\>\> Shastras, and siddhantas without exception.  
\>\>\>\> Even the Tantra-Aloka by the one and only Acharya Abhinavgupta of tenth  
\>\>\>\> century and his commentator Jayaratha of 12th century have linked the  
\>\>\>\> yogic kriyas to the same so called sayana phenomena.  
\>\>\>\> Then again, Ananda-Bodha Yati, the venerated commentator of the Yoga  
\>\>\>\> Vasishtha Maharamayana (of about sixteenth century) also has linked  
\>\>\>\> Uttarayana etc. phenomena to the shortest and longest days of the year  
\>\>\>\> and  
\>\>\>\> so on.  

\>\>\>\> In short, there is absolutely no so called \*nirayana\* phenomenon in any  
\>\>\>\> of the shastras. That is why I call it \*niraadhar.\*
