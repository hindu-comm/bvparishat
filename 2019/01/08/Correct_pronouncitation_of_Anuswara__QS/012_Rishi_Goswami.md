आपद्यते मकारो रेफोष्मप्रत्ययेष्वनुस्वारम्, यवलेषु परसवर्णं, स्पर्शेषु परेषु चोत्तमापत्तिम्। (नारदीयशिक्षा 2.4.4)  
Next line in Pa.Shi commentary is:  
अनुस्वारद्वयग्रहणं चतुष्षष्टिसंख्यामास्कन्दति "अनुस्वारौ विसर्गश्चे"ति(1) पाठात्।

Here 2 anuswara have been accepted. NaShi deals with Monuswarah.

Apadyate Makarah, Makara becomes anuswara when reph, sha Shha sa and Ha are adjacent, Makara becomes parasavarna when yavala are adjacent and Makara obtains the "antimApatti" or last letter of Varga when Sparsha is adjacent.

So if anuswara are 2, the second is little unclear to me.
