The Vajrasuchi Upanishad ([Sanskrit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanskrit_language): वज्रसूची उपनिषत्, [IAST](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IAST): Vajrasūcī Upaniṣad) is a Sanskrit text and an [Upanishad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upanishad) of [Hinduism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism). It is classified as one of the 22 Samanya Upanishads, and identified as a [Vedanta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vedanta) text.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrasuchi_Upanishad#cite_note-FOOTNOTETinoco199687-3)[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrasuchi_Upanishad#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAiyar1914vii-2) It is attached to the [Samaveda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samaveda).[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrasuchi_Upanishad#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAiyar1914110-4)[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrasuchi_Upanishad#cite_note-FOOTNOTETinoco199687-3)  
  
The text discusses the four [varnas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Varna_(Hinduism)) ([caste system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caste_system_in_India)).[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrasuchi_Upanishad#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAiyar1914111%E2%80%93112-5)[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrasuchi_Upanishad#cite_note-6) It is notable for being a sustained philosophical attack against the division of human beings,[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrasuchi_Upanishad#cite_note-moffredi442-7) and for asserting that any human being can achieve the highest spiritual state of existence.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrasuchi_Upanishad#cite_note-FOOTNOTEAiyar1914111%E2%80%93112-5)[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrasuchi_Upanishad#cite_note-apeetush596-8)[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vajrasuchi_Upanishad#cite_note-FOOTNOTERadhakrishnan1953937%E2%80%93938-9)
