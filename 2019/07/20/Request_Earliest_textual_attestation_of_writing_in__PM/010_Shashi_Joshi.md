Megh ji,

Writing/Script is a specific form of 'drawing', drawing lines - straight or curved. Cave drawings are also writing, they are visual, and meaning is straight forward.

Script on the other hand is more evolved in its meaning. Egyptians had picture script, others had 'script' script.

  

'likh' in its very raw sense means to make a scratch, a streak, line in sand, on stone, ...

  

How far back was the word used, may help in deciding what it may have meant.

  

By the way, I am in no way asserting that in the atharvaveda excerpt likhitam is 'drawn', for I am not scholar enough to do that; but I am surely asserting the *possibility* of a different interpretation, for I am curious and open enough to possibilities. And this is precisely where I welcome feedback from the vidvan-s!
