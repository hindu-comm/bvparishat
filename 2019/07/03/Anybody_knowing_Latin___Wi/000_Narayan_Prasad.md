Dear Scholars,

  Namaste.

  I am presently translating the Russian work "Путешествие из Петербурга в Москву" (1790) (Journey from St. Petersburg to Moscow) by Aleksandr Radischev.

  Radischev has quoted in Russian translation what Akiba said. The original appears to be in Latin whose French translation appeared first in 1697 and the second edition in 1702. The latter's English translation appeared in 1734 (First Volume). The relevant portion is attached for ready reference. Please see footnote \[F\].

  Part of the Latin text also appears in some Hindu scripture. But I can't remember where I read that.

  I reproduce here the Latin text from the book. Since I do not know Latin, I would request you to kindly correct it, if something is wrong anywhere.

'\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*

\[QUOTE\]

Dixit R. Akiba, ingressus sum aliquando post Rabbi Josuam in sedis secretæ locum, et tria ab eo didici. Didici I. quod non versus Orientem et Occidentem, sed versus septentrionem et austrum convertere nos debeamus. Didici II. quod non in pedes erectum, sed jam considentem se retegere liceat. Didici III. quod podex non dextra sed sinistra manu abstergendus sit. Ad haec objecit ibi Ben Hasas; usque adeo vere perfricuisti frontem erga magistrum tuum ut cacantem observares? Respondet ille, legis haec arcana sunt ad quae discenda id necessario mihi agendum fuit.
