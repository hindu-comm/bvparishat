Dear Shri Sharmaji,

Jai Shri Ram!

\<According to the dictionary Vācaspatyam (entries on Āstika and Nāstika), Āstika is the one who believes in the existence of paraloka, while the Nāstika is the one who believes in the nonexistence of paraloka, of Īśvara and adṛṣṭa. If these definitions are true, then the Sāṅkhya of Īśvarakṛṣṇa in the Sāṅkhyakārikā (where I didn’t find any mention of an adṛṣṭa, a paraloka or even an Īśvara), should be categorised as a Nāstika darśana>

The difference between a nastika and aastika has been clearly explained in the sixteenth Adyaya of the Gita, with the only difference that nastikas have been classified as of Asuri Sampat whereas Aastikas are of Daivi Sampat. Both are poles apart from one another.

\<It is even clearly mentioned in the text that an ānuśravika or vaidika solution to the duḥkha is impermanent, and hence arises a need for a permanent solution, which implies that the Vedas are not being thought of as infallible or ultimate.>

Regarding the Vaidika solution to the dukha being impermanent, it is a fact that has been reiterated by Bhagwan Krishna Himself in the second Adyaya of the Gita, the colophon of which reads as "sankhya yogo naama dwiteeyo adyayah"

यामिमाम् पुप्ष्पितां वाचं प्रवदन्त्यविपश्चितः \| वेदवादरताः पार्थ नान्यदस्तीतिवादिनः \|\|42\|\|

कामात्मानः स्वर्गपरा जन्मकर्मफलप्रदाम् \|क्रियाविशेषबहुलां भोगैश्वर्यगतिं प्रति\|\|43\|\|

भोगैश्वर्य प्रसख्तानाम् तयाअपहृतचेतसाम् \| व्यय्सायात्मिका बुद्धिः समाधौ न विधीयते \|\|44\|\|

त्रैगुण्य विषया वेदा निस्त्रैगुण्यो भवार्जुन\|\|निर्द्वन्द्वो नित्यसत्त्वस्थो निर्योग क्षेम आत्मवान्\|\|45\|\|

Then again in 9th Adyaya, it has been said further

त्रैविद्या मां सोमपाः पूतपापायज्ञैरिष्ट्वा स्वर्गतिं प्रार्थयन्ते \|\| ते पुण्यमासाद्य सुरेन्द्रलोकमश्नन्ति दिव्यान् दिवि देवभोगान् \|\|20\|\|

ते तं भुक्त्वा स्वर्गलोकं विशालंक्षीणे पुण्ये मर्त्यलोकं विशन्ति \|\|एवंत्रयी धर्ममनुप्रपन्नागतागतं कामकामा लभन्ते \|\|21\|\|

Thus it is only the Vedanta wherein lies the final salvation!

With regards and Jai Shri Ram!  

A K Kaul
