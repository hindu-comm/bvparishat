Sanskrit does connect almost all Indian languages. Let me take an example of a stanza from a Hindi poem, of Sri Maithili Sharan Gupta -

चारुचंद्र की चंचल किरणें, खेल रहीं हैं जल थल में,  
स्वच्छ चाँदनी बिछी हुई है अवनि और अम्बरतल में।  
पुलक प्रकट करती है धरती, हरित तृणों की नोकों से,  
मानों झूमरहे हैं तरु भी, मन्द पवन के झोंकों से॥

---------

See how, almost all words here are from Sanskrit. If we know our mother tongue well, we will definitely know a little bit of Sanskrit anyway.. So, teaching Sanskrit in school, does not only connect all, but makes one appreciate their mother tongue as well..Also, most of us India do know more than 1 language. For example, I know Kannada, Hindi, Nepali (and understand Marathi), English and Sanskrit.. Sanskrit words overlap almost all the Indian languages listed here. If one understands the nuances of grammar, one can easily catch up with Sanskrit. So, it definitely is a connector. I rest my case here :)
