Namaste,

1\) The term kaShAya appears in the ChAndogya upaniShad 7.26.2 तस्मै मृदितकषायाय तमसस्पारं दर्शयति भगवान्सनात्कुमारः. shankarAchArya in his commentary says कषायो रागद्वेषादिदोषः - kaShAya are the defects in the mind such as attachment, aversion, etc.

2\) gauDapAdAchArya in his mANDUkya kArika 3.44 in outlining the defects of the mind faced during meditation that are to be overcome lists kaShAya along with laya and vikShepa:

लये सम्बोधयेच्चित्तं विक्षिप्तं शमयेत्पुनः ।  
सकषायं विजानीयात्समप्राप्तं न चालयेत् ॥ ४४ ॥  
While listing the remedies for laya (dullness) and vikShepa (scattered mind) as awakening / calming respectively, he does not mention the remedy for kaShAya, which are more deeply embedded defects of the mind. shankarAchArya in his commentary says: एवं पुनः पुनरभ्यासतो लयात्सम्बोधितं विषयेभ्यश्च व्यावर्तितम् , नापि साम्यापन्नमन्तरालावस्थं सकषायं सरागं बीजसंयुक्तं मन इति विजानीयात् । ततोऽपि यत्नतः साम्यमापादयेत् ।  
When the mind is thus awakened from a state of inactivity, and turned away from sense objects, but despite this it has not attained a state of equilibrium, know that to be a state of kaShAya where the mind is an intermediate state that is still embedded with desires in a seed form. The mind has to be with effort brought into a state of equilibrium.

3)In the Vedanta sAra this is further explained:

एवमस्याङ्गिनो निर्विकल्पकस्य लयविक्षेपकषायरसास्वादलक्षणाश्चत्वारो विघ्नाः सम्भवन्ति । लयस्तावदखण्डवस्त्वनवलम्बनेन चित्तवृत्तेर्निद्रा । अखण्डवस्त्वनवलम्बनेन चित्तवृत्तेरन्यावलम्बनं विक्षेपः । लयविक्षेपाभावेऽपि चित्तवृत्तेः रागादिवासनया स्तब्धीभावादखण्डवस्त्वनवलम्बनं कषायः । अखण्डवस्त्वनवलम्बनेनापि चित्तवृत्तेः सविकल्पकानन्दास्वादनं रसास्वादः ।  
The four obstacles to the impartite cognition of the self are listed - laya (inertness), vikShepa (excessively scattered mind), kaShAya (mind embedded with latent impressions caused due to desires etc), and rasAsvAda (addiction to the pleasure arising in meditation).
