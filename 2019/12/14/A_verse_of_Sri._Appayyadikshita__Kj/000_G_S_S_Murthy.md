Respected scholars,

This is a verse from Varadarajastava of Sri.Appayyadikshita.

नाथ त्वदीयमकलङ्कमिमं मुखेन्दुम्

आपीय तृप्यति सदा वसुधा यतस्ते ।

तेनैव किं नवसुधारसगोचरोऽभूत्

इन्दुः कलङ्कमलिनीकृतमध्यभागः ॥ ८७ ॥

अ : नाथ ! वसुधा त्वदीयम् अकलङ्कम् मुखेन्दुं आपीय तृप्यति यतः कलङ्क-मलिनीकृत-मध्य-भागः इन्दुः तेन एव वसुधा-रस-गोचरः न अभूत् किम्? नव-सुधा-रस-गोचरः अभूत् किम्?

A word by word translation leads to something like this:

“Lord! The earth feels contented after drinking your blemish-free moon-like face. Is it the reason, that the moon with its central part getting soiled by the blemish is not seen by the earth (has acquired the new ambrosia)?”

What does the poet want to convey? Is this translation wrong?

I would be grateful for any help.

Thanks and regards,

Murthy
