Nakṣatra devatā and nakṣatreṣṭi are well known as Vedic. Graheṣṭi (9-planet/Navgraha homa) is known through the Kāṭhakabrāhmaṇa and the Atharva-parishista. Mantra for Ṛtu & Ahorātra can also be located in some of the Vedic texts. But, *Tithi-devatā* concept is not widely known.One can perhaps interpret sinīvālī, kuhū, anumatī and rākā, but nothing is found about other tithis.

The Parāśara Tantra and the Vṛddhagārgīya Jyotiṣa are the only two ancient Vedāṅga texts that give the names of the deities for the fifteen tithis. The Jaina Sūrya-candra prajñapti (c 5^(th) cent BC) also knows this concept. Indirectly we know that the concept should be Vedic since several Gṛhayasūtra texts refer to *tithidevatā* and Homa
