I humbly feel, source of Samputikarana-slokas may be of two types - internal and external. For instance - अर्थसिद्धिं तु वैदेह्याः पश्याम्यहमुपस्थिताम् । राक्षसेन्द्रविनाशञ्च विजयं राघवस्य च ।। (5.27.47) are the words spoken by Trijata. They are within the text. Slokas like - सीतां विदेहतनयां रामस्य दयितानघाम् । हनूमता समाश्वस्तां भूमिजां शरणं भजे ।। are taken from an external source (Janaki Saranagati Pancakam).  

  

There are other samputikarana-slokas, particularly for Sundaram. For instance, 'Jaya Pancakam,' which starts with जयत्यतिबलो रामो लक्ष्मणश्च महाबलः । राजा जयति सुग्रीवो...। This was spoken by Hanuman himself. Books of Gita press lists some of such slokas. However, deeksha-Guru prescribes what is important to the saadhaka.  

  

Scholars may throw more light on the original question, सुप्रसन्नः प्रसन्नात्मा... ।  

It is a different issue why one needs samputikarana-slokas.
