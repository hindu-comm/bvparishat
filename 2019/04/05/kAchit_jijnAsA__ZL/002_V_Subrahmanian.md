Dear Sri Amod,

  

While punarukti is a dosha when it is unwarranted, it is not a defect when the context demands. For example one of the six types of tatparya lingas (factors that are used to determine the purport of a text) is abhyaasa: repetition/iteration. Here, the repeated mention of a particular idea shows that this idea is central to that text. There are other occasions when punarukti is not a defect, as for example: The commentary of Adi Shankaracharya for the Bh.gita verse 2.24:

  

अच्छेद्योऽयमदाह्योऽयमक्लेद्योऽशोष्य एव च।  
  
नित्यः सर्वगतः स्थाणुरचलोऽयं सनातनः।।2.24।।  



  

नैतेषां श्लोकानांपौनरुक्त्यंचोदनीयम् , यतः एकेनैव श्लोकेन आत्मनः नित्यत्वमविक्रियत्वं चोक्तम्[‘न जायते म्रियते वा’ (भ. गी. २। २०)](http://advaitasharada.sringeri.net/display/bhashya/Gita?page=2&id=BG_C02_V20&hl=%E0%A4%A8%20%E0%A4%9C%E0%A4%BE%E0%A4%AF%E0%A4%A4%E0%A5%87%20%E0%A4%AE%E0%A5%8D%E0%A4%B0%E0%A4%BF%E0%A4%AF%E0%A4%A4%E0%A5%87%20%E0%A4%B5%E0%A4%BE)इत्यादिना। तत्र यदेव आत्मविषयं किञ्चिदुच्यते, तत् एतस्मात् श्लोकार्थात् न अतिरिच्यते ; किञ्चिच्छब्दतः पुनरुक्तम् , किञ्चिदर्थतः इति। दुर्बोधत्वात् आत्मवस्तुनः पुनः पुनः प्रसङ्गमापाद्य शब्दान्तरेण तदेव वस्तु निरूपयति भगवान् वासुदेवः कथं नु नाम संसारिणामसंसारित्वबुद्धिगोचरतामापन्नं सत् अव्यक्तं तत्त्वं संसारनिवृत्तये स्यात् इति ॥ २४ ॥  

  

Translation by Swami Gambhirananda:

  

It is not to be argued that 'these verses are repetitive since eternality and changelessness of the Self have been stated in a single verse itself, "Never is this One born, and never does It die," etc. (2.20). Whatever has been said there about the Self does not go beyond the meaning of this verse. Something is repeated with those very words, and something ideologically.' *Since the object, viz the Self, is inscrutable, therefore Lord Vasudeva raises the topic again and again, and explains that very object in other words so that, somehow, the unmanifest Self may come within the comprehension of the intellect of the transmigrating persons and bring about a cessation of their cycles of births and deaths.** *
