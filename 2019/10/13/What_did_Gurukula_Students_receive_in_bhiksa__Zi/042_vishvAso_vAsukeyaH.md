> > 2\. ISKCON and Gaudiya Vaisnavas follow pancharatras; that is > absoutely correct. But there is a misconception that pancharatras can > fully replace Sruti and smrti traditions in all its aspects. That is > not possible. I would like to ask one question to make it easier to > understand. Say there is a society that is running based on > pancharatra scriptures alone. What will be different punishments that > king would inflict on the subjects when they committ some crime? Will > there be someone like king or not? If king is not there then which > type of government - democratic, communistic, etc. will it have? There > are many such questions that pancharatras do not guide one at all. > Similar is the case with Parasara Smrti. In the beginning itself it > says that in Kaliyuga Parasara Smrti is the smrti to be followed. > However, it doesn't guide in majority of the practical points, i.e. it > is not a comprehensive guide to run a society or nation. However, if > you take Manu Samhita and Yajnavalkya samhita, they are the most > comprehensive guidance that are available with commentaries making > them understandable from sruti smrti context.
> > 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > **So what does it mean to say that in Kaliyuga Parasara Smrti is to be > followed and vaisnavas should follow pancaratras?** >
> 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > It means that there are some specific differences in some rules that > result from different adhikara, faith and situation that are mentioned > specifically in these granthas and they are to be followed by the > follower of that particular grantha (not giving precedence to Manu and > Yajnavalkya's guidance for those particular cases). For instance, the > prayascittas to be performed for some sins are mentioned differently > by Parasara; so in kaliyuga, for those particular sins prayascitta is > to be suggested (by brahmana) from Parasara and not from Manu. > Similarly for many sins there are prayascittas mentioned in > pancaratras (that don't involve independent demigod worship); so the > vaishnavas who follow pancaratras are to perform those prayascittas > and not those given in Manu etc. for same sins. >
> 
> > 
> > But there are many many (vast majority) of fields for which neither > Parasara nor pancaratras give any guidance.
> > 
> > 
> >   
> > 
> > 
> > Thus, if one wants to form and guide a society then one must resort to > Manu etc. scriptures otherwise one will just be dependent on one's > mental speculation (pratyaksa and anumana only) or someone's else's > speculation. That will not be sastra vidhi and will certainly result > in ultimate failure (Bg 16.23-24). >
