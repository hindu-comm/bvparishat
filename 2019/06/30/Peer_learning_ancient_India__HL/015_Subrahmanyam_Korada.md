२. छत्रादिभ्यो णः (पा 4-4-62 ) --- छात्रः

  

गौणार्थ is to be taken -- छत्रमिव च्छत्रम् --

  

गुरुणा शिष्यः छत्रवत् छाद्यः । शिष्येण गुरुः छत्रमिव परिपाल्यः --- **महाभाष्यम्**

  

So although both have to emulate an umbrella , the प्रत्यय denotes शिष्य only .

  

**लोकः** ---

  

In गुरुकुलसंप्रदाय -- both गुरु and शिष्य have to live nearby -- together .

  

Another important point is --

  

the गुरु should not behave with superiority complex ( I have asked people in our University to remove Dr / Prof etc

on the name plates) .

  

In this परिषत् everyone is a विद्वान् / विदुषी - irrespective of his / her credentials ( I discussed this aspect sometime last year ) -- this is not my invention --- it is there in the age old tradition .

  

**Panini says -- the one who knows and the one who is learning व्याकरणम् -- both are वैयाकरण - s -- तदधीते तद्वेद**

**(पा 4-2-58 )**
