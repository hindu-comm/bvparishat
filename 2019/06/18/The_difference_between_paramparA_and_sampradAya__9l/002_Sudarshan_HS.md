When you refer to *practical usage*, it could either be traditional usage or contemporary usage.

  

In **contemporary usage**, both in लोकभाषाः as well as in संस्कृतं, it appears to me that -

> 
> > One or more सम्प्रदायः will be a part of a larger परम्परा । >
> 

> 
> > As in: वैदिकपरम्परायां शाक्तसम्प्रदायः स्मार्तसम्प्रदायात् भिद्यते । >
> 

To look at a few examples in contemporary usage in Samskrtam, one may do a ctrl-F looking for those words in one of the [सम्भाषणसन्देशः magazine issues](https://sambhashanasandesha.in/public/data/2014/05/index.pdf).

  

  

The **traditional practice** appears to follow the meanings as described in Monier-Williams by you.

>

> 

> 
> > मन्त्रिणामपि हि मन्त्रिणो भवन्ति, तेषामप्यन्ये ॥ सैषा मन्त्रिपरम्परा > मन्त्रं भिनत्ति ॥ अ॰ शा॰॥ >
> 

> राज्यनाशः सुहृत्त्यागो भर्यातनयविक्रयः । प्राप्ता चण्डालता चेयमहो > दुःखपरम्परा॥ मा॰ पु॰ ८.१०१॥
