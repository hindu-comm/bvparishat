नमो विद्वद्भ्यः

  

It is ok to see towards rising and setting the sun because rays of the sun are traveling through long stretch of atmosphere. That filters out harmful ultraviolet radiation. In Midday. the stretch of atmosphere above our head is short. So it is not healthy for eyes look at the sun in 12 noon.

                                   
                           ------ Vid Joshi

  

**पूर्वमीमांसा to आयुर्वेद** ---

  

I rather think I did not explain the nuances of the verse --

  

it is not about **seeing / looking** -- but about **staring** , ie looking in a fixed manner with eyes wide open .

  

another point -- it is about ---- rising Sun -- उद्यन्तम् -- शतृप्रत्ययः - लडर्थे - present continuous tense -- not उदितम् \|  

  

अस्तंयन्तम् -- same as above -- not अस्तंगतम् ।

  

                   \*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*\*

  

A further explanation from the scholars of this practice will be highly appreciated

  

                                   
               ----- Vid Siddharth

  

We prefer to perform सन्ध्यावन्दनम् etc during सन्ध्याकाल (this practice has been there since time immemorial) -- one should expose his body to sunlight - and today we come across people suffering from skin diseases and the prescription offered by Doctors is - exposure to sunlight . Sitting in air-conditioned rooms ( in car, office , house , school etc) would certainly affect the resistance power considerably .  

  

The question is about ' staring ' rather than ' exposure ' . Even 'exposure' should be limited -- अति सर्वत्र वर्जयेत् ।

  

बालार्को प्रेतधूमश्च वृद्धा स्त्री पल्वलोदकम् ।

रात्रौ दध्यन्नभुक्तिश्च आयुःक्षीणं दिने दिने ॥ ( वीप्सायां द्विरुक्तिः -- **’ नित्यवीप्सयोः ’** - पाणिनिसूत्रम् )

  

वृद्धार्को होमधूमश्च बाला स्त्री निर्मलोदकम् ।

रात्रौ क्षीरान्नभुक्तिश्च आयुर्वृद्धिर्दिने दिने ॥

  

बालातपः प्रेतधूमः वर्ज्यं भिन्नं तथासनम् ।

न छिन्द्यात् नखलोमानि दन्तैः नोत्पाटयेत् नखान् ॥ मनु 4-69
