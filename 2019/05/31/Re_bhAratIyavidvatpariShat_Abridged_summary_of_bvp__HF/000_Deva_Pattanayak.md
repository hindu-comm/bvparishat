My view of prakriti and purusha

In classical physics matter was considered to be a different entity than energy. While energy was considered to be a continuous field, matter was considered discrete. In quantum mechanics, it was necessary to quantize energy. An electron while moving around the nucleus, occupies discrete energy levels which are known as stationary or unchanging. Whenever transitions occurs between these states light with a specific wavelength or color in the visible spectrum(the rainbow) appears.

This discrete nature of the energy state of the electron inside an hydrogen atom does not however mean that the wavefunction of the electron occupies discrete position. By wave function of an electron can be loosely considered to its "prakriti".

The point I like to highlight here is that the wave function of the electron is a continuous function of space and time, while all its stationary ( non-varying) energy levels may be loosely connected to its "purusha". The electron transitions to a different stationary energy level having a different "prakriti" and the emission of light( creation of a light particle or photon), but the energy levels of the hydrogen atom remains intact.

I find this analogy of prakriti and purusha with the wavefunction and the set of infinite energy levels of an hydrogen atom (which is a bound state of an electron and a proton) quite interesting.

The infiniteness and unchanging aspect of purusha like the infinite set of energy levels of the hydrogen atom are however necessary for the prakriti to function according to its nature(gunas) while not making any change in the purusha. The existence of infinite set of energy levels was necessary for the emission of light from the hydrogen atom. Incidentally more than 75% of the mass of Sun is attributed to hydrogen!

Devanarayan

ps; This is my thinking and a possible connection between the Vedic concepts and modern scientific knowledge. In my opinion prakriti and purusha together make the universe("jagat") and interestingly one is changeable( "khyara") and the other is ever present and unchangeable ( " Akhyara").
