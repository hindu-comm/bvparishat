As Dr. Bhat has pointed out, these are well established terms. There is a difference between *anunāsika* and *nāsikya*. The pronunciation of *anunāsika* involves both the oral track and the nasal passage, and this is clearly defined by Pāṇini: मुखनासिकावचन: अनुनासिक:. On the other hand, the *anusvāra* is defined as being a purely nasal sound (नासिका अनुस्वारस्य) and some texts call it *nāsikya*. The sounds ङ् , ञ्, ण्, न्, म्, यॅं, वॅं, लॅं and nasalized vowels are *anunāsika*using both the oral and the nasal tracks.
