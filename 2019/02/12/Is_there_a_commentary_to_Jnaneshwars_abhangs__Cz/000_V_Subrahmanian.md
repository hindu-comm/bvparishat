Saint Jnaneshwar's abhangs are very popular. Is there any authentic, scholarly, commentary, in whatever language, for these abhangs? I found this translation on the net for one of his very popular songs:  

Could someone give reference to any reliable commentary?  
  

Thanks  

subrahmanian.v  

कानडा राजा पंढरीचा  
वेदांनाही नाही कळला अंतपार याचा

> The Kanada king of Pandhari (Pandharpur)  
> Even Vedas couldn’t gauge his bounds.

निराकार तो निर्गुण ईश्वर  
असा प्रकटला असा विटेवर  
उभय ठेविले हात कटीवर  
पुतळा चैतन्याचा

> That formless, attribute less, God,  
> how he appeared like this on a brick,  
> keeping both his hands on his waist,  
> The idol of consciousness.

परब्रम्ह हे भक्तासाठी  
मुके ठाकले भीमेकाठी  
उभा राहिला भाव सावयव  
जणु कि पुंडलिकाचा

> For his devotees, this Parabramha (The absolute),  
> standing quietly on the banks of Bheema river.  
> Apparently, there stood a bodily form of  
> Pundalika’s devotion. ([Pundalik - > Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pundalik))

हा नाम्याची खीर चाखतो  
चोखोबांची गुरे राखतो  
पुरंदराचा हा परमात्मा  
वाली दामाजीचा

> He tastes Namya’s kheer (Saint Namdeo’s pudding, [Namdev - > Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Namdev)),  
> He herds Chokhoba’s cattle (Saint [Chokhamela - > Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chokhamela)),  
> He is the supreme soul of Purandar (Lord Vishnu, the majestic),  
> The protector of Damaji (Saint [Damaji - > Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damaji#Life)).
