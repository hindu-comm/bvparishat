The English used in nineteenth century works to describe Sanskrit has certain quaint usages. Example: Aorist and Patient (for karma), as a sort of pratiyogi for Agent (karta). Very few present English speakers seem to know what is absolute locative (sati saptami), or even cases, like dative and ablative. Similarly the word "moods" (used for certain lakaaras) itself needs to be explained to the current English speakers, using Sanskrit sentences like tathaastu! Same is the case with personal terminations (atmanepadi and parasmaipadi). This must be due to the significantly decreased knowledge of classical languages like Greek and Latin amongst English speakers as Prof. Deshpande suggested.

  

In all these cases it is much better to use the Sanskrit word directly, in my opinion.
