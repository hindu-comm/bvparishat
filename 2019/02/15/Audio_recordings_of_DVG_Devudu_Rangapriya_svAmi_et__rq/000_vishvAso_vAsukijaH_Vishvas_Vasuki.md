A couple kind people have read, recorded and published audio tracks of some classic kannada books for the benefit of listeners such as I, who would otherwise have been much poorer for not having had the time to read them. Though there are some flaws here and there (especially when it comes to reading sanskrit shloka-s), it is overall a great boon, in my experience of listening to them during my long walks. The recordings were mechanically normalized, renamed according to chapter-title, sped up 1.5x and curated at the below locations - please enjoy:

If you would like to contribute to this effort, or want to do something similar in a different language, or if you want to suggest books of similar caliber for future reading, please let us know offline (and not on some mailing list which I may visit only occassionally). One doesn't need to be tech-savvy - and all one needs is some time for pleasure, discipline and a mobile phone with an internet connection for uploading. Even aged retired people have contributed recordings without any trouble.  

  

Homepage for this above project is <https://sanskrit.github.io/projects/audio/kannada-audio/> .

  

----------------------  

  

Separately, as previously announced, if you have excellent sanskrit pronunciation, interest and time to contribute towards recording the entire mahAbhArata (we've completed 6k shlokas 170+ sargas of Adi-parva so far) while meeting our high recording standards ( <https://sanskrit.github.io/projects/audio/tips/#good-recording> ) , please contact me offline.
