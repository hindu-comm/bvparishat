There is a work named कृषिपराशर. It is a written in Sanskrit, dealing,
in 240 and odd verses, with agriculture as the name suggests. It is ascribed to a sage पराशर. Its date is connected with the identification of the sage. Scholars hold different opinions regarding the date. It varies from 4^(th) century BCE to 4^(th) century CE. It is a kind of ready-reckoner for an Indian farmer showing geographical and climatic conditions. It gives the basics and the names of seasons and months prevalent in ancient India. Some related astronomical and meteorological data are also supplied. It deals with several agricultural aspects like management of cattle, seed collection and preservation, agricultural processes like plowing etc.
