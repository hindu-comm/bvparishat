Interesting. In modern Kannada and Telugu that I have seen, I have come across mostly anusvara +consonant in place of anunāsika (i.e., pañcama varṇa) +consonant.

For example:

Pāṃḍava in place of Pāṇḍava (ಪಾಂಡವ, పాండవin place of ಪಾಣ್ಡವ, పాణ్డవ)

In Malayalam, we use anunāsika (i.e., pañcama varṇa) +consonant, except when the consonant happens to be kh, g, and gh.

For example പാണ്ഡവ(Pāṇḍava) not പാംഡവ (Pāṃḍava).

However, it is ശംഖ (śaṃkha), ഗംഗ (gaṃga), and സംഘ (saṃgha).
