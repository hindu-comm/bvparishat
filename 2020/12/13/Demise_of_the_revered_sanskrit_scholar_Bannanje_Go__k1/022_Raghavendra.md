\<\<\<I knew Vidyāvācaspati Padmaśrī Dr.h.c. Bannanje Govindacharya for the past 30 years as a great friend, a wonderful mentor, a profound scholar with an amazing memory, a brilliant author in Kannada and Sanskrit, and a fine human with a delightful sense of humour>\>\>

  

These poignant words of Dr. Robert Zydenbos is indeed soul-stirring. He has beautifully brought out the fond memories of the man whom he knew for about 3 decades. We all echo it.

  

\<\<\<Among his great works is his edition of the collected writings of Madhvācārya in the original redaction (the mūlapāṭha, not the corrupted pracalitapāṭha that became the basis of most of the later commentaries). He was what we may call a critical traditionalist: he loved the Mādhva Vaiṣṇava tradition into which he was born, but was highly critical of aberrations which crept in centuries after Madhva's time.>\>\>

  

Between 1961 and 1972, विद्यावाचस्पति-श्री-बन्नंजे-गोविन्दाचार्य single handedly undertook a massive project involving redaction of सर्वमूल or the collected works of श्रीमद्-आनन्दतीर्थ-भगवद्पादाचार्य from an old manuscript which was well preserved and dates back to the times of the भगवान्-आचार्य-पूज्य-पाद.

  

The texts of सर्वमूल redacted have पाठान्तर which do not militate against the original teaching of भगवान्-आचार्य-पूज्य-पाद at all.

  

There is प्रधान-पाठ and पाठान्तर in the 850 years of tradition that has gone by. The text of the पाठान्तर redacted is not so big in proportions to say the Vulgate text that has come down through an unbroken tradition is corrupted one.

  

विद्यावाचस्पति-श्री-बन्नंजे-गोविन्दाचार्य singularly strived for शुद्ध-पाठ, till his last breath he kept on talking about it and that is a statement of fact.

  

The observation to the effect that "\<\<the corrupted pracalitapāṭha that became the basis of most of the later commentaries>\>" is a figment of imagination as the \*unbroken textual tradition preserved and propagated over 8 centuries is a testimony to the प्रधान-पाठ that is being followed\*

  

\<\<\<His demise is a shattering loss. It was only befitting that his cremation took place with state honours. If Vaikuṇṭha exists, then he's there now.>\>\>

  

With due respects to the scholarship of Dr. Robert Zydenbos and the love he has for Oriental studies, I must point out that the words of condolences to the effect that "\<\<If Vaikuṇṭha exists, then he's there now.>\> is unbecoming of a scholar. A final abode exists is \*article of faith\* of billions.

  

Dr. Robert Zydenbos, who comes from the west and has a deep understanding of philology relating to the languages and linguistics of संस्कृत, प्राकृत, कन्नड and of course, his own Dutch tongue and Duestsche and English, should have known the English conjunction IF is used "conditionally", for billions of hindus there being a final abode "is not condition of being true or false".

  

Thank you & Best regards,

Raghavendra Bheemasena Rao.
