कृष्णंं वन्दे जगद्गुरुम्  
  
विद्वद्भ्यो नमः  
  
इमें मद्रचिताः कारिकाः। कृपया परीक्षतां त्रुटिः चेत् क्षमताम्।  
After a lot of confusion, I have tried to summarize the entire Nishedha into these 5 Karikas. Please correct me if I am wrong, for I only have made them based on limited examples.  
  
Doubt is only that can a क्रत्वर्थतया प्राप्त be denied पुरुषार्थतया?  
  
Karikas  
  
प्रतिषेधो ध्रुवो यत्र शास्त्रं रागविरोधकम् ।  
शास्त्राद् विधिनिषेधौ हि भिन्नार्थौ तत्र सः स्मृतः।। 1 ।।  
  
(2 places Nishedha can be Nitya. 1. Where Ragatah praapt is denied by Shastra, eg Killing is Ragamulaka denied by Shaastra and  
  
2. Where Shastratah Prapta is denied by Shastra but both prescription and denial are for different purposes. Prescription is for man but denial is for Yaga, then only Nishedha is Nitya. Eg दीक्षितस्य दानंं क्रत्वर्थतया प्रतिषिद्धम्, प्राप्तिश्च पुरुषार्थतया। Is there a prescription meant for Ritual but denial meant for man aywhere?)  
  
तुल्यार्थौ शास्त्रतोSगत्या बाधः स तत्र पाक्षिकः।  
(When prescription and prohibition are for the same purpose, then when there is no Gati, ie when we can't use Paryudasa, the Badha is optional).  
  
पर्युदासो विकल्पो वा भिन्नार्थत्वे न संभवः।। 2 ।।  
पर्युदासप्रसक्तौ च लाघवाद् बाध्यते हि सः ।  
तुल्यार्थत्वे विकल्पः स्यात् पर्युदासो न यत्र नञ्  
।। 3 ।।  
(Paryudasa or Vikalpa, both are not possible when Vidhi and Nishedha are for different purposes. When Paryduasa can be resorted to, Vikalpa gets obstructed due to Laaghava, as Vikalpa carries burden of 8 errors. Only when Nan° is not of Paryudasa meaning and when Vidhi Nishedha carry the same purpose, ie either kratvartha or Purushartha, only then one can resort to Vikalpa).  
  
  
पर्युदासो व्रते चापि कर्त्वव्यार्थो यदेष्यते।  
(Paryudasa is also used when there an Upakrama of तस्य व्रतम् because where Duty/Kartavya is desired, there cannot be a Denial).  
  
निषेध्योनर्थहेतुः स्याद् विकल्पो यत्र नोच्यते ।। 4 ।।  
(The object of prohibition/Nishedhya becomes a cause to Anartha where there is no Prevalance of Vikalpa. Ie, when either Nishedha is of Raga by Shastra, or when both Vidhi Nishedha are for different purposes.)  
  
अनर्थः क्रतुवैगुण्यं बाधो यत्रेष्टिहेतुकः ।  
अनर्थो नरकादिः स्याद् रागबाधो नरार्थकः।। 5 ।।  
(Anartha or Undesired can be of 2 types. 1 is Hinderance in Ritual and other which is leading to Narakaadi. When the prohibition is meant for Ritual, that time the Anartha is Kratuvaigunya. For eg, Ragatah Prapta स्वस्त्रीगमन and शास्त्रतः प्राप्त दानहोमादिः both are banned while performing Ritual. But the Nishedhya won't lead to Hell.  
  
2nd is when there is prohibition of Raaga by Shastra for the purpose of Man, then the Nishedhya may lead to Narakaadi. Like killing or lying.)  
  
My doubt is, that is there any Shastra meant for Kratu but the Nishedha is for Man?  
  
Namami.
