> 
> > 
> > What is the methodology used in the Mahabhashya? Is it correct to > draw rules that come subsequently in the Ashtadhyayi to prove or > disprove a previous rule?  
> > 
> > 

  

**अ॒ष्टा॒ध्या॒यी म॑हावा॒क्यरू॑पा ज्ञात॒व्या॑। यथा॑ मृ॒दा शिल्पं॒ चिकी॑र्षन् प्रथ॒मं स्थू॑लरू॒पं कृ॒त्वा ततः॑ सू॒क्ष्मतो॑ निरू॒पय॑ति॒ तथै॒वात्र॑ निय॒मा अ॑पवा॒दाश्च॑ गृहीत॒व्याः॑। न तु नि॑य॒मानां॒ दूष॑णार्था अपवा॒दाः।**

Aṣṭādhyāyī́ must be taken as a single statement. As someone modelling with clay first creates an approximate form and then modifies it with details, so should the rules and exceptions be understood here. The exceptions are not meant to disprove the rules.

**तत्र॒ तु कानि॑चित् त॒त्त्वानि॑ गृही॒त्वा तत॒स्तान्या॒श्रय॑ता सिद्धान्तान्त॒राणि॒ प्रति॑पाद्यन्ते। यथा॒ कोपि॑ पाणिनीयनिय॒मान् यु॑ञ्जा॒नः श॑ब्दरू॒पान् प्र॑तिपा॒दये॑त्।**

There a handful of axioms are accepted and then are used to prove further results. Similar to how someone may derive word forms from the rules given by Pā́ṇini.
