As Dr Sivasenani Nori-ji has already stated,

  

the 'hardness' has to be viewed in a more nuanced way than just good or bad to use 'hard' style.

  

1\. The immediate topic of Vyasa's composition of Mahabharata has to be viewed from the point of view of simultaneous targeting of multiple adhikaaris by great authors like Vyasa. पंडितपामरजनरंजक is the usual routine expression used in such contexts. But traditionally three levels of adhikaaribhedas, uttama, madhyama and adhama are discussed. Great authors write in a style from which each adhikaarigets benefittedas per his adhikaara. Granthagranthis are resolved and received by only the uttama adhikaaris. Such uttama adhikaarismay bring home their understanding to the common audience through vyaakhyaanas, adhyaapanas, pravachanas etc.

  

2\. The 'hardness' in the case of Shaastra granthas is also adhikaarioriented. Even today, research journal and dissertation writing meant for the peer consumption are hard to understand for the popular audience. Same is with the shaastra granthas. Today, there are writings that are specially written to popularize the technical understanding in the technical writings. Purana literature claims such a purpose for it and serves the purpose in many different ways including narrativizing discursive content. Otherwise shaastra granthas maintain their terse style out of concern for shaastric accuracy.

  

3\. In literary works, नारिकेलपाक is used in a वस्तूचित and रसॉचित way. Taking scholarly style of composing narrative poems as विद्वदौषध is well known. The Telugu translation by the great Srinathudu , of Sriharsha'sNaishadhakaavyamis as scholarly as the original. I keep telling that the purpose of such translations is not to make the source work easy for the target language audience. It is to retain the
विद्वदौषध nature of the source work through translation and contribute to the richness of the target language and its literature.
