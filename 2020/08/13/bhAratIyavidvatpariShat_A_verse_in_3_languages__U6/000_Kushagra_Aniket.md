I had translated this famous verse by Ramadhari Singh Dinkar many years ago. I re-discovered it today:

  

Dinkar's Hindi verse:

  

क्षमा शोभती उस भुजंग को जिसके पास गरल हो,

उसको क्या जो दंतहीन विषरहित, विनीत, सरल हो।

  

Sanskrit Translation:

  

क्षमावान् शोभते दन्तीविषवान् यो भुजङ्गमः।  
दन्तं विषं विना सर्पः क्षमया किं करिष्यति॥

  

English Translation:  
  
Mercy befits that snake alone  
Who wields some poison in its teeth,  
Not that crawler, devoid of fangs  
Who does but of meekness breathe!  

  

Compare with the dialogue between Portia and Shylock in*The Merchant of Venice*:

  

“The quality of mercy is not strain'd"
