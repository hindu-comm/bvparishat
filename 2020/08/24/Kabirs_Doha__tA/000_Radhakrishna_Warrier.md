I have seen different versions of Kabir's doha that ends in "पीस खाए संसार":



पाहन पूजे हरि मिले ,मैं तो पूजूं पहार

याते चाकी भली जो पीस खाए संसार



पाथर पूजे हरि मिले , तो मैं पूजूं पहाड़ .

घर की चाकी कोई ना पूजे, जाको पीस खाए संसार



पत्थर पूजे हरि मिले, तो मैं पूजूं पहाड़

घर की चाकी कोई ना पूजे, जाको पीस खाए संसार



If I remember correctly, what was there in my high school Hindi textbook was:



पत्थर पूजे हरि मिले, तो मैं पूजूं पहाड़

याते चाकी भली जो पीस खाए संसार



Which of the above is accepted as the correct one?



Also, I see the word पाहन used of पत्थर. I believe पाहन is a cognate of Sanskrit पाषाण meaning stone. What is the Sanskrit cognate of पत्थर / पाथर?



On another note, I have seen in Hindi the use of two forms of many words with the vowel associated with the word initial consonant short or long. Examples:



पत्थर - पाथर (पाथर is not used in modern Hindi, I believe)

बुड्ढा - बूढ़ा (possibly a cognate of Sanskrit वृद्ध)

लड्डू - लाडू



I believe Gujarati tends to use the second type (lengthened vowel associated with initial consonant) where Hindi and Gujarati have similar words.
