नमो विद्वद्भ्यः

In the Ayurveda literature prevalent in Kerala, the Sanskrit equivalent of mudira is कुलत्थ (kulattha) so the Hindi equivalent could be kulath (कुलथ). Interested to know what it is called in Hindi and other languages. Someone told me that it is called uluva in Telugu.

                                   
              ----- Vid Radhakrishna Warrier  

  

’ **कुलुत्थ**कोपधादण् ’ पा 4-4-4 ' प्राग्वहतेष्ठक् ’ 4-4-1 अधिकारः , ’ संस्कृतम् ’ पा 4-4-3 ’तेन’ -- ठको’पवादः अण् --- **कुळुत्थैः** संस्कृतम्
कौळुत्थम् - **कौमुदी** ।

  

’**कुलत्थ**कोपधादण्’ - कुले तिष्ठति कुलत्थः , अस्मादेव निपातनात् सकारस्य तकारः । केचित्पुनः सकारमेवाधीयते ( i e **कुलस्थः** ) -- **पदमञ्जरी** ।

  

**Telugu** : उलवलु (always plural -- उलुवलु - can be the originalword ) .

  

I was involved in agriculture for 21 years ,right from childhood . We had cows and she buffalos (गेदेलु in Telugu) - my duty was to get

husk from a rice mill , mix it with उलवलु (soaked in water earlier night ) - put in a wide basket - see through the animal eats it .

good for strength - milk - butter .

I too was tasting कुलुत्थ-s .
