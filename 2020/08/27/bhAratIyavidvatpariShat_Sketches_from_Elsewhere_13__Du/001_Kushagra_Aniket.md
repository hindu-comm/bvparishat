Continuing the same thought of not antagonizing the poet, here is a Sanskrit-Hindi "Manipravala" verse:

  

अपारे काव्यसंसारे

कवि ब्रह्मा नहीं और।  
रुष्टो ब्रह्मा कविः ध्येयः

कवि रूठे नहीं ठौर॥  

  

"In the magnificent world of poetry, the poet is Brahma. If Brahma is displeased, the poet should be sought. But if the poet is displeased, there is no refuge."

  

While lines 1 and 3 resemble a shloka foot, lines 2 and 4 are from a Doha.

  

There is a minor tradition of Sanskrit-Hindi mixed poetry. In the 16th century, the Mughal court poet Abdul Rahim Khan-i-Khana wrote:

  

दृष्‍टा तत्र विचित्रितांतरुलतां, मैं था गया बाग में  
काचित्तत्र कुरंगशावनयना, गुल तोड़ती थी खड़ी।  
उन्‍मद्भ्रूधनुषा कटाक्षविशिखैः, घायल किया था मुझे  
तत्‍सीदामि सदैव मोहजलधौ, हे दिल गुजारो शुकर ।।
