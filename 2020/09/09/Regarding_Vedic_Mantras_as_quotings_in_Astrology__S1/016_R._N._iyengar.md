Dear Dr. Muralikrishna,

Thanks for mentioning the work we are doing at CAHC-Jain University. Your question is not as simple as you have made it. Also, you seem to imply that, if the quotations are not there. such texts may not be considered as Vedanga .

  

My question is very simple --

Kindly read the last para of my question. (Just I have quoted some facts to support this question.)

If astrology or astronomical things are derived from vedas (which may be samhita, brahmana, aranyaka or upanishat), then they should have quoted the veda mantras for every principle/some principles. But we are not getting (or a few commentators might have quoted the veda mantras) any quotes of veda mantras from varaha to kamalakara.

  

Prof. Pejathaya has very succinctly responded though not directly.

Let me add a few points within my limited knowledge of the vast field of Veda & Astral Sciences. First, quotations of Vedic concepts: Bhattotpala in his Vivrtion Br.samhita quotesसूर्य आत्मा जगतस्तस्थुषश्च \|\| & स्वर्भानुस्तमसाविध्यद् आसुरः \|\|

Later in 11-12th Cent. Ballalasenain his Adbhutasagara quotes a text called कठश्रुति, in a few places. What is this text; does it have any link with the KaTha branch of the Yajurveda? I do not know.

VidyAmAdhavIya (14th Cent) in the 6th Chapter first verse, talks of 'time' for **Vedokta** samskAras garbhAdAna etc. As youknow, the Grhyasutra section of Kalpa (Vedanga) suggests some nakshatra, based on their properties, ugra, tIkSNa etc. Svati as an auspicious star for kanyAdAna is mentioned in the Tai.BrAhmaNa.यां कामयेत दुहितरं प्रियास्यादिति। तां निष्ट्यायां दद्यात्। Tai. Br(1.5.. 2)

  

About Kamalakara, he certainly knew about the Vedic practices and liked to match his *Viveka* with the spirit of the Vedas. See the following:
Kamalakara lived in Kashi (1658AD). A serious question should have come up among te orthodox as to which star should be taken as Dhruva The Fixed. Dhruvadarshanafor the brideis a Vedic prescription (EkAgnikanda, MantrabrAhmaNa etc). Kamalakara true to his science and Vedic spirit says *whether DhruvabhA moves or fixed*, the bride should see what was Fixed at that time, namely alpha-U.Minor. He gives the coordinates which match with the North Pole. (I will not go into details of Dhruva and precession in the Vedas)

![image.png](https://groups.google.com/group/bvparishat/attach/714fd709d03e3/image.png?part=0.1&view=1)  

Does the above make all of *Hindu astrology & astronomy* वेदाङ्ग ? No. But this depends on the interpretation of the phrase वेदाङ्गत्वम् . Personally I do not subscribe to texts and practices in Astral sciences that arose after about MahAbhAshya, ArthaShAstra, PingaLa,.... as ***original*** वेदाङ्गज्योतिष (ex:YavanajAtaka,Brhatsamhita, LampAkashAstra, Horashastras are not वेदाङ्गज्योतिष). But even these texts and practices still have links - some strong, some weak- with the Vedas that can be verified caseby case. My understanding of वेदाङ्ग is:

*that part of the orally transmitted Indic intellectual tradition closely linked with the Vedas in letter and spirit that got closed c500-200 BC*.

I am happy to see JyotishAcAryas raising such questions.  

Thanks

RN Iyengar
