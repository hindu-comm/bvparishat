"*BTW, are the two verb-forms சொல்லப்படும் and அதிகமாகும் a case of portmanteau affixes? And what about ௭ன்றும் ?*"

  

I do not know if these words qualify to be termed as portmanteau. From Suresh’s explanation, they don’t seem to be portmanteau, but I could be wrong. சொல்லப்படும் (will be said) is the passive form of சொல்லும் (will say). This sort of passive construction exists in Malayalam also.
Instead of the suffix paṭum, Malayalam uses peṭum, which is just a small difference. Paṟayum = will say, paṟayappeṭum = will be said. The paṭum / peṭum suffix lends the sense “it will so happen”. அதிகமாகும் is the combination of two words அதிகம் (tadbhava of Sanskrit adhikam) = more and ஆகும் = will become. அதிகமாகும் = will become more, will increase. ௭ன்றும் = and that …
