Dear Madhav,  
  
Skt. aśvatthá occurs already in the saṃhitās of the Ṛgveda (1.135.8; 10.97.5) and of the Atharvaveda.  
  
By the way, Mayrhofer has also taken note of the Indian tradition, for he refers to volume 2.2 of the "Altindische Grammatik" by Wackernagel/Debrunner (p. 757). Debrunner says there:  
  
"*sthāman-* in ep. kl. *aśvatthāman-* Mannsn. nach Kār\[ikā\] zu P\[āṇini\] 4, 3, 60 u. Pat\[añjali\] p. 311, 4" (= *"sthāman-* in ep. cl. *aśvatthāman-* name of a man according to Kār\[rikā\] to P\[āṇini\] 4, 3, 60 and Pat\[añjali\] p. 311, 4").  
  
Best,
