I wonder if we could look at it in another way.

Vedic mantras are suggestive in the sense that, while seemingly literal words are uttered, the syllables are organised to directly affect the nervous system. Subsequently, the device of suggestion was downgraded, in the sense that words uttered point to yet other words, while allowing the latter to remain unspoken and therefore on a comparatively more subtle level. I've been following this thread and still struggle with understanding what this has to do with Prakrits. It seems an always present possibility within any language. Please correct if I misunderstood something. It also seems that Prof Misra is right and we need to read Hart's wording carefully first.

  

Thank you,

Irene
