Definition (composed in this very metre *Mālabhāriṇī*):  
*sasajāḥ prathame pade gurū cet / sabharā yena ca mālabhāriṇī syāt* (*Vṛttaratnākaraḥ* 4.9.1a)  
  
Thus, one could call this *ardhasamavṛtta* a "frozen" form of the *Aupacchandasaka* (not *Vaitālīya*) metre, because the *Aupacchandasaka* is defined as follows:  
  
3x2 *mātrās* plus *ra ya*; 4x2 mātrās plus *ra ya*  
  
Thus, for example:  
  
◡ ◡ / — / ◡ ◡ / — ◡ — / ◡ — —  
◡ ◡ / — / — / ◡ ◡ / — ◡ — / ◡ — — (= *Mālābhāriṇī*)  
  
In other words: "As a *varṇavṛtta* this \[*Mātrāvṛtta Aupacchandasaka*\] becomes *Mālabhāriṇī*" (H. D. Velankar).  
  
\* \* \*  
  
In contrast, the *Vaitālīya* metre is defined as follows:  
  
3x2 *mātrās* plus *ra la ga*; 4x2 mātrās plus *ra la ga.*  
  
  
The corresponding *varṇavṛttas* are the *Viyoginī* metre and the *Aparavaktra* metre.  
  
*Viyoginī*: *sa sa ja ga* ; *sa bha ra la ga*

"When such a metre has the same *akṣaragaṇas* in both the halves as above \[that is, as in the metres *Mālabhāriṇī*, *Viyoginī*, and *Aparavaktra*\], it should be considered as a *varṇavṛtta*; otherwise it should be regarded as a *mātrāvṛtta."* (H. D. Velankar)
