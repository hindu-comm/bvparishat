That 'objects' are processes is hardly news. Note, for example, the marked tendency in that direction in modern mathematics. Philosophy hasn't been idle either: from Vasubandhu to Heidegger, we are invited to consider the processes which in our disconnected state we erroneously perceive as an 'objects'. Actually, the word 'erroneous' might need qualification: what's erroneous is taking hold of one of the many possibilities and claiming that it's the only possibility.
