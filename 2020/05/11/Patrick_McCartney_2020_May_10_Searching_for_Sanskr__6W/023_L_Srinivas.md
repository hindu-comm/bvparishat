Two excellent points from Sri Paturi's prior mails:

  

\> That said, the criticism against Samskrita Bharati Sanskrit too is unfair and irrational.

\> the criticism of Samskrita Bharati Sanskrit by political shenanigans who have no such grounding in Sanskrit as the traditional Sanskrit scholars

  

The criticism is clearly irrational since any register of a language has as much right to exist as any other. Also, Mr McCartney seems to write primarily in English and is clearly not a practitioner of Pandit Sanskrit, so his plea for purism may be somewhat thin. Besides he does seem to exhibit a lack of socio-linguistic consciousness.  

  

In this connection, one's reminded of the parallelism with Butler English, once considered 'chee-chee' and a marker of a people 'seen, not heard', it has lately become quite respectable at least in academia. Scholarly studies of this register of the English language have been published in India. (cf the late Priya Hosali's scholarly publications)  

  

Hope this helps,

  
Srini
