[[Ashok Aklujkar	2020-05-15, 07:29:08 [Source](https://groups.google.com/g/bvparishat/c/IaQnfJP0yk8)]]



Toward the end of the ṭīkā commentary of the second book of the Vākyapadīya or Trikāṇḍī, the 10th/11th century Kashmirian commentator (commonly taken to be Puṇya-rāja but more likely to be simply an abbreviator of Helā-rāja), employs the phrase: tri-kūṭaika-deśa-varti-triliṅgaika-deśa. Therefore, connecting “Telugu," "Telungu, etc. to “Triliṅga” must be at least a thousand-year old tradition.
