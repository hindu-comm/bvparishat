Namaste



Will the following technical definition of ' muni' from yoga-practice perspective : Gita 5-28: help to get a clarification why Buddha and his followers / Jain sadhus were / are called ' muni's ? The ' muni' practice was common to male and female.

Gita definition predates buddhist and jain classification and title.
The concept of ' moksha' varies across different disciplines and darshanas.





[Karma-Sannyasa Yoga](http://srimadbhagavadgita.net/karma-sannyasa-yoga) \| [The Yoga of the Renunciation of Action](http://srimadbhagavadgita.net/yoga-renunciation-action)

sparshan kritva bahir bahyaµsh cakshush caivantare bhruvoh, pranapanau samau kritva nasabhyantara-carinau

**yatendriya-mano-buddhir munir moksha-parayanah**
vigateccha-bhaya-krodho yah sada mukta eva sah



Translation:

The yogi who shuts out the external sense-objects and fixes his concentration between the eyebrows, stabilizing the inward and outward breaths that flow through the nostrils and thus controls the senses, the mind and intelligence, is dedicated to the attainment of liberation. He is never bound by desires stemming from lust, fear and anger and is certainly always liberated.
