The Vedic 'vivaha' is aimed at producing offispring and particularly begetting a male one at that.

  

From the Shiva Gita of the Padma Puranam:<https://sanskritdocuments.org/doc_giitaa/shivagiitaa.html?lang=sa>

  

In this text, the term 'napumsaka' is used to show the 'other' gender. I think in all texts this term alone is used to indicate all the groupings that are different from male and female. 'Orientation' is implied in this classification, it appears:

