**Dakhini**, also spelled**Dakhni**,**Dakhani**,**Dakkhani**or**Deccani**(دکنی,*dekkanī*or*dakkanī*), is variety of[Urdu](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hindustani_language)spoken by[Dakhini Muslims](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dakhini_Muslims), and a literary language of the[Deccan](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deccan)region of[India](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/India).^([\[2\]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dakhini#cite_note-Prak%C4%81%C5%9Ba%E1%B9%83-2))It arose as a language of the[Deccan sultanates](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deccan_sultanates), ca. 1300 AD, as trade and migration from the north introduced the Hindustani*[lingua franca](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lingua_franca)*. It differs from[Dehlavi Urdu](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delhi_dialect)because of the strong influence of[Marathi](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marathi_language),[Telugu](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telugu_language)and[Kannada](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kannada)spoken in the states of[Maharashtra](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maharashtra),[Telangana](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telangana),[Andhra Pradesh](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh),[Karnataka](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karnataka).
