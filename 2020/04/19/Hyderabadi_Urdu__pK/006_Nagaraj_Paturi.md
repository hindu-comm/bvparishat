The wiki page does not reflect a deep study of the language.

  

There are certain distinct grammatical features of the language that make it standout as a different language.

  

One of them is in the syntax of evidentiality. Please see the Dekkhani examples in

  

<https://web.stanford.edu/group/cslipublications/cslipublications/LFG/11/pdfs/lfg06bashir.pdf>

  

 EVIDENTIALITY IN SOUTH ASIAN LANGUAGES by Elena Bashir University of Chicago

  

examples 70 a to 76 b.

  

70-b

  

kate - usage

  

सलीम का बा (अब्बा) इस गरको बनायाकते।

  

This kate is the equivalent and influence of aṭa /aṇṭa of Telugu
anteof Kannada.

  

This syntax is untypical of Hindi / Urdu.

  

71.

  

बच्ची के सात कोनआरायकते?

  

72

  

पुराना मरीजआदाहकीम है कते।

  

( Proverb quoting style)

  

---------------------------------

  

sarkaa - usage

  

sarkaa+ ai \> sarkaai

  

sarkaa = as if / appears ( probably salook+ kaa \> sarkaa )

  

74 b

  

आज पानी आया सर्काय।( सर्का+ है \> सर्का+ ऐ \> सर्काय)

  

It appears that it rained today.

  

वो गया सर्काय।

  

It appears that he has gone

  

मेरे किताबांसलीम को दिया सर्काय ।

  

May be I gave my book to Saleem.

  

This sarkaa is another usage quite untypical of Hindi/Urdu syntax.

  

We can give many such grammatically distinct features.
