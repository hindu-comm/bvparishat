Here are three categories of traditional wooden architecture homes of Kerala. While the more modern of these might use laterite stone or local brick for walls, the older ones used only wood.  

1.  

    Nālukeṭṭŭ  (നാലുകെട്ടു്) - most common,with four halls joined
    together

    

2.  

    Eṭṭukeṭṭŭ  (എട്ടുകെട്ടു്) - rare, with eight halls joined together

    

3.  

    Patiṉāṟukeṭṭŭ (പതിനാറുകെട്ടു്) - very rare, with sixteen halls
    joined together

    

All these feature one or more open-to-sky central courtyards. I was born in aNālukeṭṭŭ built entirely of wood except the basement and foundation built of laterite stone.
