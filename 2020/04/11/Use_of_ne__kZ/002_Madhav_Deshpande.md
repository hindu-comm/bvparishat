On p. 198 of Jules Bloch's *The Formation of the Marathi Language*translated into English by Dev Raj Chanana, Bloch points out that the instrumental ending *-ena* in the masc./neut paradigms of the -*a* nouns in Sanskrit appears as -*eṃ* in Old Marathi and occasionally survives in modern Marathi. But the common instrumental ending -*ne*in modern Marathi \[and by extension in other IA languages\] does not derive from the same source. It originates as a post-position and Bloch says that it possibly derives from the root *nī* in Sanskrit in the sense of "to lead to". Here is the passage from Bloch's book -
