It is not necessary to perform amazing grammatical acrobatics. Prof. Kannan's analysis is obvious and correct:  
  
*brāhmīyaṃ \[...\] tanuḥ* (in Manusmṛti 2.28) = *brāhmī* + *iyam \[...\]* *tanuḥ*.  
  
As expected, this also corresponds to the explanation of the commentaries I have looked at:  
  
Kullūka: *brāhmī brahmaprāptiyogyeyaṃ tanuḥ*.  
Medhātithi: *brahma paramātmā kāraṇapuruṣaḥ, tasyeyaṃ sambandhinī tanuḥ śarīram. \[...\] brāhmīty anena tanuśabdena ca tadadhiṣṭhātā puruṣo lakṣyate*.  
Rāmacandra: *brāhmī brahmaprāptiyogyā iyaṃ tanuḥ*.
