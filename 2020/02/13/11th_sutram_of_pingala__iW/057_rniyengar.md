Your observation is very valid. In Vedic esoteric practices two things stand out; 'space and time' are internal/personal to the Yajamana. The Vedi sizes are measured as per the height and finger measures of the Yajamana. Similarly time measure is either akshara or prANa (breathings). These were made external for day to day use by the society is besides the issue. When Time is internal to the system, it is not measured externally but the system organizes itself by principle of self-similarity. This must have been an observation in the early ancient period and further philosophized. In practice this principle appears in the division of paksha being synchronic with number 15 and similarly the ahas (day) having 15 muhUrta. The month is 30 (tithi), hence the day also has to be 30 (muhurta). The Manushya, Pitr, Deva times are also organized on this principle. There are several other instances of self-similarity invoked in Sanskrit texts. In particular in the AlApana of great musicians, with no external time keeping, the soundwave forms get organized in a fractal (self-similar) fashion within the bounds (scale) of the RAga. Since wind, water (river) or nature organizes itself in involuted and convoluted fashion, JUB enjoins SAmagAna should be so sung.

You write "By externalizing time science makes the subject stand on its head.." This is so in classical Mechanistic sciences, which cater to almost all of our mundane needs except for understanding Consciousness, that is (self-circularly) Time.
