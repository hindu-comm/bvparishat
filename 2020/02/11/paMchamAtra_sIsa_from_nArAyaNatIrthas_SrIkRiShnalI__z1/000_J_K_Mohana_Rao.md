A paMchamAtra sIsa from nArAyaNatIrtha's SrIkRiShnalIlAtaraMgiNi,

Second wave, sixth song.

  

All pAdAs in paMchamAtra sIsa.

  

sIsa is a tElugu dESi ChaMdassu. sIsa is said to be derived from SIrShaka. Even though it follows the philosophy of SirShaka in the sense that a number of lines is followed at the end by a geeti, it does not have the lakshaNas of the Sanskrit SIrShaka. In Telugu each sIsa pAda has six iMdragaNas (UII, IIII, UUI, UIU, IIUI, IIIU) and two sUryagaNas (UI, III). It is normal to use the symbol U for guru (like S in Sanskrit) and I for laghu in Telugu. Further each pAda is subdivided into two and each of these subdivisions has its own vaDi (aksharasAmya yati). At the end of four such lines, a gIti in the form of aaTaveladi or tETagIti is added. Normally the sIsa is written with paMchamAtra iMdragaNas in khaNDagati.

  

In the song given there is no gIti. Instead of iMdragaNas, six paMchamAtras (UUI, UIU, IIUI, IIIU, UIII, IIIII) are used. The sUryagaNa at the end of the pAda ends with a guru instead of laghu as in sIsa. Again this is common in Kannada as they include IIU and UU in brahma gaNas.

  

nArAyaNatIrtha has used Telugu dvipada too in another kIrtana.

गंगाधरोऽपि तव सापि गंगा॥

शृंगारवारिधे श्रीवत्सकौस्तुभा

लंकार शिशुभाव भयविभंग॥

  

चरण (1):

अस्तु ते भद्रमिह दुस्तरभवांभोधि

विस्तरभयापहार वस्तुसुखद

कस्तूरिकातिलकविस्तीर्णफाल जग

दस्तितापादक सदास्तरहित॥

  

चरण (2):

अक्षीणसंपदिह रक्षतु धरेय मथ

शिक्षतु हरिस्तव विपक्षनिचयं

दक्षिणामूर्ति रपि दाक्षिण्य मावहतु

वीक्षतां त्वां सकलसाक्षी सदयम्॥

  

चरण (3):

दुर्गाणि वारयतु दुर्गा सरस्वती

भर्गोऽपि भर्जयतु पापवर्गं

सर्गादिसकलसंसर्गसाधक मिह

मार्गस्सदाऽवतु तवापवर्गम्॥

  

चरण (4):

परिपूर्ण बोधरस परिपंथिहर सरस

निरवधिकसंपदं दिशतु भूमा

विरचित मिदंतु शिवनारायणानंद

तीर्थेनपरमपुरुषार्थ फलिना॥
