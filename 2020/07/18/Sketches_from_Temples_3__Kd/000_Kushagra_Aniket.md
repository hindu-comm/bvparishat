Another verse on Shri Bharat Ji's sketches:  
  
सर्वेभ्यो नमः!

  

Since there is nothing that a humble poet can add to the Kirātārjunīyam, I only offer a rare meter Kulādhārī (कुलाधारी) with word play:  
  
गुडाकेशः गुडाकेशं किराताख्यं महादेवम्।  
तपोपूतः वने घोरे स रुद्रास्त्राय तुष्टाव।।

  

Translation: In the dense forest, Arjuna, who had conquered his senses (or sleep) and was purified through austerities (tapas), propitiated Mahadeva Shiva, who had matted locks (Gudaka) and appeared in the form of a hunter (Kirata), for the Pashupata weapon.

  

Explanation:  
  
गुडाका= निद्रा माया आलस्यं वा तस्या ईशः गुडाकेशः अर्जुनः  
  
=>गुडाकेशः=One who is a master of sleep (Arjuna)  
  
गुडा=स्नुही (स्नुही गुडा इत्यमरः)=A leafy plant called "euphorbia"  
  
गुडावत्केशाः जटाः यस्य सः गुडाकेशः अर्जुनः शिवः वा  
  
=>गुडाकेशः=One who has euphorbia-like hair (Arjuna or Shiva)

  

My verse is based on the first verse uttered by Shiva after the competition with Arjuna:

  

भो भो फल्गुन तुष्टोऽस्मि कर्मणाप्रतिमेन ते।

शौर्येणानेन धृत्या च क्षत्रियो नास्ति ते समः।।
