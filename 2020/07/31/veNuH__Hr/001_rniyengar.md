A similar analogy is given in the *Śāṅkhāyana Āraṇyaka* (VIII.9) also. Reference to an instrument with strings up to a hundred occurs in some places in the Vedas. One can safely infer that the fretless Vīṇā, played with the help of a short stick or plectrum was an integral part of sacred music which in turn was essential to Vedic sacrifices. A briefwrite up on this topic has been presented previously by Usha Bhise in her introduction to the critical edition of the*Nāradīya Śikṣā.*
