Honestly, I find almost all the members of this august forum much more well read, well informed, intelligent and qualified than me! So I would not be that foolish to try to confuse anyone!

\<Abhijit Nakshatra is overlapping with Uttarashadha mostly and a little bit Shravana. It is from 6 degrees 40 minutes to 10 degrees 53 minutes and 20 seconds in Nirayana Makara Rashi. The other 27 Nakshatras are each taken as 13 degrees 20 minutes.>

As per the attachment RP-Stars-1942, the Ashvini Star itself is 10 degrees away from the starting point of AshvininiNakshatra! Bharni is at the fag end of Bharni division!

The distance between Ardra and Punarvasu is whopping 22 degrees, Uttara Phalguni and Hasta 24 degrees, Swati and Vishakha 21 degrees and so on!
But the distance between Mrigashira and Arda is 5 degrees, between Chitra and Swati less than even half a degree---yes you read it right---it is less than half a degree!

And "Lahiri" Chitra has "regressed" to "Lahiri" Kanya!

As per attachment CRC stars, this is what they had lamented

Quote

In fact no arrangement at any time appears to have been satisfactory enough for all the yogataras to fall within their respective nakshatra divisions"!

Unquote

The same thing gets confirmed from the attachment Lahiri-Stars-2004, as it includes some of the stars like Abhijit which are not attached to any of the 27 nakshatras!

There is a lengthy discussion in the CRC Report as well as the "Bharatiya Jyotisha Shastra" of S B Dikshit aboutyogataras vis-a-vis nakshatras.

They have demonstrated it thoroughly that whatever Ayanamsha you take, at least 25 per cent of the Yogatars remain outside the 27 equal namesake nakshatra division.

Same is the case with Sayana equal nakshtra divisions! Since in either case the Yogataras fall outside their namesake nakshatra divisions, why not take the sayana-equal divisions, if there is an aversion towards the unequal division!

Regarding Abhijit nakshatra, when in Srimad Bhagavata while telling His Vibhutis to Uddhava, Bhagwan Krishna identifies Himself as Abhijit among nakshtras, it means it is a Yogatara to reckon with, whether or not nirayana jyotishis like it !
