This is a blog I had written on the subject, when I began my journey in
learning Sanskrit.[Sanskrit has no accent](https://medium.com/@warija/sanskrit-has-no-accent-533dce5aa863?source=your_stories_page---------------------------).

  

My understanding is that Shiksha codifies pronunciation, and if one follows it strictly, there is not going to be any variation in Sanskrit. It has one standard.

  

However, it is common to see localized pronunciations, as people tend to (including me) add the local versions. For example, many pronounce श as स, ज्ञ as Gya, mix श and ष, these are some of the mistakes I made as well.

  

Interestingly, in काव्यमीमांसा, in the chapter 7th, the poet Rajashekhar lists out how poets from different locations sound -

(राजशेखरकृतौ काव्यमीमांसायां कविरहस्ये प्रथमे ऽधिकरणे सत्पमो ऽध्यायः वाक्यविशेषाः काकुकलना पाठप्रतिष्ठा च ।)

आह स्म- "ब्रह्मन्विज्ञापयामि त्वां स्वाधिकारजिहासया ।  
गौडस्त्यजतु वा गाथामन्या वास्तु सरस्वती । ।  
  
नातिस्पष्टो न चाश्लिष्टो न रुक्षो नातिकोमलः ।  
न मद्रो नातितारश्च पाठी गौडेषु वाडवः । ।  
  
रसः को ऽप्यस्तु काप्यस्तु रीतिः को ऽप्यस्तु वा गुणः ।
