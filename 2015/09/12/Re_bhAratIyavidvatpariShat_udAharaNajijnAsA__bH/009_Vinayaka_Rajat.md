> > Its better to say that the **Pāṇinian** usage is *ānukūlyena krīḍati* > than saying that the correct/sādhu usage is *ānukūlyena krīḍati*.  
>   
> > 
> > 
> > If we describe it in terms of correct/incorrect or sādhu/asādhu, then > the previous śiṣṭas who have used krīḍate in their works will be > accused by our Pāṇinian sampradāya of using asādhu words. Ideally we > don't want to do that for śiṣṭas like Valmiki.  
>   
> > 
> > 
> > 
> > 
> > e.g. in Vālmīkīya-rāmāyaṇa, (7.32.2), — क्रीडते सह > नारीभिर्नर्मदातोयमाश्रितः  
>   
> Best is to say 'cintya'.  
>   
> > 
> > 
> > Therefore, krīḍate is also correct, but it cannot be derived easily > from Pāṇinian rules. I say 'easily' because one can still try to > derive it as Śaraṇadeva had tried:
