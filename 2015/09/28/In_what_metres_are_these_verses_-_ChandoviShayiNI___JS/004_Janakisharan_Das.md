Yes, This kind of admixture rare in Sahitya. One more interesting thing that the MS writer put two different meters in a shloka. Here is example -  
कैवल्यं ज्ञानयोगाद्यदि भवति गुरो ! मोहमात्रप्रदात्रीः  
कस्मात् सिद्धीर्जगति कतिचित्प्रार्थयन्ते विमुक्तिम् ।  
कस्मारब्धं भजति विलयं भोगमात्रावसानं  
यस्मात्तस्मादखिलविभवान्विद्धि चारब्धजातान्  
Here the first quarter follows pattern of Sragdhara metre and rest of three quarters are in mandākrāntā.  
Is this kind of admixture available in any sAhitya? What should be it considered Sragdharā, mandākrāntā or upajāti?
