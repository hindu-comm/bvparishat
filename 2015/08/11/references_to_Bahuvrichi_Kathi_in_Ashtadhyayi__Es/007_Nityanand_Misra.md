I think Hari Parshad Das is referring to this sentence in the NSM: ननु मङ्गलं न विघ्नध्वंसं प्रति न वा समाप्तिं प्रति कारणं विनापि मङ्गलं नास्तिकादीनां ग्रन्थेषु निर्विघ्नपरिस्माप्तिदर्शनादिति चेन्न। If no, Das may please let us know which sentence of the NSM he had in mind.



If yes, the sentence says ‘\[One may ask,\] surely, a benediction is not the means towards destruction of obstacles nor towards completion, due to the observation of unobstructed completion in the works of *nāstika*s even without benediction. Not thus.’ (my translation)



A) What NSM says: ‘Works of *nāstika*s get completed even without benediction.’

C) What Das concludes: ‘Benediction (मङ्गल) is mandatory for *āstika* works.’  



How does Das’ conclusion follow from NSM’s statement? Am I missing some steps in the reasoning here?
