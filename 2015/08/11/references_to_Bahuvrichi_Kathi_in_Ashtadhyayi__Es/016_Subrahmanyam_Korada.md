Dear Dr Hari

  

आशीर्नमस्क्रिया वस्तुनिर्देशो वा तन्मुखम् (I quoted this ) - means ' the beginning of a काव्यम् can be with आशीर्वाद or नमस्कार (इष्टदेवताप्रार्थनम्) or वस्तुनिर्देश - and Mallinatha did not say ’ it is मङ्गलाचरणम् ’ ।

All he said is इति शास्त्रात् काव्यादौ वस्तुनिर्देशात् कथां प्रस्तौति। Just take a second look .

  

A verse containing देवतावाचक cannot be taken as मङ्गलाचरणम् --

another scholar may claim - since it is said in श्रीभाष्यम् - सर्वेषाम् अपि शब्दानां ब्रह्मणि तात्पर्यम् - any शब्द can be taken as मङ्गलाचरणम् -- it is not there in the tradition.

  

काशिका starts with - वृत्तौ भाष्ये .. - it means the work is started with वस्तु - उपक्रम and उपासंहार are considered as part and parcel of the वस्तु ।

  

Broadly speaking , this so called मङ्गलाचरणम् in the form of verse is seen in the latter works .

  

Many शास्त्रs and दर्शनs start with अथ and वेद with ओ 3 म् (ओमभ्यादाने
पा सू) .

  

Finally , if you come across any reference to Vamana and Jayaditya as Baudhas it will be a strong evidence .

  

In either case it is okay .

  

Let us call it a day .
