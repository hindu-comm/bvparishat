**Comment: No, and the Kāśikā does not start with 'atha'. It starts with the verse 'vṛttau bhāṣye tathā dhātur', where 'vṛttau' refers to the works of previous vṛttikāras such as kuṇi etc. There is no iṣṭa-devatā-smaraṇa in the subsequent verses at all. Neither at the end. Neither in the middle. Therefore, some vidvānas surmise that they must have been bauddhas.
