Yes. This can be a a Sloka in the prayer to Gomata Surabhi, the Divine Cow in the Kingdom of Indra.

The following slokas from Brahma Vaivarta purana and Skanda Purana maintain some resemblance with this sloka.

गवामधिष्ठातृदॆवी गवामाद्या गवां प्रसू:।

गवां प्रधाना सुरभि: गॊलॊकॆ सा समुद्भवा ॥

        -ब्रह्मवैवर्तपुराणॆ

त्वं माता सर्ववेदानां त्वं च यज्ञस्य कारणम् ।

त्वं तीर्थं सर्वतीर्थानाम् नमस्तेऽस्तु सदाऽनघे ॥

           - स्कन्दपुराणॆ ब्रह्मखण्डे ।

It is clear that SURABHI is the name of the Mother of all Cows and She was born in Goloka.

She is further praised to be the Tirtha sthana for All tIrthas.

Hence it can be inferred that the Sloka in the Context can be the prayer of Surabhi or the Divine Cow.

The observations about the Grammatical accuracy of that Sloka are much discussed in the narration of Prof. Korada Subrahmanyam Garu. So those views need not be repeated.
