मौलौ गङ्गाशशाङ्कौ करचरणतले शीतलाङ्गा भुजाङ्गाः  
वामेभागे दयाद्री हिमगिरिदुहिता चन्दनं सर्वगात्रे ।  
इत्थं शीतं प्रभूतं तव कनकसभानाथ सोढुं क्वशक्तिः  
चित्ते निर्वेदतप्ते यदि भवति न ते नित्यवासो मदीये ॥

Oh shiva! On your head, you host gangA and the moon. On your arms and feet, you are draped by snakes. Thedaughter of the Himalayas, pArvatI, who is drenched in compassion for mankind, is seated to your left. You are smeared in cool sandalwood paste. Oh lord of the golden auditorium, how wouldyou withstand all this cold, if you were not permanently residing in my heart, which is a fire kiln burning with desire for mokSha!

(Extracted from “The Acharya’s Call” – Madras Speeches, with reference to ArudrAdarshanam (Ardra) – primary festival (ratha) of the Chidambaram Temple.)
