Swami Gabhirananda's translation followed the following analysis (splitting) of the sentence into clauses:



1. कर्मफललक्षणम् अपरविद्याविषयं च यत् सत्यं( अस्ति)



2\. तत् (सत्यं) आपेक्षिकम्(अस्ति)



In clause1, कर्मफललक्षणम् अपरविद्याविषयं च यत् सत्यंis उद्देश्यand
(अस्ति) (अध्याहृता / अश्रुता क्रिया) isविधेय



In clause2,तत् (सत्यं) is उद्देश्यandआपेक्षिकम्(अस्ति) isविधेय



As per this analysis (splitting) , there is a vis'Eshya- vis'EshaNa relation between कर्मफललक्षणम् and सत्यंand अपरविद्याविषयंand सत्यंas part of clause1. 



There is uddEs'ya-vidhEya relation between तत् (सत्यं) and आपेक्षिकम्(अस्ति). 



In Swami Gabhirananda's translation , 'is relatively so' means 'is relatively true'



यस्य लक्षणम् कर्मफलम् अस्ति,यस्य विषयं ( वस्तुपरत्वान्नपुंसकलिङ्गत्वं) अपरविद्या अस्ति,तस्य सत्यस्य सत्यत्वम् आपेक्षिकम् इति



You proposed:



"But we can change the sentence like this:

That subject matter of the lower knowledge constituted by the results of Karma, the Truth, is only relatively so."



Problem with this is 'is only relatively so' hereapplies to 'subject matter of the lower knowledge '. But there is no yogyataa between the two. That is , any subject matter can not be relatively so.



\>The questionnow - is there any rule the Samasa in subject must be Tatpurusha if there is only one word in the Subject?



--- There is no such rule.
