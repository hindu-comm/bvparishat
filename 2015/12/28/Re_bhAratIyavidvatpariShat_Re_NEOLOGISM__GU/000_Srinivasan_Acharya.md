Dear all,

  

English is a language that adopts many new words under the idea of neologism (coining of new words). Portmanteau, acronym, abbreviations, etc., could be good examples for them.

  

When we refer to sites such as<http://dictionary.reference.com/>, they state the origin of the word according to the available oldest literature, where the word was evident for the first time.

  

Therefore, coining such words could be fun as well as contributive.

  

Even though Kavisamaya (freedom of trope) is appreciated well, there should be some logic behind such innovative creations to avoid misapprehensions.

  

These following sites could be useful in alike contexts.

  

<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_portmanteaus>  

<https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Appendix:List_of_portmanteaux>

<http://www.portmanteaur.com/>  

<http://www.brands.so/ideas/portmanteau/blend-words-to-invent-new-words.php>  

  

As it is told yogAt rUDhirbalIyasI - usage matters the most and helps people to know them easily; lucidity and use of familiar words what make one read and comprehend well, so it is better not to create so many new words/phrases without contexts and necessities and confuse the recipients of such.
