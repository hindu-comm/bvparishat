The second one



రాజు మరణించె ఒక తార రాలిపోయె

కవియు మరణించె నొక తార గగనమెక్కె

రాజు జీవించె రాతి విగ్రహములందు

సుకవి జీవించె ప్రజల నాలుకల యందు “



is from an unparalleledmodern Telugu progressiveverse-poet Gurram Jashua.



The original line from Srinathudu is



'దివిజ కవి వరుల గుండియల్ దిగ్గు రనగ ,అరుగు చున్నాడు శ్రీనాథుడమరపురికి ”

('divija kavi varula guṁḍiyal diggu ranaga ,arugu cunnāḍu śrīnāthuḍamarapuriki ”)

This line is the last line of a verse he is believed to have sung extempore just a few minutes before his death.

It translates into English as :

Srinatha (a third person reference to himself) is going to amarapuri as the hearts of the divija-poets are banging with fear (of getting defeated by Srinathudu in court-competetions)

The young man replaced the name Srinathudu with the name Sriramudu,( a short form of Sriramachandrudu.)

The verse from Sri Gurram Jashuva translates as

King died, a star dropped down.

Poet died too, but a star rose to the skies.

The king (thereafter) lived in the stone sculptures.

The poet (thereafter)survivedon the tongues of people.
