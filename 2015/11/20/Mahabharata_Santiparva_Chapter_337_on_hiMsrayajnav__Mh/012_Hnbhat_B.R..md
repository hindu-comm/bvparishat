\> Thinking of the etymology of the wordहिंस्र from Dhaatuहिंस्हिंस्र should mean cruelty. That should include cruelty of any kind, not just to animals.

३.२.१६७*नमि*-*कम्पि*-स्म्यजस-कम-हिंस-दीपो रः ।

As per the above derivation, it should not mean cruelty, but as a noun or adjective it should mean "cruel" or one that kills others regularly.  

नम्यादिभ्यः धातुभ्यः तच्छीलादिषु कर्तृषु रः प्रत्ययो भवति। नम्रं काष्ठम्। कम्प्रा शाखा। स्मेरं मुखम्। अजस्रं जुहोति। हिंस्रं रक्षः। दीप्रं काष्ठम्। अजस्रम् इति जसु मोक्षणे नञ्पूर्वो रप्रत्ययान्तः क्रयासातत्ये वर्तते।

All the words have the same meaning as the participles and used as adjective. Exception being अजस्रम् frequent used as नञ् compound.

नम्र - bending, bowing etc. दीप्रम -- burning, shining brightly etc. Hence हिंस्र should not mean cruelty.
