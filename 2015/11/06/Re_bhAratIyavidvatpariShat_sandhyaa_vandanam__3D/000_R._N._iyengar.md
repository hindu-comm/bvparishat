Dear scholars,

Please excuse my intervention. The word सनाद्"sanaad" appears in the RV some 15 times (1.62.8;12.1.102.8; 1.164.13; 2.27.......). I presume the avyayaसना is same or is equivalent to the RV word. Since the word refers to TIME I am interested in the way it is used in RV; or at least understood by commentators. Quite interestingly the meaning is nearer to "ancient" and not "eternal" or "without beginning".

In 1.164 (Asya vaamasya) Sayana takes this word as "Sanaatanam" The Kannada translators give the meaning as "atyanta praaciina". Otherwise i could not find the meaning approaching अनादि

  

1.102: चिरकालदारभ्य

2.27:1 सर्वदा

4.56.6 सदाकालम्

7.32.24 चिरादेव आरभ्य

8.21.13 चिरादेव

10.55: पुराणः

10.78.8 चिरकालादारभ्य

10.87.19: चिरादेव आरभ्य

I do not know whether this would be useful for answering query raised by Dr.Ajit, but I like to share the info.
