Regarding the question whom we can address "Swamin!" the following two references I would like to mention here.

1. In the Dasarupaka, a treatise on Ancient Indian Dramaturgy, in the end of its 2nd Chapter there is a list of addressing words by various people. In that list **a King** is meant to be addressed as : "DEVA" or "SWAMIN". Here are the supporting slokas:

रथी सूतेन चायुष्मान् पूज्यै: शिष्यात्मजानुजा:।

वत्सेति तात: पूज्योऽपि सुगृहीताभिधस्तु तै:॥

भावोऽनुगेन सूत्री च मार्षेत्येतेन सोऽपि च।

**देव: स्वामीति** नृपतिर्भृत्यैर्भट्टेति चाधमै:।

आमन्त्रणीया: पतिवज्ज्येष्टामध्याधमै: स्त्रिय:॥

  

2\. In the Prapatti slokas of Sri Venkateswara Suprabhatam's prapatti the God Sri Venkateswara is addressed by the word "Svamin!".

Here is a Sloka:

२. श्रीमन्! कृपाजलनिधे कृतसर्वलोक!

सर्वज्ञ! शक्त! नतवत्सल! सर्वषेषिन्!

**स्वामिन्!** सुशीलसुलभाश्रितपारिजात!

श्रीवेङ्कटेश! चरणौ शरणं प्रपद्ये॥

  

In both the instances 'Svamin!' is used to call a person.
