I think the word is derived from the dhātu 'ramu' - krīḍāyām (245).
When the upasarga 'upa' is added we get the word 'uparamaṇam'. The meanings give for this are: abstinence, ceasing, etc. दुश्चरिताच्च उपरमणम् (Shānkara bhāṣya: Br.up.4.4.2. The gross meaning would be: the cessation of the 'play' of the indriyas, manas, etc.  
  

Even the word 'viramāmi', 'ramu' with 'vi' upasarga gives the meaning of cessation, 'I shall cease to...', etc.  
  

The above shown bhṣāya expression has its basis in the famous Kaṭhopaniṣad mantra:  
  

-   

    काठकोपनिषद्भाष्यम्।प्रथमोऽध्यायः।द्वितीया वल्ली।मन्त्रः२४

    

    

    *नाविरतो* दुश्चरितान्नाशान्तो नासमाहितः ।  
    नाशान्तमानसो वापि प्रज्ञानेनैनमाप्नुयात् ॥ २४ ॥
