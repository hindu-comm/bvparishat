नमो विद्वद्भ्यः

  

Since there is cultural difference it is difficult to translate -

  

विवादशीलां स्वयमर्थचोरिणीं

परानुकूलां परिहासभाषिणीम्।

अग्राशिनीम् अन्यगृहप्रवेशिनीम्

त्यजन्ति भार्यां दशपुत्रमातरम् ॥ - मूलं मृग्यम्

  

’ त्यज वैनां गृहाण वा ’ - शाकुन्तलम्

  

’आराधनाय लोकस्य मुञ्चतो नास्ति मे व्यथा ’ - उत्तररामचरितम्

  

It can be त्याग or मोचनम् / मोक्षः - but these terms won't denote the actual meaning of 'divorce' .

In case of any problem for women or any person (in need) for that matter it is the 'राजा” who is directly responsible for their welfare / protection.

  

अराजकेषु राज्येषु धर्मो न व्यवतिष्ठते ।

परस्परं खादन्ति सर्वधाधिगराजकम्॥

अदासः क्रियते दासः ह्रियन्ते चाबलाः स्त्रियः।

राजा चेन्न भवेल्लोके पृथिव्यां दण्डधारकः॥

....

चातुर्वर्ण्यं च धर्माश्च रक्षितव्याः समीक्षिताः।

धर्मसंकररक्षा च राज्ञां धर्मः सनातनः ॥ -- महाभारतम्

  

Nowhere is it clearly or unclearly stated that one can go for 'divorce' (legal separation with conditions) in our scriptures.

  

When you discuss सनातनधर्म it is totally different from 'today's law'.

  

What I mean to say is the term 'धर्म’ is pregnant with meaning , i e it includes behavior , duty , law , spirituality etc. and hence untranslatable .

  

' Religion ' - is used for 'धर्म’ - but back translation fails -

  

धर्मः = religion = मतम् (मतम् means इष्टम् - liked by some people)

  

धर्म is universal - सत्यं वद । अहिंसा परमो धर्मः etc.

  

'घटश्राद्धः’ --

  

there was a Kannada movie (Awarded) by this name some twenty years ago(I did not watch) .

  

It is a latter development , but शिष्टाः use it -

an earthen pot full of water is taken to the चतुष्पथम् ( center of the village) , पुण्याहवाचनम् is performed(I am not sure) and by naming the
नाम and गोत्रम् of the person and with the proper expression ,with whom the कर्ता wants to snap all kinds of बन्ध - the pot is hit against the floor. Thereafter the कर्ता need not follow the नियमs of अशौचम् etc. connected with the counterpart.
