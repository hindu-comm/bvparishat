Dear Scholars,

Sanskrit lovers, at one time or other face the task of assigning the correct meaning to a word in a text, which taken alone carry several possible dictionary meanings. In the earliest stage of the word, the etymological meaning might have been correct, but with time the रूढि meaning seems to gain upper hand. This poses special difficulty in interpreting ancient texts, when the रूढ्यर्थ of the original author might be at variance from the later ones. In fact over time the रूढ्यर्थ also could have changed. This points to the absolute necessity of maintaining a historical perspective in understanding Sanskrit texts.
