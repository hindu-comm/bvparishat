>  I found this expression in viShNupurANa:  
> यत्रोतमेतत्प्रोतम्  
>  I have come across the expression ओत - प्रोत in kannaDa. I want to > know the reason why:  
> प्र + ओत doesn't become प्रौत?  
> यत्र + ओत doesn't become यत्रौत?  
>   

  

It is very simple. The same participle उत with two prefixes आ and प्र derived from वेञ् तन्तुसन्ताने - to weave, (cloth) - आ+उत प्र+उत -, ओत abd प्रोत, in combination with यत्र,

  

यत्र+उतम्, -यत्रोतम्+एतत्+ प्रोतम्।

  

interwoven, is the meaning.sewn lengthwise and crosswise. Used both in combination also, in the meaning pervading everyone:

  

नैतच्चित्रं भगवति ह्यनन्ते जगदीश्वरे ।। ओतप्रोतमिदं यस्मिस्तन्तुष्यङ्ग यथा पट: । भागवतपुराणम् १०.१५.३५॥

  

Hope this is clear.
