Namaskarah.

  

"So, lack of cognition of pot is means to valid knowledge(cognition) of lack of pot."

  

Does it mean that even though a pot is existing on the ground of dark room, the existence \[bhava\] of the pot is there but due to the darkness (obstruction) no valid cognition (the pratyaksha possible is obstructed) of the pot possible?  

Or does it mean that a valid cognition of the pot is not possible due pot's absence even in a enlightened pot-less \[non-existence, abhava\] room (the pratyaksha possible is unobstructed, still the object exists somewhere else)?

Please explain.

Narayanan
