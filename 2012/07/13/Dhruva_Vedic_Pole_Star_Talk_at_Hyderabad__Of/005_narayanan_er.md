Respected Professor Iyengar,

Unfortunately we don't have a Mahabhashya on that particular Sutra, except to rely upon the Kashika as it says what is adhruva. Patanjali skips from the Rule 3.4.41 to 3.4.60 as we don't have 18 rules being discussed in between and naturally skipped out 3.4.54 too. While discussing 1.4.24 Patanjali says what is dhruva:  

इह तावदश्वात् त्रस्तात् पतितः इति। यत्तदश्वे अश्वत्वं आशुगामित्वं तत् ध्रुवं तच्च विवक्षितम्। रथात् प्रवीतात् पतितः इति यत्तद्रथे रथत्वम्। रमन्ते अस्मिन् रथः इति तद् ध्रुवं तच्च विवक्षितम्। सार्थाद् गच्छतः हीनः इति यत्तत् सार्थे सार्थत्वं सहार्थीभावः तद् ध्रुवं तच्च विवक्षितम्।

Here we do not see dhruva or adhruva in relation with the separation of certain limbs harmed or unharmed any organism. Here dhruva deals with something relationally static to something being separated from it, though the horse and the chariot are moving. Still the horse-hood and fastness in horse is dhruva. By saying vivakshitam; he aims that what was Panini intended to convey.  

The rule उपसर्गात्‌ स्वाङ्गं ध्रुवमपर्शु (6.2.177) was also under Mahabhashya discussion: मुखस्य अन्तोदात्तत्वातुपसर्गात्स्वाङ्गं ध्रुवं इति एतद् भवति विप्रतिषेधेन. But he does not give any definition to dhruva. But still Kashika gave a definition to adhruva that we saw.

The limbs of organisms are under discussion like Rules 4.1.54 etc. Again Kashika comes with a verse in this regard:

अद्रवं मूर्तिमत् स्वाङ्गं प्राणिस्थमविकारजम्। अतत्स्थं तत्र दृष्टं चेत् तेन चेत्तत्तथायुतम्। Even hair too considered as the limb. And optionally exemplified with सुकेशी, सुमुखी etc.

So it feels that the interpretations regarding dhruva and adhruva vary from comments to comments. Thanks.

Regards,

Narayanan
