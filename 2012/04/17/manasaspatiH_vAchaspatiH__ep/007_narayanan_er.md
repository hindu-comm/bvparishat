Dear Sir,

It is ṣaṣṭhī there as far as śriyaḥ is concerned. You can have a ṣaṣṭhīsamāsa, an aluk in nature and separated as well. But śriyaspatiḥ is not listed in the Kāśikā. We have a patiḥ to follow to have a suṭ in between śriyaḥ and patiḥ, so that ḥ of śriyaḥ is to be substituted with s if it is a Vedic usage. If a Vedic śriyaspatiḥ is existing it could well be justified with 8.3.53. Until then śriyaḥpatiḥ will do as an aluksamāsa. Māgha uses śriyaḥpatiḥ in the Śiśupālavadhamahākāvya.

Regards,

Narayanan
