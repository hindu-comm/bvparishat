Respected scholars,

I feel blessed to read the methodology of interpretation by various scholars like Professor Bhattacharya, Professor Jha and Professor Subrahmanyam. I sincerely thank all these prolific scholars. I thank Mr. V. Subrahmanian too for raising such a question on this platform. It seems to be an interesting type of an ākṛtigaṇa having pāraskara (pāraskaro deśaḥ-which place it is?), prastumpati (bull), taskara (thief), Bṛhaspati (the guru-god), prāyaścittiḥ (recantation) etc.  

A descriptive grammar always gives clarity than a calculated historical approach, although it is easy to assume that the prior part of the genitive compound in Bṛhaspati is from the root bṛhă vṛddhau.

Regards,

Narayanan
