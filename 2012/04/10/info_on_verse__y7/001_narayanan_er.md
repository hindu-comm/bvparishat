Yes, Sir, I have definitely seen that verse 20 years ago. There is a picture also. I remember the verse as:

अतसीपुष्पसङ्काशो न्यायो ज्ञेयो विपश्चिता। (is according to my memory, as according to you it is ध्येयो, I am not remembering which text it was)  

सिंहास्यो दक्षिणे पाशं ध्वजं वामकरे वहन्।। There is a figure on the front cover of a lion-faced having a rope and a flag. *atasī flower* : linum usitatissimum, flax, a plant with lance-shaped leaves that produces pretty sky blue or violet-blue flowers each spring and summer. But the artist who drew the figure of a flower reddish in colour, if my memory is correct. I am not sure that I have seen the same Mathuri Panchalakshani, it could be any other Nyaya text from the same Publishers. नीलपुष्पम्, अगशी, रुद्रपत्नी, उमा, देवी are the synonyms for the *atasī flower.* I failed to find a connection between siṃhāsya and atasīpuṣpasaṅkāśa with nyāya. I felt that it is an imitations of the verses like:

  

अतसीपुष्पसङ्काशं हारनूपुरशोभितम्। रक्तकङ्कणकेयूरं कृष्णं वन्दे जगद्गुरुम्।। (श्रीकृष्णाष्टकम्)  

*अतसीपुष्पसङ्काशं* शङ्कचक्रगदाधरम्। संस्थापयामि देवेशं देवो भूत्वा जनार्दनम्।। (मत्स्यपुराणम्.२६६.३५)  

*अतसीपुष्पसङ्काशं* पीतवाससमच्युतम्। ये नमस्यन्ति गोविन्दं न तेषां विद्यते भयम्।।  

*अतसीपुष्पसङ्काशं* दशवर्णात्मकं विभुम् \| जटाधरं चतुर्बाहुं नमामि कपिनायकम् \|\|  

  

Regards,

Narayanan
