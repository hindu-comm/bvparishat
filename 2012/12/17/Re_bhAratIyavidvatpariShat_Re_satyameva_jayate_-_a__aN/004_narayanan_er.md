The following versions read as: सत्यमेव जयति-  
<http://archive.org/details/upanishadssrisan00sita>  
<http://archive.org/details/MundakaUpanishadWithShankaracharyaBhashyaAndAnandagiriTika>  
If we take this reading as सत्यमेव जयते in Atmanepada:  
सत्यमेव जयते नानृतं (10 syllables, 15 matras, if जयति 14 matras)  
सत्येन पन्था विततो देवयानः। (12 syllables, 20 matras)  
येनाऽऽक्रमन्त्यृषयो ह्याप्तकामा (11 syllables, 18 matras)  
यत्र सत्सत्यस्य परमं निधानम्॥ (12 syllables, 19 matras) (मुण्डकोपनिषत्.३.६)  
As being a Vedic usage, is it covers any sound metrical balance rather than सत्यमेव जयति ? As there are 10, 12, 11, 12 are the syllables respectively in each foot.  
Then regarding कर्मव्यतिहार of जी जये (भ्वादिगणः, लँट्लकारः, कर्तरिप्रयोगः, प्रथमपुरुषः, एकवचनम्) what स्वर pattern had been followed in different readings not only for जयते or जयति but for the whole mantra-vaakya?  
Is there any method to justify rather than छान्दसं वाक्यं साधु। ? (यथादृष्टं छन्दसि विधिः etc.)  
व्यतिहारः / व्यतीहारः ( वि + अति + हृ + घञ् ), the optional form having उपसर्गस्य घञीति। ६। ३। १२२। इति दीर्घः। exchange of activity or mutual homogeneous action. If this criterion is not suitable, should we follow the way of: यथादृष्टं छन्दसि विधिः?

> On second thoughts, I was only partly correct. :) The आत्मनेपद form > जयते is a valid Paninian form only if there is कर्मव्यतिहार. This is > from the Sutra कर्त्तरि कर्मव्यतिहारे (1-3-14). So if there is विनिमय > (following काशिका) or परस्परकरणम् (following पदमञ्जरी) जयते can > result. Both happen in modern sports. One can have विनिमय if Ishant > Sharma scores a century or Virendra Sehwag takes a five-wicket haul to > help India win a match. Then we may say ईशान्तो जयते / वीरेन्द्रो जयते > / इशान्तवीरेन्द्रौ जयेते. परस्परकरणम् can occur if two individuals or > teams win for each other in order to make them qualify for or face a > weaker opponent in knock-out round when group ranking or net run > rate/net goal deficit is a criterion. परस्परकरणम् can also occur if > two individuals or teams keep winning over each other as John Isner > and Nicolas Mahut did for 136 continuous games in Wimbledon 2010.
