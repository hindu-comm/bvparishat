Dear Prof. Bhattacharya and Prof. Narang,  
The word being discussed is interesting.As I find it the U.nAdi  
sUtra-s of pA.nini don't mention this but the  
Siddhahemas'abdAnus'Asana in it's U.nAdi considers the word  
's'akandhU' at 4.97 : ' s'aker andhU.h /' : s'[kl.r.mt](http://kl.r.mt) s'aktau  
ityasmAt andhU.h pratyayo bhavati / s'akandhU.h vanaspati.h  
devatAvis'e.sas' ca / The word is here in the feminine only.The  
etymology given is like this : s'aknoti Arogya.m vardhayitum -  
vanaspati.h /  
s'akyate samupAsitum  
i.s.ta-kAma-siddhau - devatA-vis'e.sa.h /

Then it could not be only dung-smoke.And which deity does it signify ?  
Is such a name mentioned anywhere in the Vedic mythology or Jaina or  
other tradition as of a certain deity ?  
vidvA.msa.h pramA.nam /
