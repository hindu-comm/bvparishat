I think it is mistaken spelling for material cause which is used for उपादानकारण. which is translatedproximate cause also. derived from matter out of which the effect (kaarya) is produced. and not maternal (?)

  

कारण is the term in general, denoting the cause, whether material or efficient. (nimitta) कारण. Nimittakaarana is translated as n. instrumental or efficient cause (esp. the Deity as the agent in creation).

  

समवायिकारण is translated asinseparable or inherent connection , material or substantial cause (in the Vedaanta) is more commonly used .
