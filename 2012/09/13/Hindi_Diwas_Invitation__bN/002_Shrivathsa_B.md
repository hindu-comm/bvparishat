राष्ट्रभक्ताः समर्हन्ति राष्ट्रभाषां निषेवितुम् ।

खविस्तारं हि जानन्ति द्विजा न कूपभेकपाः ॥

The following is my Tiikaa on your shloka:*  
*संस्कृतभक्ता राष्ट्रभक्ता इत्याह राष्ट्रभक्ता इति। संस्कृतम् राष्ट्रभाषा व्यवहारभाषा च भवेदित्याह राष्ट्रभाषामिति। हिन्दीभाषाया इदानींतन स्थानं देवभाषया अलङ्करणीया। तदर्थं सर्वैः यतनीया इत्याशायां आह निषेवितुमिति। संस्कृतसंस्थानेषु हिन्दीभाषां प्रेरयन्तः राष्ट्रैक्यं साधयितुं न पारन्तीत्याशायामाह खेति। हिन्दीवादी शब्दं अनूद्य आह कूपेति। यथा कूपादिस्थानेषु प्राप्यमाणा मण्डूकाः वर्षर्तौ स्वीयशब्देन अन्यान् मनुष्यादीन् बाधन्ति, तथैव हिन्दीवादिनः सर्वकारकृत धनवर्षेषु संस्कृतसंस्थानेऽपि हिन्दीभाषां भृषमारोपयन्तीति योजना। हि शब्दो अवधारणार्थकः॥  
  
संस्कृत संस्थान को हिन्दीस्थान बनाने चले नादान  
पूछे गये जब नाम दिये इसे परमातृसम्मान।  
Some (by their misdirected actions and some others by their promotion of the latter, viz. the kartaa and preritaa) want the samskR^ita saMsthAna to become a hindii sthaana. When asked why, they gave it a name viz. "the act of respecting someone else's mother".  
  
संस्कृत के भक्तो को कहे प्रान्तवादी म्लान  
प्रान्तगिरा बढाने नही संस्कृत की संस्थान॥  
lit: (They don't stop at this, when others ask them why hindii divas in samskR^ita samsthAna), they call the saMskR^ita bhaktas by names of "prAntavAdii" (regionalist) and think of them as "mlaana" (sad). (Hence, to dispel their mistaken notions,) We wish to assert that the saMsthAna built for saMskR^ita is not there to promote regional languages (hindi in this case).  
comments: According to the hindiivaadis, if you are a "hindii vaadii" (promoter of hindii in an organization dedicated to saMskR^itam), you are not a praantavaadii, if you are not a saMskR^itavaadii (who want the saMsthaana to concentrate on activities concerning saMskR^itam alone), you are. Apart from questioning the relevance of hindii divas, the questioners never asked for any other divas in the saMsthAna, but the hindiivaadis want to attribute motives and rail down their opponents.  
  
पर माता पुष्ट हो जब धन दौलत से महान  
कहो उसे फिर क्यू जरूरी अन्य घर मे स्थान।  
lit: when the "paramAtA" is well fed through immense wealth (ergo, having numerous homes of her own), why does she want a place in somebody else's home?  
comments: When the "paramAtA" (hindii) is well fed by government grants, and promoted so greatly in every government establishment (every govt. establishment has a wing to promote hindii), why does she want a place in somebody else's home (samskR^itasamsthAna).  
  
यह जो पूछे नीति सम्मत उसे करे अवमान  
अद्भुत द्विज तैवान का अद्भुत भोली बखान॥  
lit: when one raises this logical and just question, he is railed down. O! wondrous is this "dvija" ("high flying bird") of Taiwan, wondrous is its innocent prattle.  
comments: some of the hindiivaadis work in taiwan. through their "high flying" skills they has the capabilty to "jump" into conclusions and attribute motives to their opponents.  
  
तिरुपति लड्डु खाओ पृथक करो पृथक सौस आदान  
मिलाने चले इनको क्यू तुम इस मे कौन सी शान॥  
मिलाने चले इनको क्यू तुम इस मे कौन सी शान॥  
lit: eat tirupati laDDu separately, and eat sauce (tomato sauce) separately, why do you wish to mix them up, what glory is in it (for you)?  
comments: here, samskR^itam is likened to tirupati laDDu and hindii to tomato sauce. former being difficult to get and divine in taste, latter being easily available and tangy in taste. both have their own places (something that the hindiivaadi seems to have missed in his hurry to attribute motives to, and rain expletives on, his opponents).

  
  
svasti,
