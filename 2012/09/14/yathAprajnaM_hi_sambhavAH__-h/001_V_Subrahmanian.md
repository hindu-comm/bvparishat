Namaste  
  
The गोपालयतीन्द्रटीका for the above bhashyam says: यथाविज्ञानं शरीरग्रहणे प्रमाणमाह - यथेति । प्रज्ञामनतिक्रम्ययथाप्रज्ञम् । सम्भवन्त्युत्पद्यन्ते इति सम्भवाः प्रजा इति शेषः । \[The is the explanation this Acharya gives to the shruti cited by Shankara in the bhashyam: "यथाप्रज्ञं हि सम्भवाः".\]

  
There is yet another shruti passage: 'तं विद्याकर्मणी समन्वारभेते *पूर्वप्रज्ञा च*' ' Br.Up. 4.4.2 which also teaches the dependence of the next birth on the stock of karma, upAsanAjnAnam and pUrvavAsanA. The shAnkara bhashyam for the above cited passage is: विद्या सर्वप्रकारा विहिता प्रतिषिद्धा चाविहिताऽप्रतिषिद्धा च । तथा कर्म विहितं प्रतिषिद्धं चाविहितप्रतिषिद्धं च समन्वारभेते सम्यगन्वारभेते अन्वालभेते अनुगच्छतः *पूर्वप्रज्ञा च* पूर्वानुभूतविषया प्रज्ञापूर्वप्रज्ञातीतकर्मफलानुभववासनेत्यर्थः ।  
  
Thus there are three things that follow a person and determine what kind of birth he will get next: vidyA = the kind of knowledge he has gained, both prescribed and prohibited. karma = the acts done, both prescribed and prohibited. pUrvaprajnA = the tendencies generated by experiencing the fruits of actions of the past.  
  
The term 'shrutam' of the KaThopanishad corresponds to the term 'vidyA' of the Br.Up.
