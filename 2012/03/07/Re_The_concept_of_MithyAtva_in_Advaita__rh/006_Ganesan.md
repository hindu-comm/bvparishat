Obviously there is no refutaion of tantrics and saakta-s here.  
This adhikaraNa is mainly for the refutation of kevalanimittakaaraNa view of some Paasupata-s (Paatanjala Yoga also included, as Samkara says here that Isvara is considered to be a 'purushavisesha' which is a typical Paatanjala view: Vide the Yogasuutra--क्लेशकर्मविपाकाशयैरपरामृष्टः पुरुषविशेष ईश्वरः ।) Probably there is a confusion here in Samkara's presentation of the opponent's views. It requires further research. For, this is not the view of Pashupata-s or the Paancharthika-s, a sub-school of the former.  
What are the 'specific' doctrines of these-tantrics and saakta-s that Samkara had "smashed" ? That is the question.  
This type of statement " Samkara had smashed the tantrics and saakta-s" resembles that one finds in the legends such as the Samkaravijaya, etc. One should be very cautious in issuinng such statements.  
Here we are discussing purely the doctrines based on the generally accepted texts composed by the acharya-s.
