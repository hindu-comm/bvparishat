Dear Dr. Sati Shankar Dutta,  
  

Moreover to the previous mail may I add these also:  
धम्मं पस्सति, सो मं पस्सति; यो मं पस्सति, सो धम्मं पस्सति। धम्मञ्हि, वक्कलि, पस्सन्तो मं पस्सति ति (सं॰ नि॰ ३.८७)  
The Sanjuttanikaaya (3.87) says: dhammam passati, so mam passati; yo maam passati, so dhammam passati, dhammanhi, Vakkali, passanto mam passati ti. (Sanskrit: धर्मं पश्यति, स मां पश्यति, यो मां पश्यति, स धर्मं पश्यति। धर्मज्ञ! वक्कलि! पश्यन्तो मां पश्यति इति। English: whoever sees Dharma, he sees me, whoever sees me, he sees Dharma, O the Knower of Dharma! Mr. Vakkali! those who are see, they see me too.)  
So, in Pali, मं (mam) is for me, in Sanskrit it is मां (maam), ultimately, there is no difference between seeing the Buddha and the Dharma.  
Regards,  
Narayanan
