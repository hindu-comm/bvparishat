Frankly I do not the meaning meant in the context of the epithet रङ्गनाथ, which may be रङ्गस्य नाथः as a तत्पुरुष and the word रङ्ग having many meanings literally and literally, any meaning suitable can be applied without any consensus of context:

  

नीलाब्जवर्णे भुजपूर्णकर्णे कर्णान्तनेत्रे कमलाकलत्रे ।

श्रीमल्लरङ्गे जितमल्लरङ्गे श्रीरङ्गरङ्गे रमतां मनो मे ॥२॥  

  

as can be seen in the above used 4 times, one of them is definitely श्रीरङ्ग.

  

लक्ष्मीं क्षीरसमुद्र राज तनयां*श्रीरङ्ग-*धामेश्वरीम् ।

दासीभूतसमस्तदेववनितां लोकैकदीपाङ्कुराम् ।

श्रीमन्मन्धकटाक्षलब्धविभवब्रह्मेन्द्रगङ्गाधराम् ।

त्वां त्रैलोक्यकुटुम्बिनीं सरसिजां वन्दे मुकुन्दप्रियाम् ॥  

  

In the above verse, श्रीरङ्ग can be the temple of श्रीरङ्ग, who again the above of श्रीसहितरङ्ग, either the dancing stage of लक्ष्मी, who is residing in the chest of Sri Vishnu. in other words, विष्णु himself. In any case, व्युत्पत्ति or उत्पत्ति has to be sought according to the context.

  

रज्यति अस्मिन् इति रङ्गः - रञ्ज् + घ - etymologicaly it has nothing to add to the epithet, leaving to the attributes for the राग by लक्ष्मी his beloved or his devotees.

  

Futher it can be explained, according to the वैष्णव literature, which I do not have access to properly explain the significance of the word.
