Friends:



 It is best to ignore Michael Witzel and his colleagues' outbursts. His work on India is his life's work of nearly 50 years and not all that impressive for that. I am primarily an industrial scientist who is devoted to correcting the disortions that scholars like him have created.



 Little over a month ago, I was in Daniel Nocera's lab (just moved from MIT to Harvard). They are doing important work on generation and storage of solar energy which will be of monumental consequence to the world in our time. I covered it in two articles that appeared in FOLKS and will be appearing in other places.



 This is far more important and affects many more lives than what anyone's opinion may be about what happened 5000 years ago.



 Actually personalities don't matter-- be it Witzel, Rajaram or whoever else. What matters is what we leave behind.



 Even in the work I have done in history, I am gratified by the response especiallyfrom young people. Here is the latest response from one of my publishers:



".... has offered to publish articles written by you on Folks Magazine in their teaching materials in German. I have given them permission with a condition that they'll send in copies of the published material when ready. They've accepted the same. I will forward you the same as soon as I have it."



 So the youth in Germany will have access to our findings, written in a sober tone without polemics. so let us all do the best we can.



 I thank everyone for the support, but we are doing well.



Sincere regards,

N.S. Rajaram
