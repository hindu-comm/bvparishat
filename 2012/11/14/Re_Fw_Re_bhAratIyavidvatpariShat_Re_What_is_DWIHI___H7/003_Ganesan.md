Here it is not the question about the name of the Upanishad.  
Here the discussion is on the interpretation of the mantra, ऋतं सत्यं . . . for which I showed one of the earliest Bhashya-s of Bhatta Bhaskara.  
  
It is the reasonable and accepted principle that any sincere student of the Veda gives importance to the earliest commentators. Accordingly Bhatta Bhaskara is one of the earlierst Pre-Sayana commentators who preceded all the later acharya-s of the Vaishnava schools.  
There are many such later commentaries on this and other part of the Upanishads which interpret clear Saiva mantra-s as applying to Vishnu.  
  
As it was remarked some time back, there is a commentary coming from a Maadhva Acharya which interprets the five Brahma mantra-s of this Mahanarayanopanishad--the pa\~ncabrahmamantra-s--as applying to Vishnu.  
  
Are we taking such commentaries as PramaaNa ??  
  
I am only pointing out the interpretations of these mantra-s by such earlier commentators as Bhatta Bhaskara.
