*स्मार्ताः यत्किमपि वैदिकानुष्ठानं प्रारभन्ते तत्संकल्पे ’ममोपात्तसनस्तदुरितक्षयद्वारा श्रीपरमेश्वरप्रीत्यर्थम्.....कर्म करिष्ये’ इति वाक्योच्चारणं कुर्वन्ति । परन्तु तदन्ते ’कायेन वाचा......श्रीमन्नारायणायेति समर्पयामि’ इति समापयन्ति । अनेन ज्ञायते तेषां संप्रदाये शिवविष्ण्वोः समानस्थानमिति । *सन्ध्यावन्दनेऽपि उपस्थानसमये ऋतं सत्यमित्यादिमन्त्रः कृष्णवर्णलक्षितविष्णोः पिङ्गलवर्णलक्षितशिवस्य च एकशरीरवर्तित्वस्तुतितया* ,*  
  
  
  
Whether the Smarta tradition gives equal importance to Siva and Vishnu or not is a different question. As it appears, in spite of such references as cited above, the smarta tradition is more inclined to Vaishnavism only which can be proved with numerous expamples which is altogether a different point.  
  
What is of importance here is:  
The word कृष्ण in the compound कृष्णपिङ्गल (in the mantra, ऋतं सत्यं परं ब्रह्म पुरुषं कृष्णपिङ्गलम् । . . .) does not at all mean that "Vishnu and Siva reside in the same body". Far from it . !!  
To clearly understand the meaning of this Mantra of the Taittiriiya AraNyaka, one should consult the Bhatta Bhaskara's valuable Bhashya on this mantra and others of this portion.  
Bhatta Bhaskara clearly states " ऋतं . . . ऋतीयते विश्व्मनुगृह्णाति इत्यृतं शिवैकस्वभावम् । परं सर्वस्मादुत्कृष्टं विश्वोत्तीर्णं ब्रह्म . . . पुरुषं पूरकं सर्वस्य चेतयमानं कृष्णपिङ्गलं देहपरिजिघृक्षायां स्त्रीपुंसशबलाकारं परशक्त्यविनाभूतं . . . विरूपाक्षं विलक्षणलोचनं
ईदृशं परं ब्रह्म भवति ।  
  
Thus this mantra beautifully portrays the Ardhanaariisvara form of Siva; just by the occurrance of the word कृष्ण one cannot jump to the conclusion that it refers to Vishnu. Here the context is the 'colour' and 'form' of the Para Brahma. One's own views and theories cannot and should not be foisted upon the Veda. There are texts and traditions on the subject of interpreting Veda Mnatra-s.  
If the word कृष्ण denotes Vishnu, then on what grounds the word पिङ्गल
is interpreted as denoting Siva ?? कृष्ण generally denotes only the black colour and here also the same.  
Where do we come across पिङ्गल denoting Siva ? Which NIghanTu or dictionary gives such a meaning ?? No commentator including SayaNa gives such a meaning for these two words.  
कृष्णपिङ्गल goes along with ऋतं सत्यम्--the pair and the pair here is the divine one, ardhanaariisvara.  
So as clearly explained by Bhatta Bhaskara, कृष्णपिङ्गल can mean only the ardhanaariisvara form of Siva. Lest any over zealous person should interpret wrongly कृष्णपिङ्गल as Half-Siva Half-Vishnu form, the Veda mantra adds finally विरूपाक्ष, which epithet applies only to Siva and to no other deity under any circumstance.
