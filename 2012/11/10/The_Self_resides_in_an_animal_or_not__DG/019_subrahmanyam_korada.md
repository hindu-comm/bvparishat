नमो विद्वद्भ्यः

सत् / ब्रह्मन् manifests into many things - which are असत् / मिथ्या  
(unreal) due to its capacity called माया - कालः , देशः etc are just  
others forms of माया / मायाशक्ति - शक्ति and द्रव्यम् are one and the  
same- it is the श्क्ति , that is there in the sword, cutting the tree,  
rather than sword itself.

Why should ब्रह्मन् venture upon such a task ? In order to see that  
every 'thing' (creature) faces its कर्म (पुण्यम् / पापम्) ।

Due to कर्म only human beings are able to perform more activities ,  
especially related to बुद्धि (intellect) compared to तिर्यक् ( animals  
, birds etc) .

Even among human beings , some cannot read with the help of eyes as  
they are blind due to पूर्वजन्मकर्मपरिपाक ।

How come असत् like a stone emerges out of सत् ?  
शंकराचार्य says it is not uncommon - गोमयात् वृश्चिकः जायते ।

A modern स्वामी explains - Hydrogen is flammable , Oxygen helps  
burning - both form into water that goes against fire .

There are two meanings of चैतन्यम् -- ब्रह्मन् , the very cause of  
Universe and the चैतन्यम् / consciousness .  
So everything in the universe is an असद्रूप of ब्रह्मन् ।

The second kind of चैतन्यम् is noticed in human beings, animals , trees etc..

सांख्यदर्शनम् runs after असत्कार्यवाद - सत् from असत् - शंकराचार्य  
questions - कथम् असतः सत् जायेत ?

In any case both with / without चैतन्यम् (living and non-living  
things) undergo change (षड् भावविकाराः - निरुक्तम्) ।

The issue - ’सर्वं चेतनावत्’ - of आत्माद्वैतदर्शनम् is widely  
discussed in महाभाष्यम् and commentaries -- taking both living and  
non-living things --

पाणिनि rules सन्प्रत्यय in the sense of - इच्छा = one wants it --

धातोः कर्मणः समानकर्तृकात् इच्छायां वा 3-1-7

Katyayana ammends - आशङ्कायाम् अचेतनेषूपसंख्यानम् ( in case of doubt  
the suffix can be added to roots , which are non-living)-this is to  
cover examples like -

अश्मा लुलुठिषति (the stone wants to roll)

कूलं पिपतिषति ( the shore / bank wants to fall)

Patanjali explains - Katyayana thinks that only living things can have  
इच्छा / wish .

He adds - not necessary to say ' non-living' - for example - श्वा  
मुमूर्षति ( the dog wishes to die) - here, in fact, although  
suffering from some disease the dog being an animal does not wish to  
die rather wishes to live longer - dog is a living thing .

Then Katyayana offers another amendment -- सर्वस्य वा चेतनावत्त्वात्  
(since everything has got चेतना / चैतन्यम्) ।  
Patanjali elaborates --

अथ वा सर्वं चेतनावत् । एवं हि आह --

कंसकाः सर्पन्ति ( the dolls of Kamsa are walking),

शिरीषो’यं स्वपिति ( the Sirisa-tree is sleeping - when the leaves and  
flowers are contracted then this usage)

सुवर्चला आदित्यम् अनुपर्येति ( Suvarcala is the wife of Sun , which  
is nothing but a ball of fire, she follows him)

आस्कन्द कपिलक इत्युक्ते तृणम् आस्कन्दति , ( a small Kapila cow grabs  
the blade of grass if offered)

ऋषिः पठति = Veda says (this is also considered as an example) or the  
quotation from Veda --

शृणोत ग्रावाणः (O ! stones ! listen )

Kaiyata explains - आत्माद्वैतदर्शनेन इति भावः । ऋषिणेति । वेदः  
सर्वभावानां चैतन्यं प्रतिपादयति इत्यर्थः।

Bhartrhari in Brahmakanda of Vakyapadiyam (127) says that - it is the  
शब्दः, in the form of चितन्यम् only that creates an urge in the  
human beings to do any activity and once the चैतन्यम् retires from the  
body the latter looks like a dry stick or a wall -

अर्थक्रियासु वाक् सर्वान् समीहयति देहिनः।  
तदुत्क्रान्तौ विसंज्ञो’यं दृश्यते काष्ठकुड्यवत् ॥

Samkhyas generally take asylum behind - आविर्भाव (revealed) and  
तिरोभाव (consealed) '

In Indian scriptures there is no God (ईश्वरः / विष्णुः) separately  
from ब्रह्मन् ।

One can perceive Brahman thru योगिप्रत्यक्षम् ।
