Dear Parishad Members,

The Roman diacritics for Sanskrit are not IPA approved but developed by Indologists and accepted by consensus (Sir William Jones onward through Whitney, Hillebrandt, Oldenberg, Macdonell etc but not the *SBE* of Maxmüller) in the nineteenth century with minor modifications (*e.g.* ś for ç) in the twentietrh. Minor variations like the employment of both ṃ and ṁ for anusvāra still continue.

Almost all the signs are Unicode/Windows 7/MS Word 2010 compatible. There is still one deficiency that is the non-recognition of the vocalic  with a ring below r (written ṛ in India with a dot below r). The ring below r that is  is available with Gandhari Unicode but, its Unicode compatibility is not clear to me. The r with a ring below is often printed like a Chinese syllable in internet postings. This Gandhari Unicode is downloadable and employable with MS Word 2010 in Windows7. For internet transactions in, say, the BVP the dot below r is safer.

The diacritic signs will be available in the Symbol repertory that opens with the Insert tab in MS Word. Many signs have built in shortcut keys; some have to be individually built up by a process that is self-evident from the repertory itself. Once the shortcut keys have been built up, it is easy to write Sanskrit in Roman.

Please indicate if this helped.
