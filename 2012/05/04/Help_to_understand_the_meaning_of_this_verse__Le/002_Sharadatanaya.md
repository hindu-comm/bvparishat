\> This verse in the metre arya is from the Hitopadesa, but not from the Pancatantra, I'm afraid. It is the sixteenth verse, as it reads:  
\> गुणिगणगणनारम्भे  
\> न पतति कठिनी ससम्भ्रमाद् यस्य।  
\> तेनाम्बा यदि सुतिनी  
\> वद बन्ध्या कीदृशी भवति॥१६॥  
\> While one notes the group of the people having quality, the chalk will not fall from whose hand out of excitement (he who has born in a good clan, who is never treated as dead). So, if the mother is having a son, then tell how the mother can be a barren woman.  
\>  
\> The idea of the bracketed portion in the meaning of sixteenth verse has been taken from the fifteenth verse, as follows:  
\>  
\> स जातो येन जातेन याति वंशः समुन्नतिम्।  
\> परिवर्तिनि संसारे मृतः को वा न जायते॥१५॥
