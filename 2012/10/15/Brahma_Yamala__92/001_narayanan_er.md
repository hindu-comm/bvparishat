Dear Dr. Raghavendra Bhat,  
Namskaromi. a (100) + cū (70) + 2 = 172 is the Samvat year of copying the text. The Prajñāpāramitā manuscript of the Buddhists, for instance has been copied in Samvat year 272, and it reads Samvat ā cū 2; that means it is 272.  
In the case of the Brahmayāmala manuscript the value of 'a' is 100 and cū is 70 and adding 2 makes 172.  
In the case of Prajñāpāramitā manuscript the value of ā is 200 and cū is 70 and adding 2 makes 272.  
So, it looks like the equations:  
Samvat a (100) + cū (70) + 2 = 172 māghaśuklāṣṭamyāṃ ādityadine....................likhitam.  
Samvat ā (200) + cū (70) + 2 = 272 vaiśākyasuklapaurṇṇamasyāṃ ādityadine .........likhitam. Here likhitam means copied, but not composed (racitam/viracitam). Similarly, the Germans ([catalogue.ngmcp.uni-hamburg.de](http://catalogue.ngmcp.uni-hamburg.de)) recorded the manuscripts. As we see:  
Samvat a (100) + cū (70) + 3 = 173 mahābhārate śatasāhasryāṃ saṃhitāyāṃ dānadharmeṣu vaiṣṇave dharmaśāstre pañcadaśamo ʼdhyāyaḥ parisamāptaḥ. And this entry is for the Mahābhārata; Anuśāsanaparvan; Dānadharma.  
Ironically, they put the number straight way in the case of the Cāndravyākaraṇa:  
śreyo ʼstu samvat 377 kārttikakṛṣṇacaturthasyāṃ(!) \|\| maṅgalavāśare\|\| śubham astuḥ(!), but we expect the pattern of:  
i (300) + cū (70) + 7 = 377 which we hardly see.  
Regards,  
Narayanan
