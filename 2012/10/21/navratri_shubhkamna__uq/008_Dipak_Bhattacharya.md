Dear Colleagues,

The views expressed by Shri Trivedi spring from some common misconception. It is not a fact that Sanskrit has any special relation with Devnagari. Devnagari was adopted in the nineteenth century by Western scholars as the script in which Sanskrit works should be printed. The use of Devnagari (the name too is not very old) for Sanskrit spread in India because of this Western measure. Before that the Assamese-Bengali- Maithili script, Nagari, Gujarati, Grantha, Javanese, Kannaḍ, Malayalam, Nandi Nagari (related to Nagari), Newari (related to the ABM mentioned above), Oriya, Śāradā,Telugu (closely related to Kannaḍ)and several local variations not mentioned here (eg., Karaṇa in Orissa), were used for Sanskrit according to region. The ABM-Newari combine and Nagari were most wide-spread but the complex character of the former proved obstacles to its adoption as the preferred script.

The matter has been discussed but I do not remember where.

Best
