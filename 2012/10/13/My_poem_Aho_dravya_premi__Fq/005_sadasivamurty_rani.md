Dear Nityanand Misra Ji!  
Thanks for your kind appreciation. It gives me immense pleasure for being supported by you all.  
As you observed in the said verse  
क्रोधस्य वा स्यात्प्रणयस्य वा स्यात्  
उद्रिक्ततामेयविधा हि विद्यते ।  
क्रोधस्य सा ह्यात्मविनाशहेतुः  
प्रेम्णस्तु सौन्दर्यसुखाधिभूमिः ॥  
there are three feet of Indravajra and one of Indravamsa. It was not a typo It was not intentional too. But after composing that sloka I observed it and allowed it into the stream. All of us know that there are Upajati compositions with Indravajra and Upendra vajra. But in my personal experience and personal collection from many ancient poets I have other kinds of upajatis with combinations of : Mandakranta and Sardulavikridita, Sardulavikridita and Sragdhara, Sikharini and Sardulavikridita and so on. Hence I did not stop my sloka from the flow. For your appreciation I here give a sloka from the ancient Ramastotras:  
  

रामं लक्ष्मणपूर्वजं रघुवरं सीतापतिं सुन्दरं (शा.वि)

काकुत्स्थं करुणार्णवं गुणनिधिं विप्रप्रियं धार्मिकम्।(शा.वि)

राजेन्द्रं सत्यसन्धं दशरथतनयं श्यामलं शान्तमूर्तिं (स्रग्धरा)

वन्देलोकाभिरामं रघुकुलतिलकं राघवं रावणारिम्॥ (स्रग्धरा)

Here we can observed the combination of Sardula vikriditam and Sragdhara. I have some scores of slokas of this kind in my collection. Surprisingly Upajati of a short metre and long metre also was permitted in ancient poetry.

This is just for your information. I most of my writings I consider such compositions as the instances of upajati.. But I never compose them intentionally.  

**Dr. Rani Sadasiva Murty**

Dear Dr. Rani Sadasiva Murthy  
  
Thanks for sharing the moving poem. Very touching indeed.  
  
I have a question on the eighteenth verse  
  
क्रोधस्य वा स्यात्प्रणयस्य वा स्यात्  
उद्रिक्ततामेयविधा हि विद्यते ।  
क्रोधस्य सा ह्यात्मविनाशहेतुः  
प्रेम्णस्तु सौन्दर्यसुखाधिभूमिः ॥  
  
The verse has 45 syllables. The first, third and fourth feet have 11 syllables conforming to the इन्द्रवज्रा metre. The second foot (उद्रिक्ततामेयविधा हि विद्यते) has 12 syllables conforming to the इन्द्रवंशा metre.  
  
Is this intended? Or is it a typo? Either दृष्टा or प्रोक्ता in place of विद्यते would make it an इन्द्रवज्रा.  
  
Thanks, Nityanand
