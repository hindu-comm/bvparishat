> I still think your first rendition रात्रीष्वासु नवासु देवि नवसू > रक्षाशु सत्सङ्गतान् is still grammatically correct where नवसू can be > parsed as नवसु + उ. The indeclinable उ is used as a पूरक and also to > call out to somebody, among other meanings which inslude anger, grace, > order, agreement, interrogation, et cetera. I am not completely sure > though as some meanings of उ are admissible only in the Vedas.  
>   
> What I do know for sure is that उ to call out to somebody is a valid > use in Laukika - the usage by Kalidasa is Pramana here - उ मेति मात्रा > तपसो निषिद्धा पश्चादुमाख्यां सुमुखी जगाम, where उ is used to call out > to somebody. As you have देवि in vocative, the अन्वय can then be उ > देवि, आसु नवासु नवसु रात्रीषु सत्सङ्गतान् आशु रक्ष which makes > complete sense to me. Dr. Bhat and other scholars can confirm if this > is correct.  
>   
> Having said that, I would say the second reading रात्रीष्वासु नवासु > देवि नवसु त्रायस्व सत्सङ्गतान् does sounds much more mellifluous with > dental consonants in each and every pada. So instead of saying the > first rendition was incorrect and the second was correct (I am not > qualified enough to say so), I would say that while both are correct, > your प्रज्ञा has done complete Kripa on the second rendition, the > Kripa was somewhat lacking in the first one.
