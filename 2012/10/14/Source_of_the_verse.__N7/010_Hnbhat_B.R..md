I think this is a possible combination.

  

पञ्चकं यः समरेन्नित्यं ,,, but the sentence will be left incomplete, without the relative clause, beginning with सः ... etc.

  

The same with the other reading also:

  

पञ्चकन्याः स्मरेन्नित्यं महापातकनाशनम्।

  

is wanting a complete sentence. Either

  

\[यत्\]स्मरेन्नित्यं\[तत्\] महापातकनाशनम्। with अध्याहार of the relative terms,

  

or स्मरेन्नित्यं महापातकनाश(नीः) or ना(शिनीः) to get a complete meaning.

  

The other reading

  

पञ्चकं ना स्मरेन्नित्यं महापातकनाशनम्।

  

is a very simple sentence, with injunction to remember the group of five (G5) which answers the recent question of Dr. Vedia also.

  

If the five are considered as G5, then the question does not arise whether they are सती-s or not, but are mandatorily considered as the प्रातःस्मरणीय on the words of पुराण.

  

  

If one questions their सतीत्व, then in spite of the mandate implied in the पुराण one cannot include them in theप्रातःस्मरणीय list.



Depends on your belief and understanding of सतीत्व.
