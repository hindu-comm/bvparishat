Respected Professor Iyengar,  
Along with your initial posting of this particular thread to the BVP, I find that you put a CC to me too. I feel blessed that you are remembering me because of, I am having an interest in Astronomy, I have to learn many a number of things from you regarding this subject. Thanks a lot for sending this mail on स्वातिः or स्वाती constellation, particularly on the term निष्ट्या which could be etymologized as from the root ष्ट्यै शब्दसंघातयोः। (भ्वादि० परस्मै० अकर्म० अनिट्) ष्ट्यायति (स्त्यायति) लोकः एकत्र समूहायते शब्दायते वा। Having नि as pre-posed, then नितरां स्त्यायति संघातरूपेण सह वर्तत इति निष्ट्या। यद्वा निर्गत्य शरीरात् स्त्यायति विस्तीर्णो भवतीति निष्ट्या पुत्री। यद्वा निर्गतो वर्णाश्रमेभ्यो निष्ट्या चण्डालस्त्री। The eminent scholars like Professor H.N. Bhat etc. have already explained the other derivations of निस् इत्यत अव्ययात् त्यप् (४। २। १०४) इत्यस्य निसो गते इति वार्तिकोक्त्या त्यप्। 'ह्रस्वात्तादौ तद्धिते (८। ३। १०१) इति षत्वम् etc. The possibility of meanings are: constellation (group of stars), daughter (one coming out of body), the caṇḍāla lady (one beyond the cast and creed) etc. Apart from these the general meanings for the term निष्ट्या there are meanings like: external, strange and foreign etc. (MMWilliams), depending upon the action of coming out or nirgamanam. Here in the case of the meaning of strange may be due to the grouping (समूहायते with or without the help of the meaning of the preposition नि-extremely).  
Moreover, regarding the caṇḍāla lady (beyond the cast and creed)-the context of निष्ट्या suits to the Dharamashastras. Regarding the constellation (group of stars) -the context of निष्ट्या suits to the Astronomical significance, as स्वातिः or स्वाती is a constellation of stars. Then, daughter (one coming out of body)- the context of निष्ट्या suits to the science of life. If we take स्त्यायति शब्दायते (one makes sound) then निष्ट्या can be the whole human speech system recommended by the Paniniyashiksha etc. But it is up to the scholars for its approval.  
Regards,  
Narayanan
