Dear Scholars,

There seems to be a confusion between the two words; one word is \`andhra' and the other is \`AANDHRA'.

There is difference in pronunciation: one beginning with short \`a' the other beginning witha long \`a' i.e., aa.(A).

The first named may be the hunters who make the animals blind. But the second one has no connection to this. They are those who speak Telugu, the language of ANDHRAS.``

Respected Swamiji,  
Namaskar. Etymologically Andhra is hunter. Hunters make the animals blind by through their songs.  
अन्ध्रः। पुं। (अन्धयति गीतेन मृगान् अन्धवत् करोति अन्ध+कर्तरि रः।) व्याधः। मृगयाजीवी।  
Kalidasa says: उद्गास्यतामिच्छति किन्नराणां तानप्रदायित्वमिवोपगन्तुम्। (Kumarasambhava.1-9). Kinnara is mountaineer hunter.  
Manu says:  
कारावरो निषादात्तु चर्मकारः प्रसूयते।  
वैदेहिकादन्ध्रमेदौ बहिर्ग्रामप्रतिश्रयौ।। (मनुस्मृतिः १०.३६)  
If the man of a mixed low caste, is being born, from mountaineer is a worker in leather. Both Andhra and Meda, being born as the sons of (Vaidehika-from the term Videha) Vaishyas by Brahmin women, are refugees outside the village.  
Regards,  
Narayanan
