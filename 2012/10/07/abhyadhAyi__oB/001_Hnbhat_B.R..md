The form is passive voice in the Aoristpast tense लुङ् of the verb अभि+धा - अभि+अधायि - can be used as translated by you in the 2nd option. तेन \[इदम्\] अभ्यधायि, सः - As the verb is carrying the double object द्विकर्मक,

  

स तमिदमभ्यधात् - तेनेदमभ्यधायि सः - It may not be possible to translate literally word by word in English as both languages have their own patterns.

  

He told him this. - This was told to him. will convey in a way the transformations.

  

In the above formation, it is परस्मैपद in the active, अभ्यधात् - and आत्मनेपद in the passive voice अभ्यधायि. This is the transformation of the same verb in परस्मैपद. And for your information, the root अभि+धा is उभयपदी, and can be used in both usages. In that case, अभ्यधित in the past tense.
