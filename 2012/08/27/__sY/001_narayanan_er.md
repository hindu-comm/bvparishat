Respected Sir,

The kernel of the Śatakas is well inclined to the Vedantic doctrines, as the eradication of the karman through the real knowledge. The śāntaṃ tejaḥ of the Nītiśataka is tuned with that of anādinidhanaṃ brahman of the Vākyapadīya. The dispute had been raised by Colebrooke & co. with a pretext that Baṭṭi and Bhatṛhari are the same. The poetic idiom and style of the Śatakas and the Rāvaṇavadha Baṭṭikāvya are separably well identical. The M.R. Kale's edition of the Nītiśataka and Vairāgyaśataka published by Oriental Publishing Co. in 1902 from Bombay is negating the Colebrooke & co. pretexts.

Regards,

Narayanan
