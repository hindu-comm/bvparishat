From the Vedic literature a few 'odd' instances are enumerated here:  

1.  हिरण्यगर्भः समवर्तताग्रे भूतस्य जातः पतिरेक आसीत् । स दाधार पृथ्वीं
    द्यामुतेमां *कस्मै देवाय* हविषा विधेम (Rg.samhitA 10.121.1) This
    mantra is cited by Shankara in the Brahmasutra Bhashyam 1.2.6.23 :
    रूपोपन्यासाच्च. The Ratnaprabha gloss clarifies: कशब्दस्य
    प्रजापतिसंज्ञात्वे सर्वनामत्वाभावेन स्मा इत्ययोगात् *एकारलोपेन
    एकस्मै देवाय* प्राणात्मने हविषा विधेम परिचरेम *इति व्याख्येयम्* ।
2.  In the Aitareya Upanishad 3.13 we have this mantra: स एतमेव पुरुषं
    ब्रह्म ततममपश्यदिदमदर्शमिती...३ (second half only cited here). In
    the shAnkara bhashyam for the word ततमम् we have the explanation:
    *तकारेणैकेन लुप्तेन तततमं* व्याप्ततमं परिपूर्णम्..
3.  In the Chandogya upanishad 5.10.6 (panchAgnividyA context) we have
    these words ...इति जायन्तेऽतो वै खलु दुर्निष्प्रपतरं यो यो...Here
    too for the word *दुर्निष्प्रपतरं* we have in the shAnkara bhashyam
    the explanation - *दुर्निष्प्रपततरं* दुर्निगमतरम् ।
    दुर्निष्प्रपतरमिति तकार एको लुप्तो द्रष्टव्यः । 4.  In similar vein we have the case of IshAvAsya Upanishad 14 where the
    word 'सम्भूतिम्’ is taken to mean ’असम्भूतिम्’ where Shankara says:
    सम्भूतिं च विनाशं च इत्यत्र *अवर्णलोपेन निर्देशो द्रष्टव्यः*
    प्रकृतिलयफलश्रुत्यनुरोधात् .   
5.  In the Yajurveda samhitA there is an expression त्मना. Here the word
    is meant to be understood as आत्मना where the 'आवर्ण' is not in the
    word.  

I heard there are pANini sutras to cover/explain/accommodate such expressions where there are akshara/varNa lopas and other 'irregularities'. There are some shlokas related to the vyAkaraNa shAstra which indicate, as an *upalakShaNa,* several cases where such not-normal usages are justified/accommodated.

While replying to an objection of an Advaitin scholar on Madhwa's commentary on the Isha upanishad, noted Madhwa scholar Dr.Bannanje Govindacharya in his work 'upaniShacchandrikA' cites the following kAshikA verse and asks: do you not remember this?  
  
परादिश्च परान्तश्च पूर्वान्तश्च अपि दृश्यते। पूर्वादयश्च दृश्यन्ते *व्यत्ययो बहुलं* स्मृतः।
