Dear Dr. Mahendar,

Writing "Panch Sahastra Matra" on a cheque leaf will not convey the meaning of "five thousand only" but one has to write: PanchaSahasraMaatram (पञ्चसहस्रमात्रम्). The term सहस्त्र does not make the sense of thousand, but सहस्र will make it. Then Panch ( पञ्च् ) also will not make more meaningful rather than when one use Pancha ( पञ्च ). Then regarding "Matra", as the stem shouldtake a case suffix to have form in neuter as Maatram. The court order certainly can encourage Sanskrit, but there should the usages be language-friendly as well.  

It could have been: पञ्चसहस्रं रुप्यकाण्येव। रुप्यकाणां पञ्चसहस्रमेव। केवलं पञ्चसहस्रं रुप्यकाणि। etc.  

The court should have ordered the Govt. that the free education of elementary Sanskrit ordinals and cardinals should be provided for the Bank officials throughout the country. Every confusion on the cheque details will lead to the withholding especially regarding the clarity of the amount. Thus it has been said:

दुष्टः शब्दः स्वरतो वर्णतो वा

मिथ्याप्रयुक्तो न तमर्थमाह।  

स वाग्वज्रो यजमानं हिनस्ति  

यथेन्द्रशत्रुः स्वरतोऽपराधात्।।  

Regards,

Narayanan
